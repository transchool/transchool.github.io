14. Directions: Give the characteristics of the different types of salads. Main Salad- Appetizer-Side Dish Salad-Dessert Salad-Accompaniment Salad-​


Answer:

Main Salad-Main course salads usually containing a portion of a high-protein foods, such as meat, fish, eggs, legumes, or cheese. Dessert salads sweet salads containing fruit, gelatin, sweeteners or whipped cream.

Appetizer-eaten to pique the appetite or to moderate the hunger stimulated by drink.

Side dish salad-A Side Dish is essentially any food served on the side of the plate or to the side of the main plate. An easy way to think of them is that Side Dishes are accompaniments and not the main course.

Dessert Salad-Dessert salads are dishes made with jellos (jellies), whipped toppings, fruits, vegetables, mayonnaise, and various other ingredients. These salads are served at some buffet and cafeterias, and are also served at potlucks and parties. They can be prepared ahead of time and are transportable.

Accompaniment salad-This salad should contrast pleasantly with the rest of the meal in color, flavor, and texture

16. Briefly discuss the characteristics of the different classification of a salad according to place in the meal. Write your answer on the space provided.1. Side Dish Salad2. Main Course Salad3. Appetizer Salad4. Separate Corse Salad5. Accompaniment Salad​


Classification of Salads According to their place in the Meal

1. Side dish salads

-It should be light and flavorful, not too much vegetable salads are often good choices. Heavier salads such as macaroni or high protein salads containing seafood, cheese are less appropriate, unless the main course is light.

2. Main course salads

-It should be large enough to serve as a full meal and should contain a substantial portion of protein. Meat, poultry and seafood salads as well as egg salad and cheese are popular choices. It should offer enough variety of flavors and textures in addition to the protein and salad platter or fruits.

3. Appetizer Salad

-It stimulate appetite which has fresh, crisp ingredients; tangy flavorful dressing; and attractive, appetizing appearance. It looks appealing because of flavorful foods like cheese, ham, salami shrimp and crabmeat. Crisp raw or lightly cooked vegetables can also be added.

4. Separate course salads

-These salads must be very light without filling. Rich, heavy dressings such as sour cream and mayonnaise should be avoided. Light salad are serve after the main course to cleanse the palate, refresh the appetite and provide a break before dessert.

5. Accompaniment Salads

-It must balance and harmonize with the rest of the meal, like any other side dish. Don't serve potato salad at the same meal at which you are serving French Fries or another starch. Sweet fruit salads are rarely appropriate as accompaniment except with such items as ham or pork.

25. give the characteristics of Accompaniment salad​


Answer:

The little pile of mixed or baby lettuce with some random slivers of veggies on top that comes with your entree is known as an accompaniment salad. It's usually served with the main course of a meal, either on a dinner plate or a salad plate. In terms of color, taste, and texture, this salad should complement the entire meal. Both raw or fried, use crisp greens, fruits, or vegetables.
