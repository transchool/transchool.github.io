1. 15 hand movement and positions in cheerdance ​


BEGINNING STANCE  — Feet together, hands  down by the side in  blades

CHEER STANCE — Feet more than shoulder, width apart, hands down by the side in blades

CLASP — Hands clasped, at the chin, elbows in

CLAP — Hands in blades, at the chin, elbows in

HIGH V — Arms extended up forming a “V”, relax the shoulders

LOW V — Arms extended down  forming a “V”

TOUCHDOWN — Arms extended straight and parallel to each other, facing in

LOW TOUCHDOWN — Arms extended straight down and parallel to each other, facing in

FRONT LUNGE — Lead leg bent with the knee over the ankle, back leg straight, feet perpendicular to each other

BOW AND ARROW —  One arm extended to the side with other arm bent at  elbow in a half “T” motion

OVERHEAD CLASP —  Arms are straight, above the in a clasp and slightly  in front of the face

TABLETOP —  Arms bent at elbow, fists in front of shoulders

LOW CLASP —  Arms extended straight down, in a clasp and slightly in front of the body

PUNCH —  One arm extended straight up, one arm on hip, in a fist

DIAGONAL —  One arm extended in a  high “V” and the other  arm extended in a low “V”

Subtract 2 from the both number of students in cheerdance and chorus to get the number of the one who only took cheerdance and chorus.

18 - 2= 16 only took cheerdance
26-2 = 24 only took chorus

And 2 who took both. To identify the no. of students that are enrolled,

15. Do tensions and extensions of arms are necessary for executing the different cheerdance movements? Why?​


Cheerleading is a sport with a huge variety of elements, different combinations and the like. During a competition, there are basic things considered in a cheer routine. Arm motions, stunts, jumps and tumblings are main factors that could contribute a lot on the scorecard. Basic elements are simply the arm lines and the synchronization and uniformity of the team when executing a routine. Arm motions are very important in cheerleading, because it is also a basis for the synchronization of the squad. They are supposed to be executed sharply and tightly. These movements must have power but not too much power that you’ll go over board. How to know that you are doing it right is when you could still see your fists in your peripherals, when someone tries to knock your arm off, it will still stay in position (like a High V) and that your shoulders will remain relaxed while hitting these arm movements.

26. what is cheerdancing?


Cheerdancing came from the two words “cheer” and “dance”. To cheer is to shout short, motivating words to boost the morale of a team or a player. On the other hand, to dance is to create movements to express emotions or gestures, usually with a rhythm.  

Cheerdancing is rooted from cheerleading. What is cheerleading? It is a performance of a routine, dominated by gymnastic moves combined with cheering or yelling, which aim to lead the crowd to cheer for a certain team during a game or sport.
