20. Plot about pluma rizal


Answer:

Jose Rizal, just like any of us, used to live an ordinary life. Just like a normal person, he underwent the mundane stages of human life such as infancy, childhood, adolescence, and adulthood. Yet, unlike any of us, Rizal inadvertently stood out because he lived by the saying, “Live life to the fullest.” He made the best out of his 35 years of existence. However, certain events and persons might have inspired him on the first place that eventually transformed his life from ordinary to an “extraordinary” one.

Rizal’s parents, Francisco Mercado and Teodora Alonso, had already introduced to him the value of education. His father owned several books in their shelf in which the young Rizal had a lot of novels to choose from and to read that suits his interest as a child. His mother also served as his first teacher. She taught him how to read and write. At an early age, he already shows brilliance as a writer with a good sense of social awareness.

An 8-year old Rizal was able to write his first poem, “Sa Aking mga Kabata.” This poem highlights love of nationality and Filipino language. Additionally, he excelled academically when he started attending schools in the Philippines, as well as in abroad. Putting his thoughts into writing became his hobby through the years. His works such as A La Juventud Filipina and El Consejo de los Dioses earned him the recognition to the Filipino youth and won an award in Manila respectively. Given the fact that Rizal’s eyes had long been opened to the awful reality during Spanish colonization, he further wrote novels, which include the famous Noli Me Tangere (Touch Me Not) and El Filibusterismo (The Reign of Greed).

23. sumarry of pluma rizal story​


Explanation:

A SELF-MADE GENIUS

Jose Rizal, just like any of us, used to live an ordinary life. Just like a normal person, he underwent the mundane stages of human life such as infancy, childhood, adolescence, and adulthood. Yet, unlike any of us, Rizal inadvertently stood out because he lived by the saying, “Live life to the fullest.” He made the best out of his 35 years of existence. However, certain events and persons might have inspired him on the first place that eventually transformed his life from ordinary to an “extraordinary” one. Rizal’s parents, Francisco Mercado and Teodora Alonso, had already introduced to him the value of education. His father owned several books in their shelf in which the young Rizal had a lot of novels to choose from and to read that suits his interest as a child. His mother also served as his first teacher. She taught him how to read and write. At an early age, he already shows brilliance as a writer with a good sense of social awareness. An 8-year old Rizal was able to write his first poem, “Sa Aking mga Kabata.” This poem highlights love of nationality and Filipino language. Additionally, he excelled academically when he started attending schools in the Philippines, as well as in abroad. Putting his thoughts into writing became his hobby through the years. His works such as A La Juventud Filipina and El Consejo de los Dioses earned him the recognition to the Filipino youth and won an award in Manila respectively. Given the fact that Rizal’s eyes had long been opened to the awful reality during Spanish colonization, he further wrote novels, which include the famous Noli Me Tangere (Touch Me Not) and El Filibusterismo (The Reign of Greed). Most likely, the former includes the story of Sisa, which was said to be inspired by what happened to his mother, Teodora, who was imprisoned due to the accusation of poisoning her sister-in-law. This taste of injustice could have aided Rizal to boost his patriotism. The latter was also written in honor of the memory of GOMBURZA who were executed due to the 1872 Cavite mutiny. With the aid of Paciano who witnessed the execution, Rizal’s eyes became widely opened to the gruesome reality of the Philippine society during their time. In a celebration of the achievement of Juan Luna’s Spoliarium and Felix Resurreccion Hidalgo’s Las Virgenes Cristianas Expuestas al Populacho, Rizal also realized and proclaimed that Filipinos do have something to be proud of. He affirmed that Filipinos do have something to show off may it be locally or internationally. His speech turned out to be a threat to the Spaniards and friars in the Philippines. Eventually, Rizal was also executed after he was able to write his last poem entitled, Mi Ultimo Adios. He did not bring with him in his grave his personal dreams for his nation. His efforts paid off and were not put into waste because his patriotism, his writings, and other contribution to the country inspired a lot of Filipinos. He mainly triggered a revolution with his pen and paper. Rizal is not born a genius. He made a “genius” out of himself. He did all those “brave and selfless deeds” not to be purely recognized, but mainly for the betterment of his nation. He chose to open his eyes, his mind, and his heart to the reality in which most Filipinos were too afraid to face and accept during the 19th century. He chose to make a stand. He chose to fight through his own interest, which is writing. He chose to write because it’s what he does best. It’s what true patriots do. They give their best for their country.


24. Bakit pluma ang ginamit ni rizal na sandata sa pagtuligsa sa mga mapang aping dayuhan?​


Question:

Bakit pluma ang ginamit ni Rizal na sandata sa pagtuligsa sa mga mapang aping dayuhan?​

Answer:

Para sa kapangyarihan ng panulat nakasalalay sa ilalim ng mga salita, nakasalalay ito sa loob ng kahulugan, ang kakanyahan, ang sagisag ng mga saloobin ng isa. Ang panulat ay isang pagpapalawak ng isip ng isang tao. Ito ay isang sasakyan kung saan dumadaloy at dumarating sa papel ang magagaling na saloobin at mga nakamamanghang ideya.  

Dahil ang gawa ni Rizal ay mayroong pangmatagalang impluwensya sa lipunang Pilipino at ang kasaysayan nito. Ang kanyang mga gawa ay hindi matatapos bilang tinta ng bolpen. Pinatunayan ni Rizal ang katotohanan na ang panulat ay mas malakas kaysa sa espada. Hindi lamang ginamit ni Rizal ang panulat upang labanan ang ating mga kolonisador ngunit siya mismo ang naging panulat, na nagsisilbing boses ng mga Pilipino sa kanyang panahon.

Short Answer:

Dahil gusto niyang patunayan ang katotohanan na ang panulat ay mas malakas kaysa sa espada.

28. kung ikaw ang nasa katayuan ni dr jose rizal pipiliin mo parin bang lumaban gamit ang pluma at papel?​


Answer:

Pipiliin ko paring lumaban tulad nga ng ginawa niya ay naging malaking tulong rin siya sa ating bayan at dahil roon ay pinahahalagahan siya ng mga Filipino

Explanation:

YES, Sa paghihimagsik at pakikipaglaban para sa sariling bayan ay hindi sa lahat ng pagkakataon ay sa paraan ng dahas. Sa simpleng lapis, papel, puso at utak na hangad ang ipaglaban ang sinuman, anoman, at kailanman ay isang uri ng paghihimagsik. Halimbawa na dito ay noong kapanahunan ng pananakop sa atin ng mga Espanyol. Alam kong lahat tayo ay kilala ang pambansang bayani na si Dr. Jose Rizal. Ang bayani ay hindi naging bayani sapagkat nakipagtagisan siya ng galing sa pakikipaglaban, nakipagsalitan ng mga talsik at bahid ng dugo kasabay nang pagwasik ng kanilang mga armas. Siya ay nakipaglaban gamit ang katatasan ng isip na siya rin mismong mas nagpasiklab ng galit ng mga mananakop. Marahil nga ay ang panulat ay mas higit pang makapangyarihan kaysa sa espada, at maaari ring hindi. Ngunit tandaan, na sa hudyat ng isang Himagsikan, hindi lamang ito umiikot sa pakikipaglaban.



P.S Ang salitang “Pluma” ay nagmula sa salitang Espanyol na ang ibig sabihin ay panulat. Nagpapakita ng impluwensiya ng pananakop ng mga ito sa ating wika at kultura.
