6. who are the characters of juan balucas at ang bayabas


The movie entitled “Juan Balucas at Ang Bayabas” is a film shown by UP Film Institute and directed by Franco Arce. The story is about Juan Balucas on a journey of saving his father who was mistakenly identified as Juan Tamad and who got stoned by the Ibong Adarna. The characters of the story are Juan Balucas, his father named John, the Ibong Adarna; the persons who helped him in his journey who are Queen Suki (queen of the fairies), Apo the “engkanto”, Noni the dwarf; the persons he has to surpass are Sipit the king crab, and Tambo the tree giant or “kapre”.

20. The speed of a car of mass 2000 kg increases from 50 m/s to 70 m/s. Caluculate the change in momentum.​


Answer:

[tex]{\boxed{\red{\sf{Change\ in\ momentum,\ \Delta p\ =\ 40,000\ kg\ ms^{-1}}}}} [/tex]

Explanation:

Need to FinD :

We need to calculate the change in momentum.

[tex] \red {\frak{Given}} \begin{cases} & \sf {Mass\ of\ the\ car,\ (m)\ =\ {\pmb{\sf{2000\ kg}}}.} \\ \\ & \sf {Initial\ velocity,\ (u)\ =\ {\pmb{\sf{50\ ms^{-1}}}}.} \\ \\ & \sf {Final\ velocity,\ (v)\ =\ {\pmb{\sf{70\ ms^{-1}}}}.} \end{cases}[/tex]

We know that, we're asked to find out the change in momentum. In order to calculate the change in momentum firstly we need to find the initial momentum and final momentum. After that, we simply just subtract the final and initial momentum and will get the final solution. Formulas used for finding the solution are as follows :-

[tex] {\boxed{\sf{\blacksquare\ {\red{p\ =\ mu}}\: \: \: ||\ \blacksquare\ {\red{p'\ =\ mv}}\: \: \: ||\ \blacksquare\ {\red{\Delta p\ =\ p'\ -\ p}}}}} [/tex]

Where,

m denotes mass.u denotes initial velocity. v denotes final velocity. p denotes initial momentum. p' denotes final momentum. Δp denotes change in momentum.

So firstly, let us find out the initial momentum by using the required formula.

[tex] \bigstar {\underline{\underline{\red{\sf{Finding\ initial\ momentum:-}}}}} [/tex]

[tex] \sf \dashrightarrow {p\ =\ mu} \\ \\ \\ \sf \dashrightarrow {p\ =\ 2000(50)} \\ \\ \\ \sf \dashrightarrow {p\ =\ 1,00,000\ kg\ ms^{-1}} \\ \\ \\ \dashrightarrow {\underbrace{\boxed{\pink{\frak{Initial\ momentum,\ p\ =\ 1,00,000\ kg\ ms^{-1}}}}}_{\sf \blue{\tiny{Initial\ momentum}}}} [/tex]

∴ Hence, the required initial momentum is 1,00,000 kg ms‐¹.

[tex]\rule{200}{3}[/tex]

Now, let us find out the final momentum by using the required formula.

[tex] \bigstar {\underline{\underline{\red{\sf{Finding\ final\ momentum:-}}}}} [/tex]

[tex] \sf \dashrightarrow {p'\ =\ mv} \\ \\ \\ \sf \dashrightarrow {p'\ =\ 2000(70)} \\ \\ \\ \sf \dashrightarrow {p'\ =\ 1,40,000\ kg\ ms^{-1}} \\ \\ \\ \dashrightarrow {\underbrace{\boxed{\pink{\frak{Final\ momentum,\ p'\ =\ 1,40,000\ kg\ ms^{-1}}}}}_{\sf \blue {\tiny{Final\ momentum}}}} [/tex]

∴ Hence, the required final momentum is 1,40,000 kg ms-¹.

[tex]\rule{200}{3}[/tex]

[tex] \bigstar {\underline{\underline{\red{\sf{Finding\ change\ in\ momentum:-}}}}} [/tex]

[tex] \sf \dashrightarrow {\Delta p\ =\ mv\ -\ mu} \\ \\ \\ \sf \dashrightarrow {\Delta p\ =\ p'\ -\ p} \\ \\ \\ \sf \dashrightarrow {\Delta p\ =\ 1,40,000\ -\ 1,00,000} \\ \\ \\ \sf \dashrightarrow {\Delta p\ =\ 40,000\ kg\ ms^{-1}} \\ \\ \\ \dashrightarrow {\underbrace{\boxed{\pink{\frak{Change\ in\ momentum,\ \Delta p\ =\ 40,000\ kg\ ms^{-1}}}}}_{\sf \blue {\tiny{Change\ in\ momentum}}}} [/tex]

∴ Hence, the required change in momentum is 40,000 kg ms‐¹.
