28. Learning Task 4 Give the pattern rule of the following sequences.1. 24, 30, 36, 42, 48 Rule:2. 60, 56, 52, 48, 44 Rule:3.99, 110, 121, 132, 143 Rule:​


Learning Task 4: Give the pattern rule of the following sequences.

To find the pattern rule of the given sequences, you have to find the difference between the two numbers in the sequences. Remember that you have to add if the sequence is in ascending order and subtract if it is in descending order.

Rule: Add 6Rule: Subtract 4Rule: Add 11

Step-by-step explanation:

Arithmetic Sequences

Sequences refer to the sets of numbers or figures that are arranged in order. Each number in the sequence is known as a term. The given sequences above are examples of arithmetic sequences. It is a type of sequence where the difference between one term and the next term is a constant.

To find the next terms of arithmetic sequences, you have to figure out the pattern rule by finding their common difference. Let us use the given sequences above as examples.

1. 24, 30, 36, 42, 48

Rule: Add 6

Subtract: 30 - 24 = 6

36 - 30 = 6

42 - 36 = 6

48 - 42 = 6

The common difference is 6 and the terms are in ascending order so you can find the next terms by adding 6.

2. 60, 56, 52, 48, 44

Rule: Subtract 4

Subtract: 60 - 56 = 4

56 - 52 = 4

52 - 48 = 4

48 - 44 = 4

The common difference is 4 and the terms are in descending order so you can find the next terms by subtracting 4.

3. 99, 110, 121, 132, 143

Rule: Add 11

Subtract: 110 - 99 = 11

121 - 110 = 11

132 - 121 = 11

143 - 132 = 11

The common difference is 11 and the terms are in ascending order so you can find the next terms by adding 11.
