5. if the unknown mineral can scratch fingernail what does it mean​


Answer: The Mohs Hardness Scale ranks the order of hardness of minerals and some common objects. For example, your fingernail can scratch the minerals talc and gypsum, with a hardness of 2 or lower. A copper penny can scratch calcite, gypsum, and talc.

Answer:

Soft - can be scratched by a fingernail, Mohs' 1-2; Medium - can be scratched by a knife or nail, Mohs' 3-5; Hard - cannot be scratched by a knife but can scratch glass, Mohs' 6-9; Diamond is the hardest known mineral, Mohs' 10.

Quartz is the most widespread mineral. Oxygen and silicon, the two most plentiful chemical elements on Earth, make up quartz. Together, oxygen and silicon atoms form tetrahedrons (three-sided pyramids). These can be stacked to form crystals.

4. Olivine

The majority of olivine is utilized in metalworking as a slag conditioner. Olivine (forsterite) with a high magnesium content is added to blast furnaces to remove impurities from metal and form slag. 5. Talc

Talc is a common silicate mineral differentiated from virtually all other minerals by its extraordinary softness (it has the lowest rating on the Mohs scale of hardness). The name soapstone is given to compact aggregates of talc and other rock-forming minerals due to their buttery or oily texture.

6. Halite

Halite, also referred to as table salt or rock salt consists of sodium chloride (NaCl). It is vital for human and animal survival. Salt is utilized in food preparation worldwide.

7. Fluorite

Fluorite's principal applications include flux in steel production, iridescent glass, enamels for kitchen appliances, hydrofluoric acid, high-performance telescopes, camera lenses, and Mohs hardness scale "Four" index mineral.

8. Graphite
