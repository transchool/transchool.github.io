3. how aware are you of the two wolves opposing wolves operating within your mind, one of which leads to pain and a diminished sense of life & the other to a joyous,meaningful and fulfulling life?


Answer:

Life

Life is a very complicated gift from God. It teaches us various things and sometimes, among those things is to be a greater person and to give more meaning to our life. With this, people often say that there are two opposing wolves operating within our minds - to simplify, the good and the bad.

I am very much aware that the two wolves operating in my mind have two different personalities. The first one being the good wolf that it is, leads to a joyous, meaningful and fulfilling life. The latter is the bad wolf - for it leads to pain and a diminished sense of life. The question, however, is which of the two is the one you are going to follow?

Every time we make a decision, we always think ahead of the things that may possibly happen. This is what we called conscious thinking. By doing this, we are being kind and considerate to other people, as shown in the following examples:

Buying clothesGiving food to other people

Buying clothes

Whenever we buy clothes or the other things we want in our life, we often consider if it would benefit us. We ask ourselves various questions such as:

Does my parents have the capability to buy this?Do I really need to buy new clothes?

Instead of buying new clothes, maybe it would be better to spend the money in other things such as helping your parents in paying the bills or treating them to a meal to a restaurant. You might not know this, but parents would genuinely be happy if we do these things.

Giving food to other people

Many people argue that we shouldn't give food to beggars. Doing so would teach them to be lazy and to always depend on others.

The Battle Between Two Wolves

An old Cherokee took his grandson aside and told him, “My son, there’s a battle between two wolves inside us all.  One represents anger, jealousy, greed, resentment, inferiority, dishonesty and selfishness. The other one represents joy, peace, love, hope, humility, kindness, compassion and truth.”

The boy gave this some thought and eventually asked, “Which one wins?”

“The one you feed.” responded the wise old Cherokee.

This internal battle is one that we all face and it’s important that we take responsibility for feeding the right one.
