13. 01. Identify whether Yoyoy Villame's novelty song "Magellan" is a Historical Primary Source or Historical Secondary Source. Explain your answer. 02. Is Yoyoy Villame's novelty song "Magellan" a credible source of historical details of Ferdinand Magellan's arrival in the Philippines? Explain your answer. pa help po​


Answer:

1. The novelty song "Magellan" by Yoyoy Villame counts as a historical secondary source. After all, it is simply a recounting of historical events based on reports of other historians. It cannot possibly be a primary source since it is not a direct account by someone who lived through those times.

2. In my opinion, it is not a credible source. After all, some of the details in the song were made up so that they would rhyme with a different line. For example, there is no way to tell for sure that he arrived at noon as the song claims. This is also true for this part: "Oh, mother, mother, I am sick . Call the doctor very quick. Doctor, doctor, shall I die? Tell my mama, do not cry . Tell my mama, do not cry . Tell my mama, do not cry." On top of that, it is a novelty song and does not claim to be a reliable source.

29. Listen to the classic song of Yoyoy Villame entitled “Magellan.” Based on the reading, identify and correct three flase notion or information in the song.


Answer:

The Villame song spoke of the Philippines being "discovered" by the famed navigator on March 16, 1521". But the National Quincentennial Committee stressed out that there were already a thriving civilization in the islands with natives engaged in robust trade with foreigners from neighboring territories long before the arrival of Magellan.Contrary to "Magellan" verse, the expedition  first landed on the island of Homonhon- not Limasawa which is now part of the Guinan town at the southern tip of Eastern Samar province. Magellan and his crew also arrived on Homonhon on March 17 not 16 as stated in the song.

This piece of literature convey a specific event in the Philippine history through using popular songs written by Filipino folk pop singer yoyoy villame about the discovery of the Philippines by Ferdinand Magellan in 1521.
