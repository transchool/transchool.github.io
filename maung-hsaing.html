2. description of Maung hsaing​


Answer:

 The maung hsaing is a set of bronze gongs lower and more mellow in tone than those of the kyi waing. The gongs of the maung hsaing are set in a rectangular frame.


3. short description of maung hsaing


The hsaing waing (Burmese: ဆိုင်းဝိုင်း, pronounced [sʰáiɴ wáiɴ]; also spelt saing waing) is a traditional Burmese folk musical ensemble, consisting of a number of different gongs and drums, as well as other instruments, depending on the nature of the performance.

Gamelan, the term for a traditional musical ensemble in Indonesia, typically refers to a percussion orchestra composed predominantly of tuned gongs of various types and metal-keyed instruments.

A pasindhèn is a female solo singer who sings with a gamelan. They may perform in dance, wayang or klenèngan performances. The pesindhèn may sing together with a gerong, but their styles and words will be different.

Gerong is the Javanese verb meaning "to sing in a chorus." Penggerong is the proper name of a member of the chorus, but often the word gerong is used to refer to the unison male chorus that sings with the gamelan. The chorus or the melody may also be called the gerongan.

A Chauk Lon Pat is a percussion instrument that is made of a set of 8 tuned drums and is played by patting the drums with the palms of the musician. It is an instrument commonly used for traditional songs and music from Myanmar which is also the origin of this instrument.

The pat waing or pat wang is a drum circle instrument used in the Burmese folk musical ensemble. This instrument has been adapted into the Thai piphat mon ensemble, where it is called poeng mang.
