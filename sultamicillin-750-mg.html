11. 2. Calculate for a suppository dosage supposing a patient needs 750 mg of a suppository and the packaged label says 250 mg. Show solution​


Answer:

To calculate the number of suppositories needed to administer 750 mg of medication, you can use the following steps:

Divide the total dosage (750 mg) by the dosage per suppository (250 mg). This will give you the number of suppositories needed to administer the total dosage:

750 mg / 250 mg/suppository = 3 suppositories

Round up to the nearest whole number if necessary. If the result is not a whole number, it is generally recommended to round up to the nearest whole number to ensure that the patient receives the full dosage of medication.

Therefore, in this case, the patient would need a total of 3 suppositories to receive 750 mg of medication.
