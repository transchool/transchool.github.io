Listening is the process of receiving language through the ears. Listening entails recognizing speech sounds and processing them into words and sentences. When we listen, we use our ears to pick up individual sounds (letters, stress, rhythm, and pauses) and our brain to translate these into messages that make sense to us.  

Two purpose of language:

Transactional- used to make transactions, transfer facts and information

Interactional- language we use to build and maintain social relationships. It creates a positive atmosphere and deeper connection with others

Here are the listening strategies based on purpose:  

1. Listening for the main idea- a type of listening in which you listen in order to understand the main points or general information presented in the audio.

2. Listening for detail- is a type of listening in which you focus on specific information and details such as hours, dates, or names.

3. Listening for a sequence is a type of listening in which you listen to obtain the information you need to act on orders and instructions.

4. Listening for a specific vocabulary  is a type of listening that involves identifying and remembering a set of words that can be easily classified, such as food and animals. The students actively listen in order to identify specific words within a spoken text. Learners deduce the meaning of each word based on their prior knowledge and the context. Learners recognize the significance of context (including the surrounding words in an utterance) in determining the meaning of a word.
