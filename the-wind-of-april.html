3. Make a short reflection about the Winds of April by Nestor Vicente Madali Gonzalez.​


Answer:

she can absorb Ang reflec it

[PLEASE BRAINLIEST ME]

Sometimes the winds are so turbulent that he can't move forward with his dreams. Even though the water around him is choppy, he manages to stay afloat and pursue his dreams, even when things seem difficult. He waits for the wind to calm down and embark on new opportunities, while also holding on to his old dreams. Like Gonzalez's other works, much of the story focuses on the countryside. The rustic landscape and the colorful locals mirror the changing attitudes of the Filipinos during the Commonwealth era, when they were still seeking independence from America. It is no surprise that Gonzalez's works include references to towns and rural people in the provinces of Romblon and Mindoro, since he was born and raised in these areas. This is evident in his novel A Season of Grace, in which the town of Pangasinan appears, and in Look, Stranger on This Island Now, in which the protagonist is from the island of Mindoro.
