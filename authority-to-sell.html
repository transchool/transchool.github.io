22. establishment authorized to buy and sell specific goods, especially motor vehicles​


Dealership

A dealership is a particular kind of business that sells specific products. Automobiles, boats, other vehicles, manufactured homes, and appliances may be among these things.

One highly popular type of business is a car dealership. They frequently sell both new and secondhand cars. They frequently also work on fixing cars. The majority of the time, new auto dealerships exclusively deal in and service cars from a single manufacturer. Used car lots frequently sell cars from a variety of manufacturers and are less likely to offer repairs.

A MOTOR VEHICLE DEALERSHIP

A MOTOR VEHICLE DEALERSHIP is a building or other structure where a franchised dealer displays new motor vehicles for sale or rent or where used motor vehicles are kept for sale, along with which there may be a motor vehicle repair shop, service station, gas station, or body shop, but it does not include any other use or activity that is otherwise defined or classified in the by-law.

What do you refer to a dealership employee as?

Selling automobiles for both private and business users is the responsibility of an automobile salesperson, or auto sales representative. They have to talk to customers about their needs, advertise deals at their dealerships, and take potential buyers on test drives, among other things.
