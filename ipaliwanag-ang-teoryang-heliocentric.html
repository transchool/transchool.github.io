Answer:

13. sa kanya nagmula ang teoryang heliocentric​


Answer:

Nicolaus Copernicus
-On February 19, 1473, Renaissance mathematician and astronomer Nicolaus Copernicus was born, who established the heliocentric model, which placed the Sun, rather than the Earth, at the center of the universe. With the publication of his research he started the so-called Copernican Recolution, which started a paradigm shift away from the former Ptolemaic model of the heavens, which postulated the Earth at the center of the universe, towards the heliocentric model with the Sun at the center of our Solar System. In 1543 Nicolaus Copernicus published his treatise De revolutionibus orbium coelestium (On the Revolutions of the Heavenly Spheres), which presented a heliocentric model view of the universe. Overall, it took about 200 years for a heliocentric model to replace the Ptolemaic model. But, Copernicus was not the first to propose a heliocentric model. Actually, even the ancient Greek philosophers argued about, as e.g. Aristarchus of Samos in the 3rd century BCE,[6] who had developed some theories of Heraclides Ponticus (speaking of a revolution by Earth on its axis) to propose what was, so far as is known, the first serious model of a heliocentric solar system.

23. Ano ang pagkakaiba sa Teoryang heliocentric at Teoryang geocentric?


Answer:

Ang geocentric at heliocentric ay mga term na ginamit na nauugnay sa solar system.

GeocentricAyon sa diksyonaryo, ang "geocentric" ay nangangahulugang "sinusukat mula sa gitna ng Daigdig." Inilarawan ng teorya ng geocentric ang isang geocentric system kung saan inilalagay ang Earth sa gitna ng solar system at ang iba pang mga bagay o planeta ay matatagpuan sa paligid nito. Ang mga pilosopo tulad nina Plato at Aristotle ay nagpalaganap ng teoryang ito na nagmumungkahi na ang lahat ng mga bagay o planeta ay pumaligid sa Daigdig, na ang mga bituin ay matatagpuan sa mga panlabas na panig, at ang buwan ay nasa kalaliman. Ang teoryang ito ay ang pinakalumang isa na binuo noong nakaraang siglo ng mga pilosopo na Greek.

25. Teoryang heliocentric halimbawa


Answer:

Copernican heliocentrism is the name given to the astronomical model developed by Nicolaus Copernicus and published in 1543. This model positioned the Sun at the center of the Universe, motionless, with Earth and the other planets orbiting around it in circular paths, modified by epicycles, and at uniform speeds.
