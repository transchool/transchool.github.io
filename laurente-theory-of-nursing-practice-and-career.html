Theory of Nursing Practice and Career by Cecilia Laurente

Cecilia Laurente is a nursing theorist with an extensive background in practicing nursing. According to her, the nursing theory is:

Categorization of nursing activities as observed in Medical-Surgical ward units in selected government and private hospitals in Metro Manila

This theory suggests how important communication is in the role of a nurse. A nurse must engage with their patients and their patient's families almost all the time. When there is a gap in communication, anxiety is one of the causes, this could pose a problem or hindrance to the goal of a nurse.

To combat this, a nurse must be confident and comfortable in their work, should be sensitive to the experiences, beliefs, and attitudes of their patients, and most of all be caring and have suggestions for coping mechanisms.

5. Interview questions for RN’s please help me due date for this is tomorrow huhu Please indicate your field of nursing practice (Communion Nurse, OR Nurse and etc.) 1. What are the challenges of your career roles? 2. What are your fulfillments of your career roles? 3. What can you advice nursing students to motivate them to pursue nursing in their chosen field?


Answer:

Challenges of a career in nursing vary depending on the specific field of practice. For example:

A Communion Nurse may face challenges in providing care for patients with complex medical conditions and managing a high volume of patients.

An OR Nurse may face challenges in managing a fast-paced and high-pressure environment, as well as the physical demands of the job.

Other common challenges across fields of nursing can include staffing shortages, long hours, and handling emotionally and physically demanding patients.

Fulfillments of a career in nursing can also vary depending on the specific field of practice. For example:

A Communion Nurse may find fulfillment in helping patients recover from illness and providing them with comfort and support.

An OR Nurse may find fulfillment in being a part of life-saving surgeries and procedures and making a direct impact on patient outcomes.

Common fulfillments across fields of nursing can include making a difference in patients' lives, being a part of a team that saves lives, and being able to grow and develop professionally.

Advice for nursing students:

Gain as much hands-on experience as possible, such as volunteering at a hospital or nursing home.

Develop strong communication and teamwork skills, as these are essential in the field of nursing.

Stay current on medical advancements and technology by attending conferences and workshops, and continuing education.

Remember why you chose nursing, and keep the patients' needs and well-being at the center of your practice.

Stay passionate about your chosen field, and continue to grow and develop professionally to reach your full potential as a nurse.
