28. what is the silica contant (high, med or low) ​


Answer:

1.In a widely accepted silica-content classification scheme, rocks with more than 65 percent silica are called felsic; those with between 55 and 65 percent silica are intermediate; those with between 45 and 55 percent silica are mafic; and those with less than 45 percent are ultramafic.

2.Mafic magmas are low in silica and contain more dark, magnesium and iron rich mafic minerals, such as olivine and pyroxene. Felsic magmas are higher in silica and contain lighter colored minerals such as quartz and orthoclase feldspar.

3.Felsic magma has the highest silica content of all magma types, between 65-70%. Thick, viscous felsic magma can trap gas bubbles in a volcano's magma chamber.Feb 17, 2020.
