Ang Pipit (The Pipit) is a popular Filipino song about a bird that is known in Tagalog as a pipit. Many Filipinos don’t realize that it’s an English word too, so they force-translate the song’s title as My Sparrow. The pipit is a bird in the family Motacillidae.

In this song, a bird has her wing injured by a rock thrown by a man. The bird was so hurt she couldn’t fly. Like a person, she spoke up, “Oh, cruel man. How pitiless your heart is. If I die, there is a pipit bird who will cry.”

Considering how plaintively sad the theme and words are of this folk song, it’s slightly incongruous with contemporary sensibilities that most canonical interpretations of the melody, such as by Pilita Corrales and the Mabuhay Singers, are very upbeat. It’s almost like gleefully making fun of a helpless creature in deep throes of pain.

19. what message does the lyricist,levi Celerio,convey in his song​


The lyricist Levi Celerio message in most of his songs is about love, loss, and longing.

Further explanation:

It's not easy to pin down the average of Celerio Levi's compositions because they're wide-ranging and often take on the complex topics and emotions. But if we look to a few of his songs and we’ll see they’re relate to love, despair, and assertive yearning.

Celerio's songs often explore the idea of unrequited love. In "Kapalaran," he sings about a man who is in love with a woman who does not return his affections. This theme is also present in "Sa Aking mga Kamay," which is about a man who is waiting for the woman he loves to come back to him.

Loss and longing are also common themes in Celerio's songs. In "Nasaan Ka Na," he sings about a man who has lost the love of his life and is desperately trying to find her again.

Levi Celerio was born on February 19, 1910, in Tondo, Manila. He was the youngest of six children. His father was a musician and his mother was a homemaker. Celerio began playing the piano at the age of three and started writing songs when he was seven.

Celerio's first published song, "Sa Kabukiran" ("In the Countryside"), appeared in 1929. He went on to write more than 4,000 songs, including such Filipino classics as "Ang Pipit" ("The Sparrow"), "Bituing Marikit" ("Beautiful Star"), and "Manila". Celerio's songs have been recorded by artists such as Jose Mari Chan, Freddie Aguilar, Regine Velasquez, and Sarah Geronimo.

29. what message does the lyricist levi celerio convey in his song pipit


The song "Pipit" simply express nationalistic sentiments and the importance of cherishing life. A bird's wing was injured by a rock thrown by a man in this song. The pain was so excruciating that the poor bird was unable to fly and fell from the tree. Before hitting the ground, the bird expressed its sadness that another bird would be pitiful and that the man was too cruel for taking the life of an innocent living being. Using the psychological concept of poetry analysis, a situation reflects a person's life in which his neighbor continues to hurt him while he is still alive, but when it passes away, when it is too late, will only grieve and cry for the fellow who hurt.

Levi wrote over 4000 songs as a composer and lyricist. Among them are popular pieces that many would call "immortal." Levi was known as a poet of Philippine music, and his songs set the standard for class and quality. Other Filipino songs that lacked luster and rhetoric were quickly forgotten and seldom heard again. But not Levi's music. They are timeless, if not classic. His lyrics best capture the full meaning and emotion of a particular theme.

Levi, who was born in Tondo on April 30, 1910, received a scholarship to the Manila Academy of Music and became the orchestra's youngest member. A large number of his songs have been written for local films, earning him the Film Academy of the Philippines' Lifetime Achievement Award. He was named National Artist in Literature and Music in 1997. The award is the highest national honor bestowed upon Filipino artists who have made significant contributions to the development of Philippine arts and the country's cultural heritage. Its goal is to recognize Filipino artistic achievement at the highest level and to promote creative expression as important to the development of a national cultural identity.
