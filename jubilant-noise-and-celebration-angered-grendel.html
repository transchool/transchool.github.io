Beowulf

The story is set in 6th century pagan Scandinavia. Beowulf, a Geat hero, comes to the aid of Hrothgar, the Danes' king, whose mead hall in Heorot has been attacked by the monster Grendel. Grendel's mother attacks the hall after Beowulf kills him and is defeated.

Here are the answers to the following questions:

TRUE 1. King Hrothgar built a mead-hall called Heorot.  

TRUE 2. Jubilant noise and celebration angered Grendel.

TRUE 3. Beowulf was a warrior from Geatland.

FALSE 4. Unferth was the political advisor of King Hrothgar.

FALSE 5. Grendel died because of a leg injury.

TRUE 6. Grendel's mother sought revenge against Beowulf.

FALSE 7. Grendel's mother killed Beowulf.

FALSE 8. Beowulf became king after King Hygelac was killed in a battle.

TRUE 9. The dragon killed Beowulf.

TRUE 10. Wiglaf was Beowulf's most trusted companion.

What is Beowulf's main message?

The heroic code of chivalry is the central theme of Beowulf. Anglo-Saxon culture has been dominated by honorable behavior and manners. Courage, bravery, and the will to fight were regarded as fundamental norms of that heroic code. As he encounters the Danes, Beowulf adheres to these norms from the start.
