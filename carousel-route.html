4. during feast there is a carnival in our place audrey like to ride on a carousel the carousel takes about of 2/5 an hour to finish if there are 8 carousel horse and 32 kids in front of audrey how long does audrey need to wait befor it is her turn to ride the carousel?​


Audrey needs to wait for 96 minutes or 1 hour and 36 minutes before her turn to ride the carousel.

Step-by-step explanation:

Word Problem

The problem above is asking for how long Audrey needs to wait before her turn to ride the carousel. There are three steps to find the answer.

Find how long the carousel takes to finish by getting the ⅖ of an hour.Find how many rides to make before Audrey's turn by dividing the number of kids by the number of carousel horses.Find how long Audrey needs to wait by multiplying the length of each ride by the number of rides to make.

Given:

length of each ride - ⅖ of an hour or ⅖ of 60

number of kids before Audrey - 32

number of carousel horses - 8

Solution:

Step 1: The word of means to multiply, ⅖ of 60.

⅖ × 60 = ‌120⁄5‌ or 24

The carousel takes 24 minutes to finish.

Step 2: Divide the number of kids by the number of carousel horses.

32 ÷ 8 = 4

4 carousel rides before Audrey's turn.

Step 3: Multiply the length of each ride by the number of rides to make.

24 × 4 = 96 mins

Since there are 60 minutes in one hour, 96 minutes is equal to 1 hour and 36 minutes.

Final Answer:

96 minutes or 1 hour and 36 minutes

8. You are riding in the outer rim of a carousel with a speed of 2 m/s. If the carousel has a radius of 5 m, how much centripetal acceleration will you feel?​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The centripetal acceleration of an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{a_{c} = \frac{v^{2}}{r}}[/tex]

where:

[tex]a_{c} =[/tex] centripetal acceleration

v = speed

r = radius

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the centripetal acceleration

[tex]a_{c} = \dfrac{v^{2}}{r}[/tex]

[tex]a_{c} = \dfrac{(\text{2 m/s})^{2}}{\text{5 m}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{a_{c} = \text{0.8 m/}\text{s}^{2}}[/tex]

Therefore, the centripetal acceleration is 0.8 m/s².

10. A 25 kg girl sitting on a carousel is moving at a speed of 4.0 m/s. If the girl is 5.0 m from the axis of the carousel, what is the centripetal force that maintains her circular motion?​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The centripetal force exerted on an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{F_{c} = \frac{mv^{2}}{r}}[/tex]

where:

Fc = centripetal force

m = mass

v = velocity

r = radius

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for Fc

[tex]F_{c} = \dfrac{mv^{2}}{r}[/tex]

[tex]F_{c} = \dfrac{(\text{25 kg})(\text{4.0 m/s})^{2}}{\text{5.0 m}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{F_{c} = \text{80 N}}[/tex]

Therefore, the centripetal force is 80 N.

Problem:

a children's carousel rotates 3 times every 2 seconds. The diameter of the carousel is 3.0 meters. What is the period of motion?​

Note:

We need to solve first for frequency before we can solve the period (T).

Given Data:

# of cycles = 3

t ( time ) = 2 s

f  ( Frequency ) = ? unknown

T ( Period ) = ? unknown

Solving for Frequency:

We can use the formula ( f = # of cycles / t )

f = # of cycles / t

  = 3 / 2 s

  = 1.5 cycles per second or /s

  = 1.5 Hz

Solving for Period ( we can use the formula ( T = 1 / f  ) )

T = 1 / f

   = 1 / 1.5 /s

   = 0.67 s

The period is 0.67 s
