The game of musical chairs, commonly referred to as "Trip to Jerusalem" involves players, chairs, and music. It is an international party standard. One chair less than the number of players is placed in a set of chairs (for example, seven players would use six chairs). The candidates round the set of chairs as music plays. Each player must locate their own chair to sit in when the music abruptly stops.

A player is eliminated if they are unable to occupy a chair. The process is then repeated until there is just one player left, who is then deemed the winner. A chair is then removed for the following round.

With a few minor variations, Wales' version of musical chairs had the boys always sitting while the girls skipped around, always outnumbering the boys. A girl would have to give up if she didn't get on the boy's lap quickly enough. The winning girl would then kiss the final boy at the conclusion of the game.

There is disagreement over the game's "Trip to Jerusalem" name's etymology. It is however recognized to have originated from its German name, Reise Nach Jerusalem ("The Journey to Jerusalem"). According to one account, the Crusades, which resulted in numerous significant losses, served as the inspiration for the name.
