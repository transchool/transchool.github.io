15. 1. Why does the liquid inside the tube of the thermometer go up and down? Does the working principle of the mercury/alcohol thermometer also true to most solids?​


Answer:

The way a thermometer works is an example of heating and cooling a liquid. When heated, the molecules of the liquid in the thermometer move faster, causing them to get a little further apart. This results in movement up the thermometer.

Explanation:

When heated, the molecules of the liquid in the thermometer move faster, causing them to get a little further apart. ... When cooled, the molecules of the liquid in the thermometer move slower, causing them to get a little closer together. This results in movement down the thermometer.

21. Ethanol or ethyl alcohol is a straight-chain alcohol with a molecular formula C2H5OH. It is a volatile and colorlessliquid that is classified as one of the oldest recreational drugs and best known as the type of alcohol found inalcoholic beverages and in thermometers. What is the percentage compositionof each components (elements) in the compound?​


Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of C₂H₅OH.

molar mass = (12.01 g/mol × 2) + (1.008 g/mol × 6) + (16.00 g/mol × 1)

molar mass = 46.07 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the percentage composition of C₂H₅OH.

For %C

[tex]\text{\%C} = \frac{\text{12.01 g/mol × 2}}{\text{46.07 g/mol}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{\%C = 52.14\%}}[/tex]

For %H

[tex]\text{\%H} = \frac{\text{1.008 g/mol × 6}}{\text{46.07 g/mol}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{\%H = 13.13\%}}[/tex]

For %O

[tex]\text{\%O} = \frac{\text{16.00 g/mol × 1}}{\text{46.07 g/mol}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{\%O = 34.73\%}}[/tex]

22. A solution for disinfecting clinical thermometers was prepared by dissolving 22.5 g of ethyl alcohol in 58.0 g of water. What is the percentage by mass of ethyl alcohol in the solution


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & \text{mass of solute} = \text{22.5 g} \\ & \text{mass of solvent} = \text{58.0 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Solve for the percent by mass using the formula.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{percent by mass} & = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{mass of solute + mass of solvent}} \times 100\% \\ & = \frac{\text{22.5 g}}{\text{22.5 g + 58.0 g}} \times 100\% \\ & = \boxed{28.0\%} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the percentage by mass of ethyl alcohol in the solution is 28.0%
