3. A rabbit gives birth to two rabbits . If a rabbit gives birth to 2 rabbits, and two rabbits in turn gives birth to two rabbits each. How many rabbits will there be after 6  number of births???


This problem involves geometric progression..
Remember that the summation of terms in a geometric progression is defined by the formula, [tex] S_{n} = a_{1} \frac{1-r^n}{1-r} [/tex]
where Sn is the total number, a1 is the first term, r is the common ratio or the multiplier to have the next term and n is the number of terms.
Knowing that the first term is 1, r is 2 and n is 6 then you'll have it as:
[tex] S_{n} = 1( \frac{1 - 2^6}{1 - 2} )[/tex]
[tex] S_{n} = 63[/tex]

28. A farmer owns a total of 16 chickens and rabbits. The chickens and rabbits have 54 legs altogether. How many rabbits are there?


Answer:

There are 11 rabbits.

Step-by-step Explanation:

Let us analyze the given problem.

What is asked?

The total number of rabbits.

What are the given?

total number of chicken and rabbits - 16

total number of chickens and rabbits' legs - 54

What is the equation?

We have two equations in this problem.

Let a represents the number of chickens.

Let b represents the number of rabbits.

1st Equation:

total of 16 chickens and rabbits

a + b = 16

2nd Equation:

54 legs altogether

We know that chicken has 2 legs and rabbit has 4 legs.

2a + 4b = 54

Solution:

a + b = 16

2a + 4b = 54

To find the total number of rabbits (b), we nees to multiply (-2) to the first equation so we can eliminate (a).

(a + b = 16)(-2)

= -2a - 2b = -32

After multiplying,

2a + 4b = 54

-2a - 2b = -32

2b = 22

2b/2 = 22/2

b = 11

Therefore, there are 11 rabbits.

For the total number of chickens subtract the number of rabbits from the whole.

16 - 11 = 5

There are 5 chickens.

Checking:

Substitute the values.

1st Equation:

a + b = 16

5 + 11 = 16

16 = 16 ✔

2nd Equation:

2a + 4b = 54

2(5) + 4(11) = 54

10 + 44 = 54

54 = 54 ✔
