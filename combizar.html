Differentiate between Atom and Molecule:

Definition: Atom- The smallest particles of matter that can enter into chemical reactions, and elements are composed of atoms. Example: (H) Hydrogen and (FE) Iron

Molecule- A more or less stable grouping of two or more atoms held together by chemical bonds. Example: H2O (Water) and NaCI (Table salt)

Structure: Atom- The smallest particle of an element that still has the properties of that element.

Molecule- When two or more atoms join together, we call it a molecule.

Constituent Elements: Atom- Made of extremely tiny particles called protons, neutrons, and eletrons.

Molecule- Consists of two or more atoms of the same element or different elements, that are chemically bond together.
