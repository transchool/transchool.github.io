3. it protects the front of the eyeball​


Answer:

The sclera (SLEER-uh) is the protective layer. This tough, fibrous tissue surrounds the eyeball and attaches to the cornea, which is the clear front surface of the eye. What we see as the white of the eye is the sclera. Over the sclera lies the conjunctiva, a clear skin layer that protects the eye from becoming dry.

The outer layer of the eyeball is a tough, white, opaque membrane called the sclera (the white of the eye). The slight bulge in the sclera at the front of the eye is a clear, thin, dome-shaped tissue called the cornea. The middle layer is the choroid.


21. ◆ What is the relationship between convergence of the eyeballs and pupil size in observing an object as it nears the eyes?​


Answer:

As an object gets closer to the eyes, the convergence of the eyeballs increases, and the pupil size decreases. This occurs because the muscles that control the shape of the lens in the eye and the size of the pupil work together to maintain a clear image of the object on the retina, the light-sensitive layer at the back of the eye. When an object is close to the eyes, the lens needs to become more curved to focus the image onto the retina, and the pupil needs to become smaller to reduce the amount of light entering the eye and prevent the image from becoming too bright. This process is known as accommodation

Answer:

Convergence of the eyeballs occurs when the eyes move inwards, towards each other, to keep an object in focus as it gets nearer to the eyes. This is accompanied by a decrease in the size of the pupil to allow less light to enter the eyes. As the object gets closer, the eyes must converge further and the pupil must become even smaller in order to maintain focus.

30. the brain of an ostrich is smaller than its eyeball​


Answer:

The ostrich's eyes are about the size of billiard balls. They take up so much room in the skull that the ostrich's brain is actually smaller than either one of its eyeballs. This may be why the ostrich, despite its tremendous running speed, is not very good at eluding predators: It tends to run in circles.
