3. Define Bits, bytes, nibble and word​


Definitions:

Bit - A bit is a binary digit, the smallest increment of data on a computer.Bytes - A byte consists of 8 adjacent binary digits (bits), each of which consists of a 0 or 1.Nibble - A nibble is a four-bit aggregation, or half an octet.Word -  A word is the natural unit of data used by a particular processor design.

Each 1 or 0 in a binary number is called a bit. From there, a group of 4 bits is called a nibble, and 8-bits makes a byte. Bytes are a pretty common buzzword when working in binary. ... Word is another length buzzword that gets thrown out from time-to-time.
