14. Write an essay about “Empowering Political Relationships Through ICT’.​


Answer:

1. Process by means of which the ICT is a fundamental instrument to fight against the social inequalities and to favour the participation of all the citizens in the democratic societies. Learn more in: The Digital Divide in Education in the Knowledge Society.

Explanation:

Answer:

ICT is often promoted as a solution to a range of social ills. This is particularly true for the use of ICT in developing countries. ICT deployment can address numerous issues but its overall aim is the empowerment and emancipation of individuals with the aim of improving society. This chapter takes a critical approach to such discourses and asks which claims to emancipation are raised and how they are underpinned. Using a Habermasian framework, the chapter undertakes a critical discourse analysis of the Egyptian ICT policy. This important document, which has inspired much African ICT policy, is analyzed to identify the validity claims it raises. These claims, many of which are explicitly aimed at emancipation and empowerment, are then contrasted with social realities as well as the overall structure of the policy document. This comparison shows that empowerment is not only not achieved, but arguably not a primary aim of the policy in the first place. Instead, claims to empowerment are used to legitimize particular aims which conflict with empowerment. The chapter concludes with a critical reflection of the chosen approach and findings. 29. Write an Reflective Essay on how ICT can change the world for better or for worst.​


Explanation:

Throughout history, the way that people interact with one another has been forever changed by the introduction of new technologies. In the modern age, the ubiquity of Information and Communications Technologies (ICTs) has had a profound impact on the way that people interact with each other, and the world in general. In this reflective essay, I will explore the potential positive and negative impacts that ICTs can have on the world, as well as how ICTs can be used to create positive change.

The most obvious positive impact that ICTs have had on the world is the way that they have enabled the spread of information. Thanks to the internet, people from all over the world now have access to vast amounts of information that can be used to educate, inform, and inspire. Additionally, ICTs have enabled the formation of online communities that allow people to connect with each other, share ideas, and collaborate on projects. This has opened up new opportunities for people to connect with each other, regardless of their location or background.

However, ICTs can also have a negative impact on the world. The spread of information can sometimes be used to spread misinformation and hate speech, which can lead to divisions among people and create an environment of mistrust and fear. Additionally, the use of ICTs can lead to a decrease in privacy, as personal data is collected and used to target people with advertisements or even manipulate them into making certain decisions.

Despite these potential negative impacts, ICTs can also be used to create positive change in the world. For example, ICTs can be used to create online platforms for people to donate to charities or causes that they believe in. Additionally, ICTs can be used to create tools to help people in need, such as medical diagnosis apps that can help diagnose diseases. Finally, ICTs can be used to create tools to help people stay informed and engaged in the political process, such as apps that allow people to track their representatives and contact them with their concerns.

In conclusion, ICTs can have both positive and negative impacts on the world. However, it is important to remember that ICTs are a powerful tool that can be used to create positive change in the world. By using ICTs responsibly, we can create a better future for ourselves and for generations to come.
