10. reading electronic learning materials as a support for vocabulary of grade 1 pupils justification​


Answer:

The effect of reading an electronic storybook (e-book) on Israeli children’s language and literacy was examined in kindergarten children (N = 40; age 5:2–6:3) compared to first graders (N = 50; age 6:3–7:4). The children in each age group were randomly assigned to two groups: an intervention group which read the e-book five times and a control group which was afforded the regular school program. Pre- and post-tests included vocabulary and word reading measures. Post-tests included story comprehension and production. Children who read the e-book exhibited significant progress in word meaning and word reading compared to the control group. Kindergarten children progressed in word reading more significantly than first graders across treatment groups. This could be explained by the ceiling effect of the first graders’ word reading level which did not leave much room for progress in this skill compared to the kindergarten children. No interaction was found between age and treatment groups. Kindergarten children exhibited a good level of story comprehension

27. Reading electronic learning materials as a support for vocabulary of grade 1 pupils


Reading electronic books in first grade to support word reading, story comprehension, and vocabulary development.

   

    A research strategy known as quantitative research places a strong emphasis on quantifying data collection and analysis. It is derived from a deductive approach with a focus on testing theory, influenced by positivist and empiricist philosophies.

By comparing kindergarteners (N = 40; age 5:2–6:3) to first graders (N = 50; age 6:3–7:4), it was shown that reading an electronic storybook (e-book) had a positive impact on the language and literacy skills of Israeli youngsters. Both the intervention group, which read the e-book five times, and the control group, which received the standard school program, were randomly assigned to the students in each age group. Word reading and vocabulary assessments were included in the pre- and post-tests. Story development and comprehension post-tests were included. Compared to the control group, children who read the e-book showed a significant improvement in word meaning and word reading. Across treatment groups, kindergarten students made higher word reading growth than first graders. The ceiling effect of first-graders' word reading may help to explain this.

29. Reading Electronic Learning Materials as a Support for Vocabulary of Grade 1 PupilsQuantitative Research Design:Explanation:.


Answer:

A quantitative research design is a research approach that involves collecting and analyzing numerical data in order to answer a research question. In the context of reading electronic learning materials as a support for vocabulary development in grade 1 pupils, a quantitative research design might involve collecting data on the number of words that students learn while using electronic learning materials, and analyzing that data to determine if there is a correlation between the use of electronic learning materials and vocabulary acquisition. This type of research design is useful because it allows researchers to test hypotheses and draw conclusions about cause-and-effect relationships based on statistical analysis of numerical data.
