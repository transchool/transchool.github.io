Paula = 2x
Jonathan = x

2x + x = 51

3x = 51

3x/3 = 51/3

x = 17

Jonathan's share was 17 mangoes.

2x

2(17)

= 34

Paula's share was 34 mangoes.


Checking:

2x + x = 51

2(17) + 17 = 51

34 + 17 = 51

51 = 51

27. Paula has a dog that weighs 3 times as much as carla's dog. The total weight of the dogs is 48 pounds. How much does Paula's dog weigh. Draw a diagram to find the weight of Paula's dog


To answer the math question, we must first indicate the values given and represent them with variables. Thus,

Carla’s Dog = x

Paula’s Dog = 3x

 

Now, translate the problem into an equation by:

 

X + 3x = 48 total weight, then find x.

 

4x/4 = 48/4

X = 12

 

Thus,

 

Carla’s dog is 12 pounds while Paula’s dog’s weight is 12 x 3 pounds or 36 pounds.
