2. objectives of RA 8423


Objectives. - The objectives of this Act are as follows:

(a) To encourage scientific research on and develop traditional and alternative health care systems that have direct impact on public health care;

(b) To promote and advocate the use of traditional, alternative, preventive and curative health care modalities that have been proven safe, effective, cost effective and consistent with government standards on medical practice;

(c) To develop and coordinate skills training courses for various forms of traditional and alternative health care modalities;

(d) To formulate standards, guidelines and codes of ethical practice appropriate for the practice of traditional and alternative health care as well as in the manufacture, quality control and marketing of different traditional and alternative health care materials, natural and organic products, for approval and adoption by the appropriate government agencies;

(e) To formulate policies for the protection of indigenous and natural health resources and technology from unwarranted exploitation, for approval and adoption by the appropriate government agencies;

(f) To formulate policies to strengthen the role of traditional and alternative health care delivery system; and

(g) To promote traditional and alternative health care in international and national conventions, seminars and meetings in coordination with the Department of Tourism, Duty Free Philippines, Incorporated, Philippine Convention and Visitors Corporation and other tourism-related agencies as well as non-government organizations and local government units.

10. Reaction paper for R.A. 8423​


Answer:

R.A. 8423, also known as the Traditional and Alternative Medicine Act of 1997, is a law that recognizes the use of traditional and alternative medicine in the Philippines. The law aims to integrate traditional and alternative medicine into the country's healthcare system and regulate the practice of traditional and alternative medicine practitioners.

One of the main benefits of R.A. 8423 is the increased access to healthcare for Filipinos, particularly those living in rural areas where traditional and alternative medicine is often the only option. The law also helps to legitimize the practice of traditional and alternative medicine and provide practitioners with the necessary training and certification.

Additionally, R.A. 8423 also includes provisions for research and development in traditional and alternative medicine, which can lead to the discovery of new treatments and therapies. This can also help to improve the quality and effectiveness of traditional and alternative medicine practices.

However, there are also some concerns about the implementation and enforcement of R.A. 8423. Some critics argue that the law does not adequately address issues of safety and efficacy, and there have been reports of unqualified practitioners taking advantage of the law to falsely claim expertise in traditional and alternative medicine.

Overall, R.A. 8423 is a step in the right direction in terms of recognizing the importance of traditional and alternative medicine in the Philippines. However, it is important to ensure that the law is properly implemented and enforced to ensure the safety and efficacy of traditional and alternative medicine practices.
