1. medical provided by medical center


MEDICAL PROCEDURES

* For this question, I will be listing some medical procedures and services that are provided by most medical centers. This will give you a preliminary context to the extent of services offered by them.

Blood Analysis

- In basic terms, this is the extraction of blood for the purposes of analyzing its  physical and chemical properties.

- Most blood analysis procedures are made to measure erythrocytes (red blood cell) and leukocytes (white blood cell) in the circulatory system.

- Some blood analysis procedures also have the ability to measure the characteristics of hemoglobin, the protein blood cells which are responsible for carrying oxygen.

- These tests are done when the appointed medical personnel theorizes that a patient has a possible disorder that may affect the blood in some form or another.

- One example of this is from Iron-deficiency anemia, wherein the low intake of iron decreases red blood cell production rates.

Urinalysis

- Is the extraction of urine for the analysis of its properties.

- Most urine tests analyze the constituent concentrations of the urine itself.

- This is done to observe if a particular percentage of waste concentration is present in the urine.

- The significant deviation from standard results might imply that something is wrong inside the patient's body, and that it is affecting their urinary system.

- A change in colour might also indicate a possible disorder or injury, and that it is affecting the patient's urinary system.

Ultrasound

- Is a medical procedure that utilizes ultrasonic (high-frequency) sound waves in order to produce images inside the human body.

- While these have a wide range of uses, the most common example said about ultrasounds are for pregnancy ultrasounds.

- In this process, the appointed medical personnel will utilize ultrasound technology in order to show the status of the baby inside the mother's womb.

- While it is safer than x-rays, it is not as reliable as the aforementioned.

NOTE:

While there are more medical procedures in the medical field, these should give you a proper preliminary understanding of the services offered.

Hospitals vary from one another in terms of their specialty, services offered, and resource availability. Their services are widely measured with scales that gauge patients’ perspective. Therefore, there is a need for research to develop a scale that measures hospital service quality in Asian hospitals, regardless of their nature or ownership. To address this research need, this study adapted the SERVQUAL instrument to develop a service quality measurement scale. Data were collected from inpatients and outpatients at 9 different hospitals, and the scale was developed using structural equation modeling. The developed scale was then validated by identifying service quality gaps and ranking the areas that require managerial effort. The findings indicated that all 5 dimensions of SERVQUAL are valid in Asian countries such as Pakistan, with 13 items retained. Reliability, tangibility, responsiveness, empathy, and assurance were ranked first, second, third, fourth, and fifth, respectively, in terms of the size of the quality gap. The gaps were statistically significant, with values ≤.05; therefore, hospital administrators must focus on each of these areas. By focusing on the identified areas of improvement, health care authorities, managers, practitioners, and decision makers can bring substantial change within hospitals.
