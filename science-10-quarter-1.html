24. science grade 6 quarter 1 week 1 learning task number 1 study the given example materials identify the type of mixture for each sample 1 to 10​


Answer:

1. homogeneous mixture

2. heterogeneous mixture

3. heterogeneous mixture

4. heterogeneous mixture

5. homogeneous mixture

6. heterogeneous mixture

7. heterogeneous mixture

8. heterogeneous mixture

9. homogeneous mixture

10. heterogeneous mixture

Explanation:

Mixtures are made up of two or more substances that are physically combined. No chemical bonds are formed in a mixture. The ratio of the substances in a mixture is not fixed. Also, it is easy also to separate components of the mixture. There are several physical techniques to separate the components of the mixture.

The mixture can either be a homogeneous or heterogeneous mixture. A homogeneous mixture forms a solution. A solution is made up of solute and solvent. A solute is the part of the solution that is dissolved in the mixture and the solvent does the dissolving of the solute. In a homogeneous mixture, the solute is evenly distributed that is why we see only a single phase mixture. The heterogeneous mixture, on the other hand, is made of substances that are not evenly distributed. We can easily distinguish the components of the mixture since they are not dissolved. There are heterogeneous mixtures that appear single-phase but when viewed under a microscope, the substances are not evenly distributed or dissolved. They are referred to as colloids and one example of this is milk.
