4. In your own word differentiate Isneg and Kalinga as to: 1. House and Village 2. Religion 3. Rituals and Beliefs


Explanation:

Isneg house is called binuron, the Isneg use wood for the posts, girders, joists, and walls, and thatch or bamboo for the roof. An interesting feature of the Isneg house is the way the bamboo roof is constructed. There are no gods or hierarchical deities in their religion only good or bad and The spiritual world of the Isneg is populated by more than 300 anito or spirits who assume various forms. While Kalinga house is a structure that is created with the use of very organic and all natural building materials that are the only things where available at that time, no use of technology being made and build by the hands of the people before us. Anito are supernatural beings in general. The Kalinga recognize a creator god, Kaboniyan, but invoke him only in moments of extreme and sudden crisis, such as an accidental death or the destruction of the rice crop by a storm.

Isnegs

It is also referred to as "Apayao" particularly the old tribe in Cordillera and they also speak Ilocano. They usually settle along the river for easy access on water supply for their livelihood. They have two common ritual which are Say-am and  pildap. Say-am celebrated by an affluent Isneg family while pildap are celebrated by more poorer member.

The two type of Isneg dance are "taduk" and "talip".

In the Philippines in which the inhabitant are composed of different social group, each have their own unique dance in accordance to their culture. By understanding their tradition, we will be able to understand each of one's culture and better relationship will grow form both ends.
