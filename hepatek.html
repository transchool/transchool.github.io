19. explain the principle of electric heater ​

Electric heating is a process in which electrical energy is converted to heat energy. 20. An electric heater running on a 120 V source draws a current of 20 A. What is the power supplied to the heater?​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & I = \text{20 A} \\ & V = \text{120 V} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the power.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P & = IV \\ & = \text{(20 A)(120 V)} \\ & = \boxed{\text{2400 W}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the power supplied to the heater is 2400 W.


21. A 2-kW electric resistance heater in a room is turned on and kept on for 50 min. The amount of energy transferred to the room by the heater is?​


Step-by-step explanation:

Concept:

The heater acts as a work input to the system. 

Work done by heater = Power × time

According to First Law of Thermodynamics:

δQ = dU + δW

Calculation:

Given:

δQ = -300 kJ (Heat is lost), t = 10 min ⇒ 10 × 60 = 600 sec, mass m = 5 kg.

Work is done to the system, by heater:

Work done by heater (δW) = Power × time = 2 × 600 ⇒ 1200 kJ.

δQ = dU + δW

-300 = dU + (-1200)

dU = 900 kJ

We know that;

dU = mcΔT

900 = 5 × 4.18 × ΔT

ΔT = 43.06 ≈ 43.1°C.

24. Description of electric heater ​


ANSWER:An electric heater is a device that converts electricity into heat, typically used for space heating in homes or offices. It consists of a heating element, often made of metal or ceramic, which is powered by electricity and generates heat when a current is passed through it. The heat is then distributed through the air using a fan or convection. Electric heaters are available in various types and sizes, including portable heaters, baseboard heaters, and wall-mounted heaters. They are often preferred for their ease of use, energy efficiency, and safety features such as automatic shut-off and overheat protection.
