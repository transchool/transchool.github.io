21. For what value(s) of k will make A(5, k) B(-1, -6) 10units from each other ? *A) -14 & 2B) 14 & -2C) 14 & 2D) -14 & -2​


Answer:

A. -14 & 2

Step-by-step explanation:

Solve by using the Distance Formula:

D = √[(x_2 - x_1)² + (y_2 - y_1)²]

Given:

(x_1, y_1) = (5, k)

(x_2, y_2) = (-1, -6)

D = 10

Solution:

D = √[(x_2 - x_1)² + (y_2 - y_1)²]

square both sides

D² = (x_2 - x_1)² + (y_2 - y_1)²

Substitute values:

D² = (x_2 - x_1)² + (y_2 - y_1)²

(10)² = (-1 - 5)² + (-6 - k)²

100 = (-6)² + (-k - 6)²

100 = 36 + (k² + 12k + 36)

100 = k² + 12k + 72

k² + 12k + 72 - 100 = 100 - 100 (SPE)

k² + 12k - 28 = 0

Factor remaining quadratic

k² + 12k - 28 = 0

(k + 14)(k - 2) = 0

Solve for k:

k + 14 = 0

k = -14

k - 2 = 0

k = 2

Therefore, A. -14 & 2 is the answer.
