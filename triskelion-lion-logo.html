20. similarities and difference of kingly lion and elderly lion​


Answer:

Here are some similarities and differences between a kingly lion and an elderly lion:

Similarities:

· Both are members of the same species (Panthera leo)

· Both have manes (although the mane of an elderly lion may be thinned or greyed)

· Both are apex predators and play important roles in their ecosystems

· Both are social animals and live in prides with other lions

Differences:

· A kingly lion is likely to be stronger and more physically fit than an elderly lion

· A kingly lion may have a fuller, darker mane compared to an elderly lion

· An elderly lion may have more difficulty hunting and competing for resources due to age-related physical decline

· An elderly lion may have a lower social status within the pride due to its advanced age and declining physical abilities.

Answer:

Similarities:

Both kingly lions and elderly lions are members of the Panthera leo species.They both have the signature mane that is characteristic of male lions.They are both apex predators, meaning that they sit at the top of the food chain.They are both social animals, living and hunting in groups known as prides.They both have an average lifespan of 10-14 years in the wild.

Differences:

Kingly lions are at the prime of their lives, while elderly lions are nearing the end.Kingly lions are at their largest and strongest, while elderly lions are beginning to weaken and shrink.Kingly lions are at the height of their reproductive powers, while elderly lions are no longer able to mate.Kingly lions are often the leaders of their pride, while elderly lions are more likely to be pushed to the periphery.Kingly lions have their whole lives ahead of them, while elderly lions are living on borrowed time.
