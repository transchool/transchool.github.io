26. PLEASE ANSWER. Nonsense - report I. Instruction: In your own words, describe how the following artists’ Filipino heritage is seen through their works. Thank you in advance! 1. Mariano Amar Catague 2. Aze Ong 3. Rodel Tapaya 4. Dexter Sy 5. Nunelucio Alvarado 6. Saudi Ahmad


Answer:

Mariano Amar Catague, Aze Ong, Rodel Tapaya, Dexter Sy, Nunelucio Alvarado, and Saudi Ahmad are six fantastic Filipino artists whose works reflect their heritage, often in subtle and unexpected ways.

Mariano Amar Catague's works allude to Filipino culture acutely and feature heavily anthropomorphic creatures and intricate patterns reminiscent of traditional textiles. For example, his vibrant and playful pieces “Council of Spirits,” utilize a vivid red, blue and yellow colour palette, which often features heavily in Filipino design and folk art.

Aze Ong challenges preconceived notions of Filipino identity through her paintings, sculptures and murals. Her pieces often invite the viewer to reconsider their own biases or conceptions of Philippine culture by incorporating figures or elements sourced from Filipino myths. Her “Kalye Paciano” mural from 2014, for example, beautifully highlights small narrative details from local mythology as she tells the story of a river goddess.

Rodel Tapaya has been heavily influenced by his home, Negros Occidental, and its vibrant communities. He reflects the beauty of localities he witnessed growing up in his paintings, whereby he depicts achingly captured everyday scenes. His “From the Edge” series emphasises the dynamic beauty of rural environments, portraying the energy of life in rural landscapes and the rich personal histories of families the artist encountered through his life in Negros Occidental.

Dexter Sy is a masterful artist with a Sino-Filipino heritage and his works reflect the unique blend of his unique personal history and his Filipino upbringing. Sy's artwork beautifully portrays moments of personal introspection or memory, and often reflects his journey of self-exploration. His works ‘The Agonizing Question’ or ‘Tubod ng mga Limot” both reflect his internal dialogue while simultaneously pointing to his cultural history.

Nunelucio Alvarado utilises traditional Philippine iconography and motifs such as parols (ornamental star-shaped Christmas lanterns), embutido (Filipino-style meatloaf) and sto. nino (a statue of the infant Jesus).This more pointedly references Alvarado’s Filipino identity, as these motifs are often encountered as part of traditional celebrations in the Philippines.

Finally, Saudi Ahmad’s works also celebrate his Filipino roots, drawing on his experiences growing up in small communities in the Philippines. Ahmad often paints scenes inspired by his parents’ home village and his works are often filled with figurative imagery which represent his reflections on Philippine history and culture. His piece “Manananggal” uses traditional imagery to depict the struggle of a soulful creature.

Each of these six Filipino artists weave their Filipino heritage into their creative works in compelling, thought-provoking and individual ways. Their pieces show an abundance of culture and pride in their identity, and allow their viewers to explore a part of the Philippines through their eyes.

29. Uses gratification theory? Semiotics?


Answer:

Uses and gratifications theory asserts that people use media to gratify specific wants and needs. Unlike many media theories that view media users as passive, uses and gratifications sees users as active agents who have control over their media consumption.

People choose and use particular media. Emphasizing a limited effects position, the media are viewed as having a limited effect on their audiences because audiences are able to exercise control over the media.
