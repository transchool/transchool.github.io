3. A 11kg object is moving at 6.4 m/s. What is its kinetic energy


Answer:

Kinetic energy is the energy an object possesses due to its motion, and is given by the formula:

Kinetic energy = 1/2 * mass * velocity^2

To find the kinetic energy of the 11 kg object moving at 6.4 m/s, we can plug in the values into the formula:

Kinetic energy = 1/2 * 11 kg * (6.4 m/s)^2

Kinetic energy = 1/2 * 11 kg * 40.96 m^2/s^2

Kinetic energy = 1/2 * 460.56 Joules

Kinetic energy = 230.28 Joules

Therefore, the kinetic energy of the 11 kg object moving at 6.4 m/s is 230.28 Joules.
