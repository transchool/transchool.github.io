What is the goal of the NSTP?

The main goal of the NSTP statute (R.A. 9163) is to foster youth participation in nation-building. Its goal is to inspire young people to become civic and/or military leaders and volunteers who could be called upon by the country in the event of a national emergency.

What are the NSTP principles?

What is the NSTP's guiding philosophy for the role of youth? 3b. They shall be motivated, trained, organized, and involved in military, literacy, civic welfare, and other comparable initiatives in the service of the nation as the nation's most important resource.

What are the three NSTP components?

The National Service Training Program (NSTP) is made up of three parts: Reserve Officers Training Corps (ROTC), Civic Welfare Training Service (CWTS), and Literacy Training Service (LTS).

Importance of the three NSTP components?

Students learn to be disciplined and efficient as a result of this training. Self-improvement, performance, and community involvement are all taught effectively in NSTP courses. The NSTP law's major purpose is to protect the youth's participation in nation-building. It is also important to raise youth awareness and enhance their physical, moral, spiritual, intellectual, and social well-being. It is vital to develop patriotism and nationalism in students and inspire kids to be civic organizers and community volunteers.
