Answer:

Explanation:

1. Teacher Anne really likes the penmanship of Joseph. It is neat and clear. She gives him high scores in his essays without reading them.

Type of Bias: Halo Effect

Explanation: Halo Effect is a bias wherein good appearances or impressions are perceived as superior or better without further examining the details. In this situation, the teacher is impressed by the aesthetic penmanship of Joseph and assumed that the student's writing content is also good. Teacher Anne has this impression that anything she finds pretty, good or pleasing to her eyes affects the personality or content.

The halo effect represents a bias altering the perception of people or brands. It is assimilated to the first impression capable of generating a selective perception of information. Judgment on an object or a person can then be affected.

2. The school's basketball team won the Division Championship. During the interview, they attributed their winning to their hard work and practice. They lost the Regional Championship the next month and they claimed it was because of the unfairness of the referee.

Type of Bias: The Self-Serving Bias

Explanation: A selfish bias would lead you to believe it's because you studied hard. On the other hand, if you failed, you might think it was because the teacher didn't explain the subject properly, the classroom was too hot, or your roommate kept you up all night before school. 'exam.

Self-serving bias is simply where you will not find yourself at fault but finds your self responsible at eventful times.

In this situation, they attributed their success to their own but will not own up to their failures and will blame someone instead.

3. Jason is writing an article about whether more boys or more girls are likely to graduate from high school. He believes that more boys graduate from high school than girls, and he has found evidence that he is right on his favorite news site.

Type of Bias: Confirmation Bias

Explanation: Confirmation Bias is a type of bias in where our thoughts, opinions, and ideas are only confirmed in any material that matches our beliefs. If a material or source which is verified says that our beliefs are opposite or they suggest it in different way, people tend to reject it because it does not match with our beliefs. This type of bias will make people believe in fake news more because it mimics what they think instead of putting the truth as importance.

Confirmation bias is the instinctive tendency of the human mind to prioritize information that confirms its way of thinking, and to neglect anything that might challenge it. In short, it is an alteration of lucidity, even of more or less assumed bad faith.
