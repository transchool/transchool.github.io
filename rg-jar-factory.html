9. explain the philosophy of RG collingwood about amusement art and magical art.​


Answer:

Collingwood refers to work that evokes predetermined emotion from its audience as 'magical art'. ... Similarly, Collingwood precludes what he describes as 'amusement art' from the definition of art proper. This is where the work makes the audience feel particular emotions by way of representation.

16. 3. Fruits and vegetables are placed in glass jars and sealed in the jars byheating the jars and then placing a rubber stopped jar top on the jar.​


Answer:

canning

Explanation:

Canning is an important, safe method of food preservation if practiced properly. The canning process involves placing foods in jars and heating them to a temperature that destroys microorganisms that could be a health hazard or cause the food to spoil. Canning also inactivates enzymes that could cause the food to spoil. Air is driven from the jar during heating, and as it cools, a vacuum seal is formed. The vacuum seal prevents air from getting back into the product bringing with it microorganisms to recontaminate the food
