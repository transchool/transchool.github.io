12. fun fact for beethoven?​


Answer:

He was unlucky in love.

Explanation:

Beethoven first fell in love with a young countess named Julie (“Giulietta”) Guicciardi in 1801, but could not marry her because he was a commoner. His famous Piano Sonata No. 14, “Moonlight,” is dedicated to her.

A few years later he met and fell in love with Josephine Brunswick, another countess, after he began giving her piano lessons in 1799. They would write a series of love letters, of which 15 by Beethoven survive, until her family pressured them to terminate the relationship.

18. what is the reflection of the beethoven symphonyno.5 op.67 by Ludwig van Beethoven​


Answer:

The Fifth Symphony of Ludwig van Beethoven, also known as Symphony No. 5 in C minor, Op. 67, is a reflection of the struggles and triumphs of the human spirit. The famous opening movement, with its distinctive four-note motive, represents the struggles and challenges that we all face in life. The piece builds in intensity and complexity, representing the perseverance and determination of the human spirit to overcome adversity and triumph in the end.

The symphony as a whole is a testament to the power of the human will to overcome obstacles and to find victory in the face of adversity. It is a reflection of Beethoven's own struggles with deafness and his determination to continue composing despite his disability. It is also a reflection of the tumultuous times in which it was written, as Europe was being torn apart by the Napoleonic Wars.

Overall, the Fifth Symphony is a powerful and moving reflection of the human spirit and its ability to overcome adversity and triumph in the end.
