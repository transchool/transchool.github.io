9. Difference between civil wedding and church wedding


Difference between civil wedding and church wedding

Answer:

Civil wedding ceremony is solemnized by a mayor or a judge. It is held at the city hall or at the office of the judge. The solemnizing officer will lecture regarding the laws stated in Family Code of the Philippines and then bless the broom and the bride. there will be an exchange of vows by the couple and presentation of gifts offering like rings, symbolic item, flowers and money While, the church wedding ceremony is actually held at the church. The church wedding can be solemnize by a priest, pastor, and minister. there will also be an exchange of vows by the couple and presentation of gifts offering like rings, symbolic item, flowers and money. A church wedding is witnessed by many people, friends and family the same with the civil wedding, not unless the invited visitors are selected. Usually the invited visitors for the civil wedding are only few. Wedding Rituals and SuperstitionsRituals are repeated behaviors based on a belief, in the absence of logic or reason, that it will affect the outcome of a given situation. Rituals are repeated behaviors based on defined patterns, whereas superstitions are repeated behaviors based on a belief, in the absence of logic or reason, that it will affect the outcome of a given situation.Superstitions are the belief that certain events or objects will bring either good or bad luck.

Here are some of the wedding rituals and superstitions:Something Old, New, Borrowed, and Blue: When someone gets married, we've all heard this rhyme, but what does it mean? Wearing "something old" represents the bride's past, while "something new" represents the happy future of the couple. The bride is supposed to borrow her "something borrowed" from a happily married person in the hope that some of that person's good fortune will rub off on her. "Something blue" represents loyalty and love.Carrying the Bride Over the Threshold: This superstition originated in medieval Europe, when many people believed that a bride was particularly vulnerable to evil spirits via the soles of her feet. The groom carried the bride into their new home to avoid bringing in any evil spirits.Dove Flying: However, if something bad happens to these doves, it will bring bad luck to the marriage as well.
