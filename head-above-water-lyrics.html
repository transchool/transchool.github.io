3. A pail of water can be whirled in a vertical path such that no water is spilled. why does the water stay in the pail, even when the pail is above your head?


Answer:

The water has inertia. The water tends to move along a straight line, but the bucket pulls it in and around in a circle

Explanation:

Let's consider the moment the pail is at the top of the circular path. If this is considered as a static picture, that is, not moving, then of course the water would fall.

However, what one might forget is that the pail is moving, that is, the water had a velocity with some vertically upward component. So, the force of gravity and the normal of the pail force the water to change the path. In other words, if it wasn't for them, the water would keep going further up.

As you will see in other chapters, a minimum speed is needed for the water not to fall.

14. You swing a bucket of water attached to a string in a circle above yourhead. What keeps the water in the bucket?a. frictionb. gravityC. centripetal fored. inertia​


Answer:

D. Inertia

Explanation:

This is an example of centripetal (circular) motion. The water stays in the bucket because of inertia. The water wants to fly off from the circle, but the bucket gets in the way and keeps it in place. This is the same effect you feel when you go around a tight corner in the car and get squished against the door.
