18. 6. Fluorine-18 which has a half-life of 110mins is used in pet scans if 100mg. of fluorine – 18 is shipped at 8:30 am , how many milligrams of the radioisotope are still active if the sample arrives at the radiology laboratory at 1:30pm .​


Answer:

[tex]m = 15.1 \: mg[/tex]

Explanation:

Given:

[tex]t_{\frac{1}{2}} = 110 \: min[/tex]

[tex]m₀ = 100 \: mg[/tex]

[tex]t = 13.5 \: h - 8.5 \: h = 5 \: h \times \frac{60 \: min}{1 \: h} = 300 \: mins \: *[/tex]

*Note: 8:30 am and 1:30 pm is in 12-hr format. These two must be converted to 24-hr format to be able to compute the time difference. 8:30 am is equivalent to 8:30 or 8.5 hrs and 1:30 pm is equivalent to 13:30 or 13.5 hrs.

Required:

[tex]m[/tex]

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the rate constant.

[tex]k = \frac{ln(2)}{t_{\frac{1}{2}}}[/tex]

[tex]k = \frac{ln(2)}{110 \: min}[/tex]

[tex]k = 0.00630134 \: /min[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the mass of the radiosotope remained.

[tex]m = m₀e^{-kt}[/tex]

[tex]m = (100 \: mg)(e^{-(0.00630134 \: /min)(300 \: min)})[/tex]

[tex]\blue{m = 15.1 \: mg}[/tex]
