Graduates of the ROTC advance program serve in all branches of the Armed Forces of the Philippines. In 2008, ROTC graduates of the officer candidate schools of the various services constituted roughly 75% of the AFP officer corps.[3] The ROTC grants qualified student-cadets scholarship benefits through a merit-based incentive program in return for an obligation of military service in the reserve force, or active duty in the AFP if given the opportunity, after graduation.

ROTC student-cadets attend college like other students, but also receive basic military training and officer training from the branch of service that handles their school's ROTC unit. The students participate in regular ROTC instruction during the school year (one school year for Basic ROTC student-cadets and three school years for Advance ROTC cadet-officers), and extended training activities during the summer, such as the ROTC Summer Camp Training (RSCT) and the Advance ROTC Academic Phase Training (ARAPT).

In today’s modern era of political facades, false promises, and a lack of

clarification in political stances, presidential debates are vital in identifying the truth

about any human being that plans to lead our country to progression and victory

within the next four years. As presidential candidates within our country,

individuals are highly revered and respected for the ideas that they exhibit to the

general public. However, a lack of clarity and concision is present in the execution of

these plans. Presidential debates give voters the opportunity to recognize

determination, genuinity, and morality in candidates on a personal and vulnerable

level.

Presidential debates epitomize the inclusive idea of putting opponents on an

impromptu ground to expose their true identity and character. For example, during

the first televised presidential debate on September 26, 1960, candidate John F.

Kennedy illustrates a tranquil, collected demeanor throughout the entire duration of

the debate, while his opponent, Richard Nixon, is noticeably anxious and sweating

profusely. Even though the natural behavior of the candidates can be viewed as a

nonchalant aspect of their general character, it can foreshadow the behavior of the

future presidents and their keen ability or inability to function to function under

immense amounts of pressure. American citizens recognize the presidential

candidate that can handle the arbitrary issues of the United States and vote

accordingly to the individual that they feel can do the job justice. It is not only a verbal debate in the polices that they plan to enforce and produce, but an unspoken,

nonverbal debate that highlights the candidate that is a natural born leader.

The competitive atmosphere illuminated in presidential debates provides

leeway to the moral compasses of our future, honorable innovators. Despite the fact

that debates are verbal arguments designed to implement the juxtaposition of

political perspectives, the body language of the competitors is equally as significant.

In the presidential debate between Al Gore and George W. Bush, Al Gore is

infamously remembered as the competitor that sighs repeatedly during George W.

Bush’s commentary. The fact that Al Gore interrupts his opponent’s argument in an

environment that provides equal opportunity to both debaters in the race elucidates

the lack of concern that he obtains for Bush’s political perspective. The body

language demonstrated allows the audience to conclude that Gore does not consent

to anything Bush has to say in contrast to the patient, matter-of-fact tone that is held

by George W. Bush at all times. George W. Bush through he debate is viewed as

mature and poised in comparison to the behavior of Gore, which is an explanatory

variable of the oppositions present through each competitor.

Presidential debates are not only necessary, but integral in distinguishing

between sincere and disingenuous candidates that are not as dedicated to the

upcoming presidential duties. The debates are a personable way to analyze political

stance, consider oppositions to relevant United States issues, and comprehend the

moral and ethical values held by the candidates and why they should apply to our

diverse country.
