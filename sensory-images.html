10. what is sensory image?


This is a collection of images that are created in your head from detailed descriptions in poetry or prose. Sensory imagery is created with details that help the reader see, smell, hear, taste, and feel (tangibly) things without actually experiencing them. Sensory Images trigger the 5 senses of a person without actually experiencing it.

26. what is sensory images?


SENSORY IMAGES

Language which appeals to five senses of a reader. Vision, sound, smell, taste and touch. Also called as imagery.

FIVE SENSORY IMAGES

VisualThis includes the images, the optical objects, the pictures or the sense of perception.

Example:

An oak tree

A sailing boat

    2. Auditory

A form of mental processing used to arrange and interpret sounds when no direct auditory stimuli occurs.

Example:

The ring on your telephone

The sound of your computer keyboard

   3. Olfactory

A chemical receipt which shapes the sense of smell.

Example:

Chlorine

New mown grass

   4.  Gustatory

Similar to or connected to food or the sense of taste.

Example:

Lemon

Salt

  5. Kinesthetic

A depiction of an event or a character's acts and gestures.

Examples:

Holding an ice cube

Throwing a ball
