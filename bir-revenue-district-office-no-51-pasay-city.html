Tax law in the Philippines covers national and local taxes. National taxes refer to national internal revenue taxes imposed and collected by the national government through the Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) and local taxes refer to those imposed and collected by the local government. The Tax Code of 1997, Revenue Issuances and BIR Rulings pertaining to national taxes are posted at the BIR website.

Government Offices

A government office where employees work A job centre is a government office in a town that displays information about available jobs and administers unemployment benefits. A land office is a government office where business concerning public lands is conducted.

1. Department of Trade and Industry (DTI) - provides consumer protection through consumer education and information dissemination programs.

2.Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) - To form a partnership or corporation.

3.Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) - To obtain a tax identification number (TIN).

4.Mayor's Office - For obtaining a city or municipal business license and paying your local business taxes.

5. Social Security System (SSS) - Provides health benefits to all salaried employees in the Philippines. SSS services are also paid for.

6.Philhealth - Have access to a comprehensive package of services, such as inpatient care, catastrophe coverage, ambulatory surgerics, delivries, and outpatient treatment for malaria and tuberculosis.

7.Pag-ibig Fund​ - Is providing a loan restructuring program to allow borrowers to offer their payments on their mortgages.

What is the significance of public services?

Public services broaden opportunities, protect the vulnerable, and raise everyone's standard of living. They are critical to our economic growth and prosperity. They help to strengthen our communities and unite us as a society.

What exactly is good public service?

Public servants can embody the servant leadership qualities of being mindful of and caring for society's underprivileged. Prioritizing the development and well-being of individuals and communities. exhibiting awareness, empathy, and foresight.

Functions. The powers and duties of the Bureau of Internal Revenue are: Reduction and collection of all internal revenue taxes, fees and charges; and. ... It shall also give effect to the administer supervisory and police powers conferred to it by the National Internal Revenue Code and special laws.

The Bureau of Internal Revenue shall be under the supervision and control of the Department of Finance and its powers and duties shall comprehend the assessment and collection of all national internal revenue taxes, fees, and charges, and the enforcement of all forfeitures, penalties, and fines connected therewith.
