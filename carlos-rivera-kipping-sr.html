8. The 4-in.-diameter shaft carries a 36-kip axial load and 2.4-kip . ft torque. determine the normal and shear stresses acting on the spiral weld that makes a 30Ëš angle with the axis of the shaft.


Given:

D = 4-in. = 1/3 ft.

P = 36 kips = 36,000 lbs.

T = 2.4 kips ft. = 2,400 lbs. ft.

θ = 30°

Solution:

For normal stress.

[tex]Sn = \frac{P}{A} [/tex]

[tex]Sn = \frac{36,000}{ \frac{\pi}{4} {(4) }^{2} } [/tex]

[tex]Sn = 2,864.79 \: psi[/tex]

For shear stress, use torsional stress.

[tex]Ss = \frac{16T}{\pi {D}^{3} } [/tex]

[tex]Ss = \frac{16(2,400)}{\pi{( \frac{1}{3}) }^{3} } [/tex]

[tex]Ss = 330,023.69 \: psi \: or \: 330.02 \: ksi[/tex]
