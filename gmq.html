Genetically modified organisms contaminate existing seeds with their altered material, passing on modified traits to non-target species. This creates a new strain of plant that was never intended in the laboratory. In North Dakota, recent studies show that 80% of wild canola plants tested contained at least one transgene. 21. diffenation of GMOs​


Answer:

GMOs, or “genetically modified organisms,” are plants or animals that have been created through gene splicing, a process that involves combining DNA from two or more different species. This process can create organisms that have characteristics that are not found in nature, such as plants that are resistant to herbicides or animals with extra muscles.

Most GMOs are created for agricultural purposes, in order to make crops more resistant to pests or herbicides, or to produce more food. However, GMOs are also used in research and medicine. For example, some GMOs are used to create medicines or to study human diseases.

GMOs are controversial because some people believe that they may pose risks to human health or the environment. However, there is no clear evidence that GMOs are harmful.
