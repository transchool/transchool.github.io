4. a female dragster in a race accelerated from stop to 60 m/s by the time it reached the finish line. she moved in a straight line and traveled from the starting line to the finish line in 8.0 sec. what was the acceleration of the female dragstershow solution po​


ACCELERATION

Problem:

» A female dragster in a race accelerated from stop to 60 m/s by the time it reached the finish line. she moved in a straight line and traveled from the starting line to the finish line in 8.0 sec. What was the acceleration of the female dragster?

Answer:[tex] \color{hotpink} \bold{7.5 \:m/s² } \\ [/tex]

— — — — — — — — — —

Formula:[tex] \underline{ \boxed{ \tt a = \frac{∆v - u}{t} }}[/tex]

where,

a is the acceleration in m/s²v is the final velocity in m/su is the initial velocity t is the time in seconds

Solution:

Substitute the given final velocity and time to the formula in order to get the answer.

- Since there's no initial velocity, then its value is zero.

[tex]\tt a = \frac{∆v - u}{t} \\ [/tex]

[tex]\tt a = \frac{60- 0}{8.0} \\ [/tex]

[tex]\tt a = \frac{60}{8} \\ [/tex]

[tex]\tt a = \underline{ \boxed{ \blue{ \tt 7.5 \: m/s²}}}[/tex]

Hence, the acceleration of the female dragster is 7.5 m/s².

6. 3. A dragster going at 15 m/s increases its velocity to 25 m/s in 4 seconds. What is itsacceleration?​


CALCULATING THE ACCELERATION

A dragster going at 15 m/s increases its velocity to 25 m/s in 4 seconds. What is its acceleration?

The formula for acceleration is final velocity decreased by the initial over the time elapsed.

[tex] \tt{a = \frac{v - u}{t} } \\ \\ \tt{a = \frac{25 \: m/s - 15 \: m/s}{4 \: secs} } \\ \\ \tt{ a = \frac{10 \: m/s}{4 \: secs} } \\ \\ \boxed{ \tt{a = 2.5 \: m/ {s}^{2} }}[/tex]

FINAL ANSWER:

Its acceleration is 2.5 m/s²

Explanation: We have equation of motion s = ut + 0.5 at²

       Initial velocity, u = 0 m/s

       Acceleration, a = ?

       Time, t = 6.70 s      

       Displacement, s = 1/4 mi = 0.25 mi = 400 m

    Substituting

                     s = ut + 0.5 at²

                     400 = 0 x 6.70 + 0.5 x a x 6.70²

                     a = 17.82 m/s²

    Now we have equation of motion v = u + at

    Initial velocity, u = 0 m/s

    Final velocity, v = ?

    Time, t = 6.7 s

    Acceleration, a = 17.82 m/s²

   Substituting

                     v = u + at  

                     v = 0 + 17.82 x 6.7

                     v = 119.40 m/s
