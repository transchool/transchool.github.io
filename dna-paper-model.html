Activity #6 DNA modeling

 

DNA structure. The DNA is comprised of molecules called nucleotides. The nucleotides are the basic building blocks of the DNA. Each nucleotide has a phosphate group, a sugar group and a nitrogen base. The four types of nitrogen bases include adenine (A), thymine (T), guanine (G), and cytosine (C).

Answer:

The simplest substitution model for nucleotides is the Jukes–Cantor (JC) one-parameter model, which assumes that all nucleotides occur in equal frequency (25%) and are substituted with equal probability. This model requires a single parameter denoting rate.

25. The nine key features of the DNA Model​


Answer:

1. DNA and its building blocks

2. Complementary base pairs in the DNA double helix.

3. The DNA double helix

4. The relationship between genetic information carried in DNA and proteins.

5. The nucleotide sequence of the human β-globin gene.

6. DNA as a template for its own duplication.

7. A cross-sectional view of a typical cell nucleus.

Explanation:

Genetic information is carried in the linear sequence of nucleotides in DNA. Each molecule of DNA is a double helix formed from two complementary strands of nucleotides held together by hydrogen bonds between G-C and A-T base pairs. Duplication of the genetic information occurs by the use of one DNA strand as a template for formation of a complementary strand. The genetic information stored in an organism's DNA contains the instructions for all the proteins the organism will ever synthesize. In eucaryotes, DNA is contained in the cell nucleus.

26. how watson and crick described the DNA model? describe​


Answer:

In its natural state, each DNA molecule is actually composed of two single strands held together along their length with hydrogen bonds between the bases. Watson and Crick proposed that the DNA is made up of two strands that are twisted around each other to form a right-handed helix, called a double helix.

Answer:

In Watson and Crick's model, the two strands of the DNA double helix are held together by hydrogen bonds between nitrogenous bases on opposite strands. Each pair of bases lies flat, forming a "rung" on the ladder of the DNA molecule. Base pairs aren't made up of just any combination of bases.
