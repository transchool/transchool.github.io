How do crafts develop in specific countries in South Asia, West Asia, and Central Asia according to functionali- traditional specialized expertise, and availability of resources? What are the similarities and differences of the cultures of the South Asian, West Asian, and Central Asi countries in relation to Philippine culture?​


Answer:

1.The traditions of South, West, and Central Asia are still relevant to the modern world in many ways. These traditions encompass various aspects of life, including cultural practices, beliefs, customs, and rituals that have been passed down from generation to generation. These traditions are an integral part of the identity of these regions and their people. They help preserve their cultural heritage and contribute to their sense of community and belonging. Moreover, these traditions have influenced modern-day practices, such as cuisine, fashion, and music, that have gained worldwide popularity.

2.South, West, and Central Asian art reflects the rich cultural heritage of these regions. Art has been an essential part of these cultures, and it has been used to convey messages, tell stories, and express emotions. The art forms of these regions include calligraphy, pottery, carpet weaving, miniature paintings, and sculptures, among others. These art forms have influenced the culture of these regions, shaping their aesthetics, values, and beliefs. They have also contributed to the development of art forms in other parts of the world.

3.Crafts in specific countries in South Asia, West Asia, and Central Asia have developed based on various factors, such as functional requirements, traditional knowledge and expertise, and the availability of resources. For instance, in countries like India and Pakistan, crafts such as weaving, embroidery, and block printing have developed due to the availability of local materials like cotton and silk, and the expertise of the local artisans. Similarly, in countries like Iran and Turkey, crafts such as carpet weaving and pottery have developed due to the availability of local clay and skilled artisans.

The cultures of South Asian, West Asian, and Central Asian countries have many similarities and differences in relation to Philippine culture. For instance, all these regions have rich cultural traditions, which are deeply embedded in their daily lives. Family values and respect for elders are also essential in these regions. However, there are also differences in terms of language, religion, and customs. For instance, South Asian countries have a predominantly Hindu and Muslim population, while West Asian countries have a predominantly Muslim population. Central Asian countries have a mix of different ethnicities and religions, including Islam, Christianity, and Buddhism

Topographically, the region is dominated by the Eurasian Plate, except for its eastern part, which lies on the North American, Amurian and Okhotsk Plates. It is divided by three major plains: the West Siberian Plain, Central Siberian Plateau and Verhoyansk-Chukotka collision zone. The Uralian orogeny in the west raised Ural Mountains, the informal boundary between Europe and Asia. Tectonic and volcanic activities are frequently occurred in the eastern part of the region as part of the Ring of Fire, evidenced by the formation of island arc such as Kuril Islands and ultra-prominent peaks such as Klyuchevskaya Sopka, Kronotsky and Koryaksky. The central part of North Asia is a large igneous province called the Siberian Traps, formed by a massive eruption occurred 250 million years ago. The formation of the traps coincided with the Permian–Triassic extinction event.

The geographic term “Asia” was originally used by ancient Greeks to describe the civilizations east of their empire. Ancient Asian peoples, however, saw themselves as a varied and diverse mix of cultures—not a collective group. Today, the term “Asia” is used as a cultural concept, while subregion classifications describe the distinct geopolitical identities of the continent. These classifications are Western Asia, Central Asia, Southern Asia, Eastern Asia, Southeastern Asia, and Northern Asia.
