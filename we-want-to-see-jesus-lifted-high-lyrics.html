5. how high must a 20kg box be lifted to give it 784J of potential energy


#BrainlyFast

Problem:

How high must a 20kg box be lifted to give it 784J of potential energy

Note:

The formula for the Potential Energy is ( P.E. = m × g × h ). The gravitational acceleration on Earth is 9.8 m/s² .

Given Data:

m ( mass ) = 5.5 kg

g (gravitational acceleration ) = 9.8 m/s²

P.E. ( Potential Energy ) = 784 J

h ( Height ) = unknown

Solution:

The formula for the Potential Energy is ( P.E. = m × g × h ), to find height, we can use the formula ( h = P.E. / m × g )

h = P.E. / m × g

  = 784 J / 5.5 kg  × 9.8 m/s²

  =  784 J /  53.9 kg·m/s²

  = 14.55 m

Answer:

The height lifted is equal to 14.55 meters

6. How much power is required to lift a 30N chair, as high as 0.20m in 2s?


The power needed is 3 watts.

Explanation:

Step 1: Enumerate the given values.

Given:

F = 30 N

d = 0.20 m

t = 2 s

Step 2: Identify what is being asked.

Required:

Power

Step 3: Solve for Power

Solution:

The formula for power is given by:

P = Fd/t

where: P - Power

F - force

d - distance

t - time

Substituting the value of F, d and t in the formula,

P = (30 N)(0.20 m)/(2 s)

P = (6 Nm)/(2 s)

P = 3 J/s or 3 watts

21. what is the potential energy of a 3 kg box lifted to 12.5 meters high?​


#BrainlyFast

Problem:

What is the potential energy of a 3 kg box lifted to 12.5 meters high?​

Note:

The Earth has a gravitational acceleration of 9.8 m/s². We can use the formula of Potential Energy which is ( P.E. = m × g × h )

Given Data:

m ( mass ) = 3 kg

h ( Height ) = 12.5 m

g (gravitational acceleration ) = 9.8 m/s²

P.E. ( Potential Energy ) = ? unknown

Solution:

The formula for the Potential Energy is ( P.E. = m × g × h ).

P.E. = m × g × h

      = 3 kg × 9.8 m/s² × 12.5 m

      = 367.5 kg·m²/s²

      = 367.5 J

Answer:

The potential energy is 367.5 kg·m²/s² or  367.5 J

28. • How much work is required to lift a 2kg object 2m high?​


The work done is 39.2 J

Explanation:

Step 1: Enumerate the given values.

Given:

m = 2 kg

d = 2m

Step 2: Identify what is being asked.

Required:

Work

Step 3: Solve for Work

Solution:

The formula for work is given by:

W = Fd

where: W - Work

F - force

d - distance

Solve for force:

F = mg

F = (2kg)(9.8 m/s²)

F = 19.6 N

Substituting the value of F and d in the formula,

W = (19.6 N)(2 m)

W = 39.2 J
