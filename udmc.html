12. Discuss the 25 articles of religion of the UMC​


The United Methodist Church (UMC) is a large Protestant Christian denomination founded in the mid-18th century as an expression of Christian unity. The UMC is a global organization which encompasses millions of members in over 130 countries and six continents. This denomination promotes a traditional Christian faith and is known for its Mission, “to make disciples of Jesus Christ for the transformation of the world.”

The UMC is guided by a document known as the "Articles of Religion" which outlines the basic tenets of Methodist faith and theology. Originally drafted by John Wesley several centuries ago, the Articles of Religion provide a succinct and comprehensive summary of the beliefs and practices of the UMC.

The Articles of Religion can be divided into two primary categories: the doctrines of God and belief in the Trinity, and the doctrine and sacraments of the Church. The doctrines of God and the Trinity include beliefs such as the existence of God as a Trinity of Father, Son, and Holy Spirit; the authority of scripture; the divinity of Christ; and the forgiveness of sin through faith, repentance, and baptism. The doctrine and sacraments of the Church outlines the manner and significance of worship services, the rules and requirements of church membership, and the sacraments of baptism and Eucharist. In addition to these two categories, the Articles of Religion also provide guidance on topics such as social justice and the spirit of Christian charity.

The Articles of Religion themselves are composed of 25 separate articles which describe Wesley's view of faith and how it is to be lived out in the Church. The first article articulates the Church's belief in God as a Trinity and establishes that the Bible is the only authoritative source for Christian truth. Subsequent articles define core Christian teachings on topics such as the incarnation of God in Jesus Christ, the fall of humankind, and the atonement of sins through the blood of Jesus. These articles affirm the true nature of the Church and stress the importance of holiness and charity. The articles also offer guidance on matters of human relationships and provide a framework for understanding Christian fellowship and mission.

In summary, the United Methodist Church’s Articles of Religion provide a comprehensive and clear expression of the beliefs and practices of the denomination. This document is the product of hundreds of years of faith formation and is integral to the continuing development of Methodist theology and mission. The Articles of Religion provide a practical way for individuals to practice the Christian faith, guiding them to a deeper level of spiritual formation and enabling them to live out a life of service and justice.
