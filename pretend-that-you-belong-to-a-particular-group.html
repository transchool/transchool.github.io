Bias refers to an author's expression of his/her own opinion on a particular issue without examining and presenting enough evidence. This arises from the author's thinking which may be influenced by personal beliefs, culture, attitudes, preferences, and past experiences.

WHAT DOES AN AUTHOR BECOME BIAS?

An author become bias when he/she persuades the reader to believe on his/her own stand of the issue by giving inaccurate information or false impression even with or without the intention of misleading the readers.
