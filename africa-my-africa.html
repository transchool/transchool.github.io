1. give three things that symbolize africa in the poem "africa my africa"​


Three Things that Symbolize Africa in the poem "Africa, my Africa"​

Tree

In the 18th line, it wrote, Impetuous son that tree young and strong. This symbolizes Africa, as it is described as a tree. This pertains to Africa's growth after the end of colonialism. Just like how a young tree is continuously growing despite hindrances that surround it.

Blood

Found in the 7th line, Your beautiful black blood that irrigates the fields, the blood talked about here means his African roots. This is an indication that wherever he may be, he knows deep inside him that his identity is of African descent.

Back

'This back that breaks under the weight of humiliation', 'This back trembling with red scars'. found in the 14th and 15th lines, the back symbolizes Africa. It tells about the hardships that Africa has experienced under the rule of colonizers.

16. What is the prevailing mood or tone of the poem africa my africa?


In the poem " Africa, My Africa" , there are three kinds of tone that can be seen. The first tone is heard in the first six and the last two lines of the poem which speaks of the speaker's feelings, which is triumphal . Triumphal in a sense that a celebratory feeling. The speaker is triumphal when he speaks of his country, Africa with pride and greatness.

The second tone is found in the line " Your beautiful black blood that irrigates the fields" which expressed sadness. The author feels sad because of the backbreaking labor of the people of Africa.

The third tone or the tertiary tone was spoken in a grave voice that is gentle yet earnest in the the last few lines.

24. What might the reason why David Diop started his poem with the line"Africa, my Africa"?


David Diop started his poem with the line "Africa, my Africa" because he wants the reader to feel like he is conversing with Africa.

Africa is a poem by French-African poet David Diop, one of the most notable poets of his generation. It talks about Africa from before and during the colonization. The poem is about hope for Africa to be glorious and successful again after colonization. In the poem, the author's attitude towards Africa is seen as realistic instead of romantic.

To further express the hope of the author, he used many literary devices and figures of speech to further convey his message. One can be found in the first line where he used personification. Personification is the attribution of human characteristics to something that is not human. The speaker attributes Africa to human characteristics. This can be seen through the first line where the author talks to Africa as if it can listen.

27. What might be the reason why Diop started his poem with the line "Africa, my Africa"?​


Answer: During his literary career, he was a proponent of Negritude. This was a political philosophy/literary movement whose scholars included statesman-poet Leopold Sedar Senghor. Negritude was a reaction to the French colonial administrative policy of assimilation; this policy was predicated on the belief that Africans possessed neither culture nor history and therefore French culture could be used to civilize them. Negritude desired a deep and almost essentialist re-grounding of Africans in the history, values, and cultures of the Black people while being open to friendship with other civilizations. The poem Africa my Africa by Diop reflects those values.
