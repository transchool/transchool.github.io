5. 368 Friend by Bradley Walton •Characters (5pts) (Write the name of the characters and include a brief description about them) •Setting (Enumerate all the setting that is included in the excerpt) •Stage Directions (Explain what stage directions are used in the text and provide examples) •Events that Comprise the Plot (Using the excerpt, provide the plot of the text by using the elements of plot) •Dialogues (Explain how are the dialogues written) •Theme (Central message of the text)


Answer:

Characters:

•Bradley: A young man who is looking for a companion.

•Friend: Bradley's friend who is always there for him.

Setting:

The excerpt takes place outside in a park on a bright, sunny day.

Stage Directions:

The stage directions used in the excerpt are descriptions of the environment and how the characters interact with each other. For example: "Bradley looked to the sky and smiled, a long pause between them as the sun shone and the birds chirped in the park."

Events that Comprise the Plot:

The plot of the excerpt follows Bradley as he meets his friend in the park. They share a moment of peace and joy before Bradley makes a request to his friend. He asks his friend to be there for him as he goes through life and his friend agrees to be his companion.

Dialogues:

The dialogue in the text is written in a conversational format. For example: "I just wanted you to know," Bradley said, "That no matter what happens, I want you to stay with me, be my companion."

Theme:

The central message of the text is that friendship and companionship is an important part of life. No matter what happens, friends can be a source of support and comfort.
