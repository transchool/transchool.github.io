15. 3. Marketers charge consumers for the cast of their goods plus a mark-sup for profit and overhead. Consumers are therefore paying more than the actual cast of a good. How do you feel about this? Do you think that there are any moral implication to this?


Answer:

Marketers add value. Take for example, grocery stores.

They transport large amounts of goods to a location where a person who cannot afford, or need, large amounts of good can purchase in small quantities. And they make it available locally. They transport much more efficiently than a single consumer can. They store the product safely.

They display logically. They make it easier to buy (think point of sale scanners). They cover the costs of individual credit card transactions - which are inefficient - and make the sale easy.

They pass on manufacturer offers - often times due to buying power and a need to bump demand for a reason.

A grocery store often sells a product BELOW cost (after overhead). A box of macaroni and cheese is only worth $X.XX in a marketplace. If their cost is too high on the product, they can’t just expect their consumers to pay 20% over the price they can get the M&C elsewhere.

So to answer your question: For most businesses, they add value. And the moral implications may not be “bad” in many cases - and are good.

What is 3 dimensional art called?

Sculpture

The Sculpture is an artwork made by the manipulation of materials resulting in a three-dimensional object.

Explanation:

Three-dimensional art pieces, presented in the dimensions of height, width, and depth, occupy physical space and can be perceived from all sides and angles. ... Sculptures have been predominant 3D art forms for centuries, evolving continually throughout different periods of art history.

What processes are used in three-dimensional art?

It includes sculpture, installation and performance art, decorative art, and product design. Two processes are responsible for all three-dimensional art: additive, in which material is built up to create form, or subtractive, where material is removed from an existing mass, such as a chunk of stone, wood or clay.

What are the 4 basic types of sculpture?

Sculptures fall into four basic categories: molded, cast, carved or assembled. The media an artist uses for molded sculptures include clay, wax, papier-mache and plaster.

What is three-dimensional visual art?

Artworks that have depth as well as height and width and that exist in space are three-dimensional. This category includes sculpture, environmental art, craft and folk art, architecture, and other mixed media art
