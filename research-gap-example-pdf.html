2. types of qualitative research pdf


Qualitative Research

The following are various types of qualitative research:

In-depth interview Focus groups Ethnographic research Content analysis Case study research

In-depth interview

This type of qualitative research is conducted by doing an interview regarding the topic. The researchers try to get the information they needed as much as possible. This can be done by one-on-one or group interview.

Focus groups

This type of qualitative research is similar to a group discussion. The people involved have similar knowledge and backgrounds relevant to the research topic they have.

Ethnographic research

Focuses on the behavior and environment of people.

Content analysis

Careful analysis of data provided that is done by experts on their respective fields.

Case study research

This type of research focuses more on a specific case and how that case could affect or influence other future researches.

25. Steps to find out research gap in research paper?


Answer:

Identify the research question: The first step is to clearly understand the research question that the paper is trying to address. This will help you to identify the scope of the research and the context in which it was conducted.

Review the literature: Conduct a thorough review of the relevant literature on the topic. Look for research papers, journal articles, conference proceedings, and other sources that address the same or similar research questions.

Identify the key findings: Read through the literature and identify the key findings from the previous research that relate to the research question.

Identify the limitations: Look for gaps or limitations in the previous research. Identify areas where the research is incomplete, inconclusive, or where there are contradictory findings.

Develop the research gap: Based on the key findings and the limitations of previous research, identify the gap in knowledge that your research can address. This will help to develop the research gap that your research paper will focus on.

Refine the research question: Refine the research question based on the research gap that you have identified. Make sure that the research question is clear, focused, and relevant to the identified gap in knowledge.

Develop the research design: Finally, develop a research design that is appropriate for addressing the research question and the identified research gap. This should include a detailed methodology, data collection and analysis techniques, and a plan for disseminating the research findings.

The research gap should be identified and presented in the introduction section of your research paper. The introduction typically provides an overview of the topic of the paper and the context of the research. It should also include a clear statement of the research problem or question, which leads to the identification of the research gap.

In this section, you should highlight the current state of knowledge and research on the topic and explain what is not yet known or understood, which creates the research gap. The research gap should be presented as a problem or a question that your study aims to address, and this should be done in a clear and concise manner.

It is important to clearly define the research gap and explain its significance, as this will help to justify the need for your study and provide a rationale for the research.

Example:

Let's say a previous research paper focused on the effect of exercise on overall physical health in middle-aged individuals. The key finding of the paper was that exercise had a positive impact on overall physical health. However, the paper did not investigate the specific impact of exercise on mental health.

In this case, the research gap could be identified as the lack of research on the relationship between exercise and mental health in middle-aged individuals. This is a gap in the current knowledge base that your research paper could potentially fill. The research question for your paper could be "What is the relationship between exercise and mental health in middle-aged individuals?"

By identifying this research gap, your research could potentially provide new insights into the impact of exercise on mental health in middle-aged individuals, which would add to the existing body of research and further advance our understanding of the topic.
