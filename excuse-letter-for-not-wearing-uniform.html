Step-by-step explanation:

see the picture for the answer and solution:)

Answer:

Wearing PE uniforms: 128 students

Wearing school uniforms: 512 students

Step-by-step explanation:

To find out how many students are wearing PE uniforms:

First, convert 20% to a decimal.

20%= 0.2

Then multiply it by the number of students

(Number of Students) (0.2) = [Students wearing PE uniforms]

(640) (0.2)= 128

To find how many are wearing school uniforms:

Method one:

[Total number of students] - [number of students wearing PE uniforms] = [students wearing school uniforms]

Method two:

Knowing 100% minus 20% is 80%, convert 80% to a decimal.

80% = 0.8

Then multiply that by the total number of students

(Number of students) (0.8) = [students wearing school uniforms]

You get the same answer of 512, either way.
