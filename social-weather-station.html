13. supposed you are a volunteer stationed in an area vulnerable to typhoon and flood hazards.how are hydrometeorological hazard maps helpful in preparing for extreme weather events of that area?​


As a volunteer in areas vulnerable to typhoon and flood hazards, hydro-meteorological hazard maps provide important information to do my duty efficiently. Hazard maps show risk areas, evacuation sites, and evacuation routes. This map is created for natural hazards, like earthquakes, volcanoes, landslides, flooding, and tsunamis. It prevents serious damage and deaths when used. Thus, it will help me to understand the risks of natural hazards and mitigate disasters.

Types of Hazard Map

There are many types of a hazard map that shows risk and vulnerability in a specific place, for example:

Hazard maps comprise a time frame location.  Susceptibility maps combine different causes that contribute to a hazard and indicates where the hazard is more likely to occur. Inventory maps display a collection of events that have occurred at a location as a reference.  An evacuation map is used by community leaders to plan for evacuation when needed.

Disaster Management Cycle

Disaster management (https://brainly.ph/question/1717634) objectives are to lessen the potential losses from hazards, give prompt and appropriate assistance to victims of disaster, and achieve quick and real recovery. Listed below are the disaster management cycle:

Mitigation of the effects of a disaster.  Examples: assigning codes and zoning; explore vulnerability; inform the community. Disaster response planning.  Examples: preparedness plans; emergency simulation; creation of warning systems. Efforts to reduce the hazards created by a disaster.  Examples: search and rescue; relief operation Recovery program of the community to normal.  Examples: temporary housing; grants; medical care.

14. which one do you think reliable the station model or the 500mb weather map​


The 500mb chart is a constant pressure chart which means that everywhere on the chart the air pressure is the same (500mb). This occurs in our atmosphere, on average, at a height of about 5600 meters or about 18,000ft above sea level but varies from place to place due to the density of the air column


Station model plots use an internationally accepted coding convention that has changed little since August 1, 1941. Elements in the plot show the key weather elements, including temperature, dew point, wind, cloud cover, air pressure, pressure tendency, and precipitation.

The temperature at 12 noon was 10°C above zero. If it decreases at the rate of 2°C per hour until midnight, at what time would the temperature be 8°C below zero?

What would be the temperature at mid-night?

Answer:

ANSWER:

From the question is given that,

Temperature at the beginning i.e., at 12 noon = 10°C

Rate of change of temperature = - 2°C per hour

Then,

Temperature at 1 PM = 10 + (-2) = 10 – 2 = 8°C

Temperature at 2 PM = 8 + (-2) = 8 – 2 = 6°C

Temperature at 3 PM = 6 + (-2) = 6 – 2 = 4°C

Temperature at 4 PM = 4 + (-2) = 4 – 2 = 2°C

Temperature at 5 PM = 2 + (-2) = 2 – 2 = 0°C

Temperature at 6 PM = 0 + (-2) = 0 – 2 = -2°C

Temperature at 7 PM = -2 + (-2) = -2 -2 = -4°C

Temperature at 8 PM = -4 + (-2) = -4 – 2 = -6°C

Temperature at 9 PM = -6 + (-2) = -6 – 2 = -8°C

∴At 9 PM the temperature will be 8°C below zero

Then,

The temperature at mid-night i.e., at 12 AM

Change in temperature in 12 hours = -2°C × 12 = - 24°C

So, at midnight temperature will be = 10 + (-24)

= - 14°C

So, at midnight temperature will be 14°C below 0.
