This instrument contains two lens systems for magnifying specimens: the ocular lens in the eyepiece and the objective lens located in the nose-piece. 29. It has to do with the individual's ability to manage their life.​


Self-determination has to do with the individual's ability to manage their life. To manage their life could mean to make choices that impact their lives in one way or another. A person's ability to manage their life is very crucial to their well-being, particularly their psychological health.

Further Explanation

Self-determination theory is a vital aspect of motivation that solves the problems of intrinsic and extrinsic motivation. The theory was proposed in 1985 by psychologists Edward L. Deci and Richard M. Ryan.

The psychologists in their book titled "Self-Determination and Intrinsic Motivation in Human Behaviour" explain the two types of motivation, which include; intrinsic and extrinsic motivation.

Intrinsic motivation is a type of motivation that comes within while extrinsic motivation is a behaviour that is motivated by positive external rewards such as money, grades, praise, etc.

However, the three major needs that driven intrinsic motivation according to self-determination theory include:

Competence: this means a person need to gain skills to achieve their desired goalsRelatedness: means people should be connected to others, which means they should a sense of belongingAutonomy: this means the desire to have the ultimate control ove one's life. It is also a major factor that helps people to achieve their desired goals.
