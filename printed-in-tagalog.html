2. print media meaning Tagalog​


Answer:

Ang print media ay isa sa pinakaluma at pangunahing uri ng komunikasyon sa masa. Kasama rito ang mga pahayagan, weeklies, magasin, monthlies at iba pang anyo ng nakalimbag na journal. Ang isang pangunahing pag-unawa sa print media ay mahalaga sa pag-aaral ng komunikasyon sa masa.

Answer:print-based media) Malawak, anumang nakasulat o nakalarawan na anyo ng komunikasyon na nagawa nang mekanikal o elektronikong gamit ang pag-print, photocopying, o digital na pamamaraan kung saan maaaring gawin ang maraming kopya sa pamamagitan ng mga awtomatikong proseso.

2. Mas makitid, ang anumang uri ng komunikasyon na ‘tinta at papel’ na hindi sulat-kamay o uri ng kamay, kasama ang mga libro, pabilog, journal, lithograp, memo, magasin, pahayagan, polyeto, at peryodiko.

Answer:

1.It contains basic biblical texts, including the Lord's Prayer, Hail Mary, and the Salve Regina, as well as the Ten Commandments, and the Sacraments of the Holy Church. The Doctrina Christiana is not only one of the earliest books printed in the Philippines.

2.The Doctrina Christiana was printed in the Philippines in 1593 as part of the effort to introduce Christianity.

3.The book was printed on paper made from mulberry.

4.The size of the volume, which is unbound, is 9⅛ by 7 inches

5.Fray Juan de Plasencia

6.King Philip II

7.It has seminal significance to the Christianity of the Philippines, and to the faith that marks a huge majority of Filipinos.

9. examples of block printing (mas maganda Po pag Tagalog)​


Answer:

Block, or relief, printing includes linocuts, lino prints, woodblock prints, rubber stamping - pretty much any method where you carve into a material (a block, plate, etc.) and print an impression of the carved surface with ink.

Tagalog:

Ang block, o relief, printing ay kinabibilangan ng mga linocut, lino print, woodblock print, rubber stamping - halos anumang paraan kung saan ka nag-ukit sa isang materyal (isang bloke, plato, atbp.) at nag-print ng impresyon ng inukit na ibabaw na may tinta.

15. the first tagalog novel printed in the Philippines?


Answer:

Nínay is the first novel authored by a native Filipino.

Answer:

Nínay is the first novel authored by a native Filipino. Originally written in the Spanish language by Pedro Alejandro Paterno when he was twenty-three years old and while living in Spain in 1885, the novel was later translated into English in 1907 and into Tagalog in 1908.
