7. in how many ways can a president of vice-president a secretary and the treasurer be elected from a class with 39 students?​


Answer:

Selecting with order or rank = 1,974,024 ways

Step-by-step explanation:

Given

n = 39 students

r = 4 officers

A. Selecting the officers with order: 1st president, 2nd vice, 3rd secretary, 4th treasurer

Solution:

[tex]P(n,r)=\frac{n!}{(n-r)!}[/tex]

[tex]P(n,r)=\frac{39!}{(35!}[/tex]

[tex]P(n,r)=\frac{39x38x37x36}[/tex]

[tex]P(n.r)=1,974,024[/tex] ways with order

B: Selecting the officers without regard to the order

Solution:

[tex]C(n,r)=\frac{n!}{(r!)(n-r)!}[/tex]

[tex]C(n,r)=\frac{89 x 38 x 37 x 36}{4 x 3 x 2 x 1}[/tex]

[tex]C(n,r)=\frac{1974024}{24}[/tex]

[tex]C(n,r) = 82,251[/tex]  ways without order

24. During the classroom election, 60% of the class voted for Nana for president. If there are 40 pupils in that class, how many pupils voted for Nana?


Answer:

24 pupils

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

60% - class voted Nana for president

40 - number of pupils in class

60%0f 40

= (.60)(40)

= 24 pupils

Therefore, there are 24 pupils voted for Nana.

24.

Step-by-step explanation:

Step 1: In the given case Output Value is 40.

Step 2: Let us consider the unknown value as x.

Step 3: Consider the output value of 40 = 100%.

Step 4: In the Same way, x = 60%.

Step 5: On dividing the pair of simple equations we got the equation as under

40 = 100% (1).

x = 60% (2).

(40%)/(x%) = 100/60

Step 6: Reciprocal of both the sides results in the following equation

x%/40% = 60/100

Step 7: Simplifying the above obtained equation further will tell what is 60% of 40

x = 24%

Therefore, 60% of 40 is 24
