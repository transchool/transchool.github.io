4. why the NSTP - MAPUA office created the 3 component of NSTP​


Answer:

The Republic Act No. 9163, which is the act that establishes the National Service Training Program for tertiary level students, stipulated that the program will have three components tertiary level students can sign up for with the purpose of enhancing civic consciousness and defense preparedness in the youth by developing the ethics of service and patriotism. These programs are the following:

Reserve-Officers’ Training Corps or ROTC which is a program institutionalized under Sections 38 and 39 of Republic Act No. 7077 intended to provide military training to tertiary level students in order to motivate, train, organize and mobilize them as part of national defense preparednessLiteracy Training Service or LTS which is a program developed to train students to become teachers of reading, writing, and number skills to school children, out of school youth, and other segments of society who are in need of their serviceCivic Welfare Training Service or CWTS refers to programs or activities contributory to the general welfare and the enhancement of life for the members of the community or the betterment of its facilities, especially those devoted to improving health, education, environment, entrepreneurship, safety, recreation and morals of the Filipino citizens.

Mapua, just like other universities, is following the rules and regulations set by the Republic Act No. 9163.

NSTP PROGRAM IN THE PHILIPPINES

1. The Civic Welfare Training Service course, mandated by Republic Act No. 9163, also known as the National Service Training Act of 2001, seeks to improve students' civic consciousness "through fostering the principles of service and patriotism" while undertaking Civic Welfare Training Service (CWTS). NSTP1 presents themes in large campus sessions that capitalize on students' excitement and idealism for nation-building, leadership, and civic action. It prepares students for genuine community service in NSTP 2 by combining active reflection in a creative dynamic learning environment.

2. The goal of this program is to acknowledge the importance of youth in nation-building, to raise awareness among young, and to nurture their physical, moral, spiritual, intellectual, and social well-being. It will instill patriotism and nationalism in the youth and encourage their participation in public and civic issues. In order to achieve these aims, the nation's most important resource, the youth, must be inspired, trained, organized, and mobilized in military training, literacy, civic welfare, and/or comparable activities to the nation's service. The primary role of the government, as stated in RA 9163, is to serve and protect its inhabitants. In turn, it must be the responsibility of all citizens to protect the security of the state, and the government may ask each citizen to provide personal, military, or civil service in order to meet this obligation.

SIGNIFICANCE OF NSTP IN ESTABLISHING LINKAGES WITH THE COMMUNITY

In order to understand how NSTP builds linkages, one must first understand the goals and objectives of the program.

The National Service Training Program (NSTP) cited the Constitution's "duty of the state to serve and protect its citizens." It emphasizes the need for youth participation in nation-building. As a result, it tries to inspire young people to become civic or military leaders and volunteers who the country could call upon if their services are required.

NSTP ensures that all three components receive the same and equal implementation in educational institutions. These are as follows:

Civic Welfare Service: It consists of programs or activities that contribute to the community's overall well-being and betterment of life for its members, as well as the enhancement of its facilities, particularly those devoted to improving citizens' health, education, environment, entrepreneurship, safety, recreation, and morals. Literacy Training Service: A curriculum that prepares students to teach reading and numeracy skills to school children, out-of-school kids, and other members of society who require their assistance. Reserve Officers Training Corps: Under Sections 38 and 39 of Republic Act No. 7077, this program was established to provide military training to tertiary students to encourage, train, organize, and mobilize them for national defense readiness.

To summarize, the program aimed to strengthen civic consciousness and defense preparedness in youths by establishing service and patriotism principles while receiving training in any three (3) program components. They created these components to enhance the youth's active participation in the general welfare.

This program has many advantages. Individuals develop a sense of patriotism. They also learn how to work in a group and be loyal to one another. Furthermore, the NSTP highlights the need for a national youth development and mobilization program to help nation-building. The program exists and operates in the framework of education and national security systems, which are significant aspects of the more extensive national development and security policy system, in light of the aforementioned scheme and goals.

These services may no longer be possible in the new normal. They may be prohibited from visiting communities, much less perform physical services for them. However, the NSTP Course has clearly had an impact on our youth. We'd seen young people volunteering in the neighborhood. This culture of volunteerism is evident in modest acts such as packing relief supplies, asking for assistance, or even founding a "community pantry" and many more. The program helps establish linkages by encouraging the young generation to participate and get involved.

22. write the reflective essay in 300 words about the significance of the programs of the NSTP in establishing linkages with the community?


Answer:

SIGNIFICANCE OF NSTP IN ESTABLISHING LINKAGES WITH THE COMMUNITY

(Essay)

What is NSTP? Why is it included in the curriculum? What is its significance in establishing linkages with the community?

     National Service Training Program or NSTP is established by the Republic Act 9163. It is intended for Tertiary Level students with an aim to inculcate in the students the spirit of nationalism and advance their involvement in public. It also aims to promote civic consciousness among the youth. Furthermore, NSTP is a program designed to develop the youth’s intellectual, physical, spiritual, moral, and social well-being and promote defense preparedness and ethics of service while undergoing training in any of its three program components. Its components are particularly designed to enhance the youth’s active contribution to the general welfare.

    The three components which establish community linkages. Those students can choose from: a) Reserve Officers’ Training Corps (ROTC), b) Literacy Training Service (LTS), and c) Civic Welfare Training Service (CWTS). ROTC is a program institutionalized under Sections 38 and 39 of Republic Act No. 7077.  It is designed to provide military training to tertiary level students in order to motivate, train, organize and mobilize them for national defense preparedness. The second option is the LTS which is a program intended to train students to become teachers of numeracy skills and literacy to school children, out-of-school youth, and other segments of society in need of their service. The last option is CWTS  which composed of programs or activities contributory to the general  welfare and the betterment of life for the members of the community or the enhancement of its facilities.

    Each of the NSTP program components shall be undertaken for an academic period of two semesters. It is equivalent to a total of six units for two semesters.

Explanation:

A Common Module phase which will focus on citizenship training; disaster awareness, preparedness, and management; drug education; environmental protection; and other national security concerns are the subjects that students enrolled in the first semester of NSTP shall undergo. After completion of the Common Module, the student must select the specific program component that he/she will pursue. Numeric grading will be used for NSTP classes which are upon the discretion of the instructor who is handling the NSTP class. Grades for NSTP will not be included in the computation of the students’ weighted average.

Graduates of the CWTS and LTS components of the NSTP shall belong to the National Service Reserve Corps (NSRC) and could be tapped by the State for literacy and civic welfare activities, especially in times of calamities while graduates of the ROTC program shall form part of the Citizen Armed Force.
