11. What is the total mechanical energy of a yoyo


The total mechanical energy of an object refers to the sum of its kinetic and potential energies.

Before the yo-yo starts moving, it possesses energy in the form of gravitational potential energy. Its amount is equivalent to the product of its mass, its height from the ground and the acceleration due to gravity (-9.8 m/s²).

Upon moving or as the yo-yo starts to fall, its gravitational potential energy decreases and transforms to kinetic energy. The amount of its kinetic energy is equivalent to the half the product of its mass and the square of its velocity. It increases as the gravitational potential energy decreases.

Take note that the total mechanical energy of the yo-yo is conserved, which means it is constant all throughout. The gravitational potential energy and the kinetic energy will constantly change, depending on the position of the yo-yo from the ground (height) and its velocity. However, the total mechanical energy will not change.

14. What Is the mechanics playing yoyo?what is the rules playing yoyo?​


Mechanics playing yoyo.

-The spinning yo-yo contains angular momentum (or rotational kinetic energy) derived from the gravitation potential energy through which the yo-yo has dropped. Usually, the string is tied loosely around the axle so that the yo-yo can continue to spin at the bottom.

Rules playing yoyo.

-Yo-Yo(s) must start spinning with the power coming from the unwinding string. All tricks must be performed while the yo-yo(s) is spinning. If the yo-yo is not spinning no points will be counted. Yo-Yo(s) must return to the contestant's hand with fully wound string(s) with its own spin power.

1.HARERA DE SAKO

MECHANICS: Karera ng Sako (sack race) - Two teams race each other around a defined area while carrying a bag. In order to complete the task, each team member must walk around the allocated area with their feet inside the sack. The race is won by the team that completes the round in the correct sequence.

RULES:

1.Make a start line and finish line with the string.

2.Stand inside your sack and hold on to the top of it.

3.When one player yells GO! hop as fast as you can to the finish line, first there wins.

2.LUKSONG TINIK

MECHANICS: The objective of the game is for all players in a team to jump over the height of the piled hands of the opposing team. A player must avoid hitting the piled hands with any part of his body or clothing. The two mothers are expected to be high jumpers.

RULES:

1. Two players on the base will sit facing each other with their right feet touching.

2. The jumpers jump over the feet.

3. Then one hand is placed above the feet with palms and fingers open, then another, and so on until all the fingers are piled one on top of the other.

3.YOYO

MECHANICS: The spinning yo-yo contains angular momentum (or rotational kinetic energy) derived from the gravitataion potential energy through which the yo-yo has dropped. Usually, the string is tied loosely around the axle so that the yo-yo can continue to spin at the bottom.

RULES:

1. Yo-Yo(s) must start spinning with the power coming from the unwinding string.

2. All tricks must be performed while the yo-yo(s) is spinning.

3. If the yo-yo is not spinning no points will be counted. Yo-Yo(s) must return to the contestant's hand with fully wound string(s) with its own spin power.

4.TUMBANG PRESO

MECHANICS: The objective is for the players to hit and knock down the can with the slipper, and for the tayà to put back the can inside a small circle a few meters away from the toe-line. When a player is tagged while recovering their slippers, they become the tayà.

RULES:

1. Players must have slippers to hit the can precisely.

2. The IT must never go to the player zone unless he or she will tag a player without slippers.

3. Players must never go to the IT zone unless he or she throw the slippers

4. Players must throw the slippers or else he or she will be the IT in the next round.

5.KADANG KADANG

MECHANICS: The players are set in a way that the first player stands behind the starting line, while the second player will stand behind the 25th mark of the line, the third player behind the 50th mark, and the fourth player stands behind the 75th mark.

RULES:

1. Primarily, kadang-kadang is a racing game. Before starting the game,

2. players should form two parallel lines with a distance of 100 meters apart to serve as the starting and finish line and then mark every 25th meter of each line.

3. The game needs four players in each team.

ANSWER: Elastic potential energy

The energy change take place as the toy operates is Elastic potential energy.

Some other common example of elastic potential energy is rubber band, hairband and etc.

Elastic potential energy is potential energy stored as a result of deformation of an elastic object, such as the stretching of a spring. It is equal to the work done to stretch the spring, which depends upon the spring constant k as well as the distance stretched.
