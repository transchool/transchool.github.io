15. Generally, compounds have chemical names and ________ names​


Answer:

Formula names

Explanation:

Scientists need to be able to identify each chemical compound and to communicate about them in an unambiguous way. Structural formulas can only be written, you can't use them in a conversation. That's why names, and formulas are both used.

25. what is the difference between trade name and generic name?​


Answer:

The brand name of a medication is the name given by the company that makes the drug and is usually easy to say for sales and marketing purposes. The generic name, on the other hand, is the name of the active ingredient.

Step-by-step explanation:

Generic drugs are copies of brand-name drugs that have exactly the same dosage, intended use, effects, side effects, route of administration, risks, safety, and strength as the original drug. ...Sample: A generic drug, one used for hypertension, is metoprolol, whereas a brand name for the same drug is Lopressor.
