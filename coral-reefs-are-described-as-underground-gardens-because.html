17. what are the different types of coral reefs Describe each the three main types of coral reefs are fringing barrier​


Different types of coral reefs and the 3 main types of coral reefs insight

Types of coral reefs

Fringing reefs is form around the coastlines of islands and continents. They are separated from the beach by a series of small, shallow lagoons. The most prevalent form of reef is a fringing reef.

Barrier reefs run along to the shore as well, although they are separated by deeper, larger lagoons. They can reach the water's surface at their shallowest spots, constituting a "barrier" to navigation.

Atolls are coral rings that form sheltered lagoons and are often found in the middle of the sea. Atolls arise when islands surrounded by surrounding reefs drop into the sea or when the sea level rises around them.

Patch reefs are tiny, isolated reefs that occur on the open bottom of an island platform or continental shelf. They are most commonly found between fringing reefs and barrier reefs.

The three main types of coral reefs are fringing, barrier, and atoll.

fringing

The most common type of reef is the bordering one. This type of reef develops just off the coast, out to sea. They build barriers along the water's edge and around the nearby islands.

atoll

When a volcanic island sinks fully below sea level, a surrounding reef continues to expand upward, forming an atoll. With a center open lagoon, atolls are often circular or oval in shape.

barrier

Barrier reefs, like bordering reefs, surround a coastline; but, instead of extending immediately out from the beach, they are separated from land by an expanse of water. This results in the formation of a lagoon of open, frequently deep water between the reef and the coast.

FRINGING REEF

A fringing reef is one of the three main types of coral reef. It is distinguished from the other main types, barrier reefs and atolls, in that it has either an entirely shallow backreef zone or none at all.

BARRIER REEF

The Great Barrier Reef is the world's largest coral reef system composed of over 2,900 individual reefs and 900 islands stretching for over 2,300 kilometres over an area of approximately 344,400 square kilometres.

ATOLL REEF

An atoll is a ring-shaped coral reef, island, or series of islets. An atoll surrounds a body of water called a lagoon. Sometimes, atolls and lagoons protect a central island. Channels between islets connect a lagoon to the open ocean or sea. Atolls develop with underwater volcanoes, called seamounts.

26. describe coral reef and mangrove swamps ecosystem​


Answer:

Coral reef and mangrove ecosystems function in a symbiotic relationship, which enhances tropical and subtropical coastal environments. Together the coral reef and mangrove ecosystems form a barrier that protects shorelines from the destructive forces of wind, waves and driven debris. Mangrove swamps are coastal wetlands found in tropical and subtropical regions. They are characterized by halophytic (salt loving) trees, shrubs and other plants growing in brackish to saline tidal waters.  Mangrove trees dominate this wetland ecosystem due to their ability to survive in both salt and fresh water.
