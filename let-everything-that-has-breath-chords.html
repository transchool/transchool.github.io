15. A chord os a circle is 18 cm long. How far is the chord from the center of the circle if the circle has a diameter of 30 cm?


Answer:

The distance from the chord to the center of thecircle is 15 cm.

Solution:

The radius of the circle is half the diameter, so r = 30 cm / 2 = 15 cm. Since the chord passes through the center of the circle, the distance from the chord to the center is equal to the radius. Therefore, the distance from the chord to the center is 15 cm.

Focus - x²=8y

one end point is at (-8,8)

y = 1/8 x²


To get the vertex, let's use the formula y = a ( x − h )² + k

Then determine the value of a, h and k

a = 1/8

h = 0

k = 0


We can now say that the parabola opens up since the value of a is a postive number.

To find the vertex simply follow (h,k), therefore the vertex is (0,0).


Now, we can find the distance from the vertex to the focus.

Use the formula 1/(4a) the do the substitution.

= 1/ (4(1/8)

Multiply the numerator to the reciprocal of the denominator

= 1(8/4)

Then to simply 8/4, We can get 2


Focus (0,2)


To find the axis of the symmetry, find the line the passes through the vertex (0,0) and the focus (0,2), therefore it is x = 0.


To get the directrix, we can use y = k - p

and now you can get the value y = -2
