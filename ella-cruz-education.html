4. Cassandra, Joan, and Ella are playing at the school's playground. Ella sits at the swing and asked her friends to push her. Cassandra used 50 Newtons to push Ella from the swing while Joan used 55 Newtons. Who will make Ella swing faster? Why? ​


Answer:

Joan will make Ella swing faster.

Explanation:

This situation can be explained by Newton's second law of motion. This law of motion tells us that acceleration is directly proportional to the amount of force applied and inversely proportional to the mass of the object. Acceleration increases as you increase the amount of force applied. On the other hand, acceleration decreases as mass increases. For example, if you want to push a block of the same mass, you can make it move faster if you apply greater force.

In the situation given, they applied the force to the same person thus mass is constant. However, Joan used 55 Newtons which is greater than the 50-N force of Cassandra. Since greater force means greater acceleration, thus, Joan will make Ella swing faster.
