A character is a person, or sometimes even an animal, who takes part in the action of a short story or other literary work.

   

The setting of a short story is the time and place in which it happens. Authors often use descriptions of landscape, scenery, buildings, seasons or weather to provide a strong sense of setting.

 

 

A plot is a series of events and character actions that relate to the central conflict.

 

The conflict is a struggle between two people or things in a short story. The main character is usually on one side of the central conflict.

On the other side, the main character may struggle against another important character, against the forces of nature, against society, or even against something inside himself or herself (feelings, emotions, illness).

   

The theme is the central idea or belief in a short story.

26. PLOT of the short story SINIGANG​


The narrative opens with Liza, a young child, and her Tita Loleng making Liza's favorite meal, sinigang. It was never Liza's time to assist her Tita Loleng in making sinigang in the past. Today, however, Liza was given the responsibility by her Tita Loleng to choose the veggies that would be used in the Sinigang. They discussed Liza's attendance at the funeral of Lem, her half-brother, as they were preparing dinner.

Lem was clearly her father's son, even though he passed away from cancer. When he was still living, Lem wasn't always the nicest to Liza. He would always refer to Liza as "sinverguenza," the shameless daughter, despite the fact that he was an acolyte for the church. During the funeral, Liza recalled that she was received by Lem's mother.

She said she wasn't as attractive as her mother. Liza simply nodded in response to Lem's mother's request for pardon. This was interpreted as a yes by Lem's mother, who then gave Liza a bear embrace. She remembered that she smelt like perspiration and perfume, but she didn't move out of politeness.

As a child, Liza used to wonder who the boy was who kept contacting his father every day. It also hurt her deeply now that she knew the solution. Always Daddy's girl, she was. When it was time for Lem to be buried, Liza's father looked at her and apologized as they watched as his casket was lowered.

Dinner will be the sinigang she and her Tita Loleng prepared. Everything will remain unchanged while changing. After telling her mother about his day, her father would ask each of them about theirs. He would then complement her on the Sinigang that they had prepared, and she would grin at him as she always did. But this time, she won't even allow her eyes to smile.
