3. What is provocation?​


Answer:

Provocation is the act of saying or doing something that is intended to anger, annoy, or incite someone else. It is a form of inciting or stimulating someone to do something, especially to commit a crime or to take action against someone or something. Provocation is often seen as a defense against a charge of assault or other violent crime, as the defendant may argue that they were provoked into committing the act in question.


4. what is meant by the element of sufficient provocation


Answer:

Provocation may be defined by statutory law, by common law, or some combination. ... In common law, provocation is established by establishing events that would be "adequate" to create a heat of passion in a reasonable person, and by establishing that the heat of passion was created in the accused.
