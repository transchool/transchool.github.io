29. what descriptions of marikina valley fault​


Archaeological evidence tells us that the Marikina Fault earthquake zone, that runs almost North-South along the Marikina Valley, from Bulacan to Cavite and Laguna, and almost exactly parallels the route of the C-5 circumferential road around Metro Manila, slips every 200-400 years (average every 310 years) delivering a magnitude 6.0-7.0 earthquake (maximum estimated 7.5). The last time the Marikina Fault earthquake zone slipped is calculated to have been in 1771AD, meaning that this geological fault is not due for another movement for about 50 more years, based on the average. So a better-safe-than-sorry philosophy suggests even more reasons to chill out on the beaches, especially around Puerto Galera.
