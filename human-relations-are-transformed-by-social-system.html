9. Explain how human relations are transformed by social systems. Illustrate with an example.​


Social Systems

A social system is a patterned network of relationships that exist between individuals, groups, and institutions to form a coherent whole. In a small, stable group, it is the formal structure of role and status that can form.

How human relations are transformed by social systems?

Humans are the most social species on the planet, which is why our society and social system influence almost every human-related aspect.

Similarly, the social system influences and transforms human relations.

1) Religion and caste are social system components that influence relationships between people of different religions or castes.

2) Cultural preferences influence human relationships in a variety of ways.

And there are many more.

11. human relations which are transformed by social system political​


The environment faced by a society presents challenges to that society, when it attempts to grow.

Society has to face the environment is behavior of all the individuals.

If the behaviors followed are “Good behaviors”, behaviors that grow society, that society will grow which has been the intention of every society since the beginning of time.

“We're the best, lets make more of us.”

We're social primates and therefore groups are essential crucial and actually we are group members before we're individuals!

We all want to be “in with the in crowd”!

Good behaviors grow society bad behaviors diminish society and therefore behaviors are the genes of social evolution. Changes in the behaviors across the entire population will either grow or see the society decline.

How does society install behaviors that grow? Religion is the institution responsible for good behavior that's why it owns morality.

So human relations in a particular society are those behaviors that will grow that society. It's different for every society, since they all face different environments, and need slightly different behaviors in over to overcome all challenges and grow the society.

The social system is what creates human relations in that society.

There are 4000 different religions because they all suggest slightly different behaviors.

Here's an example.

In the Middle East, pork and shellfish and other types of animals are not considered clean according to the religions.

In Polynesia the religions celebrate pigs.

It's obviously not the pig's fault!

We must seek a social benefit to why those societies act the way they do. Why would both societies suspect that totally opposite behaviors would make them grow?

The reaction of one society to the other is ”those people aren't like us, they're crazy, if they are even human!”.

In Polynesia pigs eat root vegetables which are plentiful and pork taste better than poi, and they are signs of wealth.

In the Middle East people were farmers. Back then they didn't have shotguns and a few pigs will destroy a field of wheat overnight.

Cows eat pasture.

Cows are much easier to milk than a sow.

So the behaviors you're talking about, human relations, are exactly what allows us to evolve as a society. Good behaviors grow society bad behaviors diminish society.

It's also allows us to discuss social evolution in a different dimension.

The environment needs to be overcome and the environment is constantly changing.

If a disease that had been causing a pig to have a cold becomes a disease that's mutates to being deadly to humans, the Polynesian’s behavior will put them at higher risk of diminishing than the Middle Eastern behavior that avoids pork in the first place.

Random changes in the environment are faced by societies with a variety of behaviors (variation) that either succeed or that society could go extinct.

Exactly analogous to biological evolution. where the environment is faced by a set of genes that either succeed in having that population continue or not.

That's social evolution. Human relations are what face the challenge of growth.
