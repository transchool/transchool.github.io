16. 3. When did tropical storm Sendong leave the PAR?​


Tropical Storm

Tropical Storm "Sendong" (Washi) was the 21st tropical cyclone that entered the country in 2011. It is also considered as the strongest that year. It formed in the Pacific Ocean, around 945 kilometers southeast of Guam on December 13, 2011, and entered the Philippine Area of Responsibility (PAR) on December 15.

Tropical storm Sendong left the par on December 18, 2011.

One of the natural phenomena related to weather, namely tropical storms.

Tropical storms are a form of tropical cyclones that occur due to concentrated low pressure originating from warm tropical oceans. This natural phenomenon can occur in any ocean basin on Earth, as long as tropical cyclones exist.

Reporting from the Encyclopaedia Britannica, several locations that have experienced tropical storms include the North Atlantic, northeastern Pacific, central Pacific, northwest and southwest Pacific, and India.

Relationship between tropical storms and tropical cyclones

In general, tropical cyclones are large storms that grow in sea waters around tropical and subtropical regions with warm sea surface temperatures.

Tropical cyclones are divided into four forms based on the Saffir-Simpson scale, namely:

Tropical depression Is a tropical cyclone with wind speeds of 37 to 63 kilometers per hour.Tropical cyclone Is a tropical cyclone with wind speeds of 64 to 118 kilometers per hour.Hurricane Is a tropical cyclone with wind speeds of more than 118 kilometers per hour.Major hurricane Is a tropical cyclone with a minimum wind speed of 180 kilometers per hour.Thus, tropical storms are a form of tropical cyclone, apart from tropical depressions, hurricanes, and major hurricanes.
