T’BOLI ACCESSORIES HILOT is a heavy brass belt that women wear at festivals. T'boli aristocracy wear bracelets and anklets with ball-bearings that create noise when the wearer marches. The bells hanging from the distinctive brass or beaded belts herald the arrival of a lady.

ATTIRE,TEXTILES, AND TAPESTRIES

B'laan traditional dress The Bilaan or B'laan are an etnolinguistic tribe who live in South Cotabato, North Cotabato, and Davao del Sur. B'laan is made up of two words: "bila," which means "home," and "an," which means "people." They are well-known for the embroidery, brass ornaments, and beadwork used in their traditional clothes. B'laan dress in abaca-woven garments embellished with embroidery, pins, and beads. B'laans, like Bagobos and T'bolis, use ikat to weave finely polished abaca textiles. IKAT is a dyeing technique in which the yarns are closely bound with strings and dyed before weaving.

T'boli The people who live in and around Lake Sebu, South Cotabato, are well-known for their intricate beadwork, exquisite knit garments, and stunning brass ornaments. They, like the Bagobos, treat the t'nalak as holy. This cloth is exchanged as part of traditional wedding ceremonies. LANG-DULAY, the renowned T'boli dream weaver, was awarded the National Living Treasures – Gawad Manlilikha ng Bayan (GAMABA).

Men are not permitted to handle any of the items used in the weaving process. It is prohibited for the weaver to mate with her husband while the fabric is being made.

T'nalak creation is a time-consuming process that takes persistence, a lot of imagination, a strong memory, and a variety of skills acquired by the weavers from a young age.  T'boli weavers are employed to dye these handspun abaca fabrics.
