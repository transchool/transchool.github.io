Glutenin (a type of glutelin) is a major protein within wheat flour, making up 47% of the total protein content. The glutenin are protein aggregates of high-molecular-mass (HMW) and low-molecular-mass (LMW) subunits with molar masses from about 200,000 to a few million, which are stabilized by intermolecular disulfide bonds, hydrophobic interactions and other forces.
