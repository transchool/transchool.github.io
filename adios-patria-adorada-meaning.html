13. What lines are most significant in meaning in Mi Ultimo Adios by Jose Rizal?


Answer:

Interpretation: Mi Ultimo Adios (My Last Farewell)

Farewell, my adored Land, region of the sun caressed,

Pearl of the Orient Sea, our Eden lost,

With gladness I give you my Life, sad and repressed;

And were it more brilliant, more fresh and at its best,

I would still give it to you for your welfare at most.

These are the words of one about to die for his country. He expresses no regret but only gladness, knowing that in giving his life, he is giving his country the greatest gift any citizen could offer.

On the fields of battle, in the fury of fight,

Others give you their lives without pain or hesitancy,

The place does not matter: cypress laurel, lily white,

Scaffold, open field, conflict or martyrdom's site,

It is the same if asked by home and Country.

Here Rizal says that it does not matter where one dies, but why one dies and to what purpose. Whether it’s “scaffold, open field, conflict or martyrdom’s site,” all death hold the same honor if given for home and Country.

I die as I see tints on the sky b'gin to show

And at last announce the day, after a gloomy night;

If you need a hue to dye your matutinal glow,

Pour my blood and at the right moment spread it so,

And gild it with a reflection of your nascent light!

Rizal’s execution was set at sunrise, thus the meaning of the first and second lines. He employs the visual senses in his poetic use of color, and then in the third and fourth lines, adds the bright red tint of his blood to the scene, and gilds it with golden sunlight. The use of these devices ignites passion in the reader, as it is felt – a hundred times more so – in the writer, even without explicit use of words signifying feeling.

My dreams, when scarcely a lad adolescent,

My dreams when already a youth, full of vigor to attain,

Were to see you, gem of the sea of the Orient,

Your dark eyes dry, smooth brow held to a high plane

Without frown, without wrinkles and of shame without stain.

17. Buod ng Patria Amanda


Buod ng Patria Amanda

Ang buhay ni Patria Amanda ay nilikha noong 1916, ito ay isang sarsarswelang orihinal na kinatha ni Amando Navarette Osorio na sinalin naman sa Filipino ni Joey A. Arrogante.  

Nagumpisa ang kwento sa isang gabing madilim nasa lungsod malapit sa Maynila habang ang orkestra ay tumutugtog si Patria Amanda na nagbabasa ng pahayagan ay siyang nagaalala sa kanyang ama na sinundo ang kanyang kapatid na nag-aaral sa Ateneo dahil ang Katipunan noon ay umalsa laban sa pamahalaan sa Balintawak at si Bonifacio ay sumigaw ng kalayaan. Sa kabilang banda, sila'y nagtatalo ng kaniyang kasintahang si Felipe Libre na lilisan upang mamundok upang ipaglaban ang layunin at makiisa sa himagsikan.  

Si Felipe ay lumisan ngunit siya ay naligaw sa paghahanap ng mga naghihimagsik ngunit sa tulong ni Damon natagpuan nila ito. Habang kausap ni Felipe ang Heneral ay biglang dumating si Damon para sabihin na bobombahin ang kanilang bayan. Agad hinanap ni Felipe si Patria na papunta rin naman ng bundok.  

Si Patria ay sumama rin sa pakikidigma, siya ay nag salawal, kamiseta, sombrero at kumuha ng baril. Habang nasa labanan, ng malaman ng Heneral na si Patria ay nakikidigma ay agad itong pinapuntahan kay Damon.  

Si Damon ay bumalik sa Heneral at kay Felipe hatid ang mensaheng patay na si Patria. Tinanong ng Heneral kung nakita nito ang bangkay at kung nakita pang buhay ngunit si Damon ay nanghula lamang na si Patria ay patay na kaya ito ulit ay pinahanap. Pagdating ni Patria sa Heneral, agad namang nagbiro si Damon na patay na si Felipe. Hinimatay si Patria, agad naman lumapit si Felipe kay Patria para gisingin. Natapos ang kwento ng patugtugin ng Heneral ang "Awit sa Yutang Natauhan."
