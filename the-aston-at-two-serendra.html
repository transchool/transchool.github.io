The  timeline using illustrations and text showing how elements form with the concept of atomic number​ has to be created as a timeline of atomic models:

Atomic model (1808)

Dalton's atomic hypothesis was a scientific theory on the nature of matter proposed in 1808 by the English physicist and chemist John Dalton. It stated that all matter was composed of microscopic, indivisible components known as atoms.

Plum-pudding model (1904)

In 1904 Thomson created what became known as the "plum pudding" model. Plum pudding is a traditional English dish that is comparable to a blueberry muffin. The electrons in Thomson's plum pudding model of the atom were immersed in a homogeneous sphere of positive charge, much like blueberries in a muffin.

Nuclear model (1911)

The "Rutherford nuclear atom" and the "Rutherford Planetary Model" were other names for the Rutherford atomic model. Rutherford defined the atom in 1911 as having a compact, dense, and positively charged core known as the nucleus. Rutherford discovered that the atom's mass is concentrated in its nucleus.

Planetary model (1913)

Neils Bohr, a Rutherford student, created a new model of the atom in 1913. He postulated that electrons orbit the nucleus in concentric circular orbits. This model, known as the planetary model, is based on the solar system.

Quantum mechanical model (1926-present)

Erwin Schrödinger (1887-1961), an Austrian physicist, exploited the electron's wave-particle duality in 1926 to formulate and solve a difficult mathematical equation that precisely represented the behavior of the electron in a hydrogen atom.

Postulates of the Atomic model:    The matter is made of small indivisible particles called atoms.    Atoms cannot be created or destroyed.    Atoms of the same element have the same properties.    Atoms of different elements have different properties.    Atoms of different elements can form compounds.

An element is composed of atoms that have the same atomic number, that is, each atom has the same number of protons in its nucleus as all other atoms of that element. Element is a pure substance consisting only of atoms.
