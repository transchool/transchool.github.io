The Suez Canal is an artificial sea-level waterway in Egypt, connecting the Mediterranean Sea to the Red Sea through the Isthmus of Suez, and separates the African continent from Asia. 19. Why does wedding in cana the first miracle of jesus?


Answer:

Jesus has never performed any miracles before his baptism. After his baptism, he attended a wedding feast in Cana and turned six stone jars of water into wine.

Explanation:

Jesus was only baptized at 30 years old. Baptism is a symbolic of a person's readiness to commit wholeheartedly to God. In his younger years, Jesus was already well-versed in the bible and would regularly visit the temple with his family on very important religious occasions.

Before his baptism, Jesus stayed with this family and helped with his siblings. He was trained by Joseph as a carpenter. As he grew older, he has come to a decision to move out and devote his life to his real father, Jehovah God.

After his baptism, he continued to talk to preach about his Father and also set out to look for disciples to help with the ministry. A few days after meeting Nathaniel, a new disciple who is from the town of Cana, they attended a wedding feast where Mary was also invited.

While Mary was helping with the preparations, she noted to Jesus that the host is low on wine. Jesus remarked that it is not left for Mary to decide whether he should do something about it or not. Mary conceded that Jesus should only act according to God's will and what is right.

Jesus then asks for the water jars to be filled and asked the attendant to bring them out. During all these, the water was miraculously turned into fine wine.

The disciples who were with Jesus during the time were the only ones who knew about this and with that miracle their belief that Jesus is the Son of God was truly strengthened. This cemented the resolve of the disciples to follow Jesus and help with spreading the good news and the promises of Jehovah God.

During those ancient times, miracles were an important part of being a prophet. Different religions that worshiped stone gods an idols abounded. To have people believe about the true God, they asked for proof. The people's faith back then were truly grounded in visual proof. As a result, God allowed prophets to make miracles on his behalf to give them proof.

Miracles were also used to foreshadow the promises that God had put forth. He promised everlasting life free from sickness and death, bountiful harvest and food, and peace throughout the world. The miracles performed by prophets on His behalf were almost always related to food, peace, health, and life.
