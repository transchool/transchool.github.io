25. the space that a figure occupies


Answer:

Volume

Step-by-step explanation:

The space that a figure occupies is called volume. This is commonly measured using cubic meters.

There are various formulas to determine the volumes of different three-dimensional objects, but the simplest ones are the cube and the rectangular prism.

The volume of a cube can be found by raising its side length to the power of 3.

The volume of a rectangular prism can be found by multiplying its length, width, and height.
