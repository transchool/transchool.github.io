1. "The Effects of Students Attendance to their Academic Performance"


"The Effects of Students Attendance to their Academic Performance" is an example of correlational quantitative research. Correlational research measures two variables, understands and assesses the relationship between them statistically. The objective of correlational research is to identify variables that have a relationship that a change in one creates more or less change in the other.  In correlational research, the survey questionnaire is commonly used to collect detailed insights from respondents about the topic.

Sample Statement of the Problem

This study aims to assess the effects of students’ attendance on their academic performance. Specifically, this study will  answer the following questions:

What is the level of respondent’s attendance status?What is the level of the respondent’s academic performance?Is there a significant relationship between the respondent’s attendance status and academic performance?

Types of Correlational Research

There are three types of correlational research:  

Survey methodArchival researchNaturalistic observation
