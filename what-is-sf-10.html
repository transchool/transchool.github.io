6. what is the sf of ( x + 5 ) ( x - 3 ) = 20 ​


✎ Problem:

What is the standard form of the equation (x + 5)(x - 3) = 20?

✎ Answer and explanation:

✍︎ The standard form of the quadratic equation is in the form ax² + bx + c = 0 where a, b, and c are real numbers and a ≠ 0.

In this case where the equation is written as a product of linear terms, multiplying the factors expands the equation to a string of terms separated by plus or minus signs. So:

(x + 5)(x - 3) = 20x² - 3x + 5x - 15 = 20x² + 2x - 15 = 20

With the right side of the equation set equal to zero,  this results to the standard form of the quadratic equation.

x² + 2x - 15 - 20 = 0x² + 2x - 35 = 0 <answer>
