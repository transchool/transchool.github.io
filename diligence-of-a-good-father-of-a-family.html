ARTICLE 1163  

Art. 1163 A person who is obliged to offer anything is also obliged to take care of it with due diligence of a good father of a family, unless the statute or stipulation of the parties demands a different level of care. Reason for provision The duty to supply the goods would be illusory if the debtor and others were to have a condition appropriate for their enjoyment by the obligee for the reason contemplated. Diligence Required – Preserving the thing, the legislation demands the diligence of a good father of a family. Effect of Breach – The obligation to conserve the thing to be delivered shall be punished by the liability for damages levied on the debtor who fails to exercise the vigilance of the good father of the family to preserve the thing. But if the debtor's inability to maintain the object is attributable to no error or neglect on the part of his unintended incident or force. He is excluded from responsibility/

30. good luck is a child of diligence state your views


As a Christian, I really do not believe in luck. Rather, I believe in favor and blessings that come from God despite of our unworthiness. Hence, the statement “Good luck is a child of diligence,” isn’t really true for me. However, I support others who said that diligence is the key to success, since this is also biblical. In the book of Galatians it is stated that a man reaps what he sows, which of course apply to all of us. When we sow diligence, a good quality, we will surely harvest good fruits as well, like success. Hard work doesn’t come naturally but should be done intentionally and consistently. Through this, all our efforts will be rewarded by our good and almighty Father. Like what Filipinos usually say, “Nasa tao ang gawa, nasa Diyos ang awa.” Indeed, because of the mercy of the Lord, everything we do will never be in vain. Sometimes we just have to patiently wait and keep on persevering.
