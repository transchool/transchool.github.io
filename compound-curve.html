Answer:

A compound curve consists of two (or more) circular curves between two main tangents joined at point of compound curve (PCC). Curve at PC is designated as 1 (R1, L1, T1, etc) and curve at PT is designated as 2 (R2, L2, T2, etc).

Parts and functions of a compound microscope

1. Parts and Functions of a Compound Microscope

2. Light Microscope • Simple – uses a single lens • Compound – uses a set of lenses or lens systems

3. Simple Light Microscope

4. Compound Microscope • Mechanical Parts – Used to support and adjust the parts • Magnifying Parts – Used to enlarge the specimen • Illuminating Parts – Used to provide light

5. Mechanical Parts • Base – Bottommost portion that supports the entire/lower microscope • Pillar – Part above the base that supports the other parts • Inclination Joint – Allows for tilting of the microscope for convenience of the user

6. Inclination Joint Pillar Base

7. • Arm/Neck –Curved/slanted part which is held while carrying the microscope • Stage –Platform where object to be examined is placed • Stage Clips –Secures the specimen to the stage

8. • Stage Opening • Body Tube –Attached to the arm and bears the lenses • Draw Tube –Cylindrical structure on top of the body tube that holds the ocular lenses

9. Draw Tube Body Tube Arm / Neck Stage

10. • Revolving/Rotating Nosepiece –Rotating disc where the objectives are attached • Dust Shield –Lies atop the nosepiece and keeps dust from settling on the objectives

11. Dust Shield Revolving Nosepiece

12. • Coarse Adjustment Knob –Geared to the body tube which elevates or lowers when rotated bringing the object into approximate focus • Fine Adjustment Knob –A smaller knob for delicate focusing bringing the object into perfect focus

13. Coarse Adjustment Knob Fine Adjustment Knob

14. • Condenser Adjustment Knob –Elevates and lowers the condenser to regulate the intensity of light • Iris Diaphragm Lever –Lever in front of the condenser and which is moved horizontally to open/close the diaphragm

15. Iris Diaphragm Lever Condenser Adjustment Knob

16. Illuminating Parts • Mirror – Located beneath the stage and has concave and plane surfaces to gather and direct light in order to illuminate the object • Electric Lamp – A built-in illuminator beneath the stage that may eb used if sunlight is not preferred or is not available

17. Mirror / Electric Lamp

18. • Substage – Iris Diaphragm • Regualtes the amount of light necesaary to obtain a clearer view of the object – Condenser • A set of lenses between the mirror and the stage that concentrates light rays on the specimen.

19. Iris Diaphragm Condenser

20. MAGNIFYING PARTS • Ocular / Eyepiece – Another set of lens found on top of the body tube which functions to further magnify the image produced by the objective lenses. It usually ranges from 5x to 15x.

21. Ocular

22. MAGNIFYING PARTS • Objectives – Metal cylinders attached below the nosepiece and contains especially ground and polished lenses • LPO / Low Power Objective – Gives the lowest magnification, usually 10x • HPO / High Power Objective – Gives higher magnification usually 40x or 43x • OIO / Oil Immersion Objective – Gives the highest magnification, usually 97x or 100x, and is used wet either with cedar wood oil or synthetic oil

23. Objectives

24. Use of the Compound Microscope

25. • Make sure all backpacks are out of the aisles before you get a microscope! • Always carry the microscope with one hand on the Arm and one hand on the Base. Carry it close to your body.

26. • Be gentle. • Setting the microscope down on the table roughly could jar lenses and other parts loose.

27. • Always start and end with lowest powered objective.

28. • Place the slide on the microscope stage, with the specimen directly over the center of the glass circle on the stage (directly over the light).

29. • If you wear glasses, take them off; if you see only your eyelashes, move closer. • If you see a dark line that goes part way across the field of view, try turning the eyepiece.

Answer:

The compound microscope is classified into three main parts. Mechanical, illuminating, and magnifying. The mechanical part comprises of (1) draw tube which holds the objectives and revolving nosepiece in place. The coarse adjustment knob allows the movement of the tube upward or downward. On the other hand, the (2) fine adjustment knob brings the specimen into sharper focus. In addition, the stage and stage clips (3) controls and holds the specimen in place. Further, (4) base provides (5) support to the compound microscope while the (6) arm is curved shape used for holding the compound microscope when transporting. The (7) inclination joint supports the compound microscope when it is in inclined position. The illuminating part consists of the (8) mirror which allows light to pass.

Explanation:

Viewing microscopic objects is made possible because of the invention of the microscope. It allows us to observe microscopic objects that are not seen by the naked eye. In fact, several discoveries and additional knowledge have been added because of the microscope.

Magnifying Parts:

The microscope contains the eyepiece and objectives. These parts have lenses that help in magnifying objects or specimen being viewed. The magnifying capacity of these parts vary and this can be usually engraved on the part.

Illuminating Parts

The mirror or light source provides light that allows us to see the specimen we are trying to view and examine. The mirror gathers light from the light source to direct it to the specimen being viewed. The condenser focuses light entering. They collect the light and direct it to the specimen.

Mechanical Parts

For support, there is the base, arm, body tube, stage, and stage clips. The coarse adjustment knob and fine adjustment knob focus and sharpen the image viewed. The nosepiece is connected to the body tube and moves or shifts from one objectives lens to another objective. The diaphragm controls the amount of light reaching the specimen. Also, the inclination joint helps in adjusting the tilt of the microscope. These parts all help in the function of the microscope which to magnify and view microscopic objects.
