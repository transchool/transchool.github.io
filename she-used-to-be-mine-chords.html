6. it is the use of chords to report melody​


Answer:

Diatonic chores

Explanation:

Say your melody comprises the notes in a C major scale (C—D—E—F—G—A—B); each one of those notes is the tonic, or root note, of its own chord. These chords are called diatonic chords, and they play an integral part in assigning chords to a melodic note.

8. What are the type of chords that uses two chords that is heard and played at the same time?​


Answer:

These are called slash chords. For instance, C/F indicates that an C major triad should be played with an added F in the bass. In some genres of modern jazz, two chords with a slash between them may indicate an advanced chord type called a polychord, which is the playing of two chords simultaneously.
