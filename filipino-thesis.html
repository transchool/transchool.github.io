Answer:

Filipino and English are the two official languages of the Philippines. In some schools, there is an enforced "English-Filipino Only" policy. The goal of this rule is that students must only speak Filipino or English in the school setting. There has been many arguments for and against it as of late.

I, for one, express my support for this policy. It is impossible to deny that there exists valid criticisms of this rule inside the school. Even so, I still believe that an "English-Filipino" Only policy will be beneficial to both the student body and the general population.

In this day and age, the internet offers plenty of educational opportunities. It has been much easier to gain access to online resources about foreign languages. However, I believe that we must first focus on our own languages before exploring other avenues. Inside the school, it will help our own understanding of our classes since Filipino and English are both used to teach the subjects. By ensuring our mastery of the two official languages of the country, we are promoting our own culture and heritage.

It is sad, but our grasp on both languages has been declining. This can be taken as a sign that our educational system is failing us. However, we must also take action by ourselves whenever we can. There are plenty of benefits to becoming expert English and Filipino speakers. Once we master the two languages, it will be easier for us to learn more. The depth of our understanding of these languages will complement our future studies in general regardless of the field. On top of that, this policy will help us learn how to properly and clearly express our thoughts to our fellow Filipinos. The goal of any language is to bridge any misunderstandings. If we cannot even speak well in our languages, it will be harder for us to feel a connection with our fellow countrymen.

The "English-Filipino" only policy will help the youth reach mastery in the two languages. This is going to be helpful for us both in our current classes and other endeavors in the future. Aside from our studies, this skill will help us connect with other people. When we are fluent in the two languages, we can easily understand and communicate with one another. The rule is well-intentioned and must be implemented well to ensure that its goal will be accomplished.
