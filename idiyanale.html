Other details of Dumakulem

He is often depicted as a powerful and professional hunter. Dumakulem is thought to be very similar to Uwinan Sana, the deity of grasslands and forests.He tied the knot to Anagolay, another major deity, regarded as the goddess of lost things. The marriage had two children: Apolaki, the god of the light, and Dian Masalanta, the goddess of lovers.

Luzon Divinities

The ancient Tagalogs believed in one creator god, based on the early accounts of the Spanish conquistador Miguel de Loarca. This go-between might be either their dead relative's spirit or any of the lower-ranking deities. Ancient gods were generally worshiped in the form of adobe carvings called likha, while dead ancestors were honored by offering the wooden images known as anito with food or gold ornamentation.

Other Luzon Divinities

BathalaAbba, this supreme deity has been identified as "may kapal sa lahat," or the creator of all. His origin is uncertain, but his name suggests influences of Hinduism. Bathala was derived from the Sanskrit bhattara, meaning "noble lord," according to William Henry Scott.

Amanikable is the tagalog god of the sea. Originally he is the god of Hunters. He replaced Aman Sinaya as the god of the sea among of the first generation gods (aside from Bathala). Amanikable was never married after his love was spurned by a beautiful mortal maiden, Maganda. In frustration, he swore vengeance against the humans by sending turbulent waves and horrible tempests in order to wreck boats and to drown men.
