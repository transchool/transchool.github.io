Neoclassicism has a different approach which embraces a new style rather than the traditional.

Neoclassicism is a term used to describe artistic and creative movements that incorporate elements of ancient Greek and Roman philosophy, culture, and art. Thousands of years ago, the classical Greeks and Romans championed principles like order, balance, and restraint that have had a lasting impact. Neoclassicism can be found in a variety of fields, including literature, theater, art, and architecture, as well as music.

Neoclassicism arose in the mid-eighteenth century, inspired by a renewed interest in the classical past as a result of archaeological discoveries in locations like Rome and Pompeii. Neoclassicism scholars wrote on Greek art, sculpture, and culture as a result of these discoveries, and they argued for a similar clean, restrained style as a method to depict a mature, and perfect society. Neoclassicism is a creative movement.
