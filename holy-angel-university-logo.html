4. How can you contribute in making the Church one/united, holy, catholic/universal and apostolic in your own ways as a teenager?​


1. Teaching a class

Your church followers undoubtedly have valuable skills to share with others (woodworking, painting, or podcasting, for example). Encourage them to teach others for an hour after church or on a weeknight, and advertise the classes in your church bulletin or video announcements. Throw in a call for donations, and the classes could even generate funds from those who can afford to give!

2. Church cleaning and maintenance

Cleaning and maintenance cost money, and those costs add up over the course of the year. Encourage church members to donate their time by vacuuming after Sunday services, mowing the grass, or repainting the church sign. These contributions may even allow fund reallocation from routine upkeep to important outreach programs.

3. Transportation assistance

If you have members who drive to church every Sunday with vacant seats in their vehicles, it’s a great (and relatively easy) opportunity to help others. Ask around to find members who lack transportation or are no longer able to drive, then match them with willing drivers. You can also organize a carpool for children’s programs while you’re at it.

4. Technological skills

Your church probably has members who are good with computers and other tech, and are willing to contribute using these highly valuable skills. Who knows, maybe you could even help them build a career in church tech! While church management software saves you lots of time and energy, it still requires an operator. Let capable volunteers perform tasks like updating the website, scheduling other volunteers, and managing the church calendar.

5. Guest chefs

Unless your church has an in-house chef, it needs someone to fry all that fish, flip all those pancakes, and grill hundreds of hot dogs at various events throughout the year. Give your church members the opportunity to get behind the grill and cook. Pair congregants who are passionate about cooking with these volunteer opportunities.

6. Explain the relationship between the Holy Sprit and the birth of the universal Church


Answer:

Pentecost is often spoken of as the “birth” of the Church. It is an apt metaphor: The Church, which you could say had been growing in utero in the persons of Mary, the Twelve, and the small community of disciples, now emerges into the world. And when she does, we can already glimpse all of the distinguishing features by which she will be known as an adult.

The Church’s most obvious feature is that she is Charismatic — she has received the Gift, the Holy Spirit, and the manifold gifts He bestows, as evidenced by the charisms of tongues and preaching. Receiving the Holy Spirit as the common Gift of the Father and the Son (Acts 2:33-36) also immediately distinguishes her as Trinitarian. She is not a political body, nor a social service, but a living Tabernacle, making pilgrimage to the Father, through the Son, in the Spirit.

The inner circle has the Latin words, “Vincit Omnia Veritas,”meaning Truth Conquers All.


Over it is two (2) laurel leaf branches symbolizing honor and achievement, framing a vinta on a wavy sea, whose giant sail has the initials of UM, with the horizon behind.
