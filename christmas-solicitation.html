26. Short Case for Discussion You were given three options to choose from. One, use the savings of the company from sale of scrap materials to fund the forthcoming employees' Christmas party; Two, solicit voluntary donations from suppliers to fund the forthcoming employees' Christmas party and Three, do not hold the employees' Christmas party this year. Analyze the advantage and disadvantages of each option and make a choice from among the three options, citin the compelling reasons for such choice.​


Answer:

Option One: Using the savings from the sale of scrap materials to fund the forthcoming employees' Christmas party

Advantages:

The company will not need to spend any additional money on the Christmas party, as the funds will come from the savings already gained from the sale of scrap materials.

This option allows the company to use the money in a way that will directly benefit the employees, which may help to boost morale and foster a positive work environment.

Disadvantages:

The savings from the sale of scrap materials may not be sufficient to cover the costs of the Christmas party, which could result in the party being scaled back or not happening at all.

Using the savings for the Christmas party may not align with the company's long-term financial goals, as the money could potentially be used for other purposes that would benefit the company in the long run.

Option Two: Solicit voluntary donations from suppliers to fund the forthcoming employees' Christmas party

Advantages:

This option allows the company to put on a larger and more extravagant Christmas party, as the funds will come from multiple sources rather than just the company's savings.

Asking for donations from suppliers can be a good way to strengthen the relationship between the company and its suppliers, as it demonstrates that the company values their partnership.

Disadvantages:

Some suppliers may not be willing or able to make donations, which could result in the company not being able to gather enough funds to cover the costs of the party.

This option may not be financially feasible for smaller suppliers, which could lead to a lack of representation among the donor base.

Option Three: Do not hold the employees' Christmas party this year

Advantages:

This option will save the company money, as they will not need to spend any funds on the party.

This option may be necessary if the company is experiencing financial difficulties or if there are other pressing priorities that require funding.

Disadvantages:

This option may have a negative impact on employee morale, as many people look forward to the annual Christmas party as a way to celebrate the end of the year and bond with their coworkers.

This option may also damage the company's reputation, as it may be seen as being ungrateful or indifferent towards the well-being of its employees.

Based on the advantages and disadvantages of each option, the best choice for the company would likely be Option Two: soliciting voluntary donations from suppliers to fund the forthcoming employees' Christmas party. This option allows the company to put on a larger and more extravagant party, while also strengthening the relationship between the company and its suppliers. Additionally, the financial burden is not solely on the company, as the funds will come from multiple sources. Of course, it is important for the company to communicate clearly with its suppliers about the purpose of the donations and to ensure that the donations are given on a voluntary basis.
