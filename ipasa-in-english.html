11. hello po pwede po ba patulong po nito sa English po please kylangan qn po Maya ipasa, sana po matulungan nyo ako mahina Kasi ako sa English ​


Answer:

Simile: "He was as fast as a cheetah" - The figure of speech compares the speed of the person to that of a cheetah. The literal language meaning is that the person is very fast.

Metaphor: "She has a heart of stone" - The figure of speech describes the person's emotional state as being hard and unfeeling. The literal language meaning is that the person is unemotional and cold-hearted.

Personification: "The wind whispered secrets in my ear" - The figure of speech gives human characteristics to the wind. The literal language meaning is that the wind made a whispering sound.

Hyperbole: "I'm so hungry I could eat a horse" - The figure of speech exaggerates the level of hunger to an unrealistic extent. The literal language meaning is that the person is very hungry.

Idiom: "To kick the bucket" - The figure of speech is an idiomatic expression that means to die. The literal language meaning is to kick a bucket.

Explanation:

Simile: "He's as strong as an ox" - This is a comparison between a person's strength and that of an ox, which is known to be very strong. The literal meaning is that the person is very strong.

Metaphor: "She's a rose among thorns" - This is a comparison between a person and a rose, which is delicate and beautiful, among a group of people who are seen as rough or unkind. The literal meaning is that the person is beautiful and special among a group of people who are not.

Personification: "The wind whispered secrets in my ear" - This is a figure of speech that gives human characteristics to an inanimate object (the wind). The literal meaning is that the wind was making a whispering sound.

Hyperbole: "I've told you a million times" - This is an exaggeration used to emphasize a point. The literal meaning is that the person has told the other person multiple times, but not exactly a million times.

Personification: "The leaves danced in the wind" - This is a figure of speech that gives human characteristics to inanimate objects (the leaves). The literal meaning is that the leaves were moving around in the wind.

*Summarizing is defined as taking a lot of information and creating a condensed version that covers the main points. An example of summarizing is writing a three or four-sentence description that touches upon the main points of a long book.

*The six major elements of fiction are character, plot, point of view, setting, style, and theme.

*Exposition, inciting incident, rising action, climax, falling action, resolution, and denouement/the ending.

Exposition: Beginning of the Story

The exposition is the beginning of the story and prepares the way for upcoming events.

Rising Action

The rising action occurs when the main problem or conflict is addressed with a form of action. The rising action always leads up to the climax.

Climax or Turning Point

The climax is the pivotal point in the story when the protagonist deals with the culmination of events.

Falling Action

The falling action occurs immediately after the climax and details the consequences -- good or bad -- that the characters must deal with after the turning point of events.

Resolution

The resolution tells us what happens to the characters after the conflict is resolved.
