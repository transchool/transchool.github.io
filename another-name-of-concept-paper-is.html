2. Another name of concept paper is


Concept Paper

A research proposal is another name for a concept paper. A concept paper is a brief document that a researcher drafts before beginning a research project to describe the study's objective, significance, and methodology. The suggested research title, a succinct summary of the topic, the study's purpose, the research questions you intend to address, the kind of data you will gather, and how you plan to get it are all included in the concept paper.

A research concept paper's main goal is to persuade the reader that the suggested research endeavor is worthwhile. This implies that the research study's novelty and interest must first be acknowledged by the reader. They must be persuaded that the research is necessary and that the objectives and questions are appropriate. Finally, they should be certain that the suggested methods for data collection are workable, likely to succeed, and able to be completed within the precise time frame allotted for this project.

For students writing proposals for undergraduate, master's, or Ph.D. projects, the concept paper format typically has a length of 2 to 3 pages. Concept papers included in funding requests may be longer than 20 pages. You were given a general outline of the important topics you should be aware of when writing concept papers in this guide. These serve two purposes: first, to make clear to the reader the goals of your project and the significance of your research topic; this is based on the formulation of a problem statement supported by evidence from your literature review.
