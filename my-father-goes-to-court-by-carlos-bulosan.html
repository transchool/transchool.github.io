1. biographical analysis about Carlos bulosan "my father goes to court"​


Answer:

This story was narrated by a four-year old kid whose family lived in a small town on Luzon. The farm of his father was destroyed by a Natural Calamity, which is flood. That is the reason why they preferred to live in a new place. They had a rich man neighbor, his children rarely go outside. Their house so tall, where their family can watch the kid’s (narrator) house. The rich man’s servants were always cooking some delicious food, like fried chicken, ham, and bacon. The kid’s family inhaling the heavenly spirit of those foods.

The narrator’s family always bond outside the Sun and bathing in the river. They always had a good time and a contagious laugh, and some people join their bonding, while the rich man only watch them. As day past on, the rich man’s family had a problem. The children became thin, the wife had a cough like the rich man. There was an opposite effect in the narrator’s family, they became bright and healthy. Their sickness was not in the food, because their servants always frying something delicious to eat. After that, their house was always close, but the aroma of the food was still drifted by the wind.

One morning, a police man knocked on the door of the narrator’s house. The rich man had a complaint to them, because they were accused by stealing the spirit of the wealth and food.

When the trial came, the narrator’s family came first. After a few minutes, the rich man’s family came with a lawyer, their body was so thin and they look sick. The narrator’s father didn’t had any lawyer, he fight on his own. The trial starts, the lawyer questioned the narrator’s father. In the first question, the father didn’t agree that they stole the spirit of the wealth and the food. The father agreed in the second question, where he state that their family hung outside the rich man’s house and inhale the food. He also agreed that when the rich man’s family became sick, their family became strong and healthy.

The rich man’s children went in shyly. They are really thin and look like sick. When it is time for the rich man, the narrator’s father was the one who question him. The rich man agreed that while their family having fun, the rich man’s family became worse and sad and they also stole the spirit of their wealth. He also agreed that the narrator’s family stole the spirit of their food by hanging outside their windows while their servants cooking the food. The narrator’s father commit to pay the rich man, he took the straw hat and collect some centavo and silver coins around the courtroom. When the father is ready, he went to the rich man and shook the hat in front of the rich man. He asked the man if he heard the spirit of money, and the rich man agreed. In that case, the father told to the man that he had already paid. After that, the case was dismissed.

The judge told to the narrator’s father the story where his uncle died because of laughing. The judge like to hear narrator’s family laughing. The kid’s sisters started to laugh, and all of the people in the courtroom laughed and the judge’s laugh was the loudest of all.

2. What is the purpose of Carlos Bulosan on writing the story of My Father Goes to Court?


Answer:

“My Father Goes to Court” points to the economic divide between the rich and poor. The rich man in the story grew envious of Bulosan and his family's ability to procure a healthy life despite struggling in poverty by enjoying spending time under the sun sharing laughter.

Explanation:

THE PURPOSE OF CARLOS BULOSAN ON WRITING THE STORY OF MY FATHER GOES TO COURT

"My Father Goes to Court" highlights the wealth gap between rich and poor. The rich man in the story grew envious of Bulosan and his family ability to live a healthy life despite their poverty by spending time outside in the sun laughing. The story also serves as an introduction to how Bulosan learned to stand up for what he believed in, similar to how his father defended himself in court. Bulosan, as an adult, would use his writings to advocate for labor rights and to raise the profile of Filipino Americans.

He based this story on folklore from the Philippines, and it contains an underlying social commentary. This work was said to be a protest against his time's economic progress. This story depicts the Filipino culture, characteristics, and way of life.

Carlos Bulusan wrote Father Goes To Court. It was about two distinct family and how they live their lives. The narrator's family was impoverished. The poor family lives happily and healthily, whereas the rich family does not have any social life.

Explanation:

Plot: The plot of the story centers around the protagonist's father, who goes to court to fight for the rights of his family and the other tenant farmers who are being exploited by a wealthy landowner.

Setting: The story takes place in the Philippines during a time when tenant farmers were struggling to assert their rights and gain fair treatment from wealthy landowners.

Characters: The main characters in the story include the protagonist, who narrates the events, and his father, who is the hero of the story. Other significant characters include the wealthy landowner and the tenant farmers who are fighting for their rights.

Point of view: The story is told from the first-person point of view of the protagonist, who is the son of the main character.

Theme: The central theme of the story is the struggle for justice and equality, as the protagonist's father fights for the rights of his family and the other tenant farmers against a powerful and ruthless landowner.
