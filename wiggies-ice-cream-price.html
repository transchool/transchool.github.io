9. an ice cream vendor sells ice cream in cone 4cm in diameter and 8cm in height. How much ice cream can it hold?


Volume of Cone Formula:

[tex] \frac{1}{3}\pi \: r^{2}h[/tex]

Given:

•Diameter = 4 cm ; radius=½diameter

Radius = 2cm

•Height = 8 cm

Solution and Answer:

[tex] \frac{1}{3}\pi \: r^{2} h = \frac{1}{3}\pi(2 \: cm)^{2} (8 \: cm) = 33.51 \: cm^{3} [/tex]

The cone can hold 33.51 cm³ of ice cream.

14. MATH CHALLENGE AGONSA: An ice cream machine is being sold by a couple since they wanted to venture into another business. They were trying to sell it for 10% off of the marked price but no one wanted to buy it. Then they tried to sell for 20% of the first sale price still no one bought the machine Finally, they offered it for 25% off the second sale price when someone bought it for P32.400.00. What was the original price of the ice cream machine? 204 m ​


Answer:

P43,200.00

Step-by-step explanation:

A: Asked

Find the original price of the ice cream machine.

G: Given

The machine was first sold at 10% off but nobody bought it.

The machine was then sold at 20% off the first sale price but still nobody bought it.

The machine was sold at 25% off the second sale price of P32,400.00.

O: Operation

To solve this problem, we need to work backwards from the final sale price to the original price. We can use the following formula:

Original price = Final price ÷ (1 - Discount rate)

N: Unknown

The original price of the ice cream machine.

S: Solution

The machine was sold at 25% off the second sale price of P32,400.00, so the second sale price was 75% of the original price.

Using the formula, we can write:

x = 32,400.00 ÷ 0.75

x = 43,200.00

Therefore, the original price of the ice cream machine was P43,200.00.

PROBLEM:

One gallon of ice cream is equal to______.

A. 4 liters of ice cream.

B. 1 liter of ice cream.

C. 3.8 liters of ice cream.

D. 2 liters of ice cream

ANSWER:

One gallon is equivalent to an approximate value of 3.785 liters or 3.8 liters when rounded off to the nearest tenths.

Therefore, the answer is letter C. One gallon of ice cream is equal to 3.8 liters of ice cream.

ADDITIONAL INFO:Gallon is a unit of measurement in terms of volume, in the imperial system and United States customary while liters is a metric unit of volume and capacity.
