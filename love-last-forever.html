How can a friendship last forever?

Friendships can last a lifetime with a little help.

First off you have to genuinely love yourself. If you are OK with yourself you are not going to be looking for trouble outside of yourself. Behind every person that hates themselves is a wake of twisted and turning bodies of friends past.

Once you are good with yourself you have to love the person. Not just who they are now, circumstantially. But love the way they are in the world, regardless of where they are.

Take for instance my buddy William Donnelly. He's still the same 16 year old when I look at him. He's still the impatient kid walking down the hall who for some reason had a lot of patience when I would ride my mountain bike to his house at 3 a.m. because I couldn't sleep and knock on his window. He never kicked me out or yelled at me. He did, however listen to me ramble until daybreak. You know the usual questions of 16 year olds: why were we born? Why are we alive? Don't you ever feel like this world isn't a good fit for you? He would just smile at me and say, you think too much. I would smile and say, yes I do.

Once you have a genuine friendship you have to grow together. Take for example Brian Fitzgerald. He and I were reporters/photographers in Arizona in the mid-90s. We were workaholics. Couldn't talk to girls to save our lives. Oh yeah, and we had a penchant for getting into unbelievable messes. Makes my head spin thinking about it.

Flash forward to the present. He's married, has a beautiful daughter and runs his own company. I am on the same path, wife, daughters and my own companies. We can take a trip together and it's instant the way we talk about our lives and businesses. If one of us was still a workaholic social misfit with trouble on their mind it's hard to integrate with the people we love back home. Because we are both growing (doesn't have to be the same direction) we can go into each other's present day lives with ease.

So far our mixture is love oneself, love your buddy and continue to grow as people.

The other thing, and this is important. You have to drive it. We call each other, text, email, send smoke signals, whatever it takes. We talk to each all the time.

We made it a priority that we all get together once a year even though we are spread out all over the country.

Hope this helps and maybe the lads I wrote about will chime in as well.

Quora has opened me up to new friendships as well. I'm not sure how the whole internet only friendship works but I'm trying.

What is Corona Virus or CoVid-19 all about?

In December 2019, there was a cluster of pneumonia cases in China especially in Wuhan. Investigations found that it was caused by a previously unknown virus, and now it was called the "2019 Novel Coronavirus"

How do viruses get their names?

Coronaviruses are a large group of viruses or its refers to a family of virus that are zoonotic. Zoonotic means, it is a disease or microbe that's spread from animals to human beings. It was named 'corona' because they consist of a core of genetic material surrounded by an envelope of proteins spikes this gives an appearance of look likes a crown. Crown in Latin is called 'Corona' and that's how these viruses get their name into it.

It initially occurred in a group of people with pneumonia, who had been associated with seafood and animal markets in Wuhan, China.

The disease has since spread from the sick to others including family members and healthcare staff such as nurse and doctors or also called as "frontliner." There are many cases at present and has spread within China and other countries and that is global pandemic today.

So, where did the virus come from?

It's known that the coronaviruses circulate in a range of animals such as birds and mammals. Sometimes these viruses can jump from animals to humans and this is called a "spillover."

How is it transmitted?

The virus is yet to be determined of how this virus is transmitted. In general respiratory its usually transmitted through "droplets" created when an infected person coughs or sneezes or through something that has been contaminated with the virus. People most at risk of infection from a novel coronavirus are those in close contact with animals, such as animal workers in Seafood Market in Wuhan China, and those who are caring for people who are infected with the virus, such as family members or healthcare workers like nurse and doctors.

How do we prevent transmission of the virus?

There are a standard hygiene practices that have been recommended to protect against infection and further spread of disease. These include;

Covering your mouth and nose with a medical mask, tissue or flexed elbow if you sneeze.Avoid close contact with those who are unwell or have those kind of symptoms.Follow the appropriate use of masks and personal protective equipment, washing hands regularly with soap and water or alcohol-based hand rub, it must be 70% alcohol content.

Actions that can be taken to prevent infection from an animal source including:

Avoiding unnecessary contact with animals, washing hands after contact with animals or animal products.And ensuring animal products are cooked thoroughly before they're consumed.It's important to stay home if you're feeling unwell, but if you have a fever, cough or difficulty of breathing seek medical care early and share your previous travel history with your health care provider.
