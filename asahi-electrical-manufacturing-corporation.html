10. The different types of business according to activities are a. Manufacturing, Corporations, Service b. Manufacturing, Merchandising, Service c. Service, Partnerships, Sole Proprietorships d. Corporations, Partnerships, Sole Proprietorships


Answer:

B

Explanation:

Manufacturing, Merchandising, and Service are business types according to activities. Manufacturing are the companies that produce finished goods consumption. They use raw materials to create and finish their products. Merchandising companies are similar to trading, wholesaling/retailing and buy and sell companies. They do not need raw materials to produce the items they will sell. They buy the finished goods already and sell on a mark-up. Service companies are the companies that sell services for a business. Examples are repair shops, agencies, parlors and spa/massage businesses.

12. The fixed portion of the semi-variable cost of electricity for a manufacturing plant is


Answer:

Any fixed portion included in a semi-variable cost is denoted as a period cost because such expenditures will be suffered irrespective of the level of increase or decrease in the activity or production done. Hence, they are basic costs in nature, which have to be done by the business to retain or maintain its normal and regular course, for example in the given case, the fixed part of semi-variable electricity might be related to basic functions of lighting being maintained in building or premises.
