9. Bert’s experienceBert was a young employee who had been working at “Dave’s Detailing” yard for a month. His job was to clean the motors of cars using a product called “Acidic Detergent”. Unknown to Bert, this product contained several hazardous substances and dangerous goods such as hydrofluoric acid, sulfuric acid and phosphoric acid. These substances are commonly used to detail (clean) cars. Bert had to empty the Acidic Detergent from a large drum into a small hand-held spray bottle. He did this by dipping a pump into the drum and squirting a bit of the liquid into the spray bottle. While doing this, Bert noticed that the fumes made his eyes and throat sore. He complained to his supervisor but he told him not to worry. When Bert sprayed the liquid onto the motors the dirt and grease came off really easily – often in a few minutes – it was GREAT stuff and seemed to make Bert’s job much easier.Bert was not provided with any protective equipment such as aprons or gloves. Nor was he told that the Acidic Detergent was capable of causing serious burns if it came into contact with his skin.
