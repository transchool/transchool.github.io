3. what are some local programs in tb dots


Answer:

In our country, one of our public health programs is the National TB Control Program (NTP). This was organized in 1978 with the help of the Department of Health (DOH).

The following are the mandate of National TB Control Program:

(1) develop policies, standards and national strategic plan

(2) manage program logistics

(3) provide leadership and technical assistance to the lower health offices/units

(4) manage data

(5) conduct monitoring and evaluation

NTP works with different offices of DOH such as:

National Center for Health Promotion (NCHP) for advocacy, communication, and social mobilization Epidemiology Bureau (EB), and the Knowledge and Management Information and Technology Services (KMITS) for data management Health Policy Development and Planning Bureau (HPDPB) for policy and strategic plan formulation Material Management Division (MMD), Central Office Bids and Awards Committee (COBAC) and Food and Drug Administration (FDA) for drug and supplies management National TB Reference Laboratory of the Research Institute for Tropical Medicine (NTRL-RITM) for laboratory network management Lung Center of the Philippines (LCP) for programmatic management of drug-resistant TB (PMDT) related researches and training activities 17 regional offices (ROs) for technical support to the provincial health offices (PHO) and implementing units Philippine Health Insurance Corporation (PhilHealth) for the TB-DOTS accreditation and utilization of the TB-DOTS outpatient benefit package

Provinces and Cities

The provincial health offices PHOs and city health offices (CHOs) are responsible for the TB control efforts in the provinces and cities

Tuberculosis Directly Observed Treatment Short Course/ Delivery of Treatment Services (TB-DOTS)

This is a control strategy recommended by the World Health Organization  TB diagnostic and treatment services Available in public health facilities As well as in private facilities Community groups, such as the community health teams and barangay health workers participate in community-level activities

National Tuberculosis Control Program Manual of Procedures (MOP)  

This is the basis for the implementation of the tuberculosis control program in all DOTS facilities in our country. It aims to:

(a) provide technical policies and guidelines on the diagnosis, treatment and counselling of TB patients

(b) specify procedures on how to put in place important NTP support health systems such as logistics, recording and reporting, and monitoring and evaluation systems

(c) guide the different organizational levels on how to conduct monitoring, evaluation, and supervision,

(d) prescribe the roles and tasks of those involved in the management of the TB control program including TB service provision

Program Indicators  :

Case Notification Rate TB Case Detection Rate Notification rate of MDR-TB Treatment Success Rate Cure Rate (New Bacteriologically confirmed) Treatment Success Rate of MDR-TB Percent of bacteriologically-confirmed MDRTB cases with negative culture after 6 months of treatment (interim outcome) Total number of presumptive TB examined Percent contribution from non-NTP care providers Number of children with TB detected and given treatment and those given IPT Percentage of TB cases in Category A and B areas with HIV counseling and testing aged 15 years old and above Percentage of DR-TB cases provided with HIV counseling and testing aged 15 years old and above Percent of TMLs within EQA standards Percent of DOTS/ Laboratory facilities with no stock-outs of anti-TB drugs and laboratory supplies in the last 6 months
