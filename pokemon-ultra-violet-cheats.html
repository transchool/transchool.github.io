11. positive effect to the plant of Ultra Violet radiation to the plants​


Answer:

Ultra Violet (UV) radiation can have both positive and negative effects on plants. Some of the potential positive effects of UV radiation on plants include:

1. Increased photosynthesis: UV radiation can stimulate photosynthesis in plants, which is the process by which plants convert sunlight into energy. This can lead to increased growth and development in plants.

2. Increased production of secondary metabolites: UV radiation can stimulate the production of secondary metabolites in plants, which are chemical compounds that are not directly involved in the plant's growth or development but can have beneficial effects on human health.

3. Enhanced resistance to pests and diseases: UV radiation can help plants build up their natural defenses against pests and diseases by activating the plant's immune system.

4. Improved seed germination and plant growth: UV radiation can promote seed germination and improve the growth of seedlings and young plants.

It is important to note that too much UV radiation can also have negative effects on plants, such as causing damage to their cells and tissues, so it is important for plants to receive the appropriate amount of UV radiation.
