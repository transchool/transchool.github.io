Raptors (birds of prey) such as eagles and hawks are members of the Accipitridae family.

On all continents except Antarctica, there are approximately 60 species of eagles and more than 250 species of hawks.

Forests, grasslands, alpine meadows, tundra, deserts, sea beaches, suburban, and urban regions are all home to eagles and hawks.

Eagles and hawks are both nocturnal birds (active during the day).

They hunt and devour a wide variety of animals.
