4. 5. Which is the scientific notation of 456,000,000,000?A. 456 x102B. 45.6 x 1010C. 4.56 x 1011D. 0.456 x1012​ (math)


Answer:

C. 4.56 × 10^11

Explanation:

To convert a decimal into scientific notation, move the decimal point until you get to the left of the first non-zero integer. The number of places the decimal point moves is the power of the exponent, because each movement represents a "power of 10".

456,000,000,000 = 4.56 × 10^11

[tex]\begin{gathered}\bold{Given:}\end{gathered}[/tex] [tex]\begin{gathered} \rm Length = 80 ft \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex]\quad\quad\quad \ \begin{gathered} \rm Width = 40 ft \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex]\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad \ \begin{gathered} \rm r = 20 ft \end{gathered}[/tex]

To find the area let use the formula of area combined shapes of the rectangle and semi-circle.

[tex]\begin{gathered}\begin{aligned} & \bold{Formula:} \\ & \quad \boxed{\begin{array}{l} \rm{A = πr² + L \cdot W} \end{array}}\, \\ \end{aligned} \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{gathered} \rm A = 3.14(20)² + 80 \cdot 40 \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{gathered} \rm A = 3.14(400) + 3,200 \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{gathered} \rm A = 1,256 + 3,200 \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{gathered} \rm A = 4456 \end{gathered}[/tex]

Therefore, the area is 4456
