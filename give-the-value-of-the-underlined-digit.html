7. Give the place value ofthe underlined digit0.827the underlined digit is 7​


7 in 0.827 has a place value of one-thousandth

Explanation:

The first digit after decimal point indicates one-tenth of a unit, second digit after decimal point indicates one-hundredth of a unit, third digit after decimal point indicates one-thousandth of a unit, and so on.

As 7 in the 0.827 comes at the third place after decimal, it has a place value of one-thousandth.

29. give the place value of the underlined digit 2.284 the underlined digit is 4​


The place value of 4 in 2.284 is thousandths.

Step-by-step explanation:

Decimals Place Value

Decimals consist of a whole number and a fractional part. The decimal numbers are the short way of writing the fractions with denominators are the power of ten. A decimal p oi nt is used to sepa rate the whole and decimal numbers.

The place value of a digit depends on its position in the number. Decimals place value are known to be the -th group. This means that the place value of the digits after the decimal p o i n t has -th unlike with the place value of whole numbers.

Remember that the first place value after the decimal p o i n t is tenths, followed by hundredths, thousandths, ten thousandths and so on.

Going back to the given number above, 2.284, let us now identify the place value of each digit.

8 - hundredths

4 - thousandths
