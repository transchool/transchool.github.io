14. Mr. Gabriel bought a rectangular residential lot for his family. It is 12 meters wide and 18 meters long. He wanted to fence it with chicken wire. How many meters of fencing material will he need?​


PERIMETER

Problem: Mr. Gabriel bought a rectangular residential lot for his family. It is 12 meters wide and 18 meters long. He wanted to fence it with chicken wire. How many meters of fencing material will he need?

Answer: Mr. Gabriel needs 60 meters of chicken wire to fence his residential lot.

[tex]\Large\mathbb{SOLUTION:}[/tex]

Perimeter is the sum of the measures of all the sides of a plane figure or polygon. It is also referred to as the distance around a plane figure or polygon.

The perimeter of a plane figure or polygon is equal to the sum of the lengths of all its sides. Deriving the formula for the perimeter of a plane figure or polygon requires knowing the number of its sides and getting the sum of all its measures.

[tex]\bold{Formula : } \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \\ \boxed{ \sf \: P = 2(l + w) \:}[/tex]

where:

P is the perimeter.l is the length.w is the width.

Given values:

l = 18 mw = 12 m

Substitute the given values into the formula and solve for P:

[tex]\sf:\implies P = 2(l + w)[/tex]

[tex]\sf:\implies P = 2(18 \: m + 12 \: m)[/tex]

[tex]\sf:\implies P = 2(30 \: m)[/tex]

[tex]\sf:\implies\bold{P = \green{\boxed{ \bold{ \purple{ \: 60 \: m \: }}}}} \\ [/tex]

Therefore, Mr. Gabriel needs 60 meters of chicken wire to fence his residential lot.

Answer:

A baníg is a handwoven mat usually used in East Asia and the Philippines for sleeping and sitting. This type of mat is traditionally made in the Philippines.

Banig.JPG

Technically, it is not a textile. Depending on the region of the Philippines, the mat is made of buri[1] (palm), pandanus or sea grass leaves. The leaves are dried, usually dyed, then cut into strips and woven into mats, which may be plain or intricate.[2]

The Samal of Sulu usually make their mats out of buri leaves. Mats from Basey, Samar use tikog leaves which are dyed in strong colours to make beautiful, unique designs. Banig mats from Bukidnon are made from sodsod grass, a ribless reed endemic to the area.


29. What are the design used in basey mats?​


Answer:

The Basey mat maker uses a border design and a central motif which often is a stylized rendition of flowers, such as sampaguita, gumamela, rose, or orchids. Most of the time, the motif is done in a contrasting color or natural tikog color.

Answer:

The raw material used in mat weaving in Basey comes from a reed plant locally known as tikog (Fimbristylis utilis), which belongs to the family Cuperaceae and has solid, jointless and usually triangular stems. The reed plant thrives well in densely forested areas and grows even in the rice fields.
