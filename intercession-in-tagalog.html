11. why we really need the intercession of mother mary in our prayer to god?​


A few years ago I went to see the tall ships that were visiting Boston harbor. On my way home I stopped at Saint Anthony’s Shrine downtown. As I was walking toward it I noticed a tall, thin man hanging around in front of the church, sort of huddled into a corner wall. Though he looked around furtively through his black-rimmed glasses and seemed a bit timid, he didn't appear to be begging. True to my New York upbringing, I began to walk quickly past, pretending not to notice. As I went by, he suddenly reached into a white plastic grocery bag, pulled out a leaflet, and waved it in my face. I realized immediately that it was one of those anti-Catholic tracts, so I didn’t take it.

After a short time praying in the church, I got an inspiration that on my way out I should stop and talk to this man. I felt reluctant, since I generally don’t approach strangers on the street and talk to them. But I thought: Here’s this person handing out anti-Catholic leaflets in front of a Catholic church, so why should I just go by without responding in some way? So I asked him, “What’s that you're handing out?”

He handed me the leaflet, listing some typical fundamentalist objections to the Catholic Church. He said he had been Catholic but now was going to another church. His big point was that the Catholic Church has “man-made” beliefs and practices, and of course, the intercession of Mary and the saints was a huge issue. So I talked to him for a while about this. I wish I could say that he converted back to the Church, but it’s never that easy. Only the grace of God can bring that about.

Hopefully, however, if Catholics had a better understanding of why we have devotion to Mary and the saints, they would not fall away so easily when challenged about this. So the question arises: Why do we ask Mary’s intercession at all? Can’t we just go directly to Jesus? Yes, we can and certainly do pray directly to God and to Jesus, who is the “one mediator between God and humankind” (1 Tim 2:5). In this, as in so many other things Catholic, it’s not a matter or either/or, but of both/and.

17. reflection Victory over the Turks Through Our Lady's Intercession


Answer:

Christian sailors joined Pope Pius V (a Dominican) in praying the Most Holy Rosary, and defeated a much larger Turkish force at the Battle of Lepanto in 1571. The battle was a turning point in repelling the Turkish invasions of Europe. To remember the battle and Our Lady’s intercession, the feast of Our Lady of the Rosary (originally Our Lady of Victory) was established on October 7th.

30. Why do Catholics turn to Mary for intercession and help?


What is intercession?

This is act of extending help for the benefit of another person. You are intervening, mediating, or acting on behalf of someone else.

Why do Catholics turn to Mary for intercession and help?

Often, you will hear the Catholic phrase "Mary, mother of God, pray for us."

Catholics often run to Mary, who was Jesus' earthly mother, to intervene on their behalf. Their belief is that through the intercession of Mary, their cries and devotion will reach God faster or will be heard much louder. These acts of piousness and devotion to Mary are known as Marian devotions.

What does the bible say?

As the bible is considered the word of God himself, let's take a look at what it says about Mary and how Mary conducted herself in relation to God and Jesus.

Highly Favored One

When we first meet Mary in the Bible, it was when she was visited by the angel Gabriel. This is where she received the prophecy and blessing that she will bear a child through the Holy Spirit.

She was greeted by the angel with the words "Greetings, you highly favored one, Jehovah is with you." (Luke 1:28)

With this, we can see that she is indeed favored by God. But why? Was it because she was beautiful? Was it because she was kind?

As we can read in the bible, Mary was surprised by the angel's message that she would bear a child. She explained that she is a virgin, never have slept with any man. She also worried about her fiance, Joseph, and how he would react since she was a faithful woman. With this, we can conclude that Mary was a woman of high moral standards and loyal to the man she loved.

While she did worry about the pregnancy, she declared "Look! Jehovah’s slave girl! May it happen to me according to your declaration." (Luke 1:26-38)

From these words, we have seen that Mary thrust her faith towards God even if she knew that she would be put in uncertainty because of the sudden pregnancy. She trusted that God would do as fit and protect her.

Mary also uttered the words “I am the handmaid of the Lord.” (Luke 1:38) It is clear that Mary is a humble woman, even appointing herself to the position of a lowly handmaid or servant of God. This is a total opposite of today's belief that she is an exalted queen.

Faith and humility are traits that are not cultivated overnight. This means that God has observed these in her for a long time and have chosen her because her traits have prepared her for an important role.

Is Mary the Mother of God?

The short answer here is no.

We need to go back to the pronouncement made by the angel who visited Mary.

“Do not be afraid, Mary, for you have found favor with God. And look! you will become pregnant and give birth to a son, and you are to name him Jesus. This one will be great and will be called Son of the Most High, and Jehovah God will give him the throne of David his father, and he will rule as King over the house of Jacob forever, and there will be no end to his Kingdom.” (Luke 1:30-33)

As you have read, Jesus will be called the Son of the Most High. So who is the highest of all? That would be God and his name is Jehovah. Therefore, it goes without saying that Mary was never the mother of any god.

Should Mary receive or be an object of such devotion?

Many times in the bible, it was shown that instead of receiving praises or veneration, Mary would defer and throw praise back to God instead.

One example is when she visited her already-pregnant relative Elizabeth. Here, Elizabeth and her unborn child were overjoyed as they were also told by the Holy Spirit of her conception. While Elizabeth praised her, Mary humbly declared that it was through God that she received mercy. Also, instead of being waited and served on, Mary acted as Elizabeth's helper at home.

Who is our intermediary to God?

In John 14: 6, Jesus clearly said, “I am the way and the truth and the life. No one comes to the Father except through me."

We all say our own prayers and are accountable for our own actions and no one else but the bible does say that for us to get through to God, we must also go through Jesus as our intermediary and no one else.

While we will learn good values from Mary, she is not an intermediary to God.
