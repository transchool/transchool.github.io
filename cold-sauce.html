20. What is the reason why cold water is used in mixing flour for sauces?​


[tex]Answer:[/tex]

Cold water prevents the premature denaturing of the proteins in the flour, leading to a less dense dough as it rests. In essence you're cooking your mixture as you form it with hot water, which is sometimes (chiabatta bread comes to mind) desired.

On the other hand, if you want to form as little of a protein mesh as possible, use ice cold water. Tempura is made that way, for example - tight protein chains prevent the dough from linking up too soon and absorbing a lot of water.
