Rediscovering the legacy of literature through Reading essay

Before discussing whether an essay is classified as literary or not, let us first examine what is meant by literature and what is meant not by literature. Based on its etymology, literature stems from Sanskrit, namely su and literature. The meaning of beautiful or good, and literature has the meaning of writing or language. That means literally literature is beautiful writing.

From the literal meaning above, it can be taken broadly that literature is a type of text or a variety of writings that not only highlight ideas but also have aesthetic aspects. Aspects of beauty in literary texts are obtained by utilizing language in its artistic function. The process of writing literature is a process of empowering language to achieve that aesthetic degree. Empowerment of language includes exploitation of diction, use of figure of speech, composition of sentences, prioritizing connotations, using symbols or symbols, choosing imagery, the power of description, even to the use of sound aspects in words or sentence structures.

Language empowerment to achieve a certain aesthetic degree makes literary texts not only convey ideas or content but also package them in beautiful expressions. Therefore it is not surprising that reading literature feels more comfortable and at home than reading other texts. Someone reading a novel feels more captivated and at home than when he reads a book on sociology, accounting or political theory.

Essay is one type of expository writing. The essay also presents a thesis but the development is different. Articles develop a thesis with reason-laden arguments and lead to certain conclusions or at least to reaffirm the thesis. Because it offers these arguments, compared to essays, articles are longer and offer analysis that is controlled by rationality, analytic logic, and objective. The presentation of rational, analytical and objective arguments also makes the expression of the article's language seem more rigid and formal. It can be said that the article is the pinnacle of ability in presenting arguments.

7. what literature is associated with the philippine legends prehistory and colonial legacyA. Asian LiteratureB. Afro-Inglo Literature C. American Literature D. Philippine Literature ​


Answer:

D. Philippine Literature ​

Explanation:

Philippine literature is literature associated with the Philippines from prehistory, through its colonial legacies, and on to the present.  Pre-Hispanic Philippine literature was actually epics passed on from generation to generation, originally through an oral tradition. However, wealthy families, especially in Mindanao, were able to keep transcribed copies of these epics as family heirloom. One such was the Darangen, an epic of the Maranaos.

Historical Context

When reading a piece of literature, especially older works, it can be difficult to see the relevance in prose from our 21st century perspective. Older novels or poetry can use language or references that escape our understanding because the context of the words and their meaning are unknown to the reader. Historical context is an important thing to consider when reading written work – it can change or amplify the overall meaning of the subject and reveal information or perspectives we otherwise would have missed. Today we’ll go over examples of how historical context plays an important role in writing and reading comprehension. Let’s get started.

Historical context is the social, political, cultural, economic, and environmental situations that influence the events or trends we see happen during that time. Therefore, if we are unfamiliar with the traditions, culture, thinking, or events happening at any time in history, we could misinterpret or lose the meaning of a piece of writing we are reading. Effective ways to consider historical context in writing is to ask the following questions:

Who is the author?

When was it written?

Are there any references or languages you don’t understand? Could they be specific to the time period?

Were there any religious, cultural, political, or economic events happening at the time that could have influenced the writer and their piece?

23. importance of rediscovering Mendel's work


The importance of rediscovering Mendel's work (pea plant experiment) is that it laid the foundation for the modern genetics. It made scientists realize that there are patterns to inheritance. It proved well that it was possible to foresee the possible combinations or characteristics that could be passed on from parents to offspring.

Gregor Mendel's experiments also made us realize the existence of genes, which he cleverly called factors because he thought these were simply factors for inheritance. Mendel's work also introduced probability and statistics into the study of inheritance, which was vaguely understood and only thought of as either a blessing from the gods. He also explained how certain characteristics would skip one generation, only to show up on another generation instead. But, all of his work were not really recognized until scientists like Hugo de Vries rediscovered his papers and continued his work on the study of inheritance.

30. Give an essay about the theme"Rediscover, Rekindle and Relive English through communication"150 words up​


Answer:

English is a language that connects people from all over the world. It is the language of international communication, business, and education. However, as time goes by, some people may begin to lose touch with their English language skills. This is where the theme of "rediscover, rekindle and relive English through communication" comes in.

To rediscover English, individuals should seek out new ways to engage with the language. This could include reading English books, watching English movies or TV shows, or even taking an English course. By exposing oneself to different forms of English, people can learn new words, grammar, and sentence structures.

Once individuals have rediscovered their love for the language, they can then begin to rekindle their skills. This can be done through practice and repetition. By speaking, writing, and listening to English on a regular basis, people can improve their ability to communicate effectively in the language.

Finally, by reliving English through communication, individuals can not only improve their language skills but also connect with others who speak English. This can be done by joining an English-speaking club, finding a language exchange partner, or even communicating with native English speakers online.

In conclusion, by rediscovering, rekindling, and reliving English through communication, people can not only improve their language skills but also connect with others from around the world. It's a great way to expand our personal and professional horizons.
