25. How we can identify the characters in the story grade 1 lesson?


Answer:

A character is someone whose activities, thoughts and feelings are revealed in a story. It does not always have to be a person. Often, in books for young readers, the characters in a story are animals. The reader learns about the characters through details in the story. Common Core Standards require the student to focus more on the text when reading. Therefore it is important that teachers help students become familiar with the elements of a story such as setting, characters and sequence of events. This develops the student’s critical reading skills.

27. WRITTEN WORK #1 Make your own narration or simple story based on a girl bless her grandma in just one or two paragraphs. Underline the cohesive devices used. The written work will be graded based on the rubric provided.​


Once upon a time, there was a girl named Sarah. She lived with her grandmother in a small village. Every day, Sarah would wake up early to help her grandmother with the chores. Despite being tired, she always made sure to bless her grandmother before going to bed.

One day, while they were having dinner, Sarah noticed that her grandmother was looking pale and weak. She immediately held her grandmother's hand and blessed her with all her heart, asking God to keep her healthy and strong. To Sarah's surprise, her grandmother's face lit up with a warm smile and she felt a surge of energy. From that day on, Sarah made it a point to bless her grandmother every day, no matter how busy she was.

Cohesive devices:

conjunctions (and, despite), pronouns (she, her, her)
