1. What is the process of excretion of crayfish


Answer:

Crayfish excrete waste directly through their bloodstream.

Explanation:

Blood is forced through the arteries that empty into spaces within the body cavity. Once the blood has drained through these spaces and bathed the organs, it collects in the large ventral sternal sinus. As the blood circulates, the green gland filters out waste materials. The fluid waste is then excreted through a pore just anterior to the mouth.

24. compare (similarities) and contrast (differences) the reproduction and development of Procambarus virginalis (Marbled crayfish) and Chanos chanos (Milk fish).


Answer:

The life cycle of the two species are very similar. The egg-laying female lays eggs in shallow water near a suitable spot for hatching. After approximately three days the tiny larvae emerge from their shells to begin living on land as filter feeders.

In order to survive they need large amounts of protein, which is obtained by catching small crustaceans called copepods. Once the young have reached adult size, around seven months after hatching, they return back into the river where they remain until they reach sexual maturity about two years later.

This time spent feeding on land makes them poor swimmers compared with their freshwater relatives, but when it comes to mating, both sexes become quite active and compete for dominance over territory. When breeding finally takes place the male fertilizes the females eggs and she lays between 10,000 to 25,000 of them at one time before returning to live on land once more.

Milk fish also lay many eggs, though these are smaller than those of their crayfish relative and hatch into tiny fry that are able to fend for themselves immediately rather than needing a few days on land first. They are then raised in ponds or tanks in close proximity to larger predators like snakes so they can quickly grow to a size where they are able to defend themselves if needed.

Although these species share a similar life cycle they still have enough differences to make them easily distinguishable from each other. The biggest difference is that Marbled crayfish are much more tolerant of pollution, being found naturally in heavily industrialized areas like New York City where the water is not safe for humans to drink. Milk fish only thrive in pristine environments however, as they require clean fresh water and lots of sunshine to produce milk-like substances called roe.

As you can see there are many similarities and differences between these two species but I think that the most important one here is that while both are very successful freshwater species, their habitats do not overlap at all, making it unlikely for either one of them to ever become an invasive species in another country.
