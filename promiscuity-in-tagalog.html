17. Write M if the statement refers to a misconception of reproductive health and F if it is a fact. E1. Menstrual bleeding means you are not pregnant. 2. Severe menstrual cramping is not normal. 3. Symptoms of STI cannot be easily observed. 4. You can only have one STI at a time. 6. 5. Pre-ejaculate fluid cannot make a woman pregnant. During an unprotected sex, a woman can still get pregnant. Condoms are not effective. 7. F M 8. Contraceptives cause abortion. 9. Contraceptives have been proven safe worldwide. 10. Sex education aims to prevent STI and even lessen promiscuity.


Answer:

1. M (Menstrual bleeding does not necessarily mean that a woman is not pregnant. Other factors such as a missed period, implantation bleeding, or hormonal imbalances can also cause menstrual bleeding.)

2. F (Severe menstrual cramping is often an indicator of a medical condition such as endometriosis or uterine fibroids and should be evaluated by a healthcare provider.)

3. F (Many STIs do not have obvious symptoms and can only be diagnosed through medical testing.)

4. M (A person can have multiple STIs at the same time.)

5. M (Pre-ejaculate fluid can contain sperm and can potentially cause pregnancy.)

6. M (During unprotected sex, a woman can still get pregnant, and relying solely on condoms is not a foolproof method of contraception.)

7. M (Condoms, when used correctly and consistently, are a highly effective method of preventing pregnancy and STIs.)

8. M (Contraceptives do not cause abortion. They work to prevent pregnancy, either by inhibiting ovulation or creating a barrier to prevent sperm from reaching an egg.)

9. F (Many forms of contraception have been extensively researched and are considered safe for most people to use, however the safety and efficacy may vary among different individuals and depend on their health history and use of the method.)

10. F (Sex education aims to provide comprehensive information on sexuality and sexual health, including the prevention of STIs and unintended pregnancy. While it can contribute to reducing promiscuity, it is not its sole aim.)
