6. How does thermal efficiency different from ideal efficiency?


Answer:

Thermal efficiency relates to processes where the input is thermal energy as in a fired power plant, a internal combustion engine or solar energy systems. It is the ratio of the mechanical output to the thermal input. Overall efficiency looks at entire systems from the initial input to the final output.

Five examples of rules in a business that would promote effectiveness:

Clear communication and decision-making processes to ensure all employees are aware of their roles and responsibilities.Regular performance evaluations to measure progress and identify areas for improvementEmployee training and development programs to enhance skills and knowledgeCollaboration and teamwork initiatives to promote a positive and productive work environmentA clear code of conduct and ethical guidelines to ensure employees act in the best interests of the company and its stakeholders.

Five examples of rules in a business that would promote efficiency:

Time management and prioritization guidelines to ensure employees use their time effectivelyAutomation of repetitive tasks to reduce labor and time costsUse of technology to streamline processes and reduce manual workStandardization of procedures to increase consistency and reduce errorsImplementation of cost-saving measures to reduce expenses and increase profitability.

25. What is the difference between technical efficiency and economic efficiency?​


Technical Efficiency in production means that as few inputs as possible are used to produce a given output.

Economic efficiency means using the method that produces a given level of output at the lowest possible cost.
