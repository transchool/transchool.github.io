10. During the pendency of the suspensive condition, the debtor has paid by mistake a sum of money. what is the remedy of the debtor before the fulfillment of suspensive condition


In a state of suspensive, the debtor has inadvertently paid a sum of money. Efforts that can be made:

1. Payments are suspended

This remedy is accessible to a debtor who has more assets than liabilities but anticipates being unable to pay those liabilities when they become due. Payment suspensions may be initiated voluntarily by the debtor or involuntarily (filed by the creditors). Financial rehabilitation, which is covered in more detail below, is an option for businesses and other juridical debtors.

2. Rehabilitation in money

If it is demonstrated that the legal debtor's continued operation is economically feasible and that its creditors can recover through the present value of payments projected in the plan, more so if the debtor continues as a going concern than if it is immediately liquidated, rehabilitation is the restoration of the legal debtor to a condition of successful operation and solvency.

3. Liquidation

In the Regional Trial Court with jurisdiction over its major office as specified in its articles of incorporation or partnership, an insolvent juridical debtor may submit a verified petition for liquidation. Insolvent legal debtors' liquidation may be voluntary (filed by the debtor) or involuntary (filed by creditors).

13. Sample situation about suspension condition?​


Answer:

A sample situation involving a suspension condition could be as follows:

John is a high school student who has been caught cheating on his final exams. As a consequence, the school's disciplinary committee has decided to suspend him for two weeks. However, they have also imposed a suspension condition that he must fulfill before he can return to school.

The suspension condition requires John to complete a two-hour academic integrity workshop within the first week of his suspension period. The workshop covers topics such as the importance of honesty and the consequences of cheating in school. The school administration wants to ensure that John understands the gravity of his actions and is less likely to repeat them in the future.

John's parents have been informed of the suspension and the condition attached to it. They are required to sign a form indicating that they understand the suspension condition and will ensure that John completes the workshop as soon as possible.

If John fails to complete the workshop within the first week of his suspension, he will not be allowed to return to school until he does so. This suspension condition is meant to ensure that John takes responsibility for his actions and learns from his mistake.
