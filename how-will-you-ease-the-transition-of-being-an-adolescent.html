7. Write a definition paragraph of how you feel being an "Adolescent"​


Being a teenager, I completely concur with the idea that everything is evolving and growing quickly. We are trying to figure out who we are, how we fit into the world, and what kind of impact we can have. It can be very difficult to keep up with everything new we have to deal with.

We experience many ups and downs at times, like we are riding a wild rollercoaster. Life is absolutely wonderful for a split second, and we feel unstoppable. On the other hand, the next thing we know, we're in a panic, feeling lost, and not knowing what the future holds. We cannot simply unwind and take it easy because so many people and things are vying for our time and attention.

Teenage life can, however, also be a ton of fun and amazing. We have the chance to discover new interests, identify our true passions, and create some of the best ever connections. The time seems perfect to gain more knowledge about who we are, the world we live in, and the opportunities available. On this crazy journey, we are all together.

Answer:How to help pre-teens and teenagers develop independence

Independence for pre-teens and teenagers is about trying new things, taking on more responsibility, making decisions by themselves, and working out who they are and what they want to be.

Achieving independence is an essential part of your child’s journey towards adulthood.

There are many things that you can do to help your child develop independence:

Show your child love and support.

Respect your child’s feelings and opinions.

Set clear and fair family rules.

Help your child develop skills for decision-making.

Give your child opportunities to practise being independent and responsible.

Work through conflicts constructively.
