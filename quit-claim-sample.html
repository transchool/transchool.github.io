3. Only 34% of people who try to quit smoking succeed. A company claims that using their chewing gum can help people quit. What is the null hypothesis?​


Answer:

null hypothesis is a type of statistical hypothesis that proposes that no statistical significance exists in a set of given observations.

Step-by-step explanation:

Hope it helps<3

Step-by-step explanation:

The null hypothesis in this scenario would be that using the company's chewing gum does not have an impact on a person's ability to quit smoking. Mathematically, this can be represented as:

H0: p = 0.34

Where p represents the probability of success in quitting smoking, and the null hypothesis states that p is equal to the historical success rate of 34%.

The alternative hypothesis, on the other hand, would be that using the company's chewing gum does have an impact on a person's ability to quit smoking and increases their chance of success. This can be represented as:

H1: p > 0.34

It's important to note that the null hypothesis is a statement of "no effect" or "no difference", and it's usually assumed to be true until proven otherwise. The alternative hypothesis is the opposite statement of the null hypothesis, representing a difference or an effect. Hypothesis testing is used to determine which hypothesis is more supported by the data
