13. Major Walter Wingfield introduced the game Answer which the racket game originated from. (tennis)


Major Walter Wingfield introduced the game Answer which the racket game originated from. (tennis)

The answer is playing Ball. Here are the Explanation. Major Walter Clopton Wingfield was a Welsh Renaissance man known for being the founder and  promoter of modern lawn tennis. Wingfield patented the game in Britain in 1874 after some had  previously merged sports such as court tennis and rackets to play a game similar to what Wingfield  called Sphairistiké– or Greek for "ball games." Wingfield's patent expired in 1877, enabling the game to grow further. He secured a patent for the game in February 1874 and had it signed by Queen Victoria.

All indications point to Wingfield having a foresight to start the game. He patented the New and  Improved Court for the Ancient Game of Tennis and began marketing his game in the spring of 1874.  The early models of modern lawn tennis consisted of a box set consisting of rubber balls, a net, stakes, court markers, and an instruction manual. He published two free tennis novels, The Book of Games  in 1873 and The Major's Game of Lawn Tennis in 1874.
