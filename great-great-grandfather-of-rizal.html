What is Communication?

Giving, receiving, and exchanging information — in other words, speaking, writing, and listening or reading — is the act of communication. Good communicators pay attention to what others are saying, speak or write clearly, and respect differing viewpoints.

The act of sending information from one place, person, or organization to another is known as communication. A source, a message, and a recipient are all part of every communication. This may appear to be a simple concept, yet communication is actually a very complicated topic.

Different Categories on Communication:

Spoken or Verbal Communication

Face-to-face, telephone, radio or tv, and other media are examples of spoken or verbal communication. The use of words to communicate a message is known as verbal communication. 

Non-Verbal Communication

Body language, gestures, how we dress or act, where we stand, and even our scent are all examples of nonverbal communication. We interact with people in a variety of subtle ways (sometimes even accidentally). The tone of one's voice, for example, can reveal one's mood or emotional condition, while hand signals or gestures can complement a verbal word. While good communication is important in both personal and professional relationships, it's your nonverbal clues or "body language" that speak the loudest. Body language is the nonverbal communication of nonverbal behavior, expressions, and mannerisms, which is typically done automatically rather than intentionally.

Written Communication

Letters, e-mails, social media, books, periodicals, the Internet, and other forms of written communication are all examples of written communication. Until recently, when it came to communicating the written word, a relatively small group of writers and publishers wielded enormous power. Today, we can all write and publish our thoughts on the internet, resulting in an explosion of knowledge and communication options.

Visualization

Graphs and charts, maps, logos, and other visuals can all be used to communicate information.

PASSIVE AND ACTIVE VOICE

Passive voice is when the subject acted on come first and the end, the action is denoted by the verb. While active voice is when the subject of the sentence performs the action of the verb.
