6. What features of the newspaper or magazine helped you to connect the pieces perfectly?


FEATURES OF THE NEWSPAPER OR MAGAZINE THAT HELPED ME TO CONNECT THE PIECES PERFECTLY ARE:

1. Headlines

2. Intro (kicker, stand-first, deck)

3. Body copy (body text)

4. Pull quotes

EXPLANATION:

1. Headlines

The title in my opinion is just as important as the layout. Once the reader opens the page the first thing that catches his attention is the layout or some dominant imagery. The second thing that will attract attention to reading the article is the headline. Readers may find the layout attractive but if the headlines are unattractive, they may skip it. Titles can vary in size and the importance of the content determines the main size. The position of the title is also important and directs the main title at the very top of the page. Headings should be set in a larger font size and clearly relate to other text elements on the page.

2. Intro (kicker, stand-first, deck)

From a design point of view the intro should be set in a type size larger than the body copy but in a much smaller size than the headline. Can make it with different typeface styles. The intro text is placed just below the title because it is a unit. The title grabs the reader's attention, makes him curious and the intro text piques the reader's curiosity by giving him more information about the article.

3. Body copy (body text)

This is the content section of the content article. The body copy should be as eye-catching as the design, as the headline and intro text. This is why it is important to experiment with different types of sizes to suit different column widths. As was done in the first stage of creating a template and had to spend a lot of time working on the contents of the magazine until it got it right. Use column and type choices to reflect brand identity and present stories in a way that fits the content.

4. Pull quotes

Very useful citations are generally found in the body element of the page. Pull quotes serve as a great tool for breaking up sections between body copy and giving the magazine content a more attractive look. Quotes can be used in conjunction with images so they can tell a story in their own way or placing quotes outside of the image can be taken directly from the body text.
