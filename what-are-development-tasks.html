16. what havighurst development task theory? in essay​


Answer:

The main assertion of the Havighurst developmental tasks theory is that development is continuous throughout a person's entire lifespan, occurring in stages. A person moves from one stage to the next by means of successful resolution of problems or performance of certain developmental tasks.

Answer:

All of these play a significant role in developing as an individual.

Physical development - Learning how to improve your hand-to-eye coordination and making decisions on the fly is improved by playing games.

Emotional Development - Understanding how others feel helps you become better at socializing. Being able to adapt to the feelings of others can help you gain a lot of friends and be understood better.

Mental Development - Playing a lot of logical games and solving logical questions helps keep your brain sharp. It gives you the edge on solving logical questions or situations.

Social Development - The ability to communicate well with different people is a skill that many people seem to underestimate. Learning how people respond to your jokes or your humor and reading their body language will help guide you towards a route where they will like you.
