2. discuss and describe pineapple marinadepork rib marinade teriyaki marinade mustard marinade​


Answer:

PINEAPPLE MARINADE

----Inducing a sour combination of real fruit, fruit juice, and soy sauce from the use of the barbecue, this golden, sweet marinade is great on meat and seafood, and keeps a bold aroma with the pineapples. Garlic powder sometimes replaces the garlic cloves and works for this recipe, too.

The ingredients in making pineapple marinade are the following;

1 cup pineapple juice, store-bought only

1 cup pineapple tidbits, optional

¼ cup sugar, brown only

¼ cup soy sauce, dark or light only

2 minced garlic cloves

PORK RIB MARINADE

---This marinade uses a delicious pork rub combined with corn syrup and vinegar to create a flavorful pork rib marinade. If you do not want to use corn syrup, then by all means use a good quality maple syrup, agave syrup, or even honey. However, keep in mind that using any of these will change the flavor of the original recipe.

The ingredients in making pork rib marinade are the following;

1 cup cider vinegar

1 cup water

1 tablespoon pork rub (use the same as you will use on the ribs)

2 tablespoon corn syrup

14. Differences of marinate and marinade


Answer:

Marinade is a liquid mixture (usually vinegar, oil, and herbs) in which meat is soaked before cooking. Marinate is the corresponding verb (i.e., to soak in marinade).

24. what is importance of marinades​


Answer:

In terms of cooking, below are the importance of marinades

1. The acid which is an ingredient of a marinade allows the meat to become tender.

2. The oil which is another ingredient of a marinade allows the flavors to penetrate inside the meat so that the inner parts of the meat, not just the outer portion will be tasty.

3. The herbs and spices (optional depending on what a cook wants to add and what flavor he or she is aiming at) gives the meat its delicious taste.

30. cooking process of sinigang na liempo​


Answer:

1. Boil the water in a cooking pot.

2. Put-in the tamarind and continue to boil for 45 minutes. Once done, remove the tamarind from the water. Set both aside.

3. Meanwhile, heat the oil on another deep cooking pot.

4. Once the oil is hot, sear the pork belly until the color turns light brown.

5. Stir-in the tomatoes and onion. Cook for 2 minutes.

6. Gently pour-in the water (used to boil the tamarind), but do not include the tamarind yet. 7. Add the beef broth and gabi afterwards.

8. Place the tamarind on a strainer and let the remaining juice out by pushing a spoon against it. Add the tamarind juice to the cooking pot where the meat is. Simmer for 60 minutes. Add more water if necessary.

9. Put-in the okra and long green chili. Cook for 3 minutes.

10. Add the kangkong (onchoy) and fish sauce. 11. Stir. Cover and turn the heat off. Let it stay covered for 5 minutes.

12. Transfer to a serving bowl.

13. Serve with warm rice.

14. Share and enjoy!
