9. Eric is ten years younger than Jason. In six years, Jason will be twice as old as Eric. What are their present ages?​


Answer:

Eric is 4 years old and Jason is 14 years old.

Step-by-step explanation:

Age Word Problem

Let us analyze the problem.

What is asked?

The present ages of Eric and Jason.

What are the given?

Let x be Jason's age.

Present Ages:

Jason - x

Eric - x - 10

(ten years younger than Jason)

In six years:

Eric - (x - 10) + 6

Jason - x + 6

- 2(x - 10) + 6

(twice as old as Eric)

We need to add 6 because these are their ages in 6 years.

What is the number sentence?

x + 6 = 2(x - 10) + 6

We have this equation, from Jason's age because we need to find his age first, for us to find Eric's.

What is the solution?

Solve. Combine similar terms.

Jason's Age in 6 Years:

x + 6 = 2(x - 10) + 6

x + 6 = 2(x - 10) + 6

x + 6 = 2x - 20 + 6

x + 6 = 2x - 14

14 + 6 = 2x - x

20 = x

In 6 years, Jason will be 20 years old.

Now, that we know Jason's age in 6 years, we can now find their present ages.

Jason's Present Age:

To find his present age, take away 6 from his age in 6 years.

20 - 6 = 14

Jason is 14 years old.

Eric's Present Age:

x = 14

x - 10

14 - 10 = 4

Eric is 4 years old.

Checking:

Let us check if our answers meet the given condition.

"In six years, Jason will be twice as old as Eric."

Eric - 4 + 6 = 10 years old

Jason - 20 years old

Yes! 20 is twice of 10. ✔

Age Word Problem

Age word problems are algebra word problems that deal with the ages of people currently, in the past or in the future. They involve ages of one person or two or more persons.

Important Statements and Equations for "Problems based on Ages":

Answering age word problems may be difficult for some. Here are some of the important tips that can help in formulating the equations.

If the present age is y, then n times the present age = ny.

If the present age is x, then age n years later/hence = x + n.

If the present age is x, then age n years ago = x – n.

30. Jason is planting rows of corn. He needs 4/9 square feet. There are 8 square feet in Jason's backyard, how many rows of corn does Jason have?


Multiplication and Division of Fraction

In mathematics, multiplication of fraction is simple as multiplying/dividing numerator by numerator, then denominator by denominator.  For division of fraction, reciprocate the second fraction then multiply. Fraction is important in real life, it was applied in finance, computation of amount of liquid and portion of the subjects.  

Fraction is defined as a numerical quantity that is not a whole number (e.g., 1/3, 2/5).

To multiply fractions, follow these three steps:   Multiply the top numbers (the numerators).   Multiply the bottom numbers (the denominators).   Simplify the fraction if necessary. To divide fractions, follow these three steps:   Reciprocate the second fraction Change the division sign into multiplication Multiply the top numbers (the numerators).   Multiply the bottom numbers (the denominators).   Simplify the fraction if necessary

The given problem is “Jason is planting rows of corn. He needs 4/9 square feet. There are 8 square feet in Jason's backyard, how many rows of corn does Jason have?”

The given problem is an application of division of fraction. When each row  requires 4/9 square feet, so divide it from the total area of the backyard.

Solution

8 ÷  4/9

It can transform into 8/1 x 9/4 = 72/4

72/4 = 18

Answer

18 rows
