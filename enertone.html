15. bohr model:how are the electons arranged​


⬆ ⬆ ⬆ ⬆ ⬆ ⬆ ⬆ ⬆ ⬆ ⬆ ⬆ ⬆ ⬆ ⬆ ⬆ ⬆

[tex]{\huge{{\tt{ANSWER}}}}[/tex]

⬇ ⬇ ⬇ ⬇ ⬇ ⬇ ⬇ ⬇ ⬇ ⬇ ⬇ ⬇ ⬇ ⬇ ⬇ ⬇

Bohr's atomic model depicts the electrons as entities occupying fixed orbits around the nucleus. Each orbit corresponds to a specific amount of energy, which increases as the orbit gets farther from the nucleus. According to Bohr, an electron can jump from a low energy to a high energy orbit when it absorbs energy. This process is called electron excitation. An electron can also jump from a high energy orbit to a low energy orbit and releases energy in the form of light, whose color depends on it's wavelength. This process is called electron de-excitation.
