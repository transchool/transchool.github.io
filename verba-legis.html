1. Simply explain ignorancia legis non execusat.


The legal principle of ignorantia juris non excusat (ignorance of the law excuses not) or ignorantia legis neminem excusat (ignorance of law excuses no one) is derived from Roman law. Essentially, it means that if someone breaks the law, he or she is still liable even if they had no knowledge of the law being broken.

9. Michael and Jane have cleaned their

hands by washing it properly with soap

and running water for 20 seconds before

they eat their food. What is the tense of

the underlined verba

a. Past perfect tense

c. Present progressive tense

b. Present perfect tense

d. Future perfect tense

Explanation:

The present perfect tense refers to an action or state that either occurred at an indefinite time in the past (e.g., we have talked before) or began in the past and continued to the present time (e.g., he has grown impatient over the last hour). This tense is formed by have/has + the past participle.

The past perfect continuous is made from had been and the -ing form of a verb:  I had been working there for a year. They had been painting the bedroom.
