12. What part of Sedaris’ sarcastic tone makes us feel amused?


Answer:

David Sedaris' use of sarcasm in his writing often relies on a contrast between what is expected or taken seriously and what is actually being said or described. This contrast creates an incongruity that can be amusing to readers, as they may be surprised or caught off guard by the unexpected or ridiculous nature of Sedaris' statements. Additionally, his sarcasm often involves exaggerating or poking fun at certain situations or people, which can be seen as a form of social commentary and can be relatable and funny for the readers.
