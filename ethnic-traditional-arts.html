Traditional dancing can be another term for folk dance, or sometimes even for ceremonial dance. The term ‘Traditional’ is more frequently used when the emphasis is on the cultural roots of the dance. A Traditional dance will therefore have arisen from a people’s cultural traditions, for example, the folk dances of indigenous populations of Europe. However, dances that have a ritual origin or purpose are not usually considered to be Traditional dances. These are known as ‘Religious dances’ instead.

Traditional dancing is generally more of a social activity rather than competitive, but it is normally choreographed. Depending on the dance type itself, Traditional dancing can be either partnered or solo, and are mainly danced in formation.

indigenous dance is an integral part of life. It is linked with the worldview of the society in which it is produced. In its real sense, indigenous dance as an art form transforms images, ideas, thoughts and feelings into movement sequence that are personally and socially significant.

14. 7.) ln this art tradition, art forms were primarily influenced by the area where our ancestors prosper A. Ethnic Tradition B. American Colonial Tradition C. Spanish Colonial Tradition D. Contemporary Art Tradition​


C. Spanish Colonial Tradition

The colonizers employed art as a means of spreading the Catholic faith when they first landed in the Philippines in 1521. The friars used pictures to recount the narrative of Christ's life and passion and to explain the ideas of Catholicism since they found it difficult to communicate. Through carved santos, the via crucis (Stations of the Cross), engravings on estampas and estampitas, and paintings on church walls, the Holy Family and the saints were first presented to the Filipino psyche.

The Spanish friars and the Chinese, the colony's main trading partner, were gradually importing novel art forms, even if the ethnic art forms such as ceramics, weaving, and metalwork were preserved. The friars' brought along icons were utilized as sculptural models. Chinese brushwork painting techniques were introduced to Filipino artists. As well, engraving was introduced.

Patronage as a notion developed. To carve, engrave, and paint, artisans were hired and compensated. Rituals took the role of the arts that were formerly practiced in a spirit of community and in a setting of community. The new patron of the arts was the church, in particular the friars.

The friars imposed stringent oversight over their work because the majority of the art created during the first two centuries of Spanish dominion was for the church. Art was only used by the church and for religious purposes up until the 19th century.
