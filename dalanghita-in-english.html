Proportion

1. 4 dalaghita to 2 melons = 8 dalaghita to ___ melons

   4: 2 = 8 : n

   4n = 16

    [tex]\frac{4n}{4}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{16}{4}[/tex]

    n = 4

    Answer: 4 melons

2. ___ lanzones to 5 oranges = 36 lanzones to 15 oranges

  n : 5 = 36 : 15

  15n = 180

  [tex]\frac{15n}{15}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{180}{15}[/tex]

  n = 12

  Answer: 12 lanzones

3. 8 guavas to 6 mangosteens = ___ guavas to 24 mangosteens

   8 : 6 = n : 24

    192 = 6n

    [tex]\frac{192}{6}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{6n}{6}[/tex]

    32 = n

  Answer: 32 guavas

Step-by-step explanation:

A ratio compares two numbers or quantities. They are expressed either as a fraction ([tex]\frac{a}{b}[/tex]) or separated by colon (a:b) Proportions show two equivalent ratios. It can be written as [tex]\frac{a}{b}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{c}{d}[/tex] or a:b = c:d.

One type of proportion is the direct proportion. This type of proportion shows a direct relationship between two quantities. It means that if one quantity increases, the other quantity also increases, and if one quantity decreases, the other quantity decreases. There are several ways to solve for direct proportion. One of which is by the means and extremes of the proportion. To solve for the unknown quantity of the proportion:

1. Multiply the extremes of the proportion and write the product on one side of the equation. Extremes are the outer numbers of the proportion.

   Ex.   4: 2 = 8 : n

2. Multiply the means of the proportion and write the product on the other side of the equation. Means are the inner numbers of the proportion.

    Ex.   4: 2 = 8 : n

            4n = 16

3. Solve for the unknown. Isolate variable on one side of the equation by dividing both sides of the equation by the number accompanying the variable. For instance the 4n,  we divide it by 4.

    Ex.  4n = 16

            [tex]\frac{4n}{4}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{16}{4}[/tex]

            n = 4

1. Express ratio into fractions:

  Ex. 4: 2 = 8 : n

2. Cross multiply the fractions. Multiply the numerator of the first fraction by the denominator of the second fraction. Place the product on one side of the equation. Also multiply the denominator of the first fraction by the numerator of the second fraction. Place this on the other side of the equation.
