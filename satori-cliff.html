10. A stone drop from as cliff reaches the bottom is 5 seconds, What is the height of the cliff? Prove that the height of the cliff is 1.16 meters


Before we answer the problem, let us discuss what really is the study involved.

KINEMATICS is one of the branches of mechanics wherein it is concerned with moving objects without having reference to the forces which has caused the motion. It also talks about the FIVE KINEMATIC VARIABLES (displacement, time, initial and final velocities, and acceleration) of the motion of a certain object.

There are 4 COMMONLY USED KINEMATIC FORMULAS which are

1. Vf = Vi + at

2. D = ((Vf + Vi)/2)(t)

3. D = (Vi)(t) + (1/2)(a)(t^2)

4. (Vf)^2 = (Vi)^2 + 2aD

In kinematics, there is what we call FREE FALL which is the motion by which the object is being dropped from a certain height. This further means that "drop" has an initial velocity of 0.

Basing on the given values, we can make use of the 3rd formula for direct substitution.

We have

D = (Vi)(t) + (1/2)(a)(t^2)

D as the height of the cliff which is unknown

Vi as the initial velocity (0 since the stone is dropped)

t as the time the stone reaches the bottom; and

a as the acceleration due to gravity = +9.81 m/s^2

You might wonder why there is a + sign on the acceleration. That is the convention we use if the object is falling (with the gravity), (-) is used if the object is thrown upward (against the gravity).

D = (Vi)(t) + (1/2)(a)(t^2)

D = (0)(5 sec) + (1/2)(9.81 m/s^2)(5sec)^2

D = 0 + (1/2)(245.25 m)

D = 122.625m

Thus, the height of the cliff is 122.625 meters. And this further proves that the height is not 1.16 meters.

From the top of a vertical cliff 40m high, the angle of depression of an object that is level with the base of the cliff is 34°. How far is the object from the base of the cliff.

[tex]\tan34 \degree = \frac{ \: 40 \: m \: }{x} \\ [/tex]

15. From the top of a vertical cliff 40m high, the angle of depression of an object that is level with the base of the cliff is 34 degrees. How far is the object from the base of the cliff


[tex]\LARGE\bold\red{\tt{ANGLE\:OF\: DEPRESSION}}[/tex]

PROBLEM:

From the top of a vertical cliff 40m high, the angle of depression of an object that is level with the base of the cliff is 34 degrees. How far is the object from the base of the cliff?

GIVEN:

height (opposite) = 40m

distance (adjacent) = ?

angle of depression = 34°

SOLUTION:

We will use the ratio of tangent

[tex] \large \tt tanθ  = \frac{opposite}{adjacent} \\ \\ \large \tt tan34 = \frac{40}{adj} \\ \\ \large \tt (adj)(tan34) = (\frac{40}{ \cancel{adj}} )( \cancel{adj}) \\ \\ \large \tt (adj)(tan34) = 40 \\ \\ \large \tt \frac{(adj)( \cancel{tan34})}{ \cancel{tan34}} = \frac{40}{tan34} \\ \\ \large \tt adj = \frac{40}{tan34} \\ \\ \red{ \boxed{ \tt adj = 59.3}}[/tex]

CONCLUSION:∴ The object is 59.3m far from the base of the cliff.

25. From a point 525 feet in horizontal distance from the foot of a vertical cliff, the angle of elevation for the top of the cliff is 36°. How high is the cliff to the nearest foot?


Answer:

381 feet

Step-by-step explanation:

Draw a right triangle

in the base write 525 feet

In the upper right of 525 feet which is the angle of elevation, write 36°

In the upper left of 525 feet is the right angle which measures 90°

The top angle measures 54°

36°+90°+54° equals 180°

Apply sine law

a/sinA equals b/sinB equals c/sinC

sinA is sin36, b is 525, sinB is sin54, sinC is sin90

We will find a, since a is the height

a/sin36 equals 525/sin54

Note: change the mode of your scientific calculator to DEGREE

Cross multiply

a (sin54) equals 525(sin36)

a(sin54) equals 308.59

Divide both sides by sin54 to have a

a(sin54)/sin54 equals 308.59/sin54

a equals 381.44

Round off to the nearest foot

381 feet
