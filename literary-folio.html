ESSAY:

The pandemic has brought about unprecedented changes in the way we live and learn. With the sudden shift to online learning, students and educators alike have had to adapt to this new mode of education. While there are certainly benefits to online learning, such as flexibility and access to a wider range of resources, it is undeniable that it has also had its fair share of challenges and negative impacts on students' behavior and scholastic performance.

One of the main issues with online learning is the lack of face-to-face interaction with teachers and classmates. This can lead to a sense of isolation and disconnection, which can negatively affect students' motivation and engagement in their studies. In addition, the lack of in-person supervision can lead to a decrease in accountability and discipline, resulting in poor time management and decreased productivity.

In conclusion, online learning has had both positive and negative effects on student behavior and scholastic performance. While it has provided flexibility and access to resources, it has also brought about challenges such as a lack of face-to-face interaction, unequal access to technology, and increased stress and difficulty for some students. It is important to address these challenges in order to provide a more equitable and effective learning experience for all students.

24. the Philippines association of campus student leaders is in the process of publishing a literary folio that include poems, short stories,comics, and other creative output written during the lockdown by students all over the Philippines. You were contracted and invited by the organization to write an essay about the effects of online learning on student's behavior and scholastic performance during the pandemic. You will produce an argumentative essay that will be included as part of the folio to be distributed nationwide. ​


Answer:

The COVID-19 pandemic has had a profound impact on the way students learn and has led to a widespread shift towards online learning. While this has certainly had some benefits, it has also had significant effects on student behavior and scholastic performance.

One of the main effects of online learning on student behavior has been the decrease in social interaction and the increase in isolation. Students who were used to learning in a physical classroom with their peers have had to adjust to learning from home, often without the same level of interaction with classmates and teachers. This lack of social interaction can lead to feelings of loneliness and isolation, which can in turn affect a student's motivation and engagement.

Another effect of online learning on student behavior has been the increased temptation to procrastinate and engage in distracting activities. With fewer structure and supervision, students may find it easier to put off their work or get sidetracked by other activities. This can lead to a decline in productivity and a decrease in overall performance.

In terms of scholastic performance, research has shown that online learning can be less effective than in-person learning, especially for students who struggle academically. This is because online learning often lacks the immediate feedback and support that students receive in a physical classroom setting. Additionally, the lack of structure and supervision in an online environment can make it more difficult for students to stay on track and complete assignments.

Overall, while online learning has certainly had some benefits, it has also had significant effects on student behavior and scholastic performance. It is important that educators and policymakers take these effects into account as they continue to navigate the challenges of the pandemic and consider the best approaches to teaching and learning in the future.
