27. 3. Discuss the strengths of quantitative research.​


Strengths of Quantitative Research

One of the strengths of quantitative data is that it allows for easy replication of results, demonstrating or disproving theories across huge groups of people. Qualitative data does not have the same replicability as quantitative data because of the wide range of emotions, feelings, and responses from participants.

Other strengths of quantitative research:

If the selection procedure is well-designed and the sample is representative of the study population, the results can be generalized.

Analyzing it is rather simple.

Data can be extremely constant, exact, and trustworthy.

What is quantitative research?

Quantitative research data is information that can be tallied and is typically collected by surveys from a large number of respondents who were chosen at random for inclusion. Quantitative research frequently includes secondary data such as census data, government statistics, health system measurements, and so on. Statistical approaches are used to analyze quantitative data. Quantitative methods are best adapted to answering what, when, and who questions, but not to how and why ones.
