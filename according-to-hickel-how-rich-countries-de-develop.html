5. Whose theory is better, Heidegger’s or Hickel’s? Why?


Explanation:

Abstract

This paper will examine some of the ways in which development has been influenced by the body of ideas known as techne, or technics. This body of ideas focuses on a central theme that envisages technology broadly defined as developing its own impetus that removes it from the control of human agency and that begins to circumscribe and even control human agency. This can be seen as having various impacts on development, notably a subsumption of any concern for human development to issues concerning process and production of outputs. The paper focuses on the approach of Heidegger as he provides an account that places technology at the centre of human being, whilst helping to distinguish both the negative (enframing) tendencies and the emancipatory (or, as Heidegger might have it, poietic) possibilities of technics. The paper concludes by identifying some of these negative and emancipatory influences in the development context.

Keywords: Technics, being, dasein, enframing, poiesis

6. Hickels concern for Economy​


Answer:

The dominant story that is handed down to us from technocrats and the media is that global poverty is rapidly decreasing, thanks to free-market globalization. But this narrative relies on a very low poverty line. If we look at more accurate poverty lines, it’s clear that the reality is more complex. The number of people living on less than $5 per day (2005 PPP), the minimum necessary for good nutrition and normal human life expectancy, has increased dramatically since the 1980s, particularly during the era of structural adjustment. It is estimated that 4.2 billion people live with less than this amount; it is nearly 60% of the world’s population. This is a blistering indictment of our global economy, and it suggests that our usual approach to development has basically failed.

16. 1. Discuss and illustrate the dangers of modern technology.2. Differentiate between traditional frameworks of progress and development and Hickel's de-developing framework.​


Answer:

The dangers of modern technology are numerous and varied. Some of the most significant dangers include:

Cybersecurity threats such as malware, phishing attacks, and data breaches, which can compromise sensitive personal and financial information.

The potential for technology to interfere with personal relationships and social interactions, leading to social isolation and reduced face-to-face communication.

The risk of job displacement and economic disruption as a result of automation and artificial intelligence.

The negative environmental impact of technology, including pollution and resource depletion.

The potential for technology to be used for nefarious purposes, such as surveillance, censorship, and propaganda.

Traditional frameworks of progress and development are typically based on the idea that economic growth and technological advancement are the primary indicators of a society's progress. This approach often prioritizes the interests of the industrialized world and is focused on increasing GDP and maximizing profits.

In contrast, Hickel's de-developing framework argues that current models of development are unsustainable and that societies should prioritize the well-being of people and the planet over economic growth. This approach advocates for a reduction in consumption and a shift towards more equitable and sustainable forms of development.

21. according to hickel what must be done insted of urging poor countries to catch up with rich ones​


Answer:

According to economist Jason Hickel, instead of urging poor countries to catch up with rich ones, what must be done is to address the root causes of poverty, inequality, and underdevelopment. This includes challenging the global economic system that perpetuates these issues and working towards more equitable distribution of resources and wealth. It also includes rethinking development models that prioritize economic growth over social and environmental well-being, and instead embracing approaches that prioritize human needs and ecological sustainability.
