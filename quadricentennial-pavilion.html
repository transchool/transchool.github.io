10. 2. From the main gate of a Pasig Central Elem. Sch., there are two pathways where visitors can walk along going to the circular pavilion. The pathways are both tangent to the garden whose center is 40 m away from the main entrance. If the area of the garden is about 706.5m², how long is each pathway?​


Answer:

The area of a circle is given by the formula A = πr², where A is the area and r is the radius. We know that the area of the garden is 706.5m² and the radius is 40m. So, we can use the formula to find the value of π.

A = πr²

π = A/r²

π = 706.5/40²

π = 3.1416

The radius of the garden is 40m and the distance from the main entrance to the center of the garden is also 40m. The pathways are tangent to the garden, so the radius of the garden is also the length of each pathway.

length of each pathway = radius of the garden = 40m

Each pathway is 40 meters long.

1. The Royal Pavilion, also known as the Brighton Pavilion, is a Grade I listed former royal residence located in Brighton, England. Beginning in 1787, it was built in three stages as a seaside retreat for George, Prince of Wales, who became the Prince Regent in 1811, and King George IV in 1820.

2. The Starry Night is an oil on canvas painting by Dutch Post-Impressionist painter Vincent van Gogh. Painted in June 1889, it depicts the view from the east-facing window of his asylum room at Saint-Rémy-de-Provence, just before sunrise, with the addition of an imaginary village.

3. Jean-Louis André Théodore Géricault was an influential French painter and lithographer, whose best-known painting is The Raft of the Medusa. Although he died young, he was one of the pioneers of the Romantic movement

- Edgar Allan Poe, To Helen.

To Helen is the first of two poems with that title written by Poe which allude to the story of Helen of Troy. Poe's 1845 revision made several rhythmic alterations to his original poem, most notably improving "the beauty of fair Greece, and the grandeur of old Rome" to read "the glory that was Greece and the grandeur that was Rome." In Greek mythology, Helen was born from an egg, the daughter of Zeus and Leda, and her personification of ideal beauty has inspired such varied artists as Guido Reni, Tintoretto, and Gustave Moreau. 5. Oedipus and the Sphinx is an 1864 oil on canvas painting by Gustave Moreau that was first exhibited at the French Salon of 1864 where it was an immediate success. It is now in the Metropolitan Museum of Art. The work was a fresh treatment of the established subject of the meeting between Oedipus and the Sphinx on the road to Delphi, notably portrayed at Sophocles' play Oedipus Rex.
