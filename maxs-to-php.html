10. h(x)=-5x^2+20x+60 max height and time to reach max height


Answer:

The maximum height is 80 (please include the unit of measure = feet, meter, etc.) after the time of 2 (please include unit of measure = seconds or minutes)

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

h(x) = -5x² + 20x + 60

a = -5b = 20c = 60

Vertex = (h, k)

k = maximum or minimum heighth = time to reach the maximum heightIf the a > 0, the graph opens upward and the parabola (graph) is at minimumIf a < 0, the graph opens downward and the parabola (graph) is at maximum

The given function has a = -5 where a < 5, therefore, it is at maximum.

Find h, the time when the function reaches its maximum height:

h = -b/2a

h = -20/2(-5)

h = -20/2(-5)

h = -20/-10

h = 2 It takes 2 (seconds) to reach the maximum height.

Find the maximum height, k:

h(x) = k  where x=h= 2.

k = -5x² + 20x + 60

k = -5(2)² + 20(2) + 60

k = -5(4) + 40 + 60

k = -20 + 100

k = 80The maximum height after 2 (time=seconds) is 80 (unit of measure = meter, feet).
