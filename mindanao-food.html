2. why is mindanao famous for their food


Because Mindanao dishes are more or less distinguishable from other regional words based on the following factors: ingredients and use of spices, cooking methods, how the dish tastes/feels in the mouth.

Pineapple, coconut vinegar (sukang maasim), ginger, garlic shoots, tamarind leaves and seeds are some of the most commonly used ingredients, while recipes like turon with Alegria sauce sinigang na dila with sili can be considered highlights. The chilli peppers may also range from triangle red chilli to blot chillies depending on what you're eating because Diwalempa Manobo tribes prefer to use these instead. Therefore it is possible that being cooked for a longer time is a standard cooking method, especially for the pork dishes. Mindanao dishes typically have an acidic taste and hot feel in the mouth due to how many ingredients contain acid - like pineapple or tamarind juice.

10. Ano ang pagkakapareho at pagkakaiba ng Sining at food delicacies ng Luzon at Mindanao.


Answer:

Luzon

Luzon, which was coined after the Tagalog word kalusunan (which means northern part), is the country’s largest island. Hence, it’s expected to have the greatest population among the three island groups. Having an area of 42,458 square miles, Luzon is further subdivided into four major areas — Northern Luzon, Central Luzon, Southern Luzon, and the National Capital Region, which is the country’s center of economic, social, cultural, and financial development.The island, which is considered to be the 15th largest island in the world, is home to tourist destinations such as Palawan, mountainous terrains, volcanoes such as Mt. Pinatubo, perfect cone-shaped Mt. Mayon, and Taal Volcano as well as numerous lakes, rivers, and plains. In terms of ethno-linguistic groups, the most prominent ones are Ilocano, Kapampangan, Bicolano, Tagalog, and Pangasinense.

Visayas

Between the regions of Luzon and Mindanao, there lies the small, scattered islands comprising Visayas — the smallest region in terms of land area. The island group of Visayas plays an important role in Philippine History as Ferdinand Magellan discovered the country during his expedition which led him to one of its small islands — Samar. According to historians, the word Visayas was named after the powerful Malayan Srivijaya Empire, which ruled over some of the Visayan islands.Visayas

Between the regions of Luzon and Mindanao, there lies the small, scattered islands comprising Visayas — the smallest region in terms of land area. The island group of Visayas plays an important role in Philippine History as Ferdinand Magellan discovered the country during his expedition which led him to one of its small islands — Samar. According to historians, the word Visayas was named after the powerful Malayan Srivijaya Empire, which ruled over some of the Visayan islands.

Mindanao

Mindanao, the second largest among the three islands and the southernmost part of the Philippines, is a main contributor of the country’s GDP in terms of agriculture, fishing, forestry, etc. Majority of Filipino Muslims live in this island group, particularly in the Autonomous Region for Muslim Mindanao (ARMM). Because it is near the island of Borneo, historians mentioned that Sultans of Mindanao became acquainted with the leaders of Borneo who introduced them to the Islam religion.While the islands of the Philippines are scattered and its geographical location can sometimes be a hindrance to the country’s development, the three major islands contribute to strengthen and preserve the Filipino traditions and cultural practices despite regional differences.
