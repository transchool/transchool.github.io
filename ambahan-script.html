Ambahans inscribed on plants in a bamboo grove [Source: Antoon Postma]

The ambahan is the traditional poetry of the Hanunuo Mangyans of Oriental Mindoro. It is usually written on bamboo in the Surat Mangyan, a centuries-old pre-Spanish script. The syllabic script and the ambahan poetry have complemented each other, contributing to their continued existence today.

Reproduced here are three extracts from the work of Antoon Postma, anthropologist and Mangyan historian, and an ambahan anthology arranged according to the Hanunuo-Mangyan lifecycle.

Answer:

Ambahan Script is a traditional script used by the Hanunoo people of the Philippines. It is used to write the Ambahan, a type of traditional poetry that is passed down orally from generation to generation. The Ambahan script is an important part of the Hanunoo culture and is used to preserve the language and traditions of the tribe.

Banig is a traditional craft in the Philippines that involves weaving together strips of dried pandan leaves to create mats, baskets, and other objects. Banig is a practical and decorative craft that has been passed down through generations in the Philippines. It is often used to make functional items such as mats and baskets, as well as decorative items such as wall hangings and table runners.

Morion Masks are traditional masks used in the Philippines during the Moriones Festival, which is held annually to commemorate the passion of Jesus Christ. These masks are made of paper mache and are painted to depict different characters from the Bible, such as Pilate and Judas. The Morion Mask is an important part of the cultural traditions of the Philippines and is used in religious rituals and celebrations.
