Coughing is the body's natural response to removing foreign objects, such as germs, viruses, dust, or irritating substances, from the respiratory tract. Even so, coughing also often indicates the presence of certain health problems, ranging from ARI, allergies, asthma, to lung cancer.

The throat and lungs normally produce little mucus or phlegm. This mucus is usually expelled through coughing and sneezing. Coughing that occurs occasionally and quickly subsides is generally still relatively normal, because it helps move phlegm which functions to keep the respiratory tract moist.

Asthma is a chronic disease of the respiratory tract characterized by shortness of breath due to inflammation and narrowing of the airways. Asthma can be suffered by all age groups, both young and old.

Asthma sufferers have more sensitive respiratory tract than normal people. When the lungs are exposed to asthma triggers, the muscles in the airways stiffen, making the airways narrow. In addition, phlegm production also increases. The combination of these conditions makes sufferers experience asthma symptoms.

Diarrhea is a disease that causes the sufferer to have frequent bowel movements with watery or watery stools. Diarrhea generally occurs due to consuming food and drink contaminated with viruses, bacteria or parasites.

Given

1. 1 kg mass with acceleration of 2.3m/s²

2. 20 N of force and 6 kg mass

3. 8 N of force and acceleration of 3.0m/s²

- Get the mass of the rock to satisfy the second formulae.

4. 5 kg mass and acceleration of 3.2m/s²

Formulae:

1. F = m x a

2. a = [tex]\frac{Force}{mass}[/tex]

3. m = [tex]\frac{Force}{Acceleration}[/tex]

   F = m x a

4. F = m x a

   a = [tex]\frac{Force}{mass}[/tex]

Solution & Answer

Note: The answers are in bold.

1. F = 1kg x 2.3m/s²

F = 2.3 N

2. a = [tex]\frac{20 N}{6 kg}[/tex]

a = 3.33... m/s²

3. M = [tex]\frac{8 N}{3 m/s^2}[/tex]

M = 2.67 kg

F = m x a

F = 2.67 kg x 4.2 m/s²

F = 11.214 N

4. F = 5 kg x 3.2 m/s²

F = 16 N

a = [tex]\frac{16 N}{2 kg}[/tex]

a = 8 m/s²

Abbreviations used:

m - mass

a - acceleration

F - force

N - Newton (Unit for force)

... - recurring (ex. 4.5555555555)
