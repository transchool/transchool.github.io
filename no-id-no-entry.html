What does the wilderness ethics means?A. Shout and cheer when you reached you destination B. Carve and leave markings to know you've been there C. Leave no trace policy D. No id no entry policy

The answer is letter B. C. Leave no trace policy. It's easy to imagine wilderness as untouchable; inviolate and distant, well away from the bustle of development and development that surrounds any area. After all, one of the reasons we explore the wilderness is to avoid the pressures of society and reconnect with nature. However, the places we call "wilderness" are much more vulnerable than they seem. Any ecosystem is a fragile equilibrium that has been built up over millennia; a single blunder, a single lost species, will tip that balance into a rapid decline. Too many visitors, no matter how much they enjoy the wild places they visit, can lead to the decline if they are not careful.

This is where the tradition of "wilderness ethics" comes into play. It is not an excuse to drive people out; everybody should be able to visit our rivers, mountains, and deserts and find happiness there. Instead, wilderness ethics aims to find a way for tourists to minimize their effect, keeping the wilderness untouched so that potential visitors will enjoy the same charm and wildness that they did. Wilderness ethics are important for outdoor experiences in the Pacific Northwest and elsewhere. Wilderness ethics, like most people who spend a lot of time in wild lands, relies "more on mindset and understanding than laws and regulations." It is a conservation ideology that seeks to keep the wilderness from being ruined by those who adore it. This is not to suggest that there aren't any laws or legislation in place. Many protected wilderness areas and National Forests have rules on how far away from trails you can lodge, how close you can spill wash water, and where fires are permitted. But, at its heart, wilderness ethics is an individual philosophy, and each traveler is free to choose their own wilderness ethics. Although intensely personal, wilderness ethics is also a communal effort; a means to preserve the wilderness wild, so that everyone will enjoy the natural beauty that gives us great pleasure. Whether by Leave No Trace or another philosophy, wilderness ethics follows one goal: to experience our world's wild places without losing them, so that they can be enjoyed by all. Remember to include others as you go on your next wilderness trek in the Pacific Northwest or anywhere your adventures lead you.

3:6:8 = x:y:16

3/x = 8/16

8x = 3(16)

x = 6

6/y = 8/16

8y = 6(16)

y = 12

16. what is the different of single entry and double entry bookkeeping​


Answer:

In a single entry system, only single entry is recorded which can be either debit or credit transaction, double entry system has a double recording method in each transaction. This means that for every debit record there is a corresponding credit entry and vice versa.

27. differentiate the journal Entry and purchase Entry​


Answer:

A journal entry is the act of keeping or making records of any transactions either economic or non-economic. Transactions are listed in an accounting journal that shows a company's debit and credit balances. The journal entry can consist of several recordings, each of which is either a debit or a credit

Purchase Credit Journal Entry is the journal entry passed by the company in the purchase journal of the date when the company purchases any inventory from the third party on the terms of credit, where the purchases account will be debited.


28. explain how keyboard entry differ from direct entry?​


Answer:

Keyboard entry is when data is entered through a keyboard whereas Direct entry does not require data to be keyed by someone. Instead the direct entry devices create machine-readable data and feed it directly into the CPU. This reduces the possibility of human error and is an economical means of data entry.
