1. 3. Consist of thin, round plates of various alloy. Ching, ching, ching is the sound you hear fromthis instrument​


the answer is cymbal

A cymbal is a common percussion instrument. Often used in pairs, cymbals consist of thin, normally round plates of various alloys. The majority of cymbals are of indefinite pitch, although small disc-shaped cymbals based on ancient designs sound a definite note (such as crotales). Cymbals are used in many ensembles ranging from the orchestra, percussion ensembles, jazz bands, heavy metal bands, and marching groups. Drum kits usually incorporate at least a crash, ride, or crash/ride, and a pair of hi-hat cymbals. A player of cymbals is known as a cymbalist.

4. Describe the following instrument 1.piri 2.ching 3.Shinobue 4.taiko 5.haegum


Answer:

1.The piri is a Korean double reed instrument, used in both the folk and classical (court) music of Korea. ... Its large reed and cylindrical bore gives it a sound mellower than that of many other types of oboe. In the typical piri, there are eight finger holes on the bamboo body.

2.Joined by a cord that runs through the center, ching are bowl-shaped, about 5 centimeters in diameter, and made of bronze alloy—iron, copper, and gold. They are struck together in a cyclical pattern to keep time and regulate the melody, and they function as the "timekeeper" of the ensemble.

3.The shinobue (kanji: 篠笛; also called takebue (kanji: 竹笛) in the context of Japanese traditional arts) is a Japanese transverse flute or fue that has a high-pitched sound. It is found in hayashi and nagauta ensembles, and plays important roles in noh and kabuki theatre music.

4.taiko, any of various Japanese forms of barrel-shaped drums with lashed or tacked heads, usually played with sticks (bachi). ... The two-headed tacked drum hung in an elaborate circular frame in court music is a gaku-daiko or tsuri-daiko.

5.The haegeum (Korean: 해금) is a traditional Korean string instrument, resembling a vertical fiddle with two strings; derived from the ancient Chinese xiqin. It has a rodlike neck, a hollow wooden soundbox, and two silk strings, and is held vertically on the knee of the performer and played with a bow.

5. One of the classics of Confucianism that is a collection of 300 poems and songs from the early Chou Dynasty. Select one: a. Ch'un Ching b. Shu Ching c. I Ching d. Shih Ching e. Li Ching


Answer: d. Shih Ching

Shih Ching (translated as "The Book of Songs" or "Classic of Poetry") is one of the classics of Confucianism that is a collection of 300 poems and songs from the early Chou Dynasty.

Sometimes, people call “Shijing Poems”, or “Book of Odes Confucius”.

Experts usually include this as a reference for their old and new Chinese poem analysis, research and studies.

**********

It is the most primitive Chinese repository of verses and it contains 300+ (or 307 to be exact) poems of folk songs and also a lot of court origins.

Court poems are more on ceremonial in its character that are designed to be sung usually at sacrifices.

However, the sacrifices are to accompany the dances, celebrations, and feasts in honour of dynastic ancestors or to adorn occasions like banquets, chases, and archeries.

Well, tradition says that it was in fact edited into its present form by Confucius himself.

They say that the book was actually assembled before, during, and even after Confucius death.

It includes fragments of works as old as Shang Dynasty dated 1766 - 1154 BCE as well as contemporary poems from the Chou feudal states. These were written or spoken by aristocratic court figures and plain folks in society.

If not the majority of the poems as oral folk art, it is clear that from the artistry of the written language, people converted Chou folk songs and court verses into poetry in written Chinese characters.

The characters used to render unpretentious direct lyrical utterances of illiterate peasants and folks that often honour them with such carefully chosen vocabulary. Thus, the heart and the soul of the folk art actually remained present however, literary subtleties were introduced.

It is apparent that the scribes who created Shih Ching were creative writers like poets and of course, not some techie tape recorders.

Hence, they clearly chose those best of what existed that they honoured it with their own style and art.

 

While folk songs are love and romantic lyrics of various kinds, complaints, satires, elegies, and even georgics, majority of the poems were composed in early Chou Dynasty also known as the pre-Confucian period.

Shih Ching predates Confucius by 3 centuries but still every so often being credited with arranging it into its current form since around 520 BC.

Hundreds of verses of poetry illustrated the proper conduct of sovereign and general rules for inculcation of propriety and even righteousness. It is believe that Confucius probably didn’t do much alteration on the original text; his agenda in it ensured that the book could survive. As he described it as “by the Odes that the mind is aroused.”

Stanzas are replete with admonitions and reproaches to the rulers overzealous for material things, power, and glory.

Therefore, the central moral lesson of this collection of poems is that a leader should rule with compassion and moderation.

20. The instrument Ching is a finger cymbal made of bronze. It is categorized as ______________. *A. AerophoneB. ChordophoneC. IdiophoneD. Membranophon​


Answer:

Idiophone

Explanation:

The ching is a Korean gong idiophone. In the past it was used as a military signaling instrument and for taech'wit'a, military processional music.

Idiophones are instruments whose own substance vibrates to produce sound (as opposed to the strings of a guitar or the air column of a flute); examples include bells, clappers, and rattles. Membranophones emit sound by the vibration of a stretched membrane; the prime examples are drums.

Examples of idiophones include the triangle, wood block, maracas, bell, and gong.

Idiophone, class of musical instruments in which a resonant solid material—such as wood, metal, or stone—vibrates to produce the initial sound. The eight basic types are concussion, friction, percussion, plucked, scraped, shaken, stamped, and stamping.

28. what is the classification of instruments ranat ek,khong wong yai,ta phon,ching,and pi nai?


Answer:

Khong wong yai

Explanation:

The khong wong yai (Thai: ฆ้องวงใหญ่, pronounced [kʰɔ́ːŋ woŋ jàj]) is a circle with gongs used in the music of Thailand. It has 16 tuned bossed gongs in a rattan frame and is played with two beaters.The player sits in the center of the circle. It is used in the piphat ensemble to provide the skeletal melody the other instruments of the elaborate ensemble. The gongs are individually tuned with beeswax under the gongs. The khong wong yai can either be played with soft beaters or hard beaters.
