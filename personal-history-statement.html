Answer:

Here is the chronological order of the persons and events in the history of the Israelites, with a brief description of each:

1. Jacob - The grandson of Abraham, Jacob and his family migrated to Egypt to escape famine and settled there, eventually becoming slaves.

2. Slavery and oppression - The Israelites were enslaved by the Egyptians and oppressed for many years.

3. Plagues - God sent ten plagues upon Egypt to convince Pharaoh to release the Israelites from slavery.

4. Exodus - Moses led the Israelites out of Egypt in a mass migration, which included the parting of the Red Sea.

5. The Burning Bush - God spoke to Moses from a burning bush and commissioned him to lead the Israelites out of Egypt.

6. Sinai Covenant - God made a covenant with the Israelites at Mount Sinai, giving them the Ten Commandments and other laws to follow.

7. Occupation of the land - Joshua led the Israelites into the Promised Land and oversaw the conquest and settlement of the land.

Note that the events described in items 2 through 4 are not necessarily in chronological order, as the exact order and duration of these events is debated by scholars. However, the overall sequence of events is generally agreed upon as described above.
