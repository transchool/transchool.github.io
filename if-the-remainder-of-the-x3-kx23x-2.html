Finding the Value of Constant k Using the Remainder Theorem:

The value of k is -2.

Solutions:

Given: x³ - kx² + 3x -2 - dividend

           x - 1 - divisor

           4 - remainder

1. Let: f(x) = x³ - kx² + 3x -2

2. According to the remainder theorem, when f(x) is divided by x - 1 then,

x - 1 = 0

x = 1

3. If x = 1 then,

f(1) = (1)³ - k(1)² + 3(1) - 2

    = 1 - k + 3 - 2

    = 4 - 2 - k

    = 2 - k

4. Find the value of k.

2 - k = 4 where 4 is the remainder

2 + (-2) -k = 4 + (-2)

-k = 2

5. Remove the negative sign of k by multiplying both sides of the equation with -1 such that,

-1 [-k = 2]

k = -2

Therefore, the value of k is -2.
