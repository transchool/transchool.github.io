1. republic act no.7394 or the consumer act​


The principal law for consumer protection in the Philippines is the Consumer Act of the Philippines (Republic Act No. 7394). ... The law anchored on the basic policy of the state to protect the interests of the consumer, promote his general welfare and to establish standards of conduct for business and industry.

Declaration of Policy. — The State shall ensure safe and good quality of food, drugs, cosmetics and devices, and regulate their production, sale, distribution and advertisement to protect the health of the consumer.

2. what is republic act 7394​


Answer:

Republic Act 7394 is more commonly known as the Consumer Act of the Philippines. This law was approved by the Senate on April 1992 for the purpose of protecting the general welfare of the consumers.

Included within this law are the following but not limited to: consumer product quality and safety, consumer products like food, drugs, cosmetics and devices, consumer hazards, protection against deceptive sales acts, consumer product and service warranties, advertising and promotion, consumer credit transaction, and National Consumers Affairs Council.

This law is to be enforced by three main government offices: Department of Health (DOH), Department of Trade and Industry (DTI) and Department of Agriculture (DA). For related questions, see the following links:
