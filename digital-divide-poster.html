1. A school collected 360 by selling tickets for a stage show. If the cost of each ticket was P8, how may tickets were sold?

What is asked: The number of the tickets that were sold.

What are given: 360 tickets and P8

What operation is to be used: Division

Number Sentence: 360 ÷ 8 = N

Answer: The tickets that were sold are 45.

2. Henry bought twice the number of balloons that Andrew bought, to decorate their houses

for Christmas. How many balloons did Andrew buy, if Henry bought 262 balloons?

What is asked: The number of balloons that Andrew bought.

What are given: 262 balloons and 2 (twice)

What operation is to be used: Division

Number Sentence: 262 ÷ 2 = N

Answer: 131 balloons that Andrew had bought.

3. Marissa wanted to decorate her house with beautiful posters. She bought 36 posters altogether. How many can she paste in each of the 12 rooms?

What is asked: The number of poster that she can paste.

What are given: 36 posters and 12 rooms

What operation is to be used: Division

Number Sentence: 36 ÷ 12 = N

Answer: The posters that Marissa can paste in 12 rooms are 3 posters.

4.Francis wrote a novel having 12 lines in each page. Total number of lines in the complete

novel is 6600. Find the number of pages in Francis's novel.

What is asked: The number of pages in Francis's novel.

What are given: 12 lines and total number of lines 6600.

What operation is to be used: Division

Number Sentence: 6600 ÷ 12 = N

Answer: There are 550 pages in the novel of Francis.

5.Lydia went shopping along with three of her friends. In a store they bought a box with 92 shades of nail polish. If they divided them evenly, then how many would each of them get?

What is asked: The number of the shades of nail polish that each of them would get.

What are given: 92 shades of nail polish and 4 (Lydia and her three friends).

What operation is to be used: Division

Number Sentence: 92 ÷ 4 = N

Answer: The four of them would get 23 shades of nail polish each.
