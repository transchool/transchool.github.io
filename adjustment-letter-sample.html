2. what is adjustment letter​


Answer:

An adjustment letter or a claim adjustment letter is a written response from a representative of a business or agency to a customer's claim letter. It explains how a problem with a product or service may (or may not) be resolved.

"An adjustment letter should begin with a positive statement, expressing sympathy and understanding. Near the start, it should let the reader know what is being done, and this news, good or bad, should be followed by an explanation. The letter should end with another positive statement, reaffirming the company's good intentions and the value of its products, but  never referring to the original problem.

MICROSCOPE

Answer:

Indicate the order of the steps in Focusing the Microscope:

(1) Adjust the stage.

(2) Adjust the coarse adjustment knob.​

(3) Adjust the fine adjustment knob.

Firstly, Adjust the stage which is look at the stage from the side, place the specimen over the hole in the stage. Secondly, Adjust the coarse adjustment knob which is look the eyepiece and slowly makes it up the objective lens - the coarse adjustment knob until the object becomes focus. Thirdly, Adjust the fine adjustment knob which is to focus the object into the next highest power look, the side of the stage then slowly rotate the object lens into (10x) and use the fine adjustment knob to make your object focus.

29. it is for adjusting the spacing between unevenly shaped letters in large font size​


Answer

Kerning is the adjusting of the space between unevenly shaped letters in font sizes. It also refers to the amount of space between each character that is typed in.

Explanation

The function of Kerning is to make the spacing between characters look evenly distributed. Although, not all space between characters is evenly distributed. For example, the characters 'VA' has a tighter kerning than 'db'. This shows that 'VA' are closer to each other compared to 'db'. You can see the gap by adding a space between 'V A' and 'db', which makes it 'V A' look further than b'.
