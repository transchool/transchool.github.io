Combine variation occurs when two or more different traits are inherited by an organism from its parents. These traits can be dominant or recessive, and they can interact in various ways to produce a range of different combinations. This is what gives rise to the incredible diversity of life on Earth.

For example, when two parents with different eye colors have a child, the child may inherit one of the eye colors from each parent, resulting in a new combination of eye colors. This is an example of combine variation at work.

Combine variation is important for understanding how traits are inherited and how they can be predicted and controlled. By studying combine variation, scientists can learn more about the genetic basis of traits and how they can be passed on from one generation to the next.
