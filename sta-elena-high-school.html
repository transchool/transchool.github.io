First case

Speed = distance / time

distance = speed x time

= 30 x 0.5 ---- (30 min = 0.5 hour)

= 15 km ---- (i)

Second case

speed = 40 km h^{-1}h

−1

distance = 40 km x 1

= 40 km ---- (ii)

Total distance = 15 km + 40 km

= 55 km

(c) average speed = total distance travelled/total time taken

24. Elena lives 200 m from her school. How long will it take her to reach her school if she walks at a rate of 2 m/s?​


[tex]\blue{••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••}[/tex]

[tex]\large\boxed{\mathbb\purple{QUESTION \: :}}[/tex]

Elena lives 200 m from her school. How long will it take her to reach her school if she walks at a rate of 2 m/s?

[tex]\blue{••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••}[/tex]

[tex]\large\boxed{\mathbb\orange{ANSWER \: :}}[/tex]

100 seconds

[tex]\blue{••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••}[/tex]

[tex]\large\boxed{\mathbb\green{SOLUTION \: :}}[/tex]

Given:

distance (d) = 200 mspeed (s) = 2 m/s

Formula:

time (t) = [tex] \frac{d}{s} [/tex]

» Substitute d as 200 and s as 2 based on the given formula.

t = [tex] \frac{200}{2} [/tex]t = 100

Therefore, it will take her 100 seconds to reach the school.
