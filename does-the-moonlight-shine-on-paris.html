Answer:

The Moon doesn't emit its own light. The brightness we perceive from the Moon is actually sunlight reflected off from the surface of the moon.

Explanation:

The moon shines because sunlight reflects off its surface. Despite the fact that it appears to emit light brightly at times, the moon only reflects 3 to 12 percent of the sunlight that reaches it.

The brightness of the moon as seen from Earth varies depending on where it is in its orbit around the planet. The moon orbits Earth once every 29.5 days, and the sun illuminates it from various angles along its circumnavigation.

The changing phases of the moon are caused by the moon's movement around the Earth, as well as the Earth's rotating around the sun. Only half of the moon's surface faces the sun at any given point in its orbit around the Earth, and so only half of the moon is visible. The other side of the surface is in shadow and faces away from the sun.
