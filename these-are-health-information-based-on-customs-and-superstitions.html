Individual ideas and experiences drive superstitions in this way, which explains why they are typically irrational and contradict scientific knowledge.

Psychologists who have looked into the origins of superstitions have discovered that they stem from the belief that there is a link between two unrelated events that happen at the same time. For example, there's a popular belief that charms provide good luck or protect you from bad luck.
