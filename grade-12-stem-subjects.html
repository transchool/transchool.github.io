The purpose of quantitative research is to attain greater knowledge and understanding of the social world. Researchers use quantitative methods to observe situations or events that affect people. Quantitative research produces objective data that can be clearly communicated through statistics and numbers.

Explanation:

24. Statement of the problem about The difference between face to face and modular learning of grade 12 STEM Students


The problem under investigation is the difference in the learning outcomes of grade 12 STEM students who underwent face-to-face instruction versus those who underwent modular instruction. The study aims to determine if there is a significant difference in the academic performance, motivation, and engagement levels between the two modes of instruction. With the increasing popularity of modular learning, it is crucial to understand its impact on student learning, especially in the STEM field where hands-on and practical applications are crucial. The results of this study can provide insights into the effectiveness of modular learning as a pedagogical approach in the STEM curriculum.

Answer:

The problem that this study aims to address is the difference between face-to-face and modular learning for Grade 12 STEM students. This study seeks to determine the impact of these two forms of learning on the academic performance and satisfaction of students in STEM subjects.

Specifically, the following questions need to be answered:

How does the academic performance of Grade 12 STEM students differ between face-to-face and modular learning?

What is the level of student satisfaction with each form of learning?

Are there any differences in the students' motivation and engagement in STEM subjects between face-to-face and modular learning?

26. RESEARCH SUBJECT PO ITO. The impact of online gaming to grade 11 STEM students . What are the Scopes and Delimitation:


Answer:

THE IMPACT OF ONLINE GAMING

In line with social development, online games also give effect to cognitive development, for instance, people who play a game online have a strong memory, better problem-solving skills, capability to synchronize their brains, hands and their eyes, and they have a high comprehension skill. But spending too much time on online games every week tend to suffer from worsened learning ability, concentration problems, poor academic performance, and decreased interactions with other people.

Explanation:

SCOPE

The scope of the study is for finding effects of playing online games to the academic performance of the students of 11 STEM.

DELIMITATION

Set time limits for play and stick to them.

Keep phones and other gadgets out of the bedroom so you won’t play into the night.

Do other activities every day, including exercise. This will lower the health risks of sitting and playing for long stretches of time.
