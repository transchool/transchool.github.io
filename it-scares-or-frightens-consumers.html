Propaganda Techniques

The practice of influencing human behavior through the manipulation of representations is known as propaganda in the broadest sense. Speaking, writing, painting, and music are all acceptable forms of representation. Manipulation might be planned or random, intentional or unconscious, and driven by politics or society.

The primary goal of propaganda is to persuade or influence an audience to support a particular agenda. It might not be objective, might present data in a biased manner to support a certain interpretation or synthesis, might employ strong language to elicit an emotional rather than a logical response.

Here is the answer to each statement:

1. Technical Jargon

This confuses or deceives the audience or consumers by using obscure or extremely technical jargon. It uses obscure or highly technical jargon to wow, confound, or trick customers. Use of unnecessary jargon designed to impress your audience rather than inform them.

2. Fear Appeals

Customers are either scared or frightened into using the goods and/or service or away from it. The appeal to fear fallacy can place when exaggerated or excessively used fear is used to convince others to embrace a notion or adopt a behavior. Fear appeals are advertisements that appeal to our emotions rather than just our rational or informational needs, and they aim to use our fear of an issue to motivate us to act.

3. Bandwagon

Because everyone is doing it, you are convinced to use a product or participate in an activity. Because it focuses on persuading people to act or think a certain way because "everyone else is doing it or everyone else thinks this, the bandwagon fallacy is also known as the appeal to common belief or the appeal to the masses.

4.Gliterring Generalities

It employs both beautiful and repulsive noises to entice or repel people from an object. These words are used to mislead us into approving and accepting things without carefully considering the evidence.

5. Name calling

This persuades you to avail the product or service because of reverse idea you heard about persons or things. a style of discussion in which someone or something is disparaged by using offensive or disparaging terms.

Many academic fields, including anthropology, child psychology, and political science, have explored this phenomena. Rhetoricians and a number of other disciplines also study it.

22. what is the word cline for scared, afraid, and frightened in their order intensity?​


Explanation:

A group of terms or ideas arranged according to their strength or degree are referred to as a cline. The terms terrified, afraid, and frightened in this situation would be in this order of increasing intensity:

Frightened, afraid, or terrified

The mildest of the three, "scared," suggests a generalized disquiet or fear. "Afraid" would be a stronger word, denoting a more focused and real dread. The strongest word would be "frightened," which denotes a profound and overpowering terror.
