4. during the pandemic there are 400 passengers taking a bus route every day in b days there are 2000 passengers find b?algebraic equation and the solution


PROBLEM:

During the pandemic there are 400 passengers taking a bus route every day. In b days there are 2000 passengers, find b.

SOLUTION:

There are 400 passengers per day. Thus, a ratio of one is to four hundred. While, in b days, there are 2000 passengers with a ratio of b is to 2000.

• We'll apply what we have learned from the lesson about ratio and proportion.

[tex] \large \boxed{\begin{array}{c} \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \boxed{\tt \frac{1}{400} = \frac{b}{2000} }\\ \tt 1(2000) = 400b \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \tt 2000 = 400b \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \tt b = \frac{2000}{400} \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \boxed{ \tt b = 5}\end{array}}[/tex]

• Notice that we applied the cross-multiplication process.

• Our algebraic equation is 1/400 = b/2000 or simply, b = 2000/400.

ANSWER:

• In five days, there will be 2000 passengers.

7. during feast there is a carnival in our place audrey like to ride on a carousel the carousel takes about of 2/5 an hour to finish if there are 8 carousel horse and 32 kids in front of audrey how long does audrey need to wait befor it is her turn to ride the carousel?​


Audrey needs to wait for 96 minutes or 1 hour and 36 minutes before her turn to ride the carousel.

Step-by-step explanation:

Word Problem

The problem above is asking for how long Audrey needs to wait before her turn to ride the carousel. There are three steps to find the answer.

Find how long the carousel takes to finish by getting the ⅖ of an hour.Find how many rides to make before Audrey's turn by dividing the number of kids by the number of carousel horses.Find how long Audrey needs to wait by multiplying the length of each ride by the number of rides to make.

Given:

length of each ride - ⅖ of an hour or ⅖ of 60

number of kids before Audrey - 32

number of carousel horses - 8

Solution:

Step 1: The word of means to multiply, ⅖ of 60.

⅖ × 60 = ‌120⁄5‌ or 24

The carousel takes 24 minutes to finish.

Step 2: Divide the number of kids by the number of carousel horses.

32 ÷ 8 = 4

4 carousel rides before Audrey's turn.

Step 3: Multiply the length of each ride by the number of rides to make.

24 × 4 = 96 mins

Since there are 60 minutes in one hour, 96 minutes is equal to 1 hour and 36 minutes.

Final Answer:

96 minutes or 1 hour and 36 minutes

11. a bus left a bus station in 5 pm and travels at the rate of 50 kph. Another bus on the same route left the bus station 1 hour later and overtakes the first bus at 7 pm. What is the rate or speed of the second bus? If you answer this, thank you for it

100kmph

Step-by-step explanation:

v1 = 50 kph ; v2 = ? kph ; t1 = 2 h ; t2 = 1 h

Let v1 = the speed of bus 1 and let v2 = the speed of bus 2

Let t1 = the travel time of bus 1 and let t2 = the travel time of bus 2

Solving for the distance traveled by bus 1

distance = speed * time

distance = v1 * t1 = 50 kph * 2 h = 100 km

The distance traveled by bus 2 is also 100 km at the time of overtaking bus 1

Solving for the speed of bus 2

speed_bus 2 = distance / time of travel

speed_bus 2 = 100 km / 1 h

speed_bus 2 = 100 km/h

The rate of the second bus is 100 km/h.

Let S1 be the speed of the first bus, and S2 be the speed of 2nd bus.

Let's solve the distance traveled by the 1st bus:

Distance = Speed * time

Distance = S1 * Time = 50kph * 2h = 100kph

The second bus overtook the first bus at the time that the first bus traveled 100 km.

Let's solve the speed of the second bus:

2nd bus = distance/travel of time

              = 100 km/1 hour

Thus, the rate of the second bus is 100km per hour (100km/h)

28. ever december an amusement park is constructed in sm san jose del monte city carousel is the most favorite ride of the kids there the diameter of the carousel is 20.5 m what is the area of carousel??​


Answer:

The formula of the area of a circle, which was the shape of the carousel:

a=πr^2

In the problem, the diameter of the carousel is 20.5m. To find its radius, we need to get the half of the given diameter:

20.5/2= 10.25m

Therefore, the radius of the carousel is 10.25m.

To solve for the area, we will use the formula a=πr^2 and substitute the given values.

a=πr^2

a=π(10.25^2)

a=π(105.0625)

a=329.89625m^2

Therefore, the area of the carousel is 329.89625m^2 (read as 329.89625 square meters).
