13. How many ways can you select 5 winners from 8 contestants?​


[tex] \large\underline \mathcal{{QUESTION:}}[/tex]

How many ways can you select 5 winners from 8 contestants?

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline \mathcal{{SOLUTION:}}[/tex]

Using the Combination formula:

Given that n=8 and r=5

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\sf{C(n,r)=\frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{C(8,5)=\frac{8!}{5!(8-5)!}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{C(8,5)=\frac{8!}{5!3!}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{C(8,5)=\frac{8×7×6×5×4×3×2×1}{5×4×3×2×1×3×2×1}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{C(8,5)=\frac{8×7×\cancel{6}×\cancel{5×4×3×2×1}}{\cancel{5×4×3×2×1}×\cancel{3×2×1}}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{C(8,5)=8×7}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{C(8,5)=56}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline \mathcal{{ANSWER:}}[/tex]

There are 56 ways

k2 = 9 ways for the second person to be chosen from the remaining people(10–1).

18. among the 9 contestants there are 15,120 ways to choose the winners how many winners were declared? please help me to get the answer​


[tex] \large\underline \bold{{SOLUTION}}[/tex]

[tex] \textrm{\small{\underline{Using the permutation formula}}}[/tex]

[tex]\longmapsto\rm{\small{P(n,r)= \frac{n!}{(n-r)!}}}[/tex]

[tex]\longmapsto\rm{\small{15,120= \frac{9!}{(9-r)!}}}[/tex]

[tex]\textrm{\small{\underline{Multiply the LCD to isolate the rationals}}}[/tex]

[tex]\longmapsto\rm{\small{(9-r)! (15,120)=9!}}[/tex]

[tex]\textrm{\small{\underline{Expand 15,120 and 9!}}}[/tex]

[tex]\rm{\scriptsize{(9-r)!(9×8×7×6×5)=(9×8×7×6×5×4×3×2×1)}}[/tex]

[tex]\textrm{\small{\underline{Canceling the possible factors}}}[/tex]

[tex]\rm{\scriptsize{(9-r)!(\cancel{9×8×7×6×5})=(\cancel{9×8×7×6×5}×4×3×2×1)}}[/tex]

[tex]\textrm{\small{\underline{We will get:}}}[/tex]

[tex]\longmapsto\rm{\small{(9-r)!=4×3×2×1}}[/tex]

[tex]\textrm{\small{\underline{Notice that 4×3×2×1 = 4!}}}[/tex]

[tex]\longmapsto\rm{\small{(9-r)!=4!}}[/tex]

[tex]\textrm{\small{\underline{Canceling the factorial sign , and solving for r}}}[/tex]

[tex]\longmapsto\rm{\small{(9-r)=4}}[/tex]

[tex]\longmapsto\rm{\small{9-4=r}}[/tex]

[tex]\longmapsto\textrm{\small{\boxed{5=\rm{r}}}}[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{ \bold{ANSWER}}[/tex]

[tex]\textsf{5 contestants}[/tex]

22. what measure of central tendency is best to use to identify the winner in a photo-liking contest a. mean b.median c.mode​


Answer:

Skewed Distributions and the Mean and Median

However, in this situation, the mean is widely preferred as the best measure of central tendency because it is the measure that includes all the values in the data set for its calculation, and any change in any of the scores will affect the value of the mean.

Step-by-step explanation:

To calculate the mean, add all your data and divide the result by the number of data. For example, if you had the number set of 3, 4, 5 and 6, you would calculate the mean by adding the numbers, which have the sum of 18. Divide 18 by 4 (the amount of numbers in your set), which results in 4.5, the mean of the set.
