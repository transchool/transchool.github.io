We are finding the point from the car to the tower and the opposite and adjacent sides are given, so we will use the tangent function.

a tan 60 = 150

= [tex]( \frac{x1+x2}{2} , \frac{y1+y2}{2} )[/tex]
x1 = -5    x2 = 9
y1 = -3    y2 = 13

Midpoint = [tex]( \frac{4}{2} , \frac{10}{2} )[/tex]

Midpoint = (2, 5)

The coordinates of the point where the new tower will be placed is at (2,5).

24. From a point, 80 m from the base of a tower, the angle of elevation to the top of the tower is 28°. How tall is the tower?


Let the height of the tower be x.

We can solve this problem using one of the trigonometric ratios (SohCahToa). Since the missing value is opposite to the angle, and the adjacent length is given, we can use tangent.

tan θ = opposite/adjacent

tan 28˚ = x/80m

(tan 28˚)(80m) = x

x = 42.53675453...m

x ≈ 42.54 meters
