The National Achievement Test (NAT) for Grade 12 students in the Philippines will be administered on January 30th and 31st, according to a memorandum released by Education Undersecretary Epimaco Densing. The test, which is designed to be progressive in nature, will measure the performance of students in various levels of skills through a multiple-choice, paper-based format. The results will be used to improve the implementation of the curriculum and for evidence-based decision-making. The test will cover 21st-century skills and primary learning topics in senior high school, including languages, humanities, communication, mathematics, science, social science, and philosophy.
