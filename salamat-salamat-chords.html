1. What is chord 5 of C major? What is chord 4 of F major? What is chord 5 of G major? Help po salamat :-) Nonsense report ​


Answer:

5 of c minor

The C major chord V is the G major chord, and contains the notes G, B, and D. This dominant chord's root / starting note is the 5th note (or scale degree) of the C major scale. The roman numeral for number 5 is 'V' and is used to indicate this is the 5th triad chord in the scale.

4 of f major

The F major chord IV is the Bb major chord, and contains the notes Bb, D, and F. This subdominant chord's root / starting note is the 4th note (or scale degree) of the F major scale. The roman numeral for number 4 is 'IV' and is used to indicate this is the 4th triad chord in the scale.

5 of g major

Chord identification

The G major chord V is the D major chord, and contains the notes D, F#, and A. This dominant chord's root / starting note is the 5th note (or scale degree) of the G major scale. The roman numeral for number 5 is 'V' and is used to indicate this is the 5th triad chord in the scale

4. Which chords are major chords and minor chords ​


ANSWER

MAJOR CHORDS V.S MINOR CHORDS

•A major chord has what's called a “natural third.” It's the third degree of the chord's respective major scale.

•A minor chord has what's called either a “minor third” or a “flat third.” It's the third degree of the chord's respective minor scale.

1.To find a minor chord, start by building a major chord. Do this by identifying the 1st, 3rd, and 5th notes in the scale. To get a minor chord, simply move the 3rd note down the fretboard by a half step, by one fret. For example, in the C major scale, the notes are C, E and G

[tex]\underline{\mathbb{PROBLEM:}}[/tex]

In the given circles, chords, secants and tangents are drawn with the indicated measurements. Find the value of x.

[tex]\red{••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••}[/tex]

[tex]\underline{\mathbb{ANSWERS:}}[/tex]

[tex]\qquad\LARGE\rm» \:\: 1. \:\green{x=14\:units}[/tex]

[tex]\qquad\LARGE\rm» \:\: 2. \:\green{x=4\:units}[/tex]

[tex]\qquad\LARGE\rm» \:\: 3. \:\green{x=6\:units}[/tex]

[tex]\red{••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••}[/tex]

[tex]\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTIONS:}}[/tex]

#1. Apply the Chord-Chord Power Theorem to solve the value of x.

[tex](2)(x) = (4)(7)[/tex]

[tex]2x = 28[/tex]

[tex] \frac{ \cancel2x}{ \cancel2} = \frac{28}{2} \\ [/tex]

[tex]x = 14[/tex]

[tex]\therefore[/tex] The length of the segment x is 14 units.

[tex]\rm[/tex]

#15. Apply the Secant-Secant Power Theorem to solve the value of x.

[tex](3)(12 + 3) = (5)(x + 5)[/tex]

[tex](3)(15) = (5)(x + 5)[/tex]

[tex]45= 5x + 25[/tex]

[tex]45 - 25= 5x[/tex]

[tex]20 = 5x[/tex]

[tex] \frac{20}{5} = \frac{ \cancel5x}{ \cancel5} \\[/tex]

[tex]4 = x[/tex]

[tex]\therefore[/tex] The length of the segment x is 4 units.

[tex]\rm[/tex]

#3. Apply the Tangent-Secant Power Theorem to solve the value of x.

[tex] {x}^{2} = (4)(4 + 5)[/tex]

[tex] {x}^{2} = (4)(9)[/tex]

[tex] {x}^{2} = 36[/tex]

[tex] \sqrt{ {x}^{2} } = \sqrt{36} [/tex]

[tex]x = 6[/tex]

[tex]\therefore[/tex] The length of the segment x is 6 units.
