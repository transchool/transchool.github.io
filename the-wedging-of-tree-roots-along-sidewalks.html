Weathering

Weathering refers to the group of destructive forces that change the physical and chemical character of rock near the earth's surface. There are two types of weathering. Several factors cause these types of weathering. Humans are included in these factors. Weathering causes soil formation.

Answers to the Multiple Choice Questions:mechanicalchemicalmechanicalmechanicalchemical

Two Types of Weathering:mechanicalchemical

Mechanical Weathering is the breaking down of rocks into smaller pieces. The change in the rock is physical with little or no chemical change.  

Examples:

frost actionabrasionpressure release

Chemical Weathering is the decomposition of rock from exposure to water and atmosphere gases including carbon dioxide, oxygen, and water vapor.

Examples:

rustingacid breakdownsolution weathering

15. the wedging of tree roots


Answer:

Biological Activity/Root Wedging:

Explanation:

Weathering processes can happen due to the activity of living organisms. Burrowing animals can break rocks and stir sediments causing physical weathering. Animals that burrow can also bring fresh material to the surface where physical and chemical weathering can work more efficiently. Plant roots in search of nutrients in water grow into fractures. As the roots grow they wedge the rock apart similar to the frost wedging process. This is called root wedging. During root growth, organic acids can form contributing to chemical weathering.
