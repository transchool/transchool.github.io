4. a rectangular shed with an area of 576m²was constracted on marks backyard. if the length of the shed is 4 times the width.question1.what is the measurement of length and width of the shed?2.what is the perimeter of the shed​


It is given that:

→ A rectangular shed of area 576 m² needs to be constructed.

→ The length of the shed is 4 times that of the width.

What needs to be found?

→ The measures of the length and width of the shed.

→ The perimeter of the shed.

Solution:

We know that:

[tex]\sf{\implies\:Area\:of\:a\:rectangle=lb}[/tex]

Here, it is given that the area is 576 m² and the length is 4 times the width of the shed.

Considering the width as x and length as 4x, we can form an equation as follows:

[tex]\sf{4x\times\:x=576\:m^2}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{4x^2=576\:m^2}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{x^2=\dfrac{576}{4}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{x^2=144}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{x=\sqrt{144}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{x=12}[/tex]

So, breadth = 12 cm

Length = 4x = 4 x 12 = 48 cm

1. What is the measurement of length and width of the shed?

Length = 48 cm

Breadth = 12 cm

2. What is the perimeter of the shed?

Perimeter of the shed = 2 (l + b)

Perimeter = 2 (48 + 12)

Perimeter = 2 x 60

Perimeter = 120 m
