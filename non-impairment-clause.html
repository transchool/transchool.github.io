25. An adjective clause that gives essential information about the noun is, Select one: a. non-restrictive clause b. none of the choices c. restrictive clause d. both clauses


There are types of information given by the adjective clause.  Sometimes, there are essential information dependent of the noun.  This type of clause is called a restrictive adjective clause, so the answer is letter a. This means that this is an important part of the sentence, especially, in writing, for essential adjective clauses do not require commas.  

I won’t be discussing much about the adjective clause, but to give you a review, here the link https://brainly.ph/question/93128

However, we will be discussing about another type of adjective clause that is less or not essential part of the sentence.

Here is an example to describe what a restrictive adjective clause (essential adjective clause) is:

Sentence A: Vegetables, which most of the time children do not eat, are very nutritious.

Sentence B: The vegetable salad which I ate last night was so delicious.

As you have noticed, Sentence A requires a comma before the adjective clause while Sentence B does not.  Why is that so?

 

Let’s look at Sentence A.  If you remove the adjective clause in this sentence, it still gives you the whole idea or the whole essence of the sentence.  Whether most children do or do not eat vegetables, they are still nutritious.  

Vegetables, which most of the time children do not eat, are very nutritious.

However, if you remove the adjective clause in this Sentence B, it doesn’t give you enough information about what kind of vegetable salad “was so delicious.” (Well, vegetable salad tastes delicious for some though.)

The vegetable salad which I ate last night was so delicious.

This sentence contains an example of a restrictive adjective clause.  

You will also see another example of restrictive (essential) adjective clause in this link https://brainly.ph/question/1443770

By the way, you should add a relative pronoun to your sentence when writing adjective clause examples. In this link, you will see examples of relative pronouns, https://brainly.ph/question/87419

We are now done discussing about the restrictive adjective clause.  Going back to Sentence A, the adjective clause here in this example is not essential to the meaning of the sentence.

Vegetables, which most of the time children do not eat, are very nutritious.

This is the second type of adjective clause, and it contains an example of a non-restrictive adjective clause.  

Take some time to answer some adjective clause exercises.  But here’s one.  Choose whether each sentence contains either restrictive or non-restrictive adjective clause.  

1. John, who was the food server, did not see the ants crawling on the desserts. (Restrictive or Non-Restrictive)

2. I want a cat that is so clean and white.  (Restrictive or Non-Restrictive)

3. My aunt does remember the day that I was born. (Restrictive or Non-Restrictive)

4. Carl, who is not so good at Math, is cheating on the test. (Restrictive or Non-Restrictive)

5. The man who took my phone is now in jail. (Restrictive or Non-Restrictive)

Answers: 1) Non-Restrictive, 2) Restrictive, 3) Restrictive, 4) Non-Restrictive, and 5) Restrictive

In summary, there are two types of clause given by the adjective clause.  One is restrictive adjective clause and the other one is non-restrictive adjective clause.  A restrictive adjective clause is essential to the meaning of the sentence.  It does not require a comma before the relative pronoun. A non-restrictive adjective clause is less or not essential to the meaning of the sentence.  It does require a comma before the relative pronoun.  This is to be reminded that it (the proper punctuation) is important when you put your sentences into writing.
