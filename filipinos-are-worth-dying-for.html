The Filipino is worth dying for.” is a statement made by the late Benigno “Ninoy” Aquino - a political opponent of then President Ferdinand Marcos. Mr. Aquino had been in political exile in the U.S. for some years and was concerned about what was occurring in the Philippines - corruption, human rights violations, poverty and so on. Mr. Aquino decided to return to the Philippines in August, 1983 even after being warned by then First Lady Imelda Marcos that he should not return as some people wanted him dead. Mr. Aquino had apparently made the above statement before embarking on the fateful flight to the Philippines where he was assassinated after descending from the plane onto the tarmac.
