10. provides for the inclusion of environmental education programs in various national institution


Answer:

The “National Environmental Awareness and Education Act of 2008” or Republic Act No. 9512 is the act to promote environmental awareness through environmental education and for other purposes.

This began during the second regular session of the 14th Congress on July 28, 2008 and was passed by the Senate and the House of Representatives on October 8, 2008.

It was signed by Manny Villar during his term as the President of the Senate and  Prospero C. Nograles, Secretary General of the House of Representative on December 12, 2008. The Act was approved by the former President of the Philippines, Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo.

Explanation:

What is RA 9512?

1. The Act shall be known as the “National Environmental Awareness and Education Act of 2008”.

2. The declaration of this policy is to be aligned with the State’s policy to protect and advance the right of the people to a balanced and healthy ecology according to the harmony of nature. It recognizes the role and involvement of the youth in nation building and the role of education to the following aspects:

● promote patriotism and nationalism

● accelerate social progress

● promote total human liberation and development

● promote national awareness on the importance of the natural resources in economic growth

● raise awareness of the importance of environmental conservation and ecological balance towards a sustainable development

3. The following government agencies are involved in the integration of the environmental education:

● Department of Education (DepEd)

● Commission on Higher Education (CHED)

● Technical Education and Skills Development Authority (TESDA)

● Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD)

● Department of Environment and Natural Resources (DENR)

● Department of Science and Technology (DOST)

The environmental education includes all environmental concepts and principles, laws, the state of international and local environment, the best practices, threats, and its impact on human well-being, responsibility of the citizens, conservation, protection and rehabilitation of natural resources, and sustainable development both on the theoretical and practical aspects.

Through the programs and activities conducted in relation to the environmental education, the policy aims for the full understanding, cooperation, and involvement of all the citizens in taking care of the environment. This aims to make the citizens realize that they are accountable and responsible for the effects of what they do to the environment and how these can also affect human well-being in return. It aims for a lifestyle with a raised awareness to create a relevant change in the environment.

4.  The involvement of the youth will also be evident through the environmental activities and awareness programs in the National Service Training Program (NSTP) under RA 9163, as a requirement of the Civic Welfare Training Service (CWTS) for all baccalaureate degree courses and vocational courses with a curriculum of at least two (2) years.  

5. In pursuance to the policy set forth in this act, the “Environmental Awareness Month” is celebrated during the month of November every year.

Answer:

The first documented Catholic Mass in the Philippines was held on March 31, 1521, Easter Sunday. It was conducted by Father Pedro de Valderrama of Ferdinand Magellan's expedition along the shores of what was referred to in the journals of Antonio Pigafetta as "Mazaua".

Today, this site is widely believed by many historians and the government to be Limasawa off the tip of Southern Leyte,[1][2] However, until at least the 19th century, the prevailing belief was that the first mass was held in Butuan.[3] This belief is maintained by some, who assert that the first mass was instead held at Masao, Butuan.[4]

To end the conflict for the issue about the first mass, the National Historical Commission of the Philippines (NHCP) panel adapted the recommendation[5] and unanimously agreed that the evidence and arguments presented by the pro-Butuan advocates are not sufficient and convincing enough to warrant the repeal or reversal of the ruling on the case by the National Historical Institute (the NHCP's forerunner). It is further strengthen by the evidence that it was only after 22 years, in 1543—when a Spanish expedition led by Ruy López de Villalobos landed in Mindanao.[6]

A lady physician is now taking on the cudgels of steering NKTI to continue its vision-mission as a world class renowned transplant Institute. She carries the distinction as the first lady Executive Director who specializes in renal pathology. Dr. Aileen Riego - Javier was sworn in by Health Secretary Enrique T. Ona last November of 2010 making her the fourth director of the Institute.

Dr. Javier is an alumna of the University of the Philippines finishing degrees in Bachelor of Science and in medicine after which taking up post-graduate training in pathology at the Philippine General Hospital (UP-PGH). As a fellow, she trained at the General and Renal Pathology of the University of Singapore and likewise at the Pediatric Pathology at the Babies’ Hospital, Columbia Presbyterian Medical Center in New York.

The pleasant and soft spoken Executive Director pioneered renal pathology in the Philippines. She is the president of the Philippine Blood Coordinating Council and the Philippine Society of Pathologists. Dr. Javier helped set up the Anatomic Pathology Division of three specialty centers to which she has been part of notably Lungsod ng Kabataan, the Lung Center of the Philippines and that of NKTI. The Institute uses an electron microscope that gives accurate diagnosis of biopsies not only for the hospital’s use but for others as well too. Dr. Javier stresses that pathology is decisive and fundamental in many areas of medical care. Doctors are dependent on the pathologist’s diagnosis of biopsies.

Moreover, just this year, Dr. Javier was a finalist in the Government Service Category of the l9th Dr. Jose P. Rizal Memorial Awards. This award selects outstanding Filipino physicians whose life and work embody the ideals of our national hero: in the conduct of their practice, in the way they live life reflective of Rizal’s love for God and country, and in the selfless service to fellowmen, especially the poor and the underprivileged. Dr. Javier received a medallion and a P150,000.00 worth of medicines from Unilab to be donated to a charitable institution of one’s choice. To be commended as a finalist in the search is very significant to her considering how she emulates the character and attitude of our national hero in her dealings with colleagues, employees and patients of the Institute.

In her assumption of the challenges of being the new Executive Director, we wish her all the success in her endeavors in the pursuit of medical excellence for NKTI. Congratulations!
