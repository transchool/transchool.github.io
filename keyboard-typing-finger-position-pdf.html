Tips for practice typing 10 finger

1. Place Your Fingers Correctly

Getting used to not looking at the keyboard is also the key to fluent 10 finger typing. You can focus your gaze on the screen while you're typing. Don't be afraid to make mistakes or typos.

Over time, you will get used to it. Inadvertently your fingers will get used to or automatically position on the correct button.

Another thing you should pay attention to is the sitting position. Make sure you sit in an upright position and adjust the position of your head. Head-to-screen distance should be 45 to 75 cm and slightly higher.

Several Tips for fast typing

1.Nothing Is Stopping You

To start a good practice, make sure when learning to type with 10 fingers there are no distractions that can block you whether it's objects around your keyboard or. Other annoying things. Make sure you are free from internal and external distractions.

2. Your Fingers Must Be In The Right Position

The layout so you have to really fit when you want to type using 10 fingers. The general layout for starting typing with 10 fingers is:

The left index finger is placed on the letter F The left middle finger is placed on the letter D The left ring finger is placed on the letter S The left little finger is placed on the letter A

As for the layout of our right hand fingers are:

The right index finger is placed on the letter J The right middle finger is placed on the letter K The right ring finger is placed on the letter L The right little finger is placed on the character ",

The left and right thumbs should be placed on the space key

3. Don't Try To Be Quick, But Be Precise

When you learn to type with 10 fingers, of course you want to be able to type quickly. Don't be swayed by this thought. what you should focus on is trying to type correctly first. Because if you can type correctly, your speed will increase by itself.

4. Speak The Letters Out Loud As You Type Them

Every time you learn a new letter, your brain has to make "relationships" between the letters and the movements to make when typing. Saying letters out loud as you type speeds up learning this method, because you not only see the letter you want to type appear on the screen (visual feedback), but also by saying the letters out loud you are providing good auditory feedback. It may seem strange at first, but this method can make a significant impact.
