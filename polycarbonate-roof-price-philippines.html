2. what is the difference between polyurethane and polycarbonate?


  Polyurethane refers to a thermoplastic polymer containing the group NHCOO: used for padding and insulation in furniture, clothing, and packaging, and in the manufacture of resins for adhesives, elastomers, and fillers while Polycarbonate is a synthetic thermoplastic resin, a linear polymer of carbonic acid, used for molded products, films, and nonbreakable windows.

They are both types of plastic materials used to manufacture a wide range of items, including common consumer goods. The difference of these materials in both chemical composition and how they are used.

Answer:

Animal roof tiles are a common feature of traditional Korean houses, known as hanok. These roof tiles are typically made of clay and are shaped to resemble various animals such as dragons, phoenixes, and other mythical creatures. These animal roof tiles are typically found on the eaves of hanok houses and are used to protect the house from water damage and to ward off evil spirits.

The common feature of East Asian houses with Philippine houses is the use of traditional materials in construction. Both East Asian and Philippine houses typically use natural materials such as wood, bamboo, and thatch to construct the roof and walls.

Another common feature is the use of sloping roofs. Both East Asian and Philippine houses typically have sloping roofs that are designed to allow for better ventilation and to protect the house from heavy rainfall.

Furthermore, both East Asian and Philippine houses often have a central courtyard or open space that serves as the focal point of the house. This open space is used for socializing and for connecting the different rooms of the house.

Additionally, both East Asian and Philippine houses are designed to be energy-efficient, with features such as large windows and doors that can be opened to allow for natural light and ventilation, and thick walls that provide insulation from the heat.

In conclusion, animal roof tiles are a common feature of traditional Korean houses, and the common feature of East Asian houses with Philippine houses are the use of traditional materials in construction, sloping roofs, central courtyard, and energy-efficient design.
