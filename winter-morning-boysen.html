Temperature is one of the principal quantities that shows the degree of heat or cold from an object/space  

Temperature is used as a guide to the level of heat energy from objects  

Temperature measures thermal energy  

The temperature of an object also depends on the kinetic energy of the object's molecules  

Temperature cannot be measured by the surface of the hand because it is not accurate but can be measured by a thermometer. Temperature units include Celsius, Fahrenheit, Reaumur or Kelvin  

The temperature of a windshield is 10°F

Since 0°C of F is 32° F the temperature has to be increased by 22 F

Or if converted to C, the increment is equal to:

Comparison of Scale C: Scale F = 5: 9

29. The temperature of a windshield on a winter morning is 10°F . If the melting point is 0°C , hiw many degrees must the temperature if the windshield be raised before the ice can melt ?​


The melting point of ice (which is the freezing point of water) is 32 deg F. So the temperature on the windshield would have to rise 22 deg F.

There are 100 Celsius degrees and 180 Fahrenheit degrees between the melting point of water and the boiling point of water.

Therefore, the needed change in windshield temperature, expressed on the Celsius scale would be:

22 Fahrenheit degrees x (100 Celsius degrees/180 Fahrenheit degrees)
