1. ano ang kahulugan ng civil society


Ano ang civil society organization?

-ito ay isang sektor ng lipunan na nasa labas ng estado o hindi kabilang sa pamahalaan kung saan nakikiisa ang mga tao upang makatulong sa kanilang mga adhikain o mga layunin. Ito ay organisasyon na binubuo ng mga mamamayan sa lipunan na hindi umaaasa sa pamahalaan.

Kinikilala ng saligang batas 1987 ang kahalagahan ng mga samahang ito sa pagtataguyod ng kaunlaran. Ipinaliwanag ni Constantino-David (1998) ang mga bumubuo sa civil society. Ito ay binubuo ng mga kilos protesta, mga lipunang pagkilos, at mga voluntary organization. Hindi kasama sa civil society organization ang mga negosyo, mga partido sa pulitika at mga samahan ng mga armadong grupo na may layuning pabagsakin ang pamahalaan.

Dalawang Kategorya ng Civil Society (David Constantino)

1. Grassroots Organizations o People’s Organizations (Pos)- naglalayong protektahan ang interes ng mga miyembro nito. Dito nahahanay ang mga sectoral group ng kababaihan, kabataan, magsasaka, mangingisda, at mga cause-oriented group

2.  Grassroot Support Organizations o Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs). - Ang mga NGOs ay naglalayong suportahan ang mga programa ng mga people’s organization.

Mga halimbawa ng NGO

1.  Operation Smile Foundation

Tumutulong sa mga kapus-palad na may mga problema gaya ng cleft lip at cleft palate.

2. Haribon Foundation for the Conservation of Natural Resources

Tumutulong sa pagkonserba ng mga likas na yaman, pagprotekta sa kapaligiran, sa kultura ng mga tribo at pamamahala sa mga wild life.

Layunin ng NGO

1. Tulungan ang mga mamamayan na makaahon sa kahirapan.

2. Tuligsain ang hindi makataong patakaran ng pamahalaan.

3. Tugunan ang mga suliranin ng mga mamamayan.

Layunin ng civil society organization

1. Layunin nito na maging kabahagi sa pagpapabago ng mga polisiya at maggiit ng accountability (kapanagutan) at transparency (katapatan) mula sa estado (Siliman, 1998)

2. Makilahok ang mga mamamayan tungo sa pagpapaunlad ng bansa.

3. Makabuo ng mga samahang direktang makikipag-ugnayan sa pamahalaan.

Kahalagahan ng civil society organization

1. Nagiging katulong ng pamahalaan sa paggawa ng mga programa para sa ikauunlad ng bansa.

2. Ayon kay Randy David (2008), sa pamamagitan ng civil society ang mga mamamayan ang pinanggagalingan ng soberanya ng isang estado. Sa pamamagitan ng paglahok sa civil society, ang mga mithiin ng mga mamamayan ang magiging batayan ng buong estado sa pamamahala ng isang bansa.

3. Isang mahusay na pagsasanay para sa demokrasiya. (Larry Diamond, 1994)  

Mga halimbawa ng Civil Society Organization

1. Research Education Institutional Development Foundation Inc.

Samahan para magbigay payo at lakas sa mga taong nais paunlarin ang ekonomiya ng bansa.

2. Citizens Crime Watch Association

Samahan para magpanatili ng kaayusan at kapayapaan sa komunidad. Ang samahan ding ito ay tumutulong sa mga alagad ng batas.

3. Catholic Media Network

Samahan para maitaguyod ang pamamahayag na may kaugnayan sa pagiging Katoliko ng mga tagapakinig.

4. Bantay Lansangan

Samahan para magpakalap ng impormasyon hinggil sa kondisyon ng mga kalsada na ipinagagawa ng pamahalaan.

5. Build Change

Samahan para magbigay tulong at suporta sa panahon ng kalamidad at sakuna.

6. adbokasiya ng civil society


Malawak ang sakop ng terminong "civil society" dahil sa ito ay binubuo ng mga mamamayang nagsama-sama dahil sa isang natagpuang adbokasiya na nais nilang isulong. Bilang isang grupo o organisasyon sa labas ng pamahalaan at ng pamilya, ito ay maituturing na boluntaryo. Ang layunin o adbokasiya ng bawat "civil society organization" ay nag-iiba-iba. Maaaring para sa kalikasan, sa kababaihan, sa manggagawa, at iba pa. Ngunit ang pagkakatulad ng mga ito ay nasa iisang layunin na ipaalam sa bayan at sa pamahalaan ang mga pangangailangan na dapat na tuguan sa isang partikular na sektor o isyu ng lipunan.

18. ano ang mensahe ng civil society ​


Answer:

Civil society can be understood as the "third sector" of society, distinct from government and business, and including the family and the private sphere.[1] By other authors, civil society is used in the sense of 1) the aggregate of non-governmental organizations and institutions that manifest interests and will of citizens or 2) individuals and organizations in a society which are independent of the government.[2]

Sometimes the term civil society is used in the more general sense of "the elements such as freedom of speech, an independent judiciary, etc, that make up a democratic society" (Collins English Dictionary).[3] Especially in the discussions among thinkers of Eastern and Central Europe, civil society is seen also as a normative concept of civic values

Explanation:

The term civil society goes back to Aristotle's phrase koinōnía politikḗ (κοινωνία πολιτική), occurring in his Politics, where it refers to a ‘political community’, commensurate with the Greek city-state (polis) characterized by a shared set of norms and ethos, in which free citizens on an equal footing lived under the rule of law. The telos or end of civil society, thus defined, was eudaimonia (τὸ εὖ ζῆν tò eu zēn) (often translated as human flourishing or common well-being), in as man was defined as a ‘political (social) animal’ (ζῷον πολιτικόν zōon politikón).[4][5][6][7] The concept was used by Roman writers, such as Cicero, where it referred to the ancient notion of a republic (res publica). It re-entered into Western political discourse following one of the late medieval translations of Aristotle's Politics into Latin by Leonardo Bruni who as a first translated koinōnía politikḗ into societas civilis. With the rise of a distinction between monarchical autonomy and public law, the term then gained currency to denote the corporate estates (Ständestaat) of a feudal elite of land-holders as opposed to the powers exercised by the prince.[8] It had a long history in state theory, and was revived with particular force in recent times, in Eastern Europe, where dissidents such as Václav Havel as late as in the 1990s employed it to denote the sphere of civic associations threatened by the intrusive holistic state-dominated regimes of Communist Eastern Europe.[9] The first post-modern usage of civil society as denoting political opposition stems from writings of Aleksander Smolar in 1978–79.[10] However the term was not in use by Solidarity labor union in 1980–1981 .

25. similarities between civil society and global society​


The activism of global civil society groups has been facilitated by a number of specific conditions. First, a number of international organisations have supported the inclusion of civil society actors within international decision-making. For example, the 1992 UN Earth Summit in Rio de Janeiro provided a means for previously scattered groups to meet and create common platforms and networks. The European Union has followed a similar approach by integrating different types of civil society organisations within its governance mechanisms. Second, the state’s priorities for the allocation of resources changed in the 1980s and 1990s due to a trend towards the privatisation of industries. In that climate, it was common to see state-owned enterprises (such as utilities) being sold off to private companies. For that reason, in many Western nations, the state’s overall role in public affairs was reduced. In this context, civil society organisations were able to subcontract many functions from the state and take up new roles as service providers. Third, the globalisation process has generated a sense of common purpose among civil society actors. This has been a trigger for internal unification – increasing the sense of solidarity among civil society organisations. It has also united the groups that want to highlight the negative sides of globalisation. Finally, through the internet, groups from different parts of the world have been able to familiarise themselves with other political realities, like-minded organisations, and alternative forms of action. In this way, they have been able to increase their political know-how and their ability to join forces in addressing common targets.
