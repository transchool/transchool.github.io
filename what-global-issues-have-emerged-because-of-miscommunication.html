The relationship between global issues and miscommunication.

Global issues are issues that happen in the whole world. Oftentimes, these are caused by differences in culture, language, and overall background of people coming from different countries. A prominent example of such issues is racism because as we know, it happens in every corner of the world. There also has been much research in the context of racism-related research, if you are interested.

Now, we also have miscommunication. Miscommunication happens when the attempt at communication fails due to certain reasons. Miscommunication is the opposite of communication. An example of miscommunication is when we want to talk to convey A to another person, and that person perceives that we want to communicate about B instead of A.

Of course, these things can be clarified by avoiding things that may cause such misunderstanding. An example would be to converse using words that don't cause miscommunication. We must also practice respect at all times.

Coincidentally, an example of a global issue that has emerged because of miscommunication is racism. There are also other issues, such as discrimination and war. More often than not, it is pretty difficult to spot miscommunication. For example, in war, probably the reason country A wants to exercise its military power is to defend the country, while neighboring country B perceives this practice as a sign that country A will invade country B. Thus, miscommunication is born.

In racism, certain cultures make people have a certain set of personalities. For example, if person A who comes from country A tends to be shy and generally avoids starting a conversation, then person B from country B who likes to converse with others might get offended if person A is avoiding person B. Meanwhile, this is not the case.

17. what global issues have emerged because of miscommunication


Language Barrier - Problems and Solutions

Humans are born to communicate and interact with one another. This world's progress thrive from the effectiveness of communication. However, because of this, many global issues have emerged. Verbal and non-verbal communication greatly impacts business, inter-culture relationships and families. Global issues could range from small arguments to big disasters.  

Let us dive into the effects of language barrier in communication:

1. Business

  There are many ways how language barrier affect international business. Many local businesses in any parts of the world would dream of entering the international market through active promotions, advertisements and employing foreign workers. While all these are good signs for a business to advance and grow, problems would also stem out from something as simple as a miscommunication while handling a potential deal. For example, two parties are trying to agree at a million dollar deal. Person A came from France and person B came from Peru. Both could not speak each other's native language. And let us say, their English skill, a language which they chose as their medium, is only in the intermediate level. The lack of vocabulary, grammar mishaps and perhaps an inability to listen effectively at a certain degree could potentially affect the result of the deal, which in turn could affect the entire company's earnings and in a bigger scale, the economy of the country itself and its global standing.

2. Culture

   While we are living in a web era where everything seems to be intertwined by information and people, the difference between culture still impacts us at a global scale. Take it for example, American English vs British English. Both are English however, we could still see them clashing from time to time because of miscommunication. The difference between certain vocabularies, slangs and jargons, as well as how they use those words and how they view things in general could affect the interaction between both cultures. One biggest issue this humanity is experiencing is racism. We may think that racism is only about skin, gender or nationality. However, the conflict does not arise from a white American and a black American just standing side by side quietly together. The conflict arises when one would misinterpret the other's non-verbal actions or even verbally. When there is a different interpretation of actions and words, then miscommunication happens. This may also be evident in battles and wars. Many recorded historical events in the past have proven how a misinterpretation of a message could lead into countries being at war and how a single non-verbal action could bring back peace. Another example to note would be a person trying to communicate to someone who is mute. The person's inability to talk due to medical reasons would naturally learn a non-verbal communicating method known as the sign language. Miscommunication arises when the other person does not understand sign language and the other could not physically speak.  

3. Relationships

    The conflict between different generations are very much present. You would hear many young and old people complain, "He or she is too young to understand." or "He or she is too old to understand." In this matter, it is not just the difference between the expressions and terms people are using and inventing, but it greatly concerns the way people think and view things. This could affect the very core of a society which is the family. If the family fails to communicate well, it creates ripples of disaster that, as you could see now, divorce, separation and many other psychological effects that impacts the generation and the succeeding ones.  

In conclusion, there are many language barrier problems and solutions. One must know that we are all connected in one way or another. Any matter could create a chain reaction that can affect all of us globally, good or bad. Although we may vary in dialect and literacy, we could avoid or decimate the effect of miscommunication by learning to be more attentively. Asking questions would greatly help any of the issues mentioned above.
