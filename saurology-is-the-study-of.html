6. why do we need to study tautology and fallacy​


Answer:

In Mathematical logic, a tautology (from Greek: ταυτολογία) is a formula or assertion that is true in every possible interpretation. An example is "x=y or x≠y". A less abstract example is "either the ball is all green, or the ball is not all green". This would be a tautology regardless of the color of the ball.

24. why did the human beings invent astrology? how do we know astrology is not a real study?


Why did human beings invent astrology?

Many cultures around the world have created some form of astrology. The curious members of certain populations for whatever reason stared at the night sky and somehow noticed and later on recorded the movement of the celestial bodies. As awareness of these movements started spreading throughout the community, people began to connect it with earthly events, with some seeing them as omens. Astrology gave clues to some of the mysteries people in ancient civilizations couldn't explain, it added meaning in their lives at a time when there really isn’t much to do like today.  

How do we know astrology is not a real study?

Astrology today is relegated to fun symbols that in a way still enrich our lives. Looking at your zodiac sign’s future still provides a certain meaning for some but that is as far as it goes. It no longer guides us in terms of important decisions. Because of how far science has gone, we know now that astrology isn’t a real study, earthly events have earthly explanations.
