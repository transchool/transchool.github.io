25. in an essay form, write your self concept that you wish others knew about you. ​


SELF CONCEPT

The image we have of ourselves is referred to as self-concept. How does one's self-image develop and alter throughout time? This picture is formed in a variety of ways, but it is primarily impacted by our relationships with significant individuals in our life. Self-concept refers to how you think about yourself, your abilities, and your distinguishing characteristics. For example, thoughts like "I am a good friend" or "I am a nice person" are part of your total self-concept.

When you're younger and still going through the process of self-discovery and identity construction, your self-concept is more changeable. These self-perceptions grow considerably more precise and organized as you get older and learn more about who you are and what matters to you.

Self-concept is, at its most fundamental level, a set of beliefs about oneself and others' reactions. It encapsulates the response to the inquiry, "Who am I?"
