Kolonyalismo

- Ang Kolonyalismo ay tumutukoy sa pag-kontrol ng isang malaki at makapangyarihang bansa sa isang maliit na bansa.

- Ang kolonyalismo ay may layong palakihin o palawakin ang sakop nilang teritoryo, makuha at mapakinabangan ang likas na yaman na tinataglay ng bansang sinakop, at makuha ang loob ng mga katutubo sa pamamagitan ng relihiyong kinagisnan ng mga mananakop.

Di-mabuting epekto ng kolonyalismo

Pagkakait ng kalayaan sa bansang nasakupan.Pangangalap at pakikinabang ng mga banyaga sa likas na yaman ng bansang sinakop.Pagmamaliit, Diskriminasyon, at Pang-aabuso.Sapilitang pagtratrabaho sa mga nasasakupan.Pag-kokontrol ng mga banyaga sa sa sistema ng pamahalaan at kalakalan.

Mabuting epekto ng kolonyalismo

Pagbubukas sa mga mata ng mga mamamayan ng nasakop na bansa sa tunay na kalagayan ng bansa.Paggising ng damdaming makabayan ng mga nasakop.Paghahangad ng kalayaan sa kamay ng mga mananakopPaghalili sa mga kultura, nakasanayan, mga salitang-hiram, at makabagong kaalaman sa mga banyaga.

25. mabuting epekto ng kolonyalismo


Colonialism Effects and Information  

Many of Manila's current urban challenges can be traced back to the acts of the Philippines' colonizers, first Spain and then the United States. Throughout these decades of colonial dominance, the Spaniards and Americans made it a point to instill one message in the Filipino people's minds: their race reigns supreme.  

Colonization's Highlights in Philipines during Spanish Era:  The Policy Of Reduccion - This process entailed forcing scattered barangays to form compact but wider groups and reducing their numbers (hence the word ""reduction"") so that they could be conveniently and efficiently summoned to church rites and accounted for their daily contributions. Privatization Of Land Ownership  - Spaniards and native principals received land grants from the Spanish governors-general, which also included shared lands. Members of the principalia (former datus, their relatives, and descendants) have sold or donated lands they thought they owned but were simply communal. The Manila Galleon Trade - The Galleon Trade between Asia and the Americas was the first and largest monopolistic trade between the two continents. It thrived for almost two and a half decades, outperforming the British, French, and Dutch East India Companies in terms of trade with Europe. The trade began in 1565, and until 1813, galleons sailed from Manila to Acapulco on a daily basis, carrying oriental merchandise and returning with mail and silver from Spain. The trade began in 1565, and galleons sailed from Manila to Acapulco on a daily basis until 1813, carrying oriental goods and returning with mail from Spain and silver from Mexico.

 

Good Effects of Colonialism  The wealth introduced to Manila by the Galleon Trade aided in the city's accelerated urbanization. Its growing population boosted agriculture productivity and industry in the surrounding region. During the second half of the 18th century, policies to expand industrial prosperity and reach the whole country opened Manila and other ports to international trade. These new inventions laid the groundwork for the current framework of the Philippine economy and culture, as well as its international ties. These reforms, however, came at a high cost, especially for the majority of Filipinos.  The Americans adopted compulsory public education with English as the language of instruction, American democracy's values and institutions, and new metropolitan facilities. They also established a system of locally elected officials and a national legislature, through which Filipinos were increasingly able to engage in the governance of their affairs.  Market demand for products ""made in the USA"" grew as a result of the influx of American manufacturers and commodities into the Philippines, as well as the injection of American ideals into the public education system. Philippine exports outstripped imports during the early years of American rule.
