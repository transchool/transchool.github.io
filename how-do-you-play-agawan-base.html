To start the game, one team is "in" and the other team is "out." The team that is "in" tries to tag the players on the other team, who are trying to run to a designated base or safe zone.

The team that is "out" tries to run from one end of the playing area to the other without being tagged by the team that is "in." If a player on the "out" team is tagged, they become part of the "in" team.

The game continues until all players on the "out" team have been tagged, at which point the teams switch roles and the game starts again.

Agawan Base

1. How do you play Agawan Base?

You play agawan base by forming a group of at least four people (for an enjoyable round, ten people should play). The group should be divided equally into two, and then look for a place that you will refer to as your “base”. Both teams should designate someone who will guard the base. The remaining players should try their best to reach the opponent’s base and touch it. If a player was first touched by an opponent, they will become a “prisoner” and will stay at the base. “Prisoners” should hold their hands together (while being glued at the opponent’s base) and reach as far as they could so that someone from their team could grab their hand and release them. If a team successfully touched the base of the opponent, they will shout “Agaw base” and will gain a point. The game restarts. Another way of winning is to turn all of your opponents into “prisoners” (with the exception of the base guard).
