1. An 48 inch chord is 7 inches from the center of the circle. How long is the chord that is 5 inches from the center?​


✒️CIRCLE

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{PROBLEM}:} [/tex]

An 48 inch chord is 7 inches from the center of the circle. How long is the chord that is 5 inches from the center?

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER}:} [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \LARGE \:\:\:\rm{20\sqrt6 \:\: inches} [/tex]

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:} [/tex]

Illustration:Draw a circle and a chord in which it is perpendicular to its distance (7in) from the center. Thus, the distance will bisect the chord (48in) into two (24in).

Draw a segment from the center to the point in which the chord touches the circle, that represents as the radius (r).Solve for the radius:

» It shows a right triangle, determine the radius as the hypotenuse using the Pythagorean Theorem.

[tex] r^2 = 24^2 + 7^2 [/tex]

[tex] r^2 = 576 + 49 [/tex]

[tex] r^2 = 625 [/tex]

[tex] \sqrt{r^2} = \sqrt{625} [/tex][tex] r = 25 [/tex]

» Therefore, the radius of the circle is 25 inches.

Illustrate (2):On the same circle draw another chord which measures x that is 5in from the center of the circle. The bisection will cut the chord into x/2.

Draw another segment from the center to the point in which the chord touches the circle, that represents as the radius (25in).

Solve for x:

» Since it forms a right triangle and we already know the radius, we will solve for x using the Pythagorean Theorem.

[tex] r^2 = (x/2)^2 + 5^2 [/tex]

[tex] 25^2 = (x/2)^2 + 5^2 [/tex]

[tex] 625 = x^2/4 + 25 [/tex]

[tex] x^2/4 = 625 - 25 [/tex]

[tex] x^2/4 = 600 [/tex]

[tex] (x^2/4)(4) = 600(4) [/tex]

[tex] x^2 = 2400 [/tex]

[tex] \sqrt{x^2} = \sqrt{2400} [/tex]

[tex] x = 20\sqrt6 [/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] The measure of the chord that is 5 inches from the center of the circle is 20√6 inches.

9. find the distance of the chord yo the center point if the radius of a circle is 10 and the chord is 10​


Answer:

5√3 units

Step-by-step explanation:

Let the center of the circle be E, the chord be AB such that A and B touch the circle, the midpoint of AB be M.

Connect point E to point B and point A.

Likewise, connect point E to point M such that EM is perpendicular to AM and BM.

Notice that this forms two right triangles.

Observe that EB and EA are the radii of the circle and the hypotenuse of the triangles and AM and BM are the bases of the two right triangles.

Thus, EB = EA = 10.

Since M is the midpoint of AB, AM = BM = 1/2(AB) = 1/2(10) = 5.

Using the Pythagorean Theorem (a² + b² = c²),

5² + EM² = 10²

25 + EM² = 100

EM² = 100 - 25

EM² = 75

EM = √75

EM = 5√3

Note that EM is the distance between the chord and the center point of the circle.

Hence, the distance of the chord to the center is 5√3 units.

24. meaning of diameter,chord,radius,center and arc


Diameter is the length of two ends of a circle

Chord is the length of two points of a circle

Radius is the length of a point from the circle to the center

Center is the point which is equidistant to every point on the circle

Arc is part of the circle which is created by two lines coming from the center to two points on the circle
diameter-any straight line segment that passes through the center of the circle.
chord-geometric line segment whose endpoints both lie on the circle.
radius-is the length of the line from the center to any point on its edge
center-is a point inside the circle and is at an equal distance from all of the points on its circumference
arc-portion of the circumference of a circle
