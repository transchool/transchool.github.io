ABOUT FILL IN THE BLANK

Fill-in-the-blank questions consist of a phrase, sentence, or paragraph with a blank spot in which the respondent fills in the word or phrase that is missing. You may build questions with a single or several blanks. There is just one valid response for every blank. For fill-in-the-blank questions, you can provide feedback on each question (question-level feedback) and each blank (choice-level feedback).

How to Complete the Blank - Tips to Save Time

- Develop a robust vocabulary and review your grammar.

- Learn to read between the lines

- Sentence structure should be practiced.

- Every day, learn new phrases, synonyms, antonyms, and idioms.

- Locate the whole meanings of sentences.

- Practice more.

Leave a blank space in place of the important word or phrase. Create a list of key-words and phrases at the bottom of the page that contains your statement (or on a different page). This will function as the key. Review your sentences and use a very light pencil to fill in the blanks with the correct responses.

You must remember the following in order to answer the Fill in the Blank questions:

- Possess an exceptional vocabulary.

- Carefully read the sentence in the question.

- Keep a close eye on grammar hints.

- Understand the sentence's context.

- Recognize a sentence's logical structure.
