Answer:

Environmental degradation  

Environmental degradation is the deterioration of the environment through depletion of resources such as quality of air, water and soil; the destruction of ecosystems; habitat destruction; the extinction of wildlife; and pollution. It is defined as any change or disturbance to the environment perceived to be deleterious or undesirable.

Causes of environmental degradation

Some species of environmental life require substantial areas to help provide food, living space and other different assets. These creatures are known to be area specific. By the time the biome is divided, the vast patches of living space no longer exist.

It becomes more problematic for the wildlife Getting the assets they need to survive. The environment continues to exist, although animals and plant life are not there to help sustain it properly.

1- Earth disturbance The most basic cause of environmental degradation is the damage to the earth. Numerous species of weed plants, for example garlic mustard, are both strange and intrusive.

A break in the environmental environment gives them the opportunity to start growing and expanding. These plants can take control over nature, eliminating local vegetation.

2- Pollution Pollution, whatever the form, whether in the air, water, earth or in the form of noise, is harmful to the environment. Air pollution contaminates the air we breathe, which causes health problems.

The water contamination Degrades the quality of the water we use to drink. Land contamination leads to surface degradation as a result of human activities.

Noise pollution can cause irreparable damage to our ears when they are exposed to loud and continuous sounds such as vehicle horns on a busy road or machines that produce high decibel noise in a factory or mill.

3- Overpopulation The rapid growth of the population puts the natural resources , Resulting in degradation of the environment. The death rate has been reduced due to better medical facilities that have resulted in a longer shelf life.

4- Landfills Landfills pollute the environment and destroy the beauty of the city. Landfills enter the city due to the large amount of garbage that is generated by households, industries, factories and hospitals.

These spaces pose a great risk to the health of the environment and the people who live there. Sanitary landfills produce unpleasant odors when they burn and cause a huge environmental deterioration.

5- Deforestation Deforestation is the clearing of trees to give way to more homes and industries. Rapid population growth and urban sprawl are two of the main causes of deforestation.

Apart from this, the use of forest land for agriculture, animal grazing, fuelwood harvesting and logging are some of the other causes. Deforestation contributes to global warming , Since the reduction of the size of the forest returns the carbon to the environment.

6- Acid rain Acid rain occurs when sulfur dioxide emissions from coal plants combine with the moisture present in the air. A chemical reaction creates this acid precipitation. Acid rain can acidify and pollute lakes and streams. It causes similar effects to the soil.

According to the United States Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), if enough acid rain falls in a given environment, it can acidify water or soil to a point where life can not be sustained. Plants die and animals that depend on them disappear. The condition of the environment deteriorates.

7- Urban Development According to many ecologists, including those at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, urban development is a major cause of environmental degradation.

8- Natural causes Phenomena like avalanches, earthquakes, tsunamis, storms and forest fires can totally crush the groups of animals and plants nearby, to the point where they can no longer survive in those areas.
