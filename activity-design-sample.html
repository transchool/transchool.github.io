4. design a personal physical activity program that would help develop the skills in playing team sports following the FITT formula and the SMART quide. please refer to the sample physical activity program in what is it​


Answer:

The FITT formula is frequency, intensity, time and type. The SMART guide is specific, measurable, attainable, relevant and time-bound.

A personal physical activity program to develop the skills in playing team sports following the FITT formula and the SMART guide would look like this:

-Frequency: 3 times per week

-Intensity: Moderate

-Time: 30 minutes

-Type: Cardio and strength training

-Specific: Exercises to improve agility, speed, and coordination

-Measurable: Track progress with a fitness tracker or app

-Attainable: Start with a manageable routine and gradually increase intensity and duration

-Relevant: Choose exercises that mimic movements performed in team sports

-Time-bound: Set a goal to improve skills within a certain timeframe

Answer:

Electricity requires a complete "loop" for current to flow. This is called a closed circuit. That is why wall outlets have two prongs and batteries have two ends (positive and negative) instead of just one. You connect both of them to a circuit and that creates a complete loop. If the loop is broken at all, it becomes an open circuit, and no current will flow.

In this project you will build your own simple circuit by disassembling a flashlight (with permission, of course). You will use your circuit as a tester to determine whether household materials are conductors or insulators. When you connect the circuit to a conductor, you will create a closed circuit and the flashlight bulb will turn on. If you connect the circuit to an insulator, you will still have an open circuit so the bulb will stay off.

Materials

Flashlight (one you can take apart)

Batteries for your flashlight

Three pieces of wire that can be cut and stripped (See Procedure for more information.)

Ruler with metric measurements

Electrical tape (and/or rubber bands)

Scissors or knife (and an adult's help)

Assortment of metal and nonmetal household materials that can be tested in your circuit

29. 00 Additional Activities Activity 5: Performance Task Directions: Choose the best sampling design that is suitable to your chosen topic/title and explain why you choose this sampling techniques. Write your answer in a separate sheet of paper. (at least 250 words) Sampling You're Chosen Research Title R.​


Answer:

Chosen Research Topic/Title: "The Impact of Social Media on Adolescent Mental Health"

Sampling Design: Stratified Random Sampling

Explanation: Stratified random sampling is an appropriate sampling design for the research topic, "The Impact of Social Media on Adolescent Mental Health." This sampling design involves dividing the population into homogeneous subgroups, known as strata, and randomly selecting a sample from each stratum. This design is suitable for this study as it helps to ensure that the sample accurately represents the population in terms of relevant characteristics, such as age, gender, and socio-economic status.

In this study, the population could be divided into strata based on age, gender, and socio-economic status, as these factors are known to impact adolescent mental health. For example, the strata could include males and females aged 12 to 18, from low, middle, and high socio-economic backgrounds. By stratifying the sample, the study can ensure that the sample accurately reflects the population in terms of these important factors and allows for a more in-depth examination of the impact of social media on adolescent mental health.

Furthermore, by using stratified random sampling, the study can reduce the risk of sampling error and increase the precision of the results. This is because stratified random sampling ensures that each stratum is represented in the sample in proportion to its representation in the population, reducing the risk of sampling bias. This, in turn, increases the generalizability of the study findings, allowing the researcher to make more confident conclusions about the population.

In conclusion, stratified random sampling is a suitable sampling design for the research topic, "The Impact of Social Media on Adolescent Mental Health." This design helps to ensure that the sample accurately reflects the population in terms of relevant characteristics, reduces the risk of sampling error, and increases the precision and generalizability of the study findings.
