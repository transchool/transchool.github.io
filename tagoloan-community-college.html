22. As a college student, explain the essence of purposive communication? ​


Answer:

Purposive communication is the process of intentionally and effectively communicating in order to achieve a specific goal or objective. In a college setting, purposive communication is essential for students to successfully navigate their academic and personal lives.

Purposive communication involves understanding the purpose of the communication, selecting the appropriate medium and message, and adapting the message to the audience. It also involves active listening, effective feedback, and conflict resolution.

Purposive communication is important in a college setting because it allows students to effectively communicate with their professors and classmates, which is essential for success in the classroom. It also allows students to effectively communicate with other members of the college community such as advisors, tutors, and administrative staff.

Purposive communication also helps students to develop important life skills such as critical thinking, problem-solving, and teamwork, which are essential for success in any profession. Additionally, it also helps in developing a sense of empathy and active listening skills.

In short, purposive communication is a critical skill for college students because it allows them to effectively communicate with others, navigate their academic and personal lives, and develop important life skills.
