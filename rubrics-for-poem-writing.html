Answer:

Poem Writing Rubric

CATEGORY

Exceptional

Good Work

Developing

Beginning

Focus on Assigned Topic

The entire poem is related to the assigned topic and allows the reader to understand much more about the topic.

Most of the poem is related to the assigned topic. The poem wanders off at one point, but the reader can still learn something about the topic.

Some of the poem is related to the assigned topic, but a reader does not learn much about the topic.

No attempt has been made to relate the poem to the assigned topic.

Creativity

The poem contains many creative details and/or descriptions that contribute to the reader's enjoyment. The author has really used his imagination.

The poem contains a few creative details and/or descriptions that contribute to the reader's enjoyment. The author has used his imagination.

The poem contains a few creative details and/or descriptions, but they distract from the poem. The author has tried to use his imagination.

There is little evidence of creativity in the poem. The author does not seem to have used much imagination.

Spelling and Punctuation

There are no spelling or punctuation errors in the final draft.

There is one spelling or punctuation error in the final draft.

There are 2-3 spelling and punctuation errors in the final draft.

The final draft has more than 3 spelling and punctuation errors.

Title

Title is creative, sparks interest and is related to the poem and topic.

Title is related to the poem and topic.

Title is present, but does not appear to be related to the poem and topic.

No title.

Imagery

Many vivid, descriptive words are used. The reader can picture the imagery in the poem.

Some vivid, descriptive words are used. The reader can somewhat picture the imagery in the poem.

The reader can figure out what to picture in the poem, but the author didn't supply much detail.

The reader has trouble figuring out what imagery the poem is using and what the author wants him/her to picture.

POEM

Trend is a fickle, fleeting thing,

A force that ebbs and flows like spring.

It comes and goes in waves and tides,

And changes with each passing stride.

It's mercurial, elusive, and free,

A shape-shifting form for all to see.

A moving target, hard to define,

That captures our attention, then resigns.

Trend is a rebel, a rule-breaker too,

A pioneer, forging paths anew.

It defies conventions, shatters norms,

And leads us down uncharted forms.

It's a chameleon, adapting with ease,

To the whims and wishes of our needs.

A reflection of our hopes and fears,

A mirror of our changing years.

Trend is a catalyst, a spark of light,

That ignites our passions, and sets us alight.

It fuels our dreams, and fires our goals,

And keeps us striving for new roles.

So embrace the trend, let it take you away,

And ride its wave, as it ebbs and sways.

For trend is a force that shapes our lives,

And gives us hope, as we thrive and thrive.

17. help po huhuhuhu Activity 5. Poem Writing! Directions: Write a poem about the different classifications of aliphatic hydrocarbons, their molecular and structural formula and properties. You will be rated based on the rubrics below. helpppp


Answer:

The simplest organic compounds are those compose of only two elements: Carbon and hydrogen. These compounds are called hydrocarbons. Hydrocarbons themselves are separated into two types: aliphatic hydrocarbons and aromatic hydrocarbons. Aliphatic hydrocarbons are hydrocarbons based on chains of C atoms. There are three types of aliphatic hydrocarbons. Alkanes are aliphatic hydrocarbons with only single covalent bonds. Alkenes are hydrocarbons that contain at least one C--C double bond, and alkynes are hydrocarbons that contain C--C triple bond. Occasionally, we find an aliphatic hydrocarbon with a ring of C atoms; These hydrocarbons are called cycloalkanes (or cycloalkenes or cycloalkynes). Because alkanes have the maximum number of H atoms possible according to the rules of covalent bonds, alkanes are also referred to as saturated hydrocarbons
