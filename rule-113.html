4. Find the reference angle of 113°


Answer:

Find the reference angle of 113°.

Find the reference angle of 113°.The reference angle is 67°.

Step-by-step explanation:

Reference Angle

The reference angle is the angle between the terminal side of the angle and the x-axis. It is always the smallest angle that you can make from the terminal side of an angle. The measure of the reference angle within the x-axis is always equal or less than 90°.

However, we have different ways in finding the reference angle depending on the quadrant where the angle lies.

(For information about the quadrant of angles, visit the link.

https://brainly.ph/question/1004171)

Quadrant 1: The reference angle is the same as the angle.

Examples:

30° = 30°

90° = 90°

Quadrant 2: 180° - angle

Examples:

120° = 60°

175° = 5°

Quadrant 3: angle - 180°

Examples:

220° = 40°

190° = 10°

Quadrant 4: 360° - angle

Examples:

290° = 70°

315° = 45°

29. what is 62% of 113?​


Answer:

54.87%

Step-by-step explanation:

Solution for 62 is what percent of 113:

62:113*100 =  

(62*100):113 =  

6200:113 = 54.87

Now we have: 62 is what percent of 113 = 54.87  

Question: 62 is what percent of 113?  

Percentage solution with steps:

Step 1: We make the assumption that 113 is 100% since it is our output value.  

Step 2: We next represent the value we seek with x.

Step 3: From step 1, it follows that 100%=113.

Step 4: In the same vein, x% = 62.

Step 5: This gives us a pair of simple equations:

100% = 113(1)

x% = 62(2)

Step 6: By simply dividing equation 1 by equation 2 and taking note of the fact that both the LHS

(left - hand side) of both equations have the same unit (%); we have

100%/ x% = 113/62

Step 7: Taking the inverse (or reciprocal) of both sides yields

x%/100% = 62/113

-> x = 54.87%

Therefore, 62 is 54.87% of 113
