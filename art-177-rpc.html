2. Can you explain about Arson of Property of Small value in Art. 323 of RPC?


Arson of property of small value is a criminal offense in the Philippines, punishable under Article 323 of the Revised Penal Code (RPC).

According to Article 323 of the RPC, the crime of arson of property of small value is committed when a person sets fire to any unoccupied building or property of small value, or any forest, field, or grassland, without the consent of the owner.

The penalty for this crime is imprisonment for a period of six months to six years, and a fine ranging from 200 to 1,000 pesos.

It is important to note that the crime of arson carries more severe penalties if the property that was burned was inhabited or was of greater value. Arson is also considered a more serious offense if it was committed with intent to gain or with malice.

In general, the crime of arson is considered a serious offense because it involves the deliberate destruction of property, which can cause significant damage and harm to individuals and communities. It is important for individuals to respect the property of others and to refrain from engaging in any activities that could cause harm or damage to others' property.


3. Can you explain about Aron of Property of Small value in Art. 323 of RPC?.


Answer:

Article 323 of the Revised Penal Code of the Philippines (RPC) pertains to the crime of Arson, which is defined as the willful and malicious burning of the property of another. Under this article, there is a provision that is known as the "Aron of Property of Small Value" which states that if the property burned is of small value or amount, the crime shall be punished by imprisonment of prision correccional in its minimum and medium periods.

Prision correccional is a penalty that is imposed for crimes that are less grave in nature. It is divided into three periods: minimum, medium, and maximum. The length of the penalty depends on the circumstances of the case and the discretion of the court.

The provision on Aron of Property of Small Value is based on the idea that the value of the property burned is a factor in determining the gravity of the crime of arson. If the value of the property is small, the crime is considered less grave and is punished accordingly. However, if the value of the property is large, the crime is considered more grave and is punished more severely.

It is important to note that the definition of "small value" in this context is not specified in the RPC, and it is left to the discretion of the court to determine the value of the property burned in each case.

29. discuiss the tree-fold rule of the RPC pn


Answer:

What is the Three-fold Rule?

The three-fold rule provides that when multiple successive penalties are imposed upon the offender, the maximum duration of his sentence shall not be more than three-fold the length of time corresponding to the most severe of the penalties imposed upon him.
