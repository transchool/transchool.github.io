9. Ang Panday by Amado V. hernandez1. What is the theme of the poem? 2. What is the evident symbol used by the author? 3. What is the meaning of the symbol in the poem? 4. Identify the setting of the literary piece. 5. Discuss the conflict in the poem briefly. 6. Describe the tone of the poem. 7. Identify the Filipino culture mentioned in the second stanza. 8. What Filipino identity was present in the third and fourth stanzas?9. Interpret the last stanza of the poem.10. In the present time, what object or symbol you can use as weapon? Justify your answer.​


Answer:

1. The theme of the poem is the transformation of a simple tool into a weapon of resistance and freedom in times of oppression and conflict.

2.The evident symbol used by the author is the "Panday" (Blacksmith) and his process of transforming the iron into an implement and eventually a weapon.

3. The symbol of the Panday and his tools represents the power of the working class and their ability to take control of their own destiny through resistance and self-determination. The iron represents the raw materials of the people, and the process of transforming it represents the transformative power of the working class.

4. The setting of the literary piece is not specified, but it could be assumed that it is set in a society where oppression and class conflict is prevalent.

5. The conflict in the poem is the social struggle of the working class against oppression and injustice.

6. The tone of the poem is one of defiance and determination.

7. The Filipino culture mentioned in the second stanza is the practice of farming and using tools such as plows to till the land.

8. The Filipino identity present in the third and fourth stanzas is that of resistance and self-determination, as the people take up arms against oppression and injustice.

9. The last stanza of the poem emphasizes the importance of the working class and their ability to resist and fight for their rights and freedom. The Panday's tools may not be flashy or powerful, but they are a symbol of the people's power and potential.

10 In the present time, an object or symbol that can be used as a weapon is the "pen" this symbol can be used as a weapon of resistance against oppression and censorship. Just as the Panday used his tools to resist oppression and fight for freedom, a pen can be used to resist censorship and oppression through writing and self-expression.
