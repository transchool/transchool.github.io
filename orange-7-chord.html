3. An 48 inch chord is 7 inches from the center of the circle. How long is the chord that is 5 inches from the center?​


✒️CIRCLE

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{PROBLEM}:} [/tex]

An 48 inch chord is 7 inches from the center of the circle. How long is the chord that is 5 inches from the center?

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER}:} [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \LARGE \:\:\:\rm{20\sqrt6 \:\: inches} [/tex]

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:} [/tex]

Illustration:Draw a circle and a chord in which it is perpendicular to its distance (7in) from the center. Thus, the distance will bisect the chord (48in) into two (24in).

Draw a segment from the center to the point in which the chord touches the circle, that represents as the radius (r).Solve for the radius:

» It shows a right triangle, determine the radius as the hypotenuse using the Pythagorean Theorem.

[tex] r^2 = 24^2 + 7^2 [/tex]

[tex] r^2 = 576 + 49 [/tex]

[tex] r^2 = 625 [/tex]

[tex] \sqrt{r^2} = \sqrt{625} [/tex][tex] r = 25 [/tex]

» Therefore, the radius of the circle is 25 inches.

Illustrate (2):On the same circle draw another chord which measures x that is 5in from the center of the circle. The bisection will cut the chord into x/2.

Draw another segment from the center to the point in which the chord touches the circle, that represents as the radius (25in).

Solve for x:

» Since it forms a right triangle and we already know the radius, we will solve for x using the Pythagorean Theorem.

[tex] r^2 = (x/2)^2 + 5^2 [/tex]

[tex] 25^2 = (x/2)^2 + 5^2 [/tex]

[tex] 625 = x^2/4 + 25 [/tex]

[tex] x^2/4 = 625 - 25 [/tex]

[tex] x^2/4 = 600 [/tex]

[tex] (x^2/4)(4) = 600(4) [/tex]

[tex] x^2 = 2400 [/tex]

[tex] \sqrt{x^2} = \sqrt{2400} [/tex]

[tex] x = 20\sqrt6 [/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] The measure of the chord that is 5 inches from the center of the circle is 20√6 inches.

[tex] (x + 3)(x + 1) = (x - 2)(x + 7) [/tex]
