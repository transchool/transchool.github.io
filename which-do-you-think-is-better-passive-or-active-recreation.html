PASSIVE RECREATION AND ACTIVE RECREATION: THE BETTER

Passive recreation and active recreation are two types of recreational activity. The two differs greatly on the amount of energy or effort exerted. In passive recreational activity, there is only little exertion of energy or effort, while active recreational activity requires someone to be highly active and consume great amount of energy and effort.

Now, as to the question which is better, for me it depends on the purpose or goal of a person.

If a person's goal is just to be relaxed or just for the sake of pastime, most likely that person will choose passive recreation.But if a person's goal is to get active, embark on a more strenuous and or adventurous activity, that person will most likely choose active recreation.

In addition, a person's decision as to which recreational activity he or she likes better is affected by factors.

Time - People who do not have that much time most likely engage into passive recreational activity compared to people who have much time.Resources - Recreational activities need equipment and facilities too. And not everyone can afford to have even a single equipment or facility.Space - Space is very important also. Some recreational activities need bigger space place.

But regardless of what one chooses, may it be passive recreational activity or active recreational activity, it is a fact that both have significant impact to a person's well-being. Both are uniquely beneficial to people.
