15. describe the chivalry of a page, esquire and a knight​


What was a Knight? A knight was a special warrior. Most knights were of noble birth. You didn't have to be a noble to become a knight, but it was a lot easier since you needed money to get the training and to buy the armor. But anyone who proved themselves in battle could be knighted. A knight pledged loyalty to their liege lord, promised to be brave in battle and protect the church and those weaker than themselves, and to be courteous to noblewomen.

How did one get to be a knight? It was not easy. You started off by becoming a page. A noble's son could start training to be a knight when he was seven years old. Nobles' sons had to train with weapons of course, but they also had to learn how to ride a horse, how to behave towards their liege lords and ladies, and even about music and the other arts. It was just like going to school, only their teachers were the squires. Once they reached a certain point in their training, they would be appointed as a squire.

A Esquire, who was generally a teenager, had a different set of duties. They had to teach the pages of course, but they also had to wait on the knights. They continued their training in battle, but also were assigned to a specific knight who completed their training. As a squire you went into battle with the knight and fought at his side. This was where you proved if you had the ability to be a knight. Once you had proved your ability, you were made a knight in a very formal ceremony.

Each knight had his own coat of arms that identified him. The pattern and colors of his coat of arms was on his shield and any other items that belonged to the knight. Knights even had flags with their coat of arms on it.

All pages, squires and knights had to follow an elaborate code of conduct. This was called Chivalry.

29. Magbigay ng ilang akdang patungkol sa chivalry.​


Ang chivalry ay isang konsepto na may kinalaman sa mga katangian ng isang kabalyero o knight, tulad ng tapang, karangalan, kabaitan, at paggalang sa mga babae. Narito ang ilan sa mga akda na patungkol sa chivalry:

"Le Morte d'Arthur" ni Sir Thomas Malory - Ito ay isang aklat tungkol sa mga kuwento ng mga kabalyero ng Round Table, kabilang na ang mga kuwento tungkol kay King Arthur, Sir Lancelot, Sir Galahad, at marami pang iba. Ipinapakita ng aklat ang mga halimbawa ng kabayanihan, tapang, at katapatan ng mga kabalyero.

"Don Quixote" ni Miguel de Cervantes - Ito ay tungkol sa isang lalaking nag-iisip na siya ay isang kabalyero at naglalakbay kasama ang kanyang loyal na tagapagsilbi na si Sancho Panza. Bagama't ito ay isang kawili-wiling kuwento, ipinapakita rin nito ang mga halimbawa ng katapangan at kagitingan.

"Sir Gawain and the Green Knight" - Ito ay isang tulang pang-Ingles mula sa Middle Ages na naglalarawan ng isang hamon ng isang kabalyero sa kanyang paghahanap ng kabayanihan at katuwiran. Nagpapakita ito ng mga halimbawa ng chivalry, kasama ang tapang, katapatan, at kabaitan.

"The Song of Roland" - Ito ay isang epikong tula na naglalarawan ng mga tagumpay at paghihirap ng mga pranses na kawal na nakipaglaban sa mga Muslim sa Espanya noong ika-8 na siglo. Ipinapakita nito ang mga halimbawa ng chivalry, tulad ng pagmamalasakit sa kapwa, katapangan, at kahandaan upang mamatay para sa kanyang bayan.

"The Knight's Tale" ni Geoffrey Chaucer - Ito ay isa sa mga kuwento sa "The Canterbury Tales" na naglalarawan ng pag-iibigan ng dalawang kabalyero sa isang prinsesa. Ipinapakita nito ang mga halimbawa ng kabayanihan, pagmamalasakit sa kapwa, at pagkakaroon ng magandang asal.
