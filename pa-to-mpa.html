If the internal pressure of a 400 mm inside diameter of a cylindrical tank is 10 MPa and the thickness of the wall is 25 mm, the stress developed if the joint efficiency is 95% is approximately 84.2 MPa.

How to solve this problem?

We can see that this problem is a hoop stress problem.Thus, we can use the formula: [tex]\sigma = \frac{pd}{(2t ( \eta )}[/tex]

Where:

[tex]\sigma[/tex] is the hoop stress

P is the pressure

d is the diameter

t is the thickness

[tex]\eta[/tex] is the efficiency

Solving:

[tex]\sigma[/tex] = [tex]\frac{10 MPa (400mm)}{2(25mm)(0.95)(25mm)}[/tex] = 84.2105 MPa

Thus, the correct choice is the letter D.

[tex]Sl = \frac{PD}{4t} [/tex]

where:

Sl = longitudinal stress

t = thickness

Solution:

[tex]t = \frac{(10)(750)}{4(120)} [/tex]

29. what does MPA stands for?​
