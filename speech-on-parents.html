7. A. Write DIRECT for direct speech and REPORTED for indirect speech.1. Father said, "Obey your parents and you will be blessed."​


1. DIRECT

Here is a definition:

DIRECT SPEECH- is the exact words that are reproduced in speech marks or quotation marks.

In the case above; 'Father said, "obey your parents and you will be blessed' is a direct speech since the speaker is reporting the words/phrase(s) in the exact same way it was said to them. Furthermore, it was quote-on-quote, so that way it is a fact that it was the exact words used by the giver of the speaker.

23. pursuiasive speechThe topic is What is the most beneficial parenting style and why?​


Answer:

Studies have found that authoritative parents are more likely to raise confident kids who achieve academic success, have better social skills and are more capable at problem-solving.

Instead of always coming to their kid's rescue, which is more typical among permissive parents, authoritative parents allow their kids to make mistakes. This offers kids the opportunity to learn while also letting them know that their parents will be there to support them.

Authoritative parenting is especially helpful when dealing with conflict, because the way we learn to deal with conflict at a young age plays a big role in how we handle our losses or how resilient we are in our adult lives.

With permissive parents, solutions to conflicts are generally up to the child. The child "wins" and the parent "loses." I've seen this approach lead to kids becoming more self-centered and less able to self-regulate.

Of course, there are times when a punishment, like taking a time out, is necessary. But the problem with constant punishment is that it doesn't actually teach your kid anything helpful. In most cases, it teaches them that the person with the most power wins, fair or not.

Let's say your 10-year-old son begs not to go to soccer practice: "I don't want to because I don't think I'm good at it."

Answer:

There are many different parenting styles, and each one can be beneficial in its own way. However, research has shown that the most effective parenting style is one that is authoritative. This style is characterized by setting high expectations for children, but also being responsive and nurturing.

Authoritative parents are firm and consistent in setting limits for their children, but they also encourage independence and give their children the freedom to make their own choices. This balance of structure and autonomy can help children learn self-discipline and decision-making skills.

Additionally, authoritative parents are warm and supportive, providing their children with emotional support and showing a genuine interest in their lives. This helps children feel loved and valued, which can boost their self-esteem and improve their mental and emotional well-being.

Furthermore, authoritative parenting can lead to better academic and social outcomes for children. Studies have shown that children raised by authoritative parents tend to have higher grades and better social skills. They are also more likely to have positive relationships with their peers and be less likely to engage in risky behaviors.

In conclusion, the most beneficial parenting style is authoritative parenting. This style is characterized by setting high expectations, providing structure and autonomy, being warm and supportive, and leading to better outcomes for children. By adopting this style of parenting, you can help your children grow into confident, well-rounded individuals.
