8. 1. describe how marine and coastal processes result to coastal hazards, and2. explain how coastal erosion, submersion and saltwater intrusion occur.​


Answer:

1.  The coastal zone is the portion of the land area that is affected by aquatic processes. It stretches from the land-bound border of tides, waves, and wind blowing coastal dunes to the point at which waves communicate dramatically with the seabed. The coastal region is a complex area of the Earth's surface, where both aquatic and atmospheric forces create rocky shores, as well as beaches and dunes, walls and tidal inlets and deltas. Atmospheric processes include temperature, moisture and currents, while waves and tides, along with sea temperature and salinity, are the main aquatic processes. The coast also protects diverse habitats, including salt marshes, mangroves, seagrasses and coral reefs. Diverse coastal ecosystem is favoured by shallow seas, ample sunshine, terrestrial and aquatic nutrients, tidal and wave flushing, and a diversity of habitat types. Therefore, The waves have only half the energy to work on the coast. Ocean waves are produced by wind blowing over the surface of the ocean.

2. Coastal cycles are natural phenomena guided by circumstance and exacerbated by human intervention. They inflict coastal destruction as a result of coastal flooding, submersion and saltwater intrusion. ... Seawalls and bulkheads are systems that are constructed parallel to shorelines to shield the coast from wave action.

11. describe the three coastal processes that affect the coastal areas​


Answer:

These are corrasion, abrasion, hydraulic action, attrition and corrosion/solution. Corrasion is when waves pick up beach material (e.g. pebbles) and hurl them at the base of a cliff. Abrasion occurs as breaking waves which contain sand and larger fragments erode the shoreline or headland.
