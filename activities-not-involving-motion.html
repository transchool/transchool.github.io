In physics, motion is the process by which an object's orientation varies over time. Displacement, distance, momentum, inertia, speed, and time are all mathematical concepts used to describe motion. A body's motion is observed by applying a frame of reference to an observer and observing the change in direction of the body relative to that frame as time passes. Kinematics is the branch of physics that studies forces and their effects on the motion; dynamics is the branch that studies forces and their effects on motion.
