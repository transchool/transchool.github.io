3. what is applied ecomics​


Answer:

Applied economics is the application of economic theory and econometrics in specific settings. As one of the two sets of fields of economics (the other set being the core), it is typically characterized by the application of the core, i.e. economic theory and econometrics to address practical issues in a range of fields including demographic economics, labour economics, business economics, industrial organization, agricultural economics, development economics, education economics, engineering economics, financial economics, health economics, monetary economics, public economics, and economic history. From the perspective of economic development, the purpose of applied economics is to enhance the quality of business practices and national policy making.

The process often involves a reduction in the level of abstraction of this core theory. There are a variety of approaches including not only empirical estimation using econometrics, input-output analysis or simulations but also case studies, historical analogy and so-called common sense or the "vernacular". This range of approaches is indicative of what Roger Backhouse and Jeff Biddle argue is the ambiguous nature of the concept of applied economics. It is a concept with multiple meanings. Among broad methodological distinctions, one source places it in neither positive nor normative economics but the art of economics, glossed as "what most economists do".

12. how do ecomic forces faciliatate the deepening of globalization


Explanation:

Economic forces play a significant role in facilitating the deepening of globalization. Here are some ways in which they contribute:

1. Market liberalization: Economic forces have facilitated market liberalization, which has led to increased trade and investment across borders. Countries have reduced barriers to trade and opened up their markets to foreign companies, allowing them to operate and compete on a level playing field.

2. Technological advancements: Technological advancements, particularly in transportation and communication, have made it easier and faster to move goods, services, and capital across borders. This has facilitated the integration of economies and allowed businesses to access new markets and resources.

3. International capital flows: The growth of international capital flows has enabled businesses to access capital from different parts of the world, facilitating investment and growth. This has also led to increased competition among businesses, which has resulted in greater efficiency and innovation.

4. Outsourcing: Economic forces have led to the outsourcing of jobs and production to countries with lower labor costs, resulting in increased trade and investment. This has allowed businesses to lower their costs and increase their profits, while also providing employment opportunities in other parts of the world.

5. Regional integration: Economic forces have facilitated regional integration, with countries forming regional trade agreements and blocs. These agreements have reduced barriers to trade and investment within the region, leading to increased economic activity and integration.

Overall, economic forces have played a significant role in facilitating the deepening of globalization, enabling businesses to access new markets and resources, and contributing to the integration of economies across the world

22. ano ang pagkakaiba ng ecomic growth at economic development?​


Answer:

Ang paglago ng ekonomiya ay nangangahulugang isang pagtaas sa totoong pambansang kita / pambansang output. Ang pagpapaunlad ng ekonomiya ay nangangahulugang isang pagpapabuti sa kalidad ng buhay at pamantayan sa pamumuhay, hal. mga hakbang sa literasiya, pag-asa sa buhay at pangangalaga sa kalusugan. Ceteris paribus, aasahan namin ang paglago ng ekonomiya upang paganahin ang higit na kaunlarang pang-ekonomiya.

Economic growth means an increase in real national income / national output. Economic development means an improvement in the quality of life and living standards, e.g. measures of literacy, life-expectancy and health care. Ceteris paribus, we would expect economic growth to enable more economic development.
