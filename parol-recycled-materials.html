11. How to make a parol? ​


How to Make Your Own Parol

A. Gather the Materials

The kit includes:

10 Flat bamboo sticks5 short bamboo postsString3 sheets Japanese paper

You will need:

ScissorsGlue or paste

B. Make the Frame

Step 1: Make a V-shape with four sticks. Place the ends of 2 sticks with smooth sides up, over 2 sticks with smooth sides down and tie them with 8 turns of string, 1/2 inch from the ends. Form a “V” by separating 2 sticks having opposite sides.

Step 2: Insert 2 sticks to the “V”, overlapping shown, and then tie with 8 turns of string at joint 2.

Step 3: Insert 2 sticks between joints 1 & 2, overlapping sticks as shown, then tie at joint 3.

Step 4: Between joints 1&2, insert two sticks, again in overlapping pattern. Now tie the ends at joints 4 & 5.

Step 5: Align the frame to make a symmetrical five-sided star.

Step 6: Insert the posts at five points by pulling apart the star frame. Tying with string is not needed as the tension of the frame will hold the posts in place.

Let's Compare Photosynthesis and Cellular Respiration

Photosynthesis

  1. Cell structure involved: Chloroplast

  2. Starting materials/ raw materials: carbon dioxide (CO2) and water (H2O)

  3. End product: Glucose (C6H12O6) and Oxygen (O2)

  4. Energy Requirement: light energy/ sunlight

Cellular Respiration

  1. Cell structure involved: Mitochondria

  2. Starting materials/ raw materials: Glucose (C6H12O6) and Oxygen (O2)

  3. End product: carbon dioxide (CO2) and water (H2O)

  4. Energy Requirement: chemical energy/ ATP

Explanation:

Plants make food in the form of glucose by using energy from the sun to transform carbon dioxide (CO2) from air and water (H2O) from the soil. It produces glucose (C6H12O6) and oxygen (O2). This process is called photosynthesis. The process takes place in the chloroplast of the plant cells. This cell organelle contains chlorophyll which helps in capturing light energy. On the other hand, cellular respiration is the reverse of photosynthesis. In this process, both plants and animals break down glucose (C6H12O6) into carbon dioxide (CO2) and water (H2O). The process releases energy in the form of adenosine triphosphate (ATP). The ATP is used for the different processes within the cell that requires energy. The process can take place in the presence or absence of oxygen. Aerobic respiration requires oxygen and produces more ATPs or energy. Anaerobic respiration takes place in the absence of oxygen and produces lactic acid or alcohol and produces a small amount of ATPs. It takes place in the mitochondria.
