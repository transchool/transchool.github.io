9. Why does teachers are in charge for the ballot box during election? ​


Teachers are often chosen to serve as poll workers or in charge of the ballot box during elections because they are considered to be neutral and impartial individuals who are trusted to uphold the integrity of the electoral process.

As educators, teachers are often seen as responsible and trustworthy members of their community, and they are trained to manage complex situations and maintain order in their classrooms. These skills make them well-suited to the task of ensuring that the voting process runs smoothly and that all eligible voters are able to cast their ballots without interference or bias.

In addition, many teachers have experience working with people from diverse backgrounds and have developed strong communication and problem-solving skills, which can be essential when dealing with a large number of voters on election day.

Overall, teachers' commitment to fairness, honesty, and the democratic process make them an ideal choice to help manage elections and ensure that the voices of all citizens are heard.

5. From among the 36 teachers in a college, one principal, one vice- principal and the teacher-in charge are to be appointed.

ANS. 36 x 35 x 34    =  ³⁶P₃

SOLUTION.

- Need to appoint:  

- 1 principal (P), 1 vice principal (VP), 1  teacher in charge (T).

- Principal in 36 ways.  any of teachers.

- Vice principal in 35 ways from any teacher excepting the principal.

- Teacher in charge in 34 ways,  any teacher excepting  principal and vice principal.

- total no. of ways = 36 × 35 × 34

                                 = 42840
