25. what is then writers goal in writing the text about ex-parte motion for extension to submit compromise agreement?


The writer's goal in writing an ex-parte motion for extension to submit compromise agreement is to persuade the court to grant an extension (more time needed). An ex-parte motion means the lawyer is pleading on behalf of one party or an outside party. An compromise agreement, on the other hand is an extra-judicial agreement where two or more parties will agree on terms that is both acceptable to them. This then lessens the case for litigation or court interventions.

We already have a very slow judicial system and if cases that are non-essential still reach the courts, it will overwhelm the system. There are cases where an amicable settlement could be reached, then it will be the better option. Amicable settlement means a compromise agreement.

An example of this is a case of disagreement over child visitation. Fathers have the right to visit their children but most often than not, mothers restrict fathers from visiting their children unless they pay (needless to say, fathers should provide). Instead of going thru a very difficult lawsuit that would negatively affect the child, the parties could just enter into a compromise agreement. This compromise agreement has to be made formal, in writing, and must be presented to the judge for approval.
