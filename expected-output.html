18. Make a list of an expected output during the production process.​


Answer: Here are some examples of expected outputs during the production process:

Project plan: The production process begins with the creation of a project plan that outlines the scope, goals, and timeline of the project. The project plan serves as a roadmap for the production team to follow and helps ensure that the project stays on track.

Design concepts: Depending on the project, the production process may involve the creation of design concepts such as sketches, storyboards, or prototypes. These design concepts help to communicate the vision of the project to stakeholders and ensure that everyone is on the same page.

Production schedule: The production process typically involves the creation of a production schedule that outlines the specific tasks and deadlines for each stage of the project. The production schedule helps to keep the project on track and ensures that all tasks are completed in a timely manner.

Production assets: During the production process, various assets may be created, such as scripts, storyboards, 3D models, animations, or audio files. These assets are used to create the final product and are typically reviewed and approved by stakeholders before moving on to the next stage.

Quality assurance: Throughout the production process, quality assurance is essential to ensure that the final product meets the required standards. This may involve testing and reviewing the product at various stages of development to identify and address any issues.

Final product: The ultimate output of the production process is the final product, whether it's a film, video game, website, or physical product. The final product should meet the objectives outlined in the project plan and be delivered on time and within budget.

Overall, the expected outputs during the production process depend on the nature of the project and the goals of the production team. By setting clear objectives, creating a production plan, and managing the production process effectively, the team can ensure that the final product meets the desired standards and achieves the intended outcome.
