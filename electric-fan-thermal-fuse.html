18. possible problem in thermal fuse​


Answer:

A thermal fuse will explode when the temperature rises over a certain threshold. This is one and often a problem when we talk about thermal fuse. How can you know if a thermal fuse has blown? To see whether your thermal fuse has blown, contact the right side of your multimeter lead to the fuse, then repeat with the left multimeter lead. If the multimeter needle does not move, the thermal fuse has blown. Replacing a thermal fuse is simple and inexpensive, but accessing the fuse may be difficult, and depending on where the manufacturer placed it, it may require a fair amount of disassembly, but disassembling an appliance without prior knowledge or attempting to repair any appliance yourself is extremely dangerous because you could be electrocuted or start a fire in your home.


19. how to test flat iron thermal fuse if it is good or defective


Answer:

Remove the two wire leads from the thermal fuse and touch a multitester probe to each wire terminal on the fuse. If you get a reading of infinity, the fuse is fine. Any reading less than infinity indicates a triggered thermal fuse you will need to replace.
