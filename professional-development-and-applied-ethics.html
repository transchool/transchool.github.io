15. Differentiate personal ethics from professional ethics.


Answer:

Personal ethics refers to the ethics that a person identifies with in respect to people and situations that they deal with in everyday life. Professional ethics refers to the ethics that a person must adhere to in respect of their interactions and business dealings in their professional life. Keeping personal and professional ethics separate requires you to examine them on a case-by-case basis to do right by your business while not selling out your personal beliefs. Review the law. If a certain course of action is illegal, it doesn't matter if your professional and personal ethics are conflicting.
