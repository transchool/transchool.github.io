4. Classify the changes according to Social change, Cultural Change, and Political Change.


Answer:

Social change refers to changes in the structure and organization of society, including changes in demographics, family structure, social institutions, and social norms.

Cultural change refers to changes in the shared beliefs, values, customs, behaviors, and artifacts that characterize a group or society. This can include changes in art, literature, music, fashion, and technology.

Political change refers to changes in the distribution of power and the organization of government, including changes in laws, policies, and political institutions. This can include changes in the form of government, political parties, and the rights and responsibilities of citizens.

22. How will you define social, cultural and political changes?​


Answer:

Social change

Social change involves alteration of the social order of a society. It may include changes in social institutions, social behaviours or social relations.

Cultural change

Culture change is a term used in public policy making that emphasizes the influence of cultural capital on individual and community behavior. It has been sometimes called repositioning of culture, which means the reconstruction of the cultural concept of a society.

Political change

Political change occurs when the rulers in a country lose power or the type of governance in the country changes. Governance is the type of system used to rule a country. Examples of types of governance would be democracy and monarchy. Political change is a normal function of internal and external politics.
