The Real Audiencia de Manila (English: Royal Audience of Manila) was the Real Audiencia of the Spanish East Indies, which included modern-day Guam, the Northern Mariana Islands, Palau, Micronesia and the Philippines. Similar to Real Audiencias throughout the Spanish Empire, it was the highest tribunal within the territories of the Captaincy General of the Philippines, a dependency of the Viceroyalty of New Spain.

The Governor-General of the Philippines was appointed as its highest judge, although on many occasions his absence forced other members to rule the tribunal and assume temporary civilian and military powers.

10. In your how interpretation, why or how did Governor Izquierdo and Pardo de Tavera turn to have different accounts on the Cavite Mutiny?​


Answer:

In my interpretation, they had different accounts on the Cavite Mutiny because of their blood and ties to the people being accounted for. Governor Izquierdo was a Spanish governor, hence was biased towards the Spanish. This is why he claimed that those in the mutiny were trying to overthrow the Spanish government in Cavite. Governor Izquierdo took offense to it. However, Pardo de Tavera was a Filipino historian. He didn't simply look at it one way like Governor Izquierdo, but identified the reasons for the mutiny. This is why Pardo de Tavera claimed that they were not aiming for a revolt, but it was rather a simple strike to express their discontent towards the heavy taxes being levied on them.
