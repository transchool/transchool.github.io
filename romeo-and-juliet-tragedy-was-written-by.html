10. What could have been done to prevent this tragedy from occurring about romeo and Juliet.


“Romeo and Juliet” is a classic tragedy written by William Shakespeare. It follows a story of a young couple. They met and fell in love right at first sight. However, their families were against their relationship. The lovers then tried to trick their families. Sadly, they both ended up dead.

What could have prevented the tragedy? Romeo and Juliet should have been more reasonable. Romeo and Juliet were so drunkenly in love that it made them irrational. They met once but decided that life was worth gambling over for each other. They should have listened to the counsel of the people around them, even just a little bit.  Romeo and Juliet’s families should have reconciled. Both families should have settled their indifferences to move on with their lives in general. But they did not. This pushed Romeo and Juliet to elope. Eventually, the tragedy happened and both families were at loss.  Romeo and Juliet should have been counseled better by the friar. Friar Laurence did not really talk some sense to them. Instead, he condoned the reckless actions of both Romeo and Juliet. He was trusted by the lovers and was of older age than the two. He could have guided them to the right way.  Romeo and Juliet should have improved their plans. Romeo did not know Juliet was only sleeping because the friar’s messenger did not reach him. Friar Laurence and Juliet should have made sure the message reached Romeo before executing the plan.
