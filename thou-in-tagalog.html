4. similarity of I-It and I-thou?


Answer:

According to Buber, human beings may adopt two attitudes toward the world: I-Thou or I-It. I-Thou is a relation of subject-to-subject, while I-It is a relation of subject-to-object. ... I-Thou is a relationship of mutuality and reciprocity, while I-It is a relationship of separateness and detachment.

23. Psalms 22:1-3 My God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me? why art thou so far from helping me, and from the words of my roaring? O my God, I cry in the daytime, but thou hearest not; and in the night season, and am not silent. But thou art holy, O thou that inhabitest the praises of Israel."reflection for this verse​


Although the words in this verse express desperation in the situation due to hardship and suffering, he tries his best to keep his faith in the Lord. The first two sentences express deep sorrow and yet the third tries to reassure his confidence that he, in time, will be put away from his suffering.

Whenever we are put into difficult circumstances, we are often quick to question the Lord and his power. We question why he allows hardships and pain to come in the world. However, in these times is when it is most important for us to hold on to hope and trust in Him as in his power, we are to be lifted from our situation.
