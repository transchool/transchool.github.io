Technology can guide the smooth transition and  support the new ideas of the 12st century learning environment with the means of physical and digital arrangements. It includes online courses which is more common, access to the outside world, and the information given by the online community.

13. POEM ANALYSIS OF 21st Century Haiku Part IV


The 21st Century Haiku Part IV depicts how the world changed. The whole poem showed the transition that happened through time.

In the first stanza, the author seemed to be confused about the time at that moment after checking his phone. This stanza depicts how technology affects us in our daily habits. Watches are usually used to check the time. However, now we have smartphones that show us time easily and quickly.

In the second stanza, the poet used figures of speech when he mentioned the bear without regret can post anything on social media anonymously using a meme.

In the third stanza, the author talked about the advancement of technology yet it still isn't enough to create jetpacks or those that are totally futuristic.

In the fourth stanza, the author mentioned the ancestors who used to hunt just to have something to eat but now we have the resources around us in any supermarket.

In the fifth stanza, he mentioned about how flowers used to be wild and bananas used to have seeds but the advancements in technology and science made it possible for us to create artificial stuff.

Michael Donaghy, ‘Black Ice and Rain’.

Explanation:

Published in 2000 in his collection Conjure, this poem is the earliest on this list, appearing at the very beginning of the current century. Donaghy’s early death, aged just 50, in 2004 robbed the poetry world of more of his poetry, but ‘Black Ice and Rain’ stands as one of the greatest dramatic monologues, spoken by a man at a party who tells a stranger his sorry tale.

This poem takes in everything from religious belief to personal tragedy, treating them with postmodern irony and using a car crash caused by ‘black ice and rain’ as the focus.
