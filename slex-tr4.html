8. A 3 000 kg car moving at a velocity of 30 km/h along SLEX is accelerated by a force of 2 600 N. What will be its velocity after 15 seconds? (neglect friction) ​


Formula:

Newton's second law of motion

[tex]F=ma=m\dfrac{\Delta v}{\Delta t}=m\bigg(\dfrac{v-v_0}{t-t_0} \bigg)[/tex]

Solution:

Given:

[tex]F=2600\text{ N}\\m = 3000\text{ kg}\\v_0 = 30 \text{ km/h}\times\dfrac{1000\text{ m}}{\text{ km}} \times \dfrac{1\text{ hr}}{3600\text{ s}}\\\text{}\quad\:\,=8.333\text{ m/s}\\t = 15\text{ s};\:t_0=0\text{ s}[/tex]

Hence,

[tex]v=\dfrac{F(t-t_0)}{m}+v_0\\\\\text{}\:\,\,=\dfrac{(2600\text{ N})(15\text{ s}-0\text{ s})}{3000\text{ kg}}+8.33\text{ m/s}\\\\\text{}\:\,\,=21.33\text{ m/s}\times\dfrac{1\text{ km}}{1000\text{ m}}\times\dfrac{3600\text{ s}}{1\text{ hr}} \\\\v=\boxed{76.8\text{ km/h}}[/tex]
