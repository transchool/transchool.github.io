3. what is the energy of a motorcycle moving slowly at the top of a hill


Answer:

It has gravitational potential energy and kinetic energy

Explanation:

One of the energies shown in this example is the gravitational potential energy.

Gravitational potential energy is the stored energy from the  Earth's gravitational pull on the object.  

Formula

GPE = mgh

where

GPE     is the gravitational potential energy, the unit is in Joules (J)

m         is the mass, the unit is in kg

h          is the height of the object, the unit is in meter

The gravitational potential energy is directly proportional to the mass, gravitational pull of the Earth and the mass of the object. This means that there is energy due to the vertical position of the object.

Another form of energy is kinetic energy. This is the energy of an object when it is moving. This means that there is no kinetic energy when there is no  motion.

Formula

KE = ¹/₂mv²

where

KE     is the kinetic energy, the unit is in Joules (J)

m      is also the mass of the object, the unit is in kg

v      is the speed or velocity of the moving object, the unit is in m/s

The formula for kinetic energy states that there is kinetic energy when the mass of the object is in motion.

Now, since the motorcycle is moving slowly at the top of a hill, this means that the motorcycle has kinetic energy because it is moving at a slow speed. It also has gravitational potential energy due to its height or  position above the ground.

7. what is the total mechanical energy (TIME) of a 1000 kg motorcycle resting on the top of a 10m hill? g = 9.8m/s²​


#BrainlyFast

Problem:

what is the total mechanical energy (TIME) of a 1000 kg motorcycle resting on the top of a 10m hill? g = 9.8m/s²​

Given Data:

m ( mass ) = 1000 kg

a ( acceleration ) = 9.8 m/s²

h ( height ) = 10 m

Solution:

We can solve the problem using the Formula ( P.E. = m × a × h ).

P.E. = m × a × h

      = 1000 kg × 9.8 m/s² × 10 m

      = 98000 kg·m²/s²

      = 98000 J

total mechanical energy ( TIME = P.E. + K.E. )

TIME = P.E. + K.E.

          = 98000 J + 0

         = 98000 J

Answer:

total mechanical energy  = 98000 J

8. what is the energy of a motorcycle moving slowly at the top of a hill


Answer:

A motorcycle moving slowly at the top of a hill has kinetic energy.

Explanation:

For this question, we can choose from kinetic energy or potential energy. Let us first define these energies so that we can analyze which energy is more appropriate for this situation.

Kinetic energy - the energy of an object because of its motion

Mathematical expression: KE = [tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex] m[tex]v^2[/tex] wherem = mass of objectv = velocity of object

Potential energy - the energy of an object due to its position, arrangement or state.

Mathematical expression: PE = mgh where

m = mass of objectg = gravitational accelerationh = height

Potential energy can be transformed into kinetic energy. For example, a ball is placed on the top of a table. As the ball stays on the table, it posses potential energy. The amount of potential energy of a ball depends on the ball's mass, the height of the table, and the gravitational acceleration. If the ball drops from the table down to the ground, then its potential energy is converted into kinetic energy. As the ball moves downward, potential energy decreases, and the kinetic energy increases.

For our situation, the motorcycle is already moving. Even if it is moving at a slow speed, it is still moving. In addition, it is not stated that the motorcycle is moving down from the top of the hill; it only said that it is moving slowly at the top of the hill, therefore, no height is involved. Hence, the energy of this object is kinetic energy.
