A Shuttlecock

A shuttlecock (also called a birdie or shuttle) is a high-drag projectile used in the sport of badminton. It has an open conical shape formed by feathers or plastic (or a synthetic alternative) embedded into a rounded cork (or rubber) base. The shuttlecock's shape makes it extremely aerodynamically stable. Regardless of initial orientation, it will turn to fly cork first, and remain in the cork-first orientation.

28. How many times can you contact the birdie on your side on the return?


The birdie can only be hit once on each side of the net.

Explanation:

Badminton is a game played by two or four players on a rectangular court with a high net across the middle. The players try to score points by hitting a small object called a shuttlecock (birdie) across the net using a racket. A match consists of the best of 3 games of 21 points. Every time there is a serve there is a point scored.

The shuttlecock or birdie can not be hit more than once in succession Hitting or touching the shuttlecock more than once during play on will result in a foul and points will be awarded to the opponent. Even if for a doubles game (2 people per team) the shuttlecock cannot be hit twice, one of the players must be able to return the shuttlecock to the opponent's playing area to get points.
