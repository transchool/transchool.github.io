Pre-Colonial Form of government.

Before the Spaniards came to the Philippines. There were components of Filipino culture that most Filipinos could not make a distinction about, particularly those who had become citizens recently. Prior to the Spanish colonization of the Philippines, the local population of the Philippines lived in communities known as barangays. A barangay was the smallest unit of governance and had between 30 and 100 families. It was separate from the other group and was led by a datu. Several barangays typically usually live close to one another to protect one another in situations of conflict and other emergencies. The occupant of the rank of datu will pass it on to the eldest son or eldest daughter. Later, though, any barangay member could become a chieftain based on his competence and capabilities. He was responsible for overseeing and maintaining the members of his barangay as necessary. In exchange, they were required to pay tribute to the datu, assist him in cropping, and support him in a fight to defend the barangay. When he wished to pass legislation back in the day, a datu had a council of elders to help him out. A town crier known as the umalohokan wrote the rule down and delivered it to the entire barangay.

Graphic Art

A two-dimensional piece of art known as graphic art is accomplished by utilizing both manual and digital printing techniques. It is feasible to multiply the work by applying appropriate the printing technique. The word "graphics" is derived from the Greek word "graphein," which meant to write or draw.

Industrial art

Industrial art means concept art that gives the impression of being semi-finished or unfinished looks.

27. why is the japanese colonization period considered the golden era of philippine short story and tagalog drama


Between 1941 – 1945, Philippine Literature was interrupted in its development when the Philippines was again conquered by another foreign country, Japan. Philippine literature in English came to a halt. Except for the TRIBUNE and the PHILIPPINE REVIEW, almost all news papers in English were stopped by the Japanese.  This had an advantageous effect on Filipino Literature, which experienced renewed attention because writers in English turned to writing in Filipino. Juan Laya, who used to write in English turned to Filipino because of strict prohibitions of the Japanese regarding any writing in English.  The weekly LIWAYWAY was placed under strict surveillance until it was managed by a Japanese named Ishiwara.  In other words, Filipino literature was given a break during this period. Many wrote plays, poems, short stories, etc. Topics and themes were often about life in the provinces.
