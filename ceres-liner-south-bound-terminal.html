17. A person with mass of 60 kg standing on the dwarf planet Ceres would weigh 16.2 N. What is the strength of gravity on the surface of Ceres? ​


✎The Problem:

A person with mass of 60 kg standing on the dwarf planet Ceres would weigh 16.2 N. What is the strength of gravity on the surface of Ceres?

✎The Concept:

Weight is the amount of gravitational force exerted by a celestial body on an object with mass. The formula can be expressed as:

Weight = Mass × Gravity

✎The Solution:

Given that

Weight: 16.2 NMass: 60 kgGravity: ?

The problem involves modifying the formula for weight. It can now be expressed as:

Gravity = Weight / Mass

So:

Gravity = 16.2 N / 60 kg

Gravity = 0.27 m/s²
