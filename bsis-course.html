1. why did you choose BSIS course??


Answer:

As a matter of fact, I did not choose it, it chose me! I only knew about computer science when I entered University. When speaking to the science course convenor, I told him my passion was people and computers, and then he told me to take information systems as a co-major. Indeed, my times in Information Systems was one of the best times of my life. In particular, in systems analysis and design, we were able to solve real world problems by discovering design patterns - general solutions to commonly occuring patterns. This type of thinking has even helped me in my software development and save much time. Information systems courses are very holistic covering everything from analysis and design, to development and deployment of a software project. Thus through information systems you will learn how to identify peoples problems, and oversee a solution (not always software in some cases) from start to finish, to ultimately solve the users problem, which usually leads to solving a problem to a bigger group too, like society. Information systems helped me work better with people, as almost all the projects were group projects, and helped me handle conflict better. If you can handle people, then you sure can handle computers
