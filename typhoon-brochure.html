20 TERMINOLOGIES AND DEFINITION

a. Earthquake - a natural disaster caused by the shaking of the Earth's surface due to the movement of tectonic plates.

b. fault - a crack in the Earth's crust where two tectonic plates meet and move relative to each other.

c. typhoon - a tropical cyclone that forms in the western Pacific Ocean.

d. comet - a small, icy celestial body that releases gas and dust as it approaches the sun.

e. asteroid - a small, rocky celestial body that orbits the sun.

f. meteor - a small, rocky or metallic object that enters the Earth's atmosphere and burns up.

f. Richter scale - a logarithmic scale used to measure the magnitude of earthquakes.

g. seismic waves - waves of energy that travel through the Earth's crust and cause earthquakes.

h. plate tectonics - the scientific theory that explains the movement of the Earth's crustal plates and their interactions.

i. epicenter - the point on the Earth's surface directly above the focus of an earthquake.

j. aftershock - a smaller earthquake that follows the main shock of a larger earthquake.

k. fault line - the line that separates two tectonic plates that are moving relative to each other.

l. subduction zone - a region where one tectonic plate is forced beneath another and sinks into the mantle.

m. volcanic eruption - the release of ash, gas, and magma from a volcano.

n. tsunamis - large ocean waves caused by earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, or meteor impacts.

o. landslide - the movement of rock, soil, or debris down a slope due to gravity.

p. ground rupture - a crack in the Earth's surface caused by movement along a fault.

q. intensity - a measure of the shaking produced by an earthquake at a specific location.

r. magnitude - a measure of the size of an earthquake, usually expressed as the Richter magnitude scale.

FIVE QUESTIONS

a. Why do earthquakes happen?

Earthquakes happen due to the movement of tectonic plates and the release of stored energy in the Earth's crust.

b. How does plate tectonics contribute to earthquakes?

Plate tectonics contributes to earthquakes because it causes the movement of tectonic plates, which can create stress and strain in the Earth's crust that eventually leads to earthquakes.

c. Why are earthquakes more common in some areas than others?

Earthquakes are more common in some areas than others because they are caused by the movement of tectonic plates, and these plates are more active in some regions than others.

d. How are tsunamis generated by earthquakes?

Tsunamis are generated by earthquakes when the movement of tectonic plates causes the seafloor to rise or fall suddenly, creating large ocean waves.

e. How can we predict earthquakes?

Earthquakes can be predicted to some extent by monitoring the Earth's crust for changes in the level of stress and strain, and by studying the pattern of past earthquakes in a region.

Answer:

Before Typhoon

Keep yourself updated. With the various media platforms, it is next to impossible to not be updated of the supposed typhoon’s movement.

Inspect and clean up your house especially your drainage system. Check to see if there’s a need to fix your house such as holes on your roofs, damaged doors, windows or ceilings. Have them fixed as soon as possible. Do check as well your drainage system. If there’s clogging, have them fixed or remove any material that may possibly cause the clogging.

Store ample amount of ready-to-eat foods and water. Make sure that the foods and water that you will store is adequate enough for you and your family and will last for few days. Ready-to-eat foods such as canned/packed foods are especially helpful when cooking is no longer feasible.

If you have second or third floors or any part in your house that is elevated, have your things especially those that generate electricity placed in there. This will keep water from coming in contact with these stuffs.

If you are advised to evacuate, do so. These people who will inform you of the need for evacuation are more knowledgeable than anyone else. So don’t be hard-headed, at least not during this time of distress.

Always have your emergency kits with you. Your emergency kit may include first-aid supplies, candles, flashlights, life vests and battery-operated radio.

During Typhoon

If no advice to evacuate was announced, then better stay inside your house. Keep yourself calm and postpone any scheduled travels.

Keep on monitoring the typhoon’s movement through your television, radio or the internet.

If you see signs of water rising, better turn off the main sources of electricity. Do keep your electric-powered items stored in higher areas and refrain from using them during flood.

Do not wade along flooded areas to keep yourself from contacting water-borne diseases. If it is inevitable, wear protective gears such as raincoats and boots to protect yourself.

If you are told to evacuate, calmly comply.

After the Typhoon

Monitor and update yourself with the progress of the typhoon through radio, television or the internet (should this be accessible).

If your house was one of the heavily damaged, make sure that you heed to the advice of the authorities regarding its safety and stability. If they are uncertain of your house’s condition, do not go yet. If the house didn’t have that much damage, have your house inspected and do the needed repairs immediately. Just be safe in inspecting your house and avoid scattered debris.

Watch out for live wires or any electrical outlet that may be submerged in water. If you don’t have the sufficient knowledge on electrical wirings, have a knowledgeable person inspect these wires as well as your appliances before you actually use them again.

Wear protective gears such as boots. Water-borne diseases such as Leptospirosis do not only spread out during typhoon but also after the typhoon. The bacteria that cause this disease may still be present in moist soils or scattered debris and if these contacts with your open wound, the chances of developing such disease is very much likely.

Boil water before drinking as they may be contaminated.

Clean up. Dispose things that may be a ground for mosquitoes to breed. Such stuffs may include tires, cans or pots.

Suplementary Brochures -

Often compared to booklets. Considered one of the simplest brochures to create, half-fold consists of two internal panels and a front and back cover.

Promotional Brochures -

Produced by making two bends. The size of the panels must be equal.

Leave-behind Brochures -

One of the obvious advantages is this format’s transformability. It offers more room for the designer’s imagination.

Point of Sale Brochures -

The form of this brochure looks like a zig-zag. Put simply, it includes six parts of equal size, created by double bending.
