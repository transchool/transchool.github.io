12. Assignment: (SCIENCE) will be checked on September 6, 2022. A. Find the definition of the following terms: 1. Epicenter 2. Magnitude 3. Focus 4. Seismic Waves 5. Triangulation


Answer:

Definition of the following :

1. Epicenter - the part of the earth's surface directly above the focus of an earthquake.

2. Magnitude - a measure of the total amount of energy released by an earthquake, as indicated on the Richter scale.

3. Focus - the focus of an earthquake is a point at some depth in the earth from where radiation of seismic waves starts.

4. Seismic Waves - are caused by the sudden movement of materials within the Earth, such as slip along a fault during an earthquake. Volcanic eruptions, explosions, landslides, avalanches, and even rushing rivers can also cause seismic waves.

5. Triangulation - the measurement of the elements necessary to determine the network of triangles into which any part of the earth's surface is divided in surveying.

Answer:

Difference Between Joint and Disjoint Set

Consider two sets X and Y. Assume that both the sets X and Y are non-empty sets. Thus, X ⋂ Y is also a non-empty set, the sets are called joint set. In case, if X ⋂ Y results in an empty set, then it is called the disjoint set.
