1. why Amorsolo painted Defense of the filipina woman's honor


Answer:

Because during the Japanese occupation of the Philippines during World War II, Amorsolo’s customer base dissipated due to the hard economic times. It was a real challenge to him to acquire painting supplies. The transportation to rural places were difficult to find and so it deprived Amorsolo from visiting these places where he normally painted his popular landscapes. Typical pastoral scenes were replaced by the depictions of a war-torn nation. He spent his days at his home near the Japanese garrison, where he sketched war scenes from the house's windows or rooftop. He documented the destruction of many landmarks in Manila and the pain, tragedy and death experienced by Filipino people, with his subjects.  

Explanation:

About the painting "Defense of the Filipina Woman's Honor"

• It is one of Amorsolo's famous war era painting is the “Defense of a Filipina Woman's Honor”  

• The painting showed a Filipino man holding a bolo about to defend a woman who is either his wife or daughter, from being raped by an unseen Japanese soldier.

Other painting of Amorsolo during World War II

The rape of Manila (1942) Bombing of Intendencia (1942) The Explosion (1944)

Amorsolo frequently portrayed the lives and suffering of Filipina women during World War II.

The wartime paintings of Amorsolo were exhibited at the Malacañang Presidential Palace.

7. honor is _________ (s-lv-c example) ​


Answer:

Explanation:

5 examples of S-LV-C adjective

are:

Mother is industrious.

Children are noisy.

Babies are cute.

Rhodora seems nervous.

I am tall.

S-LV-C stands for

S - Subject

LV -Linking Verb

C - Complement

Subject  

It is what we talk about in  sentence. It can be in a form of a nour or a pronoun. It is the doer of the action or the main verb but in the pattern S-LV-C, there is no action verb but only complement.

Linking Verb

Linking verb is a verb that links the subject and the other words in the sentence like another noun or an adjective. It does not tell an action word nor it precedes an action word. Linking verbs used are “be” verbs (am, is, are, was, were) or sensory verbs (appear, feel, look, grow) or what we call the auxilliary verbs because these are not action verbs.

Complement

It is the same thing as the subject or this is the description of the subject. Complement can also be called as modifier of the subject. In this case, the pattern asked is S-LV-C adjective so the complement in the given examples are adjectives.

In sentence number one in the given examples, “Mother is industrious”, Mother is the subject,' is' is the linking verb and industrious is the adjective complement. It is an adjective because it describes the subject. It tells something about mother so it is called a complement of the subject complement in the sentence.

25. It is the term used to refer to the priest , either man or woman who held honorable office


Answer

The title used for priest and priestess that held a high or honorable position is called the "Reverend" or "His Reverence". It is commonly used to address priests and nuns in offices of the Catholic faith.

"Reverend" or "Reverence" is an honorific added before the name of a clergy and/or minister. The word "Reverend" originated from the Latin word "Revereri" which in English means, to respect or to revere.

For Catholic priestesses, the term "Mother" or "Sister" is also added between the "Reverend" and the name. For Catholic Priests, the term "Father" and "Brother" is used. Examples would be "Reverend Mother Olga" and His "Reverence Father J. Francis". Non-catholic Christians use the term "Pastor" (both for male and female) to address their church's leaders.
