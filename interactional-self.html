2. how the self and the me self interact with each other​


Answer:

Mead studied philosophy & sociology and developed the Theory  of the social self based on the argument that the self is a social emergent.  His belief was that are three activities through which the self is developed and are Language, Play & Game.   Language allows the person to take on the "role of the other" allowing them to respond to his/her own gestures. Play allows the individual to "take on the role of others" (pretend) or (role playing) allowing them to express the expectations of significant others.  Game allows the individual to comprehend the rules of the game.

Basically the "self" has two sides  the "Me" represents the expectations & attitudes of others and is often known as the organized set of attitudes of others assumed by the individual.  The "I" is the response to the "Me" or the person's individuality a major instrument of social control or the way the community exercises control over the conduct of its individual members.

DEFINITION OF INTERACTIONAL SELF:

Interactional self is the descriptions of someone's strengths or advantages and weaknesses in intimate relationships. Besides, it's also about relationships to family, friends, society, and strangers, in social settings.

EXPLANATION:

THREE TYPES OF INTERACTIONAL SELF:

In every active interaction implies the existence of interpersonal communication, and vice versa, every interpersonal communication contains interaction. It's hard to separate the two. On that basis, Shaw (1976:10) distinguishes interaction into three types, namely verbal interaction, physical interaction, and emotional interaction.

Verbal interaction occurs when two or more people make contact with each other by means of articulation. The process occurs in the form of exchanging conversations with each other.Physical interaction that occurs when two or more people make contact by using body languages. For example facial expressions, body position, body movements, and eye contact.Emotional interaction occurs when individuals make contact with each other by expressing feelings. For example, shedding tears as a sign of sadness, emotion, or even too happy.

14. Self perception via social interaction


The self-perception via social interaction is a situation in which an individual gets his/her self-perception through the influence he/she gets from social interaction.

Detailed explanation:

Self-perception is an individual's view of himself that he gets from the results of learning or experience from the surrounding environment, which can be from family, friends, co-workers, and others. Simply put, self-perception is the way a person perceives himself.While social interaction is a reciprocal relationship between two or more people that is dynamic and can affect the individual, one of which is the way he views or perceives himself.

For example, A child who is often ridiculed by his bad friends will have low self-esteem, he will look at and feel himself really ugly. While another child, who finds supportive friends who always support him and give him appreciation, will have high self-confidence, he will see and feel he is a gifted person.
