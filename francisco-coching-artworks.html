13. materials used in the artworks of the two national artist Carlo Francisco and Guillermo tolenteno​


Answer:

The Philippines is home to a long and rich history of excellence in visual arts. This tradition started as early as the 19th century with Damian Domingo, also known as the Father of Filipino Painting, who was the first Filipino to paint a self-portrait. He was followed by greats such as Juan Luna, who painted the world-renowned Spoliarium, andFabián de la Rosa, uncle and mentor to Fernando Amorsolo.

Mastery of the visual arts has been passed down through the decades, and the Philippine government has sought to reward that through the Order of National Artists. The Order of National Artists, also known as Orden ng mga Pambansang Alagad ng Sining, is the highest national recognition given to Filipinos who have made significant contributions to the growth and development of arts in the Philippines. Jointly administered by the National Commission for Culture and the Arts (NCCA) and the Cultural Center of the Philippines (CCP), it’s an honor conferred by the President of the Republic based on recommendations by both institutions. Here’s a list of the greatest names in Philippine visual arts who have been given this honor.

You can’t talk about Philippine painting without talking about Fernando Amorsolo, who is also known as the Grand Old Man of Philippine Art. Born in Manila, he was educated at the Art School of the Liceo de Manila, the University of the Philippines School of Fine Arts, and spent time abroad in Spain and the United States. Lauded for his skill in depicting light and form, Amorsolo is well known for his paintings of idyllic Philippine rural life. He rejected Western ideals of beauty and sought to promote Filipino beauty instead, and his use of chiaroscuro became an artistic trademark that set his work apart from the rest. Some of his most famous pieces include Maiden in a Stream (1921), The Mestiza (1943), and Planting Rice (1946).

27. 1. How can you say that a visual artwork has movement? 2. When do you say that an artwork has harmony? 3. How can your knowledge of the elements and principles of art help you in appreciating the mural painting of Carlos “Botong” Francisco entitled Bayanihan?​


1. A visual artwork has movement, of course. The dance itself is an artistic expression primarily based on movement. Movement is also used in visual art but in a unique way. The illusion of action in a work of art is achieved through the artistic principle of visible movement. Any part of a composition, or the complete thing, can undergo motion.

The visual aspects of art, including colour, line, texture, shape, form, value, and space, can convey the sense of motion in a work of art. Incorporating these components in various configurations allows for a wide range of possible motion.

The artist uses the principle of movement to lead the viewer's eye into, around, and out of work.

2. Harmony in art generally is created when an artwork's elements merge coherently. Some details can be reused without losing their cohesiveness overall. Neither monotony nor anarchy characterizes harmony. The two go together so well.

There are various ways of expressing harmony. Use colour as a medium, and create a balance between the marks and the shapes you paint.

3. Carlos Francisco created several works, one of which is titled Bayanihan. The artwork titled Bayanihan, which translates to "becoming a bayan," is often used to symbolize a strong sense of solidarity and collaboration among members of a community.

The Tagalog word "bayan," which means "nation," "town," or "community," is the source of the Filipino custom known as Bayanihan (buy-uh-nee-hun). The Filipino concept of "bayanihan," or "being in a bayan," is central to Filipino identity since it emphasizes the importance of community and cooperation.

Carlos Francisco's prints and, later, more personal paintings epitomize Romanticism's emphasis on the individual, the subjective, the imaginative, and the emotional.
