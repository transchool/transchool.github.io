Sperm Transport in the Female Reproductive Tract

Sperm transport within the female reproductive tract is a cooperative effort between the functional properties of the sperm and seminal fluid on the one hand and cyclic adaptations of the female reproductive tract that facilitate the transport of sperm toward the ovulated egg. Much of the story of sperm transport in the female reproductive system involves the penetration by the sperm of various barriers along their way toward the egg.

During coitus in the human, semen is deposited in the upper vagina close to the cervix. The normal environment of the vagina is inhospitable to the survival of sperm, principally because of its low pH.
