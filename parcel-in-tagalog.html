2. parcel b is 3 times as heavy as parcel a. the total mass of parcel a and parcel b is 5 kg. how heavy is parcel a?​


Answer:

1,25 kg

Step-by-step explanation:

Parcel b = 3 x mass of parcel a

a + b = 5 kg

How heavy is parcel a?

Parcel b = 3 x mass of parcel a

b = 3a

a + b = 5 kg

a + 3a = 5 kg

4a = 5 kg

a = ⁵/₄ kg

a = 1 ¹/₄ kg

a = 1 ²⁵/₁₀₀ kg

a = 1,25 kg

3. The mass of parcel A is thrice the mass of the parcel B. If parcel A is 12kg heavier than a parcel B, what is the total mass of both parcels?


Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

Let us represent the mass of parcel A as A and the mass of parcel B as B. We can then express the given as the following expressions:

A = 3B

A = B + 12

Notice that the LHS (Left Hand Side) of both equations are A. Hence, we can equate their RHS (Right Hand Side). This can be also thought of as substituting the value of A from the other equation.

3B = B + 12

2B = 12

B = 6

Substitute into either equation in order to get A,

A = 3(6)

A = 18

A = 6 + 12

A = 18

Therefore, the total mass of the parcels is 6 + 18 or 24 kg.
