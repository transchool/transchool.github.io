The story of “The World is an Apple” deals with the struggle of Filipino in the lower bracket of society in their everyday life. It depicts social…show more content…

Mario’s family happened to be in a low state of living in which they were deprived to had a decent meal on the grounds that they were poor and didn’t have the capacity to purchase the family’s needs. Mario had to struggle every day to feed his family. He is a father whose primary instinct was to consider the family’s survival regardless that it implied letting himself fall in the trap of temptation. The apple symbolizes the temptation. It gives off an impression of being big and red that could pull in a man to have it unlike the one that Mario gave to his daughter, Tita, instead ―a small green apple “but she just threw it away, saying it was not a real apple. Then she cried.” In this way, what Mario could provide for his little girl was insufficient to sustain her needs. Her daughter was undernourished. She needs nutritious food unlike the “lugao” or “biscuits” that his wife, Gloria, buys for her that’s just what their money could afford. That’s the reason he was enticed to steal an apple for his daughter suspecting that it would make her happy in light of the fact that he had by one mean or another figured out how he could reconsider the possible result of his action, that he was committing pilfering and might lose something important to him ― his job. He did not think “to bring home pandesal, or rice, or milk and not those ‘delicious’ apple” that are just for the rich because it is what her daughter’s want.
