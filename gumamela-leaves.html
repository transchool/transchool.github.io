The gumamela is a fast-growing evergreen shrub with dark green and glossy leaves with dicot flowers. The leaves are oval in shape, and they measure between 3 inches to 4.2 inches long. 17. Your mother will be asking you to help propagate plants using the leaves. Which leaves of plants will you choose for reproduction? * 1 point A. Fern and moss B. Santan and Violet C. Katakataka and Begonia D. Sunflowers and Gumamela This is a required question​


A. Fern and Moss

Ferns are a group of plants with a true vascular system (Tracheophyta) but never produce seeds for sexual reproduction. Instead of seeds, this group of plants releases spores as a means of dissemination and propagation, resembling groups of organisms such as mosses and fungi.

Ferns are distributed in all parts of the world, except for areas of perennial snow and oceans, with a tendency to be found growing in places that are infertile for agriculture. The total known species is about 12,000, with an estimated 1,300 to 3,000 more species of which grow in the Malesia region.

Moss plants are a group of small plants that are included in Bryophytina. This plant has shown clear differentiation between nutrient absorbing organs and photosynthetic organs but does not yet have true roots and leaves. This group of plants also do not have true vessels. Instead of roots, the nutrient absorbing organs are rhizoids.

Moss plant leaves can photosynthesize. Mosses are pioneer plants, which grow somewhere before other plants can grow. This happens because moss plants are small but form colonies that can cover a large area. Dead plant tissue becomes a source of nutrients for other mosses and other plants.
