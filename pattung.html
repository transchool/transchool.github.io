1. What is the pattung​


Explanation:

The pattung is used by the baki (native priest) during rituals and ceremonies. Larger ones are played by men in the himong revenge dance. Other areas in Ifugao refer to the instrument as bangibang.


2. What is the Similarities of Toppaya Style and Pattung Style?​


Answer:

toppaya" style, the musicians play the surface of the gangsa with their hand while in a sitting position, with a single gangsa resting on the lap of each musician. In the "pattung" style, a gangsa is suspended from the musician's left hand and played with a padded stick held in the musician's right hand. In the "pattung" style of playing, the players are standing, or they keep in step with the dancers while bending forward slightly.


3. Compare the toppaya style and Pattung style of playing Gangsa. ​


Answer:

Gangsa is played in two ways. One way is called "toppaya" and the other is called "pattung." In "toppaya" style, the musicians play the surface of the gangsa with their hand while in a sitting position, with a single gangsa resting on the lap of each musician.

GANGSA

The gangsa is the most famous musical instrument of the Cordilleran people in the northern Philippines. The gangsa is a flat bronze metal gong without a boss at its center. It is used as an ensemble instrument that is played in two ways. The two manners of playing the gangsa are gangsa toppaya and gangsa pattung. In gangsa pattung style, all the players in the ensemble are in standing position. The gangsa is suspended and held by one hand of the player while the other hand strikes the surface of the gangsa with a wooden mallet or a padded stick. Each gangsa in the ensemble are arranged according to their sizes, producing different indefinite pitches as a result of the vibration of the body of the gangsa as it is being hit by striking its surface. The staggered entrances of sounds created by the gangsa players in an interlocking manner creates a resultant melody distinctive of the music of the Cordillerans.
