13. сатуFrom Ezekiel Moreno's life story, what does it take to be missionary?​


Answer:

Saint Ezekiel Moreno

August 19

Ezekiel (1848-1906) was an Augustinian Recollect known for his simple and open spirit. He was deeply devoted to the Sacred Heart of Jesus. He ministered for many years in foreign lands. He stongly defended the rights of the Church.

He was born in Alfaro, Logrono, Spain, April 9, 1848. His family was poor in material goods but committed in faith. Ezekiel professed his vows as an Augustinian Recollect in 1865. He was ordained a priest in Manila, Philippines in 1871.

During the next 15 years, he worked at bringing the Gospel of Jesus to the people of the Philippines. He was called back to Spain in 1885. There he served as Rector of the College and Novitiate of Monteagudo.

He then went to Colombia in 1888, where he devoted himself to missionary activity. He also brought renewal to the Augustinian Recollect communities in the region where he was living and working.

Ezekiel was in 1894 named the first Vicar Apostolic of Casanare, and ordained bishop of Pasto, where he remained until 1906. During this time there was war in Colombia. Ezekiel showed himself a voice of strength for his people.

In 1906 a diagnosis of cancer caused him to reluctantly return to Spain for treatment.

There he died August 19, 1906. He was beatified by Pope Paul VI in 1975 and canonized by Pope John Paul II in 1992.

19. what is ezekiel's unique prophetic style?​


Answer:

Ezekiel, also spelled Ezechiel, Hebrew Yeḥezqel, (flourished 6th century BC), prophet-priest of ancient Israel and the subject and in part the author of an Old Testament book that bears his name. Ezekiel’s early oracles (from c. 592) in Jerusalem were pronouncements of violence and destruction; his later statements addressed the hopes of the Israelites exiled in Babylon. The faith of Ezekiel in the ultimate establishment of a new covenant between God and the people of Israel has had profound influence on the postexilic reconstruction and reorganization of Judaism.

25. 3. Message: What themes lie at the heart of Ezekiel’s prophetic message? 34:1-6 and 37:1-14. The prophet of Ezekiel.


CHAPTER 34:1-6

Ezekiel's prophetic message is focused on the theme of proper leadership. In chapter 34, he addresses the issue of poor leadership in Israel, criticizing the leaders for failing to care for and protect the people. He accuses them of being selfish and self-serving and calls on them to take responsibility for the well-being of the nation and to serve as true shepherds to their flock. Ezekiel presents himself as a model of good leadership, calling on the leaders to follow his example and to take their duties seriously.

CHAPTER  37:1-14

In chapter 37, Ezekiel presents the vision of the "valley of dry bones," which symbolizes the potential for Israel to be reborn and restored after a period of spiritual and political stagnation. The bones come together and are given new life, representing the rebirth of the nation and the renewal of its covenant with God. This vision serves as a reminder that even in the darkest times, there is hope for those who turn to God and seek his guidance.

SUMMARIZE

Overall, Ezekiel's message emphasizes the need for robust and responsible leadership and the potential for renewal and restoration for those who turn to God. He calls on the leaders of Israel to take their responsibilities seriously and to serve as true shepherds to the people. He also encourages the people to have faith in God's plan and to trust in his power to restore and renew their nation.
